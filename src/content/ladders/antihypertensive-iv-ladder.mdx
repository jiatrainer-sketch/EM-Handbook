## 🎯 Purpose

Stepwise IV antihypertensive selection ใน hypertensive emergency — choose agent by **end-organ damage + comorbidity**

**ใช้ใน:** BP &gt; 180/120 + acute end-organ damage (encephalopathy, ACS, APE, dissection, eclampsia, AKI)

---

## 🎯 BP Reduction Goals

| Scenario | Target | Timeframe |
|---|---|---|
| **Most HT emergency** | ↓ MAP 20–25% in **1st hr** | Then to 160/100 in 2–6 hr |
| **Aortic dissection** | SBP **&lt; 120** + HR &lt; 60 | **ASAP** (&lt; 20 min) |
| **Ischemic stroke (not for tPA)** | ≤ 220/120 — permissive | No rapid ↓ |
| **Ischemic stroke (tPA candidate)** | &lt; 185/110 before + &lt; 180/105 × 24 hr | Before + during tPA |
| **Hemorrhagic stroke / ICH** | SBP **&lt; 140** | &lt; 1 hr (INTERACT-3) |
| **Eclampsia / severe pre-eclampsia** | SBP &lt; 160, DBP &lt; 110 | &lt; 60 min |
| **ACS / APE** | ↓ gradual, target comfort + symptom relief | |

---

## 🪜 Step 1 — Nicardipine (first-line in most)

### Indication
- **Versatile default** — good in most HT emergency
- Neuro (ICH, SAH, encephalopathy), AKI, eclampsia, post-op
- **Not** in severe aortic stenosis, decompensated HF

### Dose
- Start **5 mg/hr** IV infusion
- ↑ 2.5 mg/hr q 5–15 min → max **15 mg/hr**
- Once target reached → maintenance 3 mg/hr

### Pros / Cons
- ✅ Titratable, no reflex tachycardia (some), safe in renal failure
- ❌ Peripheral vein irritation (central preferred if &gt; 12 hr)

---

## 🪜 Step 2 — Labetalol (alternative)

### Indication
- **Dissection** (combine with nitroprusside or esmolol), **pregnancy**, pheochromocytoma (after α-block), sympathetic excess
- β-blockade + α-blockade (7:1 IV)

### Dose
- **Bolus:** 20 mg IV over 2 min; repeat q 10 min, doubling (40, 80, 80) max **300 mg cumulative**
- **Infusion:** 0.5–2 mg/min (start 2 mg/min)
- Avoid: bronchospasm, bradycardia, 2°/3° AV block, decompensated HF, cocaine-induced

---

## 🪜 Step 3 — Esmolol (dissection, rapid titrate)

### Indication
- **Aortic dissection** — HR control essential (target &lt; 60)
- Post-op, thyroid storm, perioperative HT

### Dose
- **Loading:** 500 mcg/kg IV × 1 min
- **Infusion:** start 50 mcg/kg/min → ↑ 50 q 5 min → max **300 mcg/kg/min**
- t½ 9 min → rapid on/off

---

## 🪜 Step 4 — Nitroglycerin (NTG)

### Indication
- **ACS, pulmonary edema** (preload reduction dominant)
- NOT first-line for pure HT (venodilator &gt;&gt; arterial)

### Dose
- Start **10–20 mcg/min** IV
- ↑ 5–10 mcg/min q 5 min → max 200 mcg/min

### Cons
- Tachyphylaxis &gt; 24 hr
- Avoid: **recent PDE-5 inhibitor** (sildenafil/tadalafil — severe hypotension)
- Avoid: RV infarct (preload-dependent)

---

## 🪜 Step 5 — Nitroprusside (last-line, rarely used now)

### Indication
- Refractory HT emergency, aortic dissection (combine with β-blocker first)
- **Rarely needed** with modern agents

### Dose
- Start **0.3 mcg/kg/min** → max 10 mcg/kg/min
- Max ขนาดยา ใน 24 hr to avoid cyanide toxicity

### Risks
- **Cyanide / thiocyanate toxicity** (> 48 hr, renal failure) — monitor lactate, anion gap
- Steal phenomenon in CAD (↓ coronary perfusion)
- Light-sensitive (wrap in foil)
- Requires close monitoring (art line preferred)

---

## 🪜 Step 6 — Hydralazine (pregnancy, simple cases)

### Indication
- **Severe pre-eclampsia / eclampsia** (historic first-line, nicardipine/labetalol preferred now)
- Where infusion pump unavailable

### Dose
- 5–10 mg IV q 20 min (max 20–30 mg cumulative)
- Onset 10–20 min, duration 3–8 hr

### Cons
- Reflex tachycardia (avoid in dissection, ACS)
- Unpredictable response → hypotension risk

---

## 🗺 Scenario → Agent choice

| Scenario | First-line | Alt / avoid |
|---|---|---|
| **Encephalopathy / AKI** | **Nicardipine** | Labetalol; avoid nitroprusside in renal failure |
| **Stroke (HIC)** | **Nicardipine** | Labetalol |
| **Ischemic stroke / tPA window** | **Nicardipine** or labetalol | — |
| **ACS / APE** | **NTG** ± labetalol | — |
| **Aortic dissection** | **Esmolol** → then nicardipine or NTG (β-block FIRST) | Avoid hydralazine (reflex tachy) |
| **Eclampsia** | **Labetalol** or **hydralazine** or **nicardipine** | Avoid nitroprusside (cyanide in fetus) |
| **Pheochromocytoma** | **Phentolamine** (α-block) → then β-block | NEVER β-block alone |
| **Cocaine-induced** | Benzo + **phentolamine / nitro** | NEVER β-blocker (unopposed α) |
| **Decompensated HF + HT** | **NTG** + loop diuretic | Avoid β-block acute |

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Critical Pitfalls)

<Callout type="danger" title="IV Antihypertensive Pitfalls">

**ลด BP เร็วเกินไป** — target ↓ 20–25% MAP ใน 1 hr  
→ เร็วกว่านั้นเสี่ยง ischemic stroke, AKI, MI (permissive HT concept)

**ใช้ β-blocker alone ใน cocaine / pheochromocytoma** — unopposed α vasoconstriction  
→ ใช้ benzo + phentolamine / CCB / nitro ก่อน

**Hydralazine ใน dissection** — reflex tachycardia เพิ่ม shear stress  
→ β-blocker FIRST (esmolol / labetalol), then vasodilator

**Nitroprusside &gt; 48 hr** — cyanide toxicity (โดยเฉพาะ renal failure)  
→ Monitor lactate, acidosis, mental change; switch เมื่อได้

**PO agent ใน true HT emergency** — erratic absorption, no titration  
→ IV pump essential; PO after stabilization

**Use IV clonidine / diazoxide** — obsolete, no advantage  
→ Stick to nicardipine / labetalol / esmolol / NTG

**Asymptomatic "HT urgency" treated as emergency** — no end-organ damage  
→ Oral regimen; don't crash BP

</Callout>
