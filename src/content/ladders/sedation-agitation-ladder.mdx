## 🎯 Purpose

Stepwise approach to acute agitation in ED — **verbal de-escalation → PO → IM → IV**, matched to etiology

**ใช้ใน:** Severe agitation (violent, self-harm risk, impeding care) ใน ED

---

## 🪜 Step 0 — Verbal de-escalation + safety

- **Calm, reassuring** tone; single staff member designated speaker
- Remove audience; safe environment (no objects to throw/grab)
- Offer food, water, phone call (simple needs)
- **Always try first** — often sufficient in psychiatric agitation without delirium

**ถ้ายังไม่ได้ → ไปขั้นต่อไปพร้อม consider chemical restraint**

---

## 🎯 Choose agent by etiology

| Cause | First-line agent |
|---|---|
| **Primary psychiatric** (psychosis, mania) | Haloperidol or olanzapine + lorazepam |
| **Delirium / medical** | Haloperidol monotherapy (avoid benzo unless withdrawal) |
| **Alcohol / benzo withdrawal** | **Benzo** (diazepam / lorazepam) — NOT haloperidol |
| **Stimulant / sympathomimetic** (cocaine, meth) | **Benzo first** (midazolam / lorazepam); avoid antipsychotic |
| **Unknown + severe** | **Ketamine** (see step 3) |
| **Head injury / hypoxia / hypoglycemia** | Fix underlying cause first; sedate as needed |

---

## 🪜 Step 1 — Oral (cooperative patient)

### Olanzapine (Zyprexa)
- **10 mg PO ODT** (orally disintegrating) — fast onset 15–30 min
- Safer than haloperidol (less EPS, QT risk lower)
- ไม่ใช้ IM + benzo ใน 1 hr (respiratory depression warning)

### Lorazepam (Ativan)
- **2 mg PO** — if anxiety / withdrawal component
- 30–60 min onset

### Combo (mild-moderate)
- Olanzapine 10 mg ODT + lorazepam 2 mg PO

<Callout type="info" title="Project BETA principle">

Avoid restraint if possible; offer medication as **collaborative** step.  
Phrasing: "Would you like something to help you feel calm?"

</Callout>

---

## 🪜 Step 2 — IM (uncooperative, not severe)

### Haloperidol 5 mg IM + lorazepam 2 mg IM ("B-52")
- Classic combo; onset 15–30 min
- Avoid benzo ใน delirium (paradoxical)
- Avoid IM olanzapine + IM benzo (hypoxia/hypotension warning)

### Droperidol 5 mg IM (if available)
- Faster onset than haloperidol
- ECG QT &gt; 440 ms = caution (FDA black box, debated)

### Midazolam 5 mg IM
- **Alcohol/stimulant intoxication**
- Fast IM absorption (unlike lorazepam)
- Short t½ → may need redose

### Ziprasidone 10–20 mg IM
- 2nd-gen antipsychotic, less EPS

**Reassess 15 min** — redose if inadequate (haloperidol 5 mg, midazolam 2.5 mg)

---

## 🪜 Step 3 — IV or IM (severe, emergent)

### Midazolam IV
- **2.5–5 mg IV q 5 min** titrate — endpoint: calm, responsive, RR ≥ 10
- Onset 2–5 min IV
- Prep: airway equipment + naloxone + flumazenil ready

### Haloperidol IV 2–5 mg q 10 min
- Caution QTc (get ECG when possible)
- Max 20 mg/day

### **Ketamine** — if refractory or severe (pharmacologic restraint)
- **Dissociative dose: 4–5 mg/kg IM** (rapid onset 5 min) — "ketamine takedown"
- **IV: 1–2 mg/kg slow push**
- Maintains airway reflexes, BP (unlike benzo)
- Emergence phenomenon — consider midazolam 2.5 mg IV on wake-up
- Watch for laryngospasm (rare), hypersalivation (atropine 0.5 mg)
- **Best choice** for severe agitation unknown cause, trauma, scene

### Dexmedetomidine infusion (ICU/monitored)
- 0.2–0.7 mcg/kg/hr — α2 agonist, "cooperative sedation"
- Post-intubation or prolonged agitation
- Bradycardia, hypotension

---

## 🪜 Step 4 — Intubation + ICU sedation

### Indication
- Severe agitation uncontrolled with all above
- Respiratory failure from sedation
- Airway protection needed (coma, trauma)

### Post-intubation sedation
- **Propofol** 25–75 mcg/kg/min (primary)
- **Ketamine** 0.5–2 mg/kg/hr (analgosedation, BP-sparing)
- **Dexmedetomidine** 0.2–1.4 mcg/kg/hr (no respiratory depression → facilitates extubation)
- ± **Fentanyl** 25–100 mcg/hr (analgesia)
- **Avoid** paralytic unless ventilating (paralyzed + awake = catastrophic)

## 🗺 Quick Ladder

```
Step 0: Verbal de-escalation + safe environment

Step 1 (oral, cooperative):
  Olanzapine 10 mg ODT ± Lorazepam 2 mg PO
  Reassess 30 min

Step 2 (IM):
  Haloperidol 5 mg IM + Lorazepam 2 mg IM
  OR Midazolam 5 mg IM
  OR Droperidol 5 mg IM
  Reassess 15 min — redose PRN

Step 3 (IV, severe):
  Midazolam 2.5–5 mg IV q 5 min
  OR Ketamine 4–5 mg/kg IM (unknown etiology, severe)
  Airway prep + monitoring
  Reassess 5 min

Step 4 (refractory):
  Intubation + sedation (propofol / ketamine / dex)
  ICU consult
```

---

## 📊 Monitoring (post-sedation)

- **Airway, RR, SpO₂** — q 5 min × 30 min after IM/IV
- **BP, HR** — q 15 min × 1 hr
- **GCS / RASS** — target RASS 0 to -1 (calm, responsive)
- **ECG** — if haloperidol/droperidol used (QTc)
- **Continuous pulse ox + supplemental O₂**

---

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Critical Pitfalls)

<Callout type="danger" title="Sedation Pitfalls">

**ข้าม verbal de-escalation** — chemical restraint ตอนไม่จำเป็น  
→ Always try step 0 first (often works in psychiatric agitation)

**Haloperidol ใน alcohol withdrawal** — ↓ seizure threshold  
→ **Benzo** เป็น first-line in withdrawal

**Benzo ใน delirium** — paradoxical agitation, prolongs delirium  
→ Haloperidol monotherapy ใน delirium (no benzo)

**ไม่ monitor post-sedation** — respiratory depression, aspiration  
→ Continuous SpO₂ + staff bedside × 30 min minimum

**IM olanzapine + IM benzo** — respiratory depression / hypotension  
→ FDA warning — separate by ≥ 1 hr

**Ketamine ใน true head injury + elevated ICP** — historic concern (debated now)  
→ Safe in most trauma; avoid in porphyria, schizophrenia

**ไม่ consider medical cause** — agitation = symptom  
→ Check glucose, vitals, head CT if trauma; hypoxia, hypoglycemia, sepsis, thyroid storm

**Restraint without sedation** — injury + rhabdo risk  
→ Restraint = temporary bridge; sedate within 5–10 min

</Callout>

## 📝 Copy-Ready Order

```
Acute agitation — [suspected cause: __]

Step 0: Verbal de-escalation × 5 min

Trigger point for chemical restraint:
- Self-harm, staff harm, impeding care

Mild-mod (cooperative):
- Olanzapine 10 mg ODT + Lorazepam 2 mg PO
- Reassess 30 min

Uncooperative (IM):
- Haloperidol 5 mg IM + Lorazepam 2 mg IM
  OR Midazolam 5 mg IM (if stimulant / unknown)
- ECG (QTc baseline)
- Reassess 15 min; redose half-dose PRN

Severe / refractory:
- Midazolam 2.5–5 mg IV q 5 min (airway prep)
- OR Ketamine 4 mg/kg IM
- Post-sedation: SpO₂ continuous, RR q 5 min × 30 min
- Glucose, vitals, head CT if trauma suspected

Etiology workup:
- CBC, BMP, glucose, EtOH, UA tox
- CT head (trauma, AMS)
- Treat underlying cause

Document: RASS, precipitant, response, side effects
```
