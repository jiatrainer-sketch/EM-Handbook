## 🎯 Purpose

Stepwise IV/PO analgesia ใน ED — **start at step matching severity**, titrate up if inadequate, down if side effects

**ใช้ใน:** Acute pain ใน ED (trauma, renal colic, post-op, musculoskeletal)

---

## 📊 Severity Assessment — NRS 0–10

| NRS | Severity | Starting step |
|---|---|---|
| 1–3 | Mild | Step 1 |
| 4–6 | Moderate | Step 2 |
| 7–10 | Severe | Step 3 |

---

## 🪜 Step 1 — Non-opioid (NRS 1–3)

### Paracetamol (first-line)
- **PO:** 500–1000 mg q 4–6 hr (max **4 g/day**; 3 g ถ้า chronic alcohol / liver disease / wt &lt; 50 kg)
- **IV:** 1 g IV q 6 hr (over 15 min)
- Safer than NSAID ใน CKD, GI bleed, elderly

### NSAID (ถ้าไม่มี contraindication)
- **Ibuprofen** 400 mg PO q 6–8 hr (max 2400/day)
- **Ketorolac** 30 mg IV/IM × 1; then 15 mg q 6 hr × 5 days max (elderly 15 mg)
- **Diclofenac** 50 mg PO TID
- Contraindications: CKD (Cr &gt; 1.5), active GI bleed, heart failure, NSAID allergy, anticoag

### Combined
- Paracetamol + NSAID = synergistic, sparing effect

---

## 🪜 Step 2 — Mild-moderate opioid (NRS 4–6)

### Tramadol
- **PO:** 50–100 mg q 6 hr (max 400 mg/day; 300 ถ้า &gt; 75 yr)
- **IV:** 50–100 mg slow over 3 min q 6 hr
- Less respiratory depression, lowers seizure threshold, avoid in seizure disorder

### Codeine (+ paracetamol)
- 30–60 mg q 4 hr (with paracetamol, often combo tab)
- Variable CYP2D6 metabolism → avoid in pediatrics (FDA warning), post-tonsillectomy

### Or: low-dose morphine / pethidine
- ถ้า severe → skip to step 3

<Callout type="warning" title="NSAID + opioid combo">

Paracetamol + NSAID + tramadol = multimodal, often reduces opioid need by 30–50%  
(WHO calls this "step 1 + step 2")

</Callout>

---

## 🪜 Step 3 — Strong opioid (NRS 7–10)

### Morphine (reference opioid)
- **IV:** 0.1 mg/kg (= ~5–10 mg) q 10 min **titrate** — endpoint: pain ≤ 4 + RR ≥ 10 + GCS ≥ 14
- **IM:** 5–10 mg (avoid in shock — slow absorption)
- **PO:** 10–30 mg q 4 hr (ER: MST 10–30 mg q 12 hr)

### Fentanyl (preferred in hemodynamic instability, renal failure)
- **IV:** 1–2 mcg/kg (50–100 mcg) q 5–10 min
- Faster onset (2–3 min), shorter duration (30–60 min)
- No histamine release → safer in asthma, hypotension
- **IN (intranasal)**: 1.5 mcg/kg for pediatric — no IV needed

### Pethidine (meperidine) — **avoid**
- Seizures (normeperidine metabolite)
- Not recommended in most modern guidelines
- ใช้เฉพาะ post-op shivering (25 mg IV)

### Hydromorphone (Dilaudid)
- 0.015 mg/kg IV (= 1 mg) q 10 min
- 5–7× more potent than morphine; similar duration

---

## 🪜 Step 4 — Adjuncts / refractory pain

### Ketamine (low-dose analgesic)
- **IV:** 0.1–0.3 mg/kg bolus over 10 min; then 0.1–0.3 mg/kg/hr infusion
- Works well ใน opioid-tolerant, chronic pain, trauma
- Side effect: dissociation, hypertension (uncommon at low dose)

### Regional anesthesia
- Serratus anterior block (rib fracture)
- Fascia iliaca block (hip fracture) — **strongly recommended**
- Digital block, hematoma block

### Adjuncts
- **Gabapentin** 300–600 mg PO (neuropathic)
- **IV lidocaine** 1–1.5 mg/kg (renal colic, chronic pain)
- **Dexamethasone** 4–8 mg IV (migraine, radicular pain)

---

## 🎯 Scenario-specific choices

| Scenario | First-line |
|---|---|
| **Renal colic** | **Ketorolac 30 mg IV** (NSAID superior to opioid) + paracetamol |
| **Rib fracture** | Multimodal: paracetamol + NSAID + opioid + **serratus block** |
| **Hip fracture (elderly)** | **Fascia iliaca block** + paracetamol (avoid systemic opioid as much) |
| **Migraine** | Metoclopramide + ketorolac + fluids (no opioid first-line) |
| **MSK sprain** | NSAID + paracetamol; topical diclofenac gel |
| **Burn** | IV morphine titrate; early analgesia before dressing |
| **Abdominal pain** | Analgesia ไม่บดบัง dx — give early (paracetamol → morphine if severe) |

---

## ⚠️ Reversals

### Naloxone (opioid reversal)
- **Low dose**: 0.04–0.1 mg IV, titrate q 2–3 min
- Goal: restore RR ≥ 10, not full reversal (avoid precipitated withdrawal)
- **Short t½** (30–90 min) — may need redose or infusion; observe ≥ 4 hr

---

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Critical Pitfalls)

<Callout type="danger" title="Analgesia Pitfalls">

**Oligoanalgesia** — under-treating pain (fear of addiction/side effects)  
→ Pain is 5th vital sign; systematic reassessment q 30 min

**Opioid-only** — multimodal better + lower opioid dose  
→ Paracetamol + NSAID first, opioid as add-on

**Full-dose opioid in elderly** — start low, go slow  
→ Morphine 2–4 mg IV (vs 5–10 in young), reassess q 10 min

**NSAID ใน CKD / HF / GI bleed** — นิรโทษกรรมไม่ได้  
→ Check Cr, H/O GI bleed, heart failure first

**Pethidine default ไม่ได้** — seizure risk + no advantage  
→ Morphine / fentanyl เป็น first-line

**Opioid discharge script เยอะไป** — driver ของ opioid crisis  
→ ≤ 3-day supply, non-refill, reassess at follow-up

**ไม่ reassess หลัง analgesia** — titration ต้องใช้ data  
→ NRS reassess q 15–30 min after IV opioid

</Callout>

## 📝 Copy-Ready Order

```
Analgesia (acute pain)

NRS: __ /10
Contraindications checked: [renal, GI bleed, allergy, HF]

Step 1 (mild):
- Paracetamol 1 g PO/IV q 6 hr
- Ibuprofen 400 mg PO q 6 hr (if no CI)

Step 2 (mod):
- + Tramadol 50 mg PO/IV q 6 hr PRN

Step 3 (severe):
- Morphine 5 mg IV q 10 min PRN (max 20 mg)
  OR Fentanyl 50 mcg IV q 10 min (if renal/BP)
- Reassess NRS + RR + GCS q 15 min
- Naloxone 0.4 mg available

Adjunct: [regional block, ketamine, lidocaine]
Reassess NRS + side effects q 30 min × 2 hr
```
