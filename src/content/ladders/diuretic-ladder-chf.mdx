## 🎯 Purpose

Stepwise diuretic intensification ใน ADHF — escalate if inadequate response after 2 hr

**ใช้ใน:** ADHF, pulmonary edema, refractory peripheral edema

---

## 🎯 Response Target

- **UOP ≥ 100–150 mL/hr** × 4 hr after IV bolus
- **Net negative** 1–3 L/day (more aggressive in APE)
- Weight ↓ 0.5–1 kg/day
- Symptom relief (dyspnea, orthopnea)

---

## 🪜 Step 1 — IV loop diuretic (first-line)

### Furosemide (Lasix)
- **Loop-naïve** (never on furosemide): 20–40 mg IV bolus
- **Chronic oral user**: **IV dose = 2–2.5× daily PO dose** (DOSE trial)
- e.g. 80 mg PO daily → 160–200 mg IV bolus

### Bumetanide (more potent, fewer ototox)
- 1 mg = 40 mg furosemide
- Start 0.5–1 mg IV; max 10 mg/day

### Torsemide
- Better PO bioavailability (90% vs 50% furosemide)
- 10 mg = 40 mg furosemide
- Preferred as transition PO

### Bolus vs Infusion
- **Bolus**: equal efficacy to infusion (DOSE trial)
- **Infusion**: consider if ≥ 240 mg/day furosemide, unstable BP
- Infusion rate: 5–20 mg/hr (after bolus)

---

## 🪜 Step 2 — Escalate IV loop (2 hr after step 1)

### If UOP inadequate (&lt; 100 mL/hr)
- **Double the bolus**: 40 → 80 → 160 → 320 mg
- Max single IV dose **furosemide 200 mg**, daily total **600 mg** (beyond this = diminishing returns)

### Switch to continuous infusion
- Maintains steady diuresis, avoids "rebound" NaCl reabsorption
- Continue bolus q 12 hr + infusion

---

## 🪜 Step 3 — Sequential nephron blockade (loop + thiazide)

### Add **Metolazone** (PO, once daily)
- **2.5–10 mg PO** 30 min before loop
- Synergistic — blocks distal tubule NaCl reabsorption (compensatory after chronic loop)
- Can unleash **massive diuresis** → monitor K, Mg, volume

### Alternatives
- **Hydrochlorothiazide** 25–50 mg PO/IV (similar)
- **Chlorothiazide** 500 mg IV (available IV, rapid)

<Callout type="warning" title="Sequential blockade cautions">

- **Monitor K, Mg q 6–12 hr** — rapid hypokalemia, hypomagnesemia
- **Hyponatremia** — free water retention unopposed
- **AKI** — volume depletion if over-diurese
- Start low (metolazone 2.5 mg), hold after 2–3 doses if euvolemic

</Callout>

---

## 🪜 Step 4 — Carbonic anhydrase inhibitor / vaptan

### Acetazolamide (ADVOR trial 2022)
- **500 mg IV daily** × 3 days added to loop diuretic
- ↑ decongestion + more sustained
- Watch for metabolic acidosis
- Best in hypervolemic patient with relatively preserved renal function

### Tolvaptan (selective V2 antagonist)
- **For hyponatremia + HF** (Na &lt; 125)
- 15 mg PO daily
- ↑ free water excretion (aquaresis), no electrolyte loss
- Cost-prohibitive in many settings

---

## 🪜 Step 5 — Ultrafiltration / CRRT

### Indication
- **Refractory volume overload** despite max diuretic combo
- **AKI** with oliguria not responding
- Severe hyponatremia with overload

### Options
- **Slow continuous ultrafiltration (SCUF)** — fluid only
- **CVVH / CVVHDF** — fluid + solute (if AKI)
- **IHD** — if stable, rapid correction

**Consult nephrology** — access, anticoag, prescription

---

## 🪜 Step 6 — Inotropes / vasodilators (hemodynamic reasons)

### Low output state → add inotrope
- **Dobutamine** 2.5–10 mcg/kg/min — β₁ agonist, ↑ CO, ↑ renal perfusion → responds to diuretic
- **Milrinone** 0.125–0.375 mcg/kg/min — PDE3 inh, better in pulmonary HT (no β1 → avoid tachycardia)
- Beware: both can cause arrhythmia + hypotension

### Afterload reduction (if BP permits)
- **NTG** 10–20 mcg/min — preload + some afterload
- **Nitroprusside** 0.3 mcg/kg/min — afterload reduction (CCU/ICU, BP ≥ 100)

---

## 🗺 Quick Ladder (ADHF in ER)

```
Hour 0:
  Furosemide IV bolus (2–2.5× home PO dose, or 40 mg if naïve)
  NTG sublingual 0.4 mg ± infusion (if SBP > 100)
  NIV (CPAP/BiPAP) if hypoxic or dyspneic
  Monitor UOP hourly

Hour 2:
  If UOP < 100 mL/hr → double IV bolus
  Reassess BP, lung auscultation, SpO₂

Hour 4–6:
  Still inadequate → infusion + metolazone 2.5 mg PO
  K, Mg replacement

Hour 12:
  Still refractory → consider acetazolamide, inotrope (low CO),
  ultrafiltration consult
```

## 📊 Monitoring

| Parameter | Frequency |
|---|---|
| UOP (strict I/O) | Hourly × 4 hr, then q 2 hr |
| BP, HR | q 15 min × 1 hr, then q 1 hr |
| Weight | Daily AM, same scale |
| BMP (Na, K, Cr) | q 6–12 hr |
| Mg, PO₄ | Daily |
| SpO₂ | Continuous |

---

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Critical Pitfalls)

<Callout type="danger" title="Diuretic Pitfalls">

**ขนาด IV ใน chronic loop user น้อยไป** — ignored home dose  
→ IV dose = 2–2.5× home PO dose (DOSE trial)

**Add thiazide ก่อน max IV loop** — no synergy benefit yet  
→ Max IV loop first (furosemide 320 mg bolus or 20 mg/hr infusion)

**Over-diurese → pre-renal AKI** — monitor Cr + UOP + exam  
→ Stop/hold if Cr ↑ 30% from baseline; gentle fluid challenge

**ไม่ replace K/Mg** — arrhythmia, esp. with metolazone  
→ K target 4.0, Mg 2.0 (transcellular link)

**Hyponatremia worsening** — thiazide in hyponatremic patient  
→ If Na &lt; 130, caution; tolvaptan preferred

**Ignore response after 2 hr** — late escalation = worse outcome  
→ Reassess at 2 hr; escalate stepwise

**PO furosemide ใน ADHF** — poor absorption from gut edema  
→ IV until euvolemic + stable × 24 hr, then transition

**DDx for refractory diuresis**: pre-renal from over-diuresis, ↓ CO (need inotrope), medication nonadherence, severe salt intake, NSAID

</Callout>
