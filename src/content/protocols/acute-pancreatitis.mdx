## 🎯 Diagnosis — ต้องมี 2 ของ 3

1. **Abdominal pain** typical (epigastric, radiating to back, worse supine)
2. **Lipase or amylase &gt; 3× ULN** (lipase more specific, stays ↑ longer)
3. **Imaging**: characteristic findings (CT/MRI/US)

---

## 🧠 Etiology (90% from 2 causes)

| Cause | Frequency |
|---|---|
| **Gallstones** (biliary) | 40–50% |
| **Alcohol** | 30% |
| **ERCP post-procedure** | 5–10% |
| **Hypertriglyceridemia** (&gt; 1,000 mg/dL) | 1–4% |
| **Hypercalcemia** | &lt; 1% |
| **Drugs** (azathioprine, valproate, didanosine, estrogens, etc.) | 2% |
| **Autoimmune** (IgG4, type 2) | 1% |
| **Infection** (mumps, coxsackie, HIV) | rare |
| **Idiopathic** | 10–15% |

**Thai context**: gallstones common; alcoholic pancreatitis ใน rural men; liver fluke / cholangiocarcinoma workup in endemic NE Thailand

---

## 🧪 การตรวจวินิจฉัย (Workup)

### Initial
- **Lipase** (preferred over amylase — specificity higher, remains elevated longer)
- **CBC, BMP, Ca, Mg, PO4, glucose**
- **LFT** (↑ ALT &gt; 3× ULN = gallstone pancreatitis likely)
- **Triglyceride** (suspect if &gt; 1,000)
- **Lactate** (severity marker)
- **CRP at 48 hr** (&gt; 150 = severe)

### Imaging
- **Abdominal US** — first-line (assess biliary tract, gallstones)
- **CT with contrast** — NOT routine at presentation; do at 72 hr if not improving or suspect complication
- **MRCP** — if suspect CBD stone + LFT trending up
- **EUS** — idiopathic / recurrent, microlithiasis

<Callout type="warning" title="CT timing">

- Day 1–2: often normal or inflammation only → delays treatment
- **Day 3–5**: optimal for detecting necrosis (30% of severe cases)
- ถ้าอาการ severe จาก start → MRI/CT ได้เลย

</Callout>

## 📊 Severity (Revised Atlanta)

| Severity | Criteria |
|---|---|
| **Mild** | No organ failure, no local/systemic complications |
| **Moderate** | Transient organ failure (&lt; 48 hr) or local complication |
| **Severe** | **Persistent organ failure &gt; 48 hr** (resp/CV/renal) |
| **Critical** | Severe + infected (peri)pancreatic necrosis |

### BISAP (bedside, predicts mortality ≥ 3)
- **B**UN &gt; 25 mg/dL
- **I**mpaired mental status
- **S**IRS ≥ 2
- **A**ge &gt; 60
- **P**leural effusion

**BISAP ≥ 3 = mortality ~7%**, ≥ 5 ~22%

### Other scores: Ranson (complex, requires 48 hr), APACHE-II, HAPS

---

## 💊 การรักษา (Management)

### Phase 1: First 24 hr — **Aggressive IVF + Analgesia**

<Callout type="success" title="Early aggressive IV fluid resuscitation">

**Lactated Ringer's preferred over NS** (less metabolic acidosis, possibly ↓ SIRS)

- **5–10 mL/kg/hr × 12–24 hr** (goal: UOP &gt; 0.5 mL/kg/hr, HR &lt; 120, BUN trending down)
- Avoid over-resuscitation (> 4 L/day without improvement risks ARDS, abdominal compartment syndrome)
- Reassess q 6 hr with VS, UOP, Hct, BUN, lactate

</Callout>

### Phase 2: Analgesia
- Morphine, fentanyl, or hydromorphone IV PRN
- NSAIDs usually avoided (AKI concern); tramadol less preferred
- Scheduled + breakthrough doses

### Phase 3: Nutrition
- **NPO only until nausea/vomiting resolved** (usually 24–72 hr)
- **Early oral feeding** (low-fat diet, clear → full as tolerated) — better outcomes vs prolonged NPO
- Severe: **enteral feeding** (NG or NJ) within 72 hr — ↓ infection vs TPN
- TPN only if enteral impossible

### Phase 4: Antibiotics
- **NOT routine** for pancreatitis
- **Only for**:
  - Infected necrosis (gas on CT, positive FNA culture, clinical deterioration)
  - Cholangitis co-existent
  - Extra-pancreatic infection (UTI, pneumonia)
- **Carbapenem** (meropenem) preferred for infected necrosis

### Phase 5: Cause-specific

**Gallstone pancreatitis:**
- **Early ERCP (within 24 hr)** only if: cholangitis OR persistent biliary obstruction (↑ bili, CBD dilation)
- **Cholecystectomy** during same admission (mild/moderate) to prevent recurrence

**Alcoholic pancreatitis:**
- Alcohol cessation counseling
- Thiamine 100 mg IV, folate, B12
- CIWA-Ar monitoring, benzodiazepines for withdrawal

**Hypertriglyceridemia-induced (TG &gt; 1,000):**
- Insulin infusion 0.1–0.3 U/kg/hr (+ D5 if BG drops) to drive down TG
- Apheresis if severe, not responding, pregnancy
- Long-term: fibrate, omega-3, lifestyle

---

## 🚨 Complications

### Local
- **Necrosis** (sterile → infected): 30% of severe; CT gas = infected
- **Pseudocyst** (≥ 4 wk): observe if asymptomatic
- **Walled-off necrosis** (WON)
- **Pancreatic duct disruption**

### Systemic
- **ARDS** (SIRS-mediated)
- **AKI**
- **Abdominal compartment syndrome** — IAP &gt; 20; percutaneous or surgical decompression
- **DIC**

---

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Pitfalls)

<Callout type="danger" title="Pancreatitis Pitfalls">

**Over-resuscitation** — &gt; 250 mL/hr after the first 24 hr → ARDS, abdominal compartment syndrome  
→ Re-assess q 6 hr; if BUN not trending down on IVF, reassess (cardiac, ACS)

**Routine antibiotics** — no benefit, breeds resistance; only for infected necrosis  
→ FNA + C/S → meropenem ถ้า confirmed

**Delay early feeding** — prolonged NPO worsens outcomes  
→ Oral/enteral once pain + N/V better (24–72 hr)

**Miss hypertriglyceridemia cause** — TG ถูก consume during acute phase → check early!  
→ Insulin drip if TG &gt; 1,000

**Miss cholangitis overlap** — fever + RUQ + jaundice → urgent ERCP  
→ check LFT + CBD on imaging

**Abdominal compartment syndrome** — distended abdomen + ↓ UO + ↑ peak pressure  
→ Bladder pressure, decompression if &gt; 20 mmHg with organ dysfunction

</Callout>

```
4. Antiemetics: ondansetron 4–8 mg IV q 8 hr
5. Electrolyte replacement (K, Mg, Ca)
6. Early feeding once tolerated: clear → full low-fat
7. Cause-specific:
   - Gallstone + cholangitis → ERCP < 24 hr
   - Gallstone no cholangitis → cholecystectomy same admission
   - TG > 1,000 → insulin drip
   - Alcohol → thiamine, CIWA
8. VBG, lactate, BUN q 6 hr initially
9. CT abd if not improving by day 3
10. Admit [ward / ICU if BISAP ≥ 3 or organ failure]
```
