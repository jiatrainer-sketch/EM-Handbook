## 🎯 Criteria (severe features)

**Severe pre-eclampsia** = BP ≥ 140/90 after 20 wk **+ ANY** of:

- **BP ≥ 160/110** on 2 readings 4 hr apart (or severe single reading)
- **Thrombocytopenia** (&lt;100,000)
- **Liver dysfunction** (AST/ALT &gt; 2× ULN, severe RUQ/epigastric pain)
- **Renal insufficiency** (Cr &gt; 1.1 or doubled from baseline)
- **Pulmonary edema**
- **New CNS sx** (severe HA, visual disturbance, altered mental status)

**Eclampsia** = new-onset seizure in pre-eclamptic / post-partum woman

---

## 🚨 Initial Actions (first 15 min)

1. **Call OB + neonatology STAT**, left lateral decubitus
2. **IV access** × 2, continuous BP + SpO₂, foley catheter
3. **Labs:** CBC, platelet, LFT, BUN/Cr, uric acid, LDH, coag, UA (protein), fibrinogen, T&S
4. **Fetal monitoring** (NST / US) — assess viability + gestational age
5. **MgSO₄ loading** (seizure ppx) — do NOT wait

---

## 💊 Magnesium Sulfate

**Loading:** 4–6 g IV over 20 min *(dilute in 100 mL NS/D5W)*
**Maintenance:** **1–2 g/hr IV drip** × 24 hr post-delivery (or 24 hr after last seizure)

### Monitoring (Mg toxicity)
| Clinical | Mg level |
|---|---|
| Therapeutic | 4–7 mg/dL (1.8–3.5 mmol/L) |
| ↓ DTR | ≥ 8 mg/dL — **hold infusion, recheck** |
| Resp depression | ≥ 12 mg/dL |
| Cardiac arrest | ≥ 25 mg/dL |

### Antidote
- **Calcium gluconate 1 g IV over 10 min** if Mg toxicity
- Hold Mg, support respiration

### ⚠️ Renal dose adjustment
- If oliguria (&lt; 30 mL/hr) or Cr &gt; 1.2: **halve maintenance to 0.5–1 g/hr**
- Monitor Mg level q4–6h

---

## 🩺 BP Control

### Target
- **SBP &lt; 155, DBP &lt; 105** (avoid precipitous drop — ↓ cerebral + placental perfusion)
- Don't target normotension — may cause placental insufficiency
- Lower SBP by ~ 25% in first hour, then gradual

### 1st-line drugs

**Labetalol 20 mg IV** (preferred in pregnancy)
- Repeat 40 → 80 mg q10 min (max 300 mg)
- Or infusion 1–2 mg/min, titrate
- Avoid: asthma, CHF, heart block

**Hydralazine 5–10 mg IV** q 20 min
- Max 30 mg total
- Side: reflex tachycardia, HA
- Good if asthma (labetalol contraindicated)

**Nifedipine 10 mg PO** (immediate-release) q 20 min
- 3rd line if IV unavailable
- Avoid sublingual (unpredictable drop)

### AVOID
- **ACE-I / ARB** (teratogenic, can use postpartum)
- **Nitroprusside** (cyanide toxicity to fetus; only if refractory + delivering imminently)
- **Atenolol** (IUGR)

---

## 🧠 Eclampsia (Seizure)

1. **Protect airway**, left lateral decubitus, O₂
2. **MgSO₄ 4 g IV over 5 min** (bolus) — 1st line, NOT benzo
3. If seizure continues after 2nd dose → add **lorazepam 2–4 mg IV**
4. Recurrent seizure → **phenytoin / levetiracetam** + CT head
5. Treat HTN per above
6. **Delivery** as soon as maternal stable (regardless of gestational age in severe eclampsia)

---

## 🤰 Definitive Treatment = Delivery

### Indications for immediate delivery
- Eclampsia
- Severe pre-eclampsia ≥ 34 wk
- HELLP with progression
- Placental abruption, DIC
- Fetal distress, IUFD
- Pulmonary edema
- Renal failure
- Refractory HTN

### 24–48 hr expectant management (if stable)
- 24–33⁶ wk: **betamethasone 12 mg IM q24h × 2 doses** (lung maturity)
- Continue Mg, BP control, fetal monitoring
- Delivery if deterioration

## ⚠️ Red Flags

```
🚩 Severe RUQ / epigastric pain
   - HELLP (hemolysis, elevated LFT, low plt) or liver hematoma
   - Check LFT, platelet, LDH, haptoglobin urgently

🚩 Scotoma, blurry vision, retinal detachment
   - Cerebral + retinal edema — immediate delivery

🚩 Oliguria / anuria
   - Reduce MgSO4 (toxicity risk)
   - Cautious fluid — pulmonary edema risk

🚩 Over-aggressive BP reduction
   - SBP drop > 30 mmHg/hr → placental hypoperfusion
   - Target 140–155 / 90–105 — not normotension

🚩 Missing atypical eclampsia (postpartum)
   - Up to 6 wk postpartum; may have no prior HTN
   - AMS / seizure + any postpartum → check BP + MgSO4
```
