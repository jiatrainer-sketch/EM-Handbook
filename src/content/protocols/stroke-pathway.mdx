## 🎯 Criteria / Diagnosis

### Clinical: **BE-FAST**

- **B** — Balance (sudden loss)
- **E** — Eyes (sudden vision loss, diplopia)
- **F** — Face drooping
- **A** — Arm weakness
- **S** — Speech difficulty (slurred, aphasia)
- **T** — Time (note **Last Known Well**)

### Critical time marker: **Last Known Well (LKW)**

⚠️ **ไม่ใช่เวลาที่เจอ** — คือเวลาสุดท้ายที่คนไข้ **ปกติ**

Wake-up stroke → use time going to bed

### Stroke types (ต้องแยกก่อน treat)

| | Ischemic (~85%) | Hemorrhagic (~15%) |
|---|---|---|
| Mechanism | Clot blocks artery | Vessel ruptures |
| Treatment | tPA / thrombectomy | BP control, reverse anticoag |
| BP target | ≤ 185/110 (pre-tPA) | **≤ 140 SBP** |
| Imaging | CT often normal early | CT shows hyperdensity |

⚠️ **ต้อง CT non-contrast เสมอ ก่อนตัดสินใจ** — ลักษณะทาง clinical แยกไม่ได้

### Stroke mimics (rule out!)

1. **Hypoglycemia** — check BS ทันที
2. **Seizure / Todd's paralysis** — check witness / seizure hx
3. **Complex migraine**
4. **Conversion**
5. **Bell's palsy** (forehead spared = UMN stroke; forehead affected = LMN)

## ⚡ Immediate Actions — **Time is brain**

### Step 1: Parallel processing (0–10 min) — **Stroke fast track**

1. **ABC + 2 IV**
2. **Vital signs + SpO₂** (O₂ only if SpO₂ &lt; 94%)
3. **Bedside BS** (rule out hypo)
4. **12-lead ECG** (AFib? MI?)
5. **NIHSS score** (baseline severity)
6. **Document LKW precisely**
7. **Medication history** (antiplatelet, anticoagulant, recent bleed/surgery)
8. **Alert stroke team / CT**

### Step 2: CT brain non-contrast STAT (&lt; 25 min from arrival)

**CT findings:**
- **Hemorrhage** → NOT tPA, go to hemorrhagic pathway
- **No hemorrhage + early ischemic signs minimal** → candidate for tPA
- **Large established infarct** (&gt; ⅓ MCA) → NOT tPA (bleeding risk)

**CTA head + neck** (add if available) → LVO detection for thrombectomy  
**CT perfusion** → extended window (up to 24 hr) thrombectomy selection

### Step 3: Ischemic Stroke → **tPA if eligible**

**tPA (Alteplase) eligibility:**

**Inclusion:**
- Ischemic stroke confirmed (no hemorrhage on CT)
- Disabling deficit (NIHSS usually ≥ 4, but any disabling deficit)
- Onset-to-treatment &lt; **4.5 hr** from LKW
- Age ≥ 18

**Absolute contraindications (bleeding risk):**

```
🚫 Any intracranial hemorrhage on CT
🚫 History of ICH ever
🚫 Ischemic stroke or severe head trauma < 3 months
🚫 Active internal bleeding
🚫 Platelet < 100,000
🚫 INR > 1.7 or PT > 15 sec
🚫 Heparin within 48 hr with elevated aPTT
🚫 DOAC within 48 hr (unless normal specific level)
🚫 SBP > 185 or DBP > 110 despite treatment
🚫 Glucose < 50 or > 400 mg/dL
🚫 Suspected aortic dissection
🚫 Endocarditis
🚫 Known AVM, aneurysm, neoplasm with ↑ bleed risk
🚫 Intracranial / intraspinal surgery < 3 months
🚫 GI malignancy or bleed within 21 days
🚫 Intraaxial intracranial neoplasm
```

**Relative (weigh benefit vs risk, 3–4.5 hr window stricter):**
- Age &gt; 80
- NIHSS &gt; 25
- Anticoagulation use (even INR ≤ 1.7)
- DM + prior ischemic stroke
- Major surgery within 14 days

### tPA administration

**Dose: 0.9 mg/kg (max 90 mg)**
- **10% as IV bolus over 1 min**
- **90% as IV infusion over 60 min**

```
@ BW 60 kg:
  Total dose: 0.9 × 60 = 54 mg
  Bolus (10%): 5.4 mg IV push over 1 min
  Infusion (90%): 48.6 mg IV over 60 min
```

### BP management for tPA

**Pre-tPA target: ≤ 185 / 110**
- Labetalol 10–20 mg IV (may repeat × 1)
- Nicardipine drip 5 mg/hr, titrate by 2.5 mg/hr q 5–15 min (max 15 mg/hr)
- Clevidipine if available

**ถ้าลดไม่ลง → not eligible for tPA**

**Post-tPA target: ≤ 180 / 105 × 24 hr**
- Check BP q 15 min × 2 hr, q 30 min × 6 hr, then q 1 hr × 16 hr

### Step 4: Thrombectomy evaluation (LVO)

**Candidate for mechanical thrombectomy:**
- Large vessel occlusion (ICA, M1, basilar)
- NIHSS ≥ 6 usually
- Pre-stroke mRS ≤ 1
- **Standard window: 0–6 hr from LKW**
- **Extended window: 6–24 hr** with favorable perfusion imaging (DAWN / DEFUSE-3)

→ Transfer to comprehensive stroke center STAT if LVO

### Step 5: Hemorrhagic Stroke

**BP target: ≤ 140 SBP** (INTERACT-2, ATACH-2)
- Aim to reach within 1 hr
- Nicardipine drip OR labetalol

**Reverse anticoagulation:**

| Agent | Reversal |
|---|---|
| **Warfarin** | **Vitamin K 10 mg IV** + **4F-PCC** (preferred) or FFP |
| **Dabigatran** | **Idarucizumab 5 g IV** (2 × 2.5 g 15 min apart) |
| **Apixaban / Rivaroxaban** | **Andexanet alfa** (if available) OR 4F-PCC 50 U/kg |
| **Heparin** | **Protamine 1 mg per 100 U heparin given last 2–3 hr** |
| **Enoxaparin** | Protamine (partial reversal) |
| **Antiplatelet** | Platelet transfusion (benefit debated) + DDAVP 0.4 mcg/kg |

**Neurosurgery consult:**
- Cerebellar hemorrhage &gt; 3 cm → evacuate
- Hydrocephalus → EVD
- Large lobar ICH → consider evacuation

### Step 6: Admit + monitor

- **Stroke unit** (proven benefit)
- NIHSS q 1 hr × 6 hr, then q 2–4 hr
- **Post-tPA: no antiplatelet/anticoagulant × 24 hr**
- CT brain at 24 hr (before starting antiplatelet)
- DVT prophylaxis: mechanical first, chemical after 24 hr if no bleed

## 💊 Drug Preparation (BW 60 kg)

### Alteplase (tPA)

```
Dose:     0.9 mg/kg (max 90 mg)
          @ BW 60 kg = 54 mg total
          
Bolus:    10% = 5.4 mg IV over 1 min
Infusion: 90% = 48.6 mg IV over 60 min

Prep:     Reconstitute 50 mg vial + 50 mL SW = 1 mg/mL
          DO NOT shake (denature)
          Use within 8 hr at room temp
```

**⚠️ Monitor:**
- BP q 15 min × 2 hr, q 30 min × 6 hr, q 1 hr × 16 hr
- Neuro check q 15 min × 2 hr
- Stop infusion if: severe headache, acute BP rise, N/V, worsening neuro
  → CT brain STAT to r/o ICH

**🫘 Renal:** ไม่ต้องปรับ  
**🧓 Elderly (&gt; 80):** ให้ได้ แต่ relative — discuss with family

---

### Labetalol (BP control)

```
Bolus:    10–20 mg IV over 2 min
          May repeat q 10 min (max 300 mg total)
Drip:     2 mg/min IV (titrate to 8 mg/min)
```

**Contraindications:**
- Asthma / COPD severe
- Heart block
- Decompensated heart failure
- Bradycardia

**🫘 Renal:** ไม่ต้องปรับ  
**🧓 Elderly:** ลดเริ่มต้น 5–10 mg

---

### Nicardipine drip (BP control)

```
Prep:     Nicardipine 25 mg + NSS 240 mL = 0.1 mg/mL
Start:    5 mg/hr = 50 mL/hr
Titrate:  +2.5 mg/hr q 5–15 min (max 15 mg/hr = 150 mL/hr)
```

**🫘 Renal:** ไม่ต้องปรับ initial  
**🧓 Elderly:** start low (2.5 mg/hr)

---

### Vitamin K + 4F-PCC (warfarin reversal)

```
Vitamin K:  10 mg IV over 30 min (NOT push — anaphylaxis risk)
4F-PCC:     Dose by INR + weight
            INR 2–4:   25 U/kg (max 2,500 U)
            INR 4–6:   35 U/kg (max 3,500 U)
            INR > 6:   50 U/kg (max 5,000 U)
            @ BW 60 kg, INR 3: 1,500 U IV
FFP (if no PCC):  10–15 mL/kg IV = 600–900 mL @ 60 kg
```

---

### Idarucizumab (dabigatran reversal)

```
Dose:     5 g IV total
          Give as 2 × 2.5 g IV bolus, 15 min apart
Onset:    minutes
```

## ⚠️ Critical Pitfalls

```
🚩 Time is brain
   - Every 1 min lost = 1.9 million neurons
   - Door-to-needle target ≤ 45 min (Thai fast track)
   - Door-to-CT target ≤ 25 min

🚩 Always check BS first
   - Hypoglycemia mimics stroke
   - Fix BS → if deficit resolves = hypo, not stroke

🚩 LKW ≠ time discovered
   - Wake-up stroke = last time seen normal (often bedtime)
   - Document exact time

🚩 Hemorrhage before tPA
   - CT is mandatory
   - Clinical can't distinguish ischemic vs hemorrhagic

🚩 BP > 185/110 = no tPA until controlled
   - Don't skip this
   - Treat aggressively
   - If persistent → not eligible

🚩 Post-tPA bleeding
   - Worsening neuro, headache, vomiting, BP rise
   - STOP infusion
   - CT brain STAT
   - Cryoprecipitate 10 U + platelets if ICH

🚩 Angioedema post-tPA (ACE-I users)
   - 1–5% incidence
   - Orolingual swelling
   - Stop tPA, airway management
   - Treatment: similar to anaphylaxis

🚩 Warfarin reversal = Vit K + PCC (not just FFP)
   - FFP slow + volume overload
   - PCC preferred (4 factors)
   - Vit K always needed (PCC alone insufficient)

🚩 Don't miss LVO
   - Thrombectomy window up to 24 hr
   - NIHSS ≥ 6 + fixed gaze / aphasia / neglect suggests LVO
   - Transfer even if past tPA window

🚩 Glucose 140–180 post-stroke
   - Avoid hyper + hypoglycemia
   - Both worsen outcome

🚩 Swallow screen before any PO
   - Aspiration pneumonia risk
   - Keep NPO until nurse/SLP cleared
```

## 📝 Copy-Ready Order

```
STROKE FAST TRACK ACTIVATED at: _____ (time)
Last Known Well: _____ (time)

1. ABC, O₂ only if SpO₂ < 94%
2. IV access × 2 (for tPA + contrast)
3. Continuous ECG, SpO₂, BP (both arms first)
4. Bedside BS STAT → correct if < 70 or > 400
5. 12-lead ECG (AFib? MI?)
6. NIHSS documented by trained nurse/doctor
7. Labs STAT:
   - CBC + platelets, PT/INR, aPTT
   - BMP (glucose, BUN/Cr, electrolyte)
   - Troponin (r/o MI)
   - Pregnancy (if applicable)
   - Type & screen
8. CT brain non-contrast STAT (target < 25 min from arrival)
   + CTA head/neck if LVO suspected (NIHSS ≥ 6)
9. Assess tPA eligibility per checklist
10. BP control pre-tPA (target ≤ 185/110):
    - Labetalol 10–20 mg IV, repeat q 10 min
    - Nicardipine drip 5 mg/hr, titrate
11. If ISCHEMIC + eligible + < 4.5 hr:
    - Alteplase 0.9 mg/kg (max 90 mg)
      @ BW 60 kg = 54 mg total
      Bolus 5.4 mg over 1 min + infuse 48.6 mg over 60 min
    - Admit stroke unit
    - BP q 15 min × 2 hr, q 30 min × 6 hr, q 1 hr × 16 hr
    - Post-tPA BP target ≤ 180/105 × 24 hr
    - NO antiplatelet/anticoagulant × 24 hr
    - Repeat CT brain at 24 hr
12. If LVO (ICA, M1, basilar):
    - Transfer to comprehensive stroke center for thrombectomy
    - Window: 0–6 hr standard, 0–24 hr with perfusion imaging
13. If HEMORRHAGIC:
    - BP target ≤ 140 SBP (Nicardipine drip)
    - Reverse anticoagulation:
      * Warfarin: Vit K 10 mg IV + 4F-PCC 25–50 U/kg
      * Dabigatran: Idarucizumab 5 g IV
      * Xa inhibitor: Andexanet alfa OR 4F-PCC 50 U/kg
    - Neurosurgery consult STAT
    - Reverse INR < 1.4
14. Swallow screen before any PO
15. DVT prophylaxis:
    - Mechanical (SCD) immediately
    - Chemical (LMWH) after 24 hr if no bleed
16. Glucose target 140–180
17. Admit stroke unit / ICU
18. Consult: neurology, neurosurgery (if bleed), IR (if thrombectomy)
```
