## 🎯 Diagnostic Criteria (Tokyo 2018)

### Definite cholangitis
**A** (systemic inflammation) + **B** (cholestasis) + **C** (imaging)

### A. Systemic inflammation
- Fever &gt; 38°C
- WBC &gt; 10,000 or &lt; 4,000
- CRP ≥ 1 mg/dL

### B. Cholestasis
- Jaundice (T.bili ≥ 2 mg/dL)
- Elevated LFT (ALP, GGT, AST, ALT &gt; 1.5× ULN)

### C. Imaging
- Biliary dilation (CBD &gt; 6 mm)
- Evidence of etiology (stone, stricture, stent)

**Charcot's triad** (classic, low sensitivity ~ 50–70%):
- Fever
- RUQ pain
- Jaundice

**Reynolds' pentad** (severe):
- Charcot's + **hypotension + altered mental status**
- Indicates severe / shock → mortality &gt; 20%

---

## 📊 Severity (TG18)

| Grade | Criteria | Management |
|---|---|---|
| **I — Mild** | No organ dysfunction, responds to medical Rx | Abx, monitor, elective biliary drainage |
| **II — Moderate** | ≥ 2 of: WBC &gt; 12k/&lt; 4k, T &gt; 39, age ≥ 75, bili ≥ 5, albumin &lt; 70% LLN | Abx + early biliary drainage (within 24–48 hr) |
| **III — Severe** | Organ dysfunction (cardio, neuro, resp, renal, hepatic, hematologic) | Emergent biliary drainage + ICU |

---

## 🧠 Etiology

- **Choledocholithiasis (CBD stone)** — most common cause
- **Biliary stricture** (malignant: pancreatic, cholangiocarcinoma; benign: post-op, PSC)
- **Biliary stent occlusion**
- **Parasitic** (Clonorchis, Opisthorchis — Thailand endemic!)
- **Post-ERCP / biliary procedure**
- **Mirizzi syndrome** (stone in cystic duct compressing CBD)

## 🧪 การตรวจวินิจฉัย (Workup)

### Must-do
- **Vital signs + mental status** (sepsis + Reynolds pentad screen)
- **Abdominal exam** (RUQ tenderness, Murphy's, guarding)
- **Jaundice, scleral icterus**

### Labs
- **CBC + differential** (leukocytosis, thrombocytopenia in sepsis)
- **LFT** (cholestatic pattern: ↑ ALP, GGT, direct bili > indirect)
- **Amylase / lipase** (may be ↑ from concurrent pancreatitis)
- **BUN / Cr + electrolytes** (AKI in sepsis)
- **Lactate** (sepsis / hypoperfusion)
- **Coag (PT/INR)** (cholestasis → vitamin K deficiency)
- **Blood culture × 2** (positive in 30–60% — key for targeted abx)

### Imaging
- **US abdomen** (first-line)
  - CBD dilation &gt; 6 mm (age-adjusted: 1 mm per decade > 60)
  - GB stone, GB wall thickening
  - Intrahepatic duct dilation
- **CT abdomen + contrast** — if US unclear, looking for abscess / mass
- **MRCP** — non-invasive, best for ductal anatomy + stone localization
- **Endoscopic US (EUS)** — if small distal CBD stone suspected
- **ERCP** — both diagnostic AND therapeutic (see below)

---

## 🚨 Initial Management

### First hour
1. **IV × 2, NPO**, type & screen
2. **IVF resuscitation** (NS or LR, 30 mL/kg if sepsis/shock over 3 hr)
3. **Blood cultures × 2** (peripheral + port if present)
4. **Empirical antibiotics within 1 hr** (see below)
5. **Analgesia** (morphine historically avoided due to sphincter of Oddi spasm; meperidine alternative; modern practice: any opioid OK in ED)
6. **Antipyretic** (acetaminophen IV, avoid NSAIDs if AKI/coagulopathy)
7. **Correct coagulopathy** (vitamin K 10 mg SQ/IV; FFP if active bleeding + ERCP plan)
8. **Call GI for ERCP** (timing per severity)

---

## 💊 Empirical Antibiotics

### Mild–moderate (community-acquired, no prior abx)
- **Ceftriaxone 2 g IV q24h + metronidazole 500 mg IV q8h** × 5–7 days
- **Piperacillin-tazobactam 4.5 g IV q6–8h** × 5–7 days (mono-agent)
- **Ampicillin-sulbactam 3 g IV q6h** (β-lactam allergy: cipro + metronidazole)

### Severe / healthcare-associated / recent abx
- **Piperacillin-tazobactam** + consider vanco if MRSA
- **Meropenem 1 g IV q8h** (ESBL concern, nosocomial)
- **Cefepime + metronidazole** (pseudomonas coverage)
- **Vancomycin** if biliary stent / post-procedure (enterococcus)

### Duration
- **5–7 days** after source control (drainage)
- **7–10 days** if persistent bacteremia
- Transition to PO (cipro + metronidazole or amox-clav) once clinically improved

---

## 🔧 Source Control (Biliary Drainage)

### Timing by severity
- **Grade I (Mild)**: within 48–72 hr, elective; can try medical Rx
- **Grade II (Moderate)**: **within 24–48 hr** (or urgent if not responding to medical Rx in 24 hr)
- **Grade III (Severe)**: **EMERGENT — within 12–24 hr** (don't wait)

### Options (in order of preference)

**1. ERCP with sphincterotomy + stone extraction / stent** — 1st line
- Most effective, minimally invasive
- Success > 90%
- Risks: post-ERCP pancreatitis, bleeding, perforation

**2. PTC (percutaneous transhepatic cholangiography)** — if ERCP fails
- Radiologically placed external drain
- Useful in post-Billroth II, hilar strictures

**3. Surgery** (CBD exploration) — rare today
- Last resort if above fail

---

## 🎯 Monitor / Expected Course

### Initial 24 hr
- Fever → should defervesce within 24–48 hr of abx + drainage
- Lactate → trend down
- WBC → start decreasing 24–48 hr

### Red flags during treatment
- **No clinical improvement in 24 hr** → upgrade abx, urgent drainage
- **Persistent / rising lactate** → shock, incomplete source control
- **New AMS** → worsening sepsis, hepatic encephalopathy

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Pitfalls)

<Callout type="danger" title="Acute cholangitis — Pitfalls">

**Treating as "cholecystitis" and missing cholangitis**
→ Cholecystitis: fever + RUQ (no jaundice, no CBD dilation)
→ Cholangitis: + jaundice + CBD > 6 mm + need ERCP
→ Missing → sepsis, mortality

**Delaying ERCP in severe cholangitis**
→ Medical Rx alone inadequate
→ Drainage is the definitive Rx

**Underestimating mortality**
→ Mortality without proper Rx: 50–80%
→ With prompt Rx: 5–10%
→ Reynolds pentad = ICU + emergent drainage

**ERCP in severe coagulopathy**
→ Reverse INR < 1.5 with FFP before procedure
→ But don't delay indefinitely — balance risk

**Missing Thai-specific etiology**
→ Liver fluke (Opisthorchis) in endemic areas (NE Thailand)
→ Stone formation + cholangiocarcinoma risk
→ Consider praziquantel after acute Rx

**Ignoring pancreatitis overlap**
→ Check lipase; if high, may affect timing of ERCP
→ Gallstone pancreatitis + cholangitis = ERCP urgently

**Wrong abx for biliary source**
→ Must cover GN (E. coli, Klebsiella) + anaerobes
→ Enterococcus coverage if stent/post-op

**Missing benign vs malignant obstruction**
→ Painless jaundice + weight loss → rule out CA (head of pancreas, cholangio)
→ CT + tumor markers (CA 19-9) + biopsy during ERCP

</Callout>
