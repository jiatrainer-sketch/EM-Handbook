## 🎯 Initial Approach

<Callout type="danger" title="Unstable AFib = electrical cardioversion STAT">

Signs of instability (any one):
- Hypotension (SBP &lt; 90)
- Acute heart failure / pulmonary edema
- Ongoing ischemia (chest pain + ST changes)
- Altered mental status

→ **Synchronized DC cardioversion 120–200 J biphasic** (after sedation)

</Callout>

### ABCs + Monitoring
- IV × 2, O₂ if needed, continuous ECG, NIBP
- 12-lead ECG → confirm AFib (irregular narrow-complex, absent P waves)
- Labs: CBC, BMP, Mg, TSH, troponin, BNP, CXR, ± D-dimer
- Echo (TTE) — LV function, LAE, valve, pericardium

## 📊 Rate vs Rhythm Control — Stable Patient

### Rate control (most patients)
Target: **HR &lt; 110 (lenient)** for asymptomatic; &lt; 80 at rest / &lt; 110 exertion if symptomatic

| Drug | Dose | Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Metoprolol** | 2.5–5 mg IV over 2 min, q 5 min × 3; then PO 25–100 mg BID | 1st line most |
| **Diltiazem** | 0.25 mg/kg IV over 2 min; then 5–15 mg/hr infusion | CHF without reduced EF; asthma |
| **Digoxin** | 0.25 mg IV q 2 hr × 2–3 (max 1 mg/24 hr) | Heart failure, elderly, cannot tolerate BB/CCB |
| **Amiodarone** | 150 mg IV over 10 min → 1 mg/min × 6 hr → 0.5 mg/min | Critically ill, HF (has both rate + rhythm effect) |

<Callout type="warning" title="ระวัง!">

**Calcium channel blockers (diltiazem/verapamil) + HFrEF = contraindicated**  
→ ใช้ BB หรือ amiodarone แทน

**Pre-excitation (WPW) + AFib** — DO NOT use AV nodal blockers (BB, CCB, adenosine, digoxin)  
→ Procainamide or ibutilide; unstable → DC cardioversion

</Callout>

### Rhythm control — consider if
- Young patient, first episode
- Symptomatic despite rate control
- Heart failure precipitated by AFib
- Recent onset (&lt; 48 hr)

**Options:**
- **Amiodarone** 150 mg IV → infusion (most versatile)
- **Ibutilide** 1 mg IV over 10 min (low EF caution)
- **Flecainide/Propafenone** — only if structurally normal heart
- **Electrical cardioversion** — planned, with sedation

## 🧬 Anticoagulation

### CHA₂DS₂-VASc Score

| Factor | Points |
|---|---|
| **C** — Congestive HF | 1 |
| **H** — Hypertension | 1 |
| **A₂** — Age ≥ 75 | **2** |
| **D** — Diabetes | 1 |
| **S₂** — Stroke/TIA/TE | **2** |
| **V** — Vascular disease (CAD, PAD) | 1 |
| **A** — Age 65–74 | 1 |
| **Sc** — Sex category (female) | 1 |

### Indication for anticoagulation

| CHA₂DS₂-VASc | Men | Women |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | No AC | No AC |
| 1 | Consider AC | No AC (sex alone doesn't count) |
| ≥ 2 | **AC recommended** | ≥ 3 AC recommended |

### DOAC choice (preferred over warfarin for most non-valvular AF)

| Drug | Dose | Renal adjustment |
|---|---|---|
| **Apixaban** | 5 mg BID | 2.5 mg BID if 2 of: age ≥ 80, Cr ≥ 1.5, BW ≤ 60 kg |
| **Rivaroxaban** | 20 mg QD with food | 15 mg QD if CrCl 15–50 |
| **Dabigatran** | 150 mg BID | 75 mg BID if CrCl 15–30 |
| **Edoxaban** | 60 mg QD | 30 mg QD if CrCl 15–50, BW ≤ 60, or P-gp inhibitor |

**Warfarin** (target INR 2–3) — use in:
- Mechanical valve
- Moderate/severe mitral stenosis (rheumatic)
- CrCl &lt; 15 (DOAC limited)
- Cost constraint

### HAS-BLED Score (bleeding risk) — don't use to exclude AC
**Use to identify modifiable risk factors** (uncontrolled HTN, meds, alcohol, labile INR)

<Callout type="info" title="Cardioversion + anticoagulation">

- **AF &gt; 48 hr OR unknown duration**: 3 weeks AC before → cardiovert → 4 weeks AC after  
  (OR **TEE-guided**: r/o LA thrombus → cardiovert → 4 weeks AC)
- **AF &lt; 48 hr, low risk**: cardiovert, AC per CHA₂DS₂-VASc
- **Unstable → cardiovert now**, start AC ASAP after

</Callout>

## 🏥 Disposition

### Discharge if
- Rate controlled (HR &lt; 110 at rest)
- Hemodynamically stable
- Anticoagulation plan + follow-up
- No active ischemia, HF, or underlying cause needing inpatient workup

### Admit if
- Unstable or required cardioversion
- Precipitating condition (MI, PE, sepsis, HF)
- Heart failure exacerbation
- New-onset AF with hemodynamic concern
- Cannot achieve rate control outpatient

## ⚠️ Pitfalls

<Callout type="danger" title="AFib Pitfalls">

**AV nodal blocker in WPW + AFib** — can cause VF  
→ If wide + irregular + &gt; 200 bpm = pre-excited AF → Procainamide or DC cardioversion

**Cardioverting &gt; 48-hr AF without AC** — stroke risk  
→ 3 wk AC OR TEE first OR accept risk if unstable

**Under-anticoagulation** — elderly + falls is NOT an absolute contraindication  
→ Benefit usually outweighs risk; assess fall risk factors; consider LAA closure if truly unable

**Missing precipitant** — PE, MI, sepsis, hyperthyroidism, electrolyte  
→ Always check TSH, troponin, consider CT-PA if risk factors

**Giving digoxin in renal failure** — accumulation → toxicity  
→ Check dose, level, CrCl; prefer BB/CCB in AKI

</Callout>
