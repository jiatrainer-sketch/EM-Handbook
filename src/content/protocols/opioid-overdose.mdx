## 🎯 Toxidrome

<Callout type="danger" title="Opioid toxidrome — classic triad">

1. **Miosis** (ม่านตาหดเล็ก) — pinpoint pupils
2. **Respiratory depression** (RR &lt; 12, shallow)
3. **Decreased consciousness** (CNS depression)

→ **Naloxone + airway support** is lifesaving

</Callout>

### Other features
- Bradycardia, hypotension
- Hypothermia
- Decreased bowel sounds
- Track marks (IV users)
- Pulmonary edema (non-cardiogenic — esp. with opioid OD reversal)

### ⚠️ Exceptions
- **Meperidine, tramadol** — may cause **seizures** (not classic)
- **Propoxyphene** — cardiotoxicity, wide QRS
- **Methadone** — long half-life, **QT prolongation → Torsades**
- **Fentanyl / analogues** — very high potency, chest wall rigidity

## 💉 Naloxone — Antidote

### Dosing (titrate to RR + airway protection, **not** full alertness)

| Route | Dose | Onset |
|---|---|---|
| **IV bolus** | **0.04–0.4 mg** titrate (start low!) | 1–2 min |
| **IM** | 0.4–2 mg | 5–10 min |
| **Intranasal** | 2–4 mg in 1 nostril | 2–5 min |
| **Infusion** | 2/3 of effective bolus per hour | — |

<Callout type="warning" title="Start LOW dose to avoid precipitated withdrawal">

- Chronic opioid users: **start 0.04 mg IV** (dilute 0.4 mg amp in 10 mL NSS, give 1 mL)
- Titrate q 2–3 min to achieve **RR &gt; 12, airway protected** — not full awake
- Over-aggressive reversal → violent withdrawal, N/V, agitation, pulmonary edema

**Acute non-opioid user** (naive, kid): 0.4–2 mg IV can be given more liberally

</Callout>

### Infusion for long-acting opioids (methadone, fentanyl patch, sustained-release)
```
Effective bolus dose = X mg
Infusion rate = (2/3 × X) mg/hr
Titrate to RR > 12
Duration: often 8–24 hr
```

Remember: **naloxone t½ = 60–90 min**; many opioids longer → re-sedation possible → observe!

## 🏥 Observation / Disposition

### Observation time after naloxone
- **Heroin, morphine, oxycodone (short-acting)**: 4–6 hr after last dose OR last naloxone
- **Methadone, fentanyl patch, sustained-release**: **24 hr** minimum
- **Tramadol, meperidine**: seizure risk → 12–24 hr
- **Any pulmonary edema / aspiration**: admit

### Discharge criteria (short-acting only)
- RR &gt; 12 for ≥ 1 hr without naloxone
- Normal mental status
- Normal SpO₂ on RA
- Able to ambulate
- Social support / follow-up arranged

### Admit if
- Long-acting opioid ingestion
- Pulmonary edema
- Seizure (tramadol, meperidine)
- Mixed OD (benzo, alcohol)
- Cannot observe ≥ 4 hr
- Polysubstance risk

## 💊 Supportive Care

- Airway protection (bag-mask if RR low; intubate if prolonged)
- O₂ via NC/mask
- Continuous monitoring
- **IV fluids** for hypotension
- Pulmonary edema: high O₂, consider CPAP/BiPAP, diuretic

### Tramadol-specific
- **Seizure risk** dose-dependent
- Rx seizure: benzodiazepine (diazepam, lorazepam)
- Serotonin syndrome risk with other serotonergic drugs

### Methadone-specific
- **ECG**: QT interval; Torsades risk
- Continuous ECG; Mg IV if QT prolonged

---

## 🤝 Harm Reduction + Disposition

- Offer **naloxone take-home kit** (if available) + family education
- Screen for **OUD** (opioid use disorder)
- Referral to substance use treatment
- Medication for OUD: **buprenorphine** or **methadone** referral
- Screen for HIV, HCV, HBV (IVDU population)
- Needle exchange, HIV PEP discussion

---

## ⚠️ Pitfalls

<Callout type="danger" title="Opioid OD Pitfalls">

**Over-reverse with naloxone** — acute withdrawal, pulmonary edema  
→ Start low (0.04 mg), titrate to breathing, not full awake

**Discharge too early** — re-sedation possible as naloxone wears off  
→ Observe 4–6 hr (short-acting) or 24 hr (long-acting)

**Miss polysubstance** — benzo, alcohol co-ingestion  
→ Check ETOH level, consider flumazenil carefully

**Fail to recognize methadone/LSR** — long half-life  
→ Infusion + 24 hr observation

**Miss tramadol seizure** — different pathway  
→ Benzo for seizure; monitor for serotonin syndrome

**Skip naloxone kit discharge** — missed prevention opportunity  
→ Give kit + education; refer for OUD treatment

</Callout>

## 📝 Order template

```
Opioid Overdose

Agent suspected: [__ heroin/morphine/tramadol/methadone/fentanyl/other]
Dose/time: __
Co-ingestion: [benzo/alcohol/stimulant/other]

Initial: RR__ SpO₂__% GCS__ pupils__ BP__

1. Airway: BVM, intubate if inadequate or prolonged
2. O₂ to SpO₂ > 94%
3. Naloxone:
   - Dilute 0.4 mg amp in 10 mL NSS → 0.04 mg/mL
   - Start 0.04 mg IV; titrate q 2 min to RR > 12
   - If infusion needed (long-acting): 2/3 effective bolus / hr
4. IV access, continuous monitor, ECG
5. Labs: glucose, ETOH, tox screen, CBC, BMP, ABG, CK
6. CXR (aspiration, NCPE), ECG (methadone → QT)
7. [If seizure — tramadol/meperidine] Diazepam 5 mg IV
8. Observe:
   - Short-acting: 4–6 hr post last naloxone
   - Long-acting/LSR: 24 hr
9. Discharge planning:
   - Naloxone kit + education
   - OUD screening / referral (buprenorphine)
   - HIV/HCV screen if IVDU
10. Admit if long-acting, pulm edema, seizure, polysubstance
```
