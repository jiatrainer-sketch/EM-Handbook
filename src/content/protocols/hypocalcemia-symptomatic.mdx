## 🎯 When to treat urgently

### Severe hypocalcemia (treat regardless of level)

- **Tetany** (carpopedal spasm, Chvostek sign, Trousseau sign)
- **Seizure**
- **Stridor / laryngospasm**
- **Cardiac arrhythmia** (prolonged QT, torsades, bradycardia, heart block)
- **Altered mental status**

### Level thresholds (adults)

| Corrected Ca | Severity |
|---|---|
| 8.5–10.5 mg/dL (2.1–2.6 mmol/L) | Normal |
| **7.5–8.4** | Mild (often asymptomatic) |
| **6.5–7.4** | Moderate |
| **&lt; 6.5** | Severe (often symptomatic) |

### Ionized Ca (more accurate)

| Ionized Ca | Level |
|---|---|
| 4.5–5.3 mg/dL (1.1–1.3 mmol/L) | Normal |
| &lt; 4.0 (1.0 mmol/L) | Severe |

---

## 🧮 Corrected Calcium Formula

```
Corrected Ca = measured Ca + 0.8 × (4.0 − albumin)
```

- Use if albumin &lt; 4.0 g/dL
- Ionized Ca is preferred if available (no albumin correction needed)

### Example

- Measured Ca = 6.5, albumin = 2.5
- Corrected = 6.5 + 0.8 × (4.0 − 2.5) = 6.5 + 1.2 = **7.7 mg/dL**

---

## 🔍 Causes

- **Hypoparathyroidism** (post-thyroidectomy, autoimmune)
- **Hypomagnesemia** (often concurrent; correct Mg first)
- **Vitamin D deficiency / malabsorption**
- **CKD** (↓ active vitamin D, hyperphosphatemia)
- **Acute pancreatitis** (fat saponification)
- **Tumor lysis / rhabdomyolysis** (Ca + PO₄ deposition)
- **Massive transfusion** (citrate chelation)
- **Medications:** bisphosphonates, denosumab, calcitonin, loop diuretics

## 💊 Acute Management

### Severe symptomatic — **IV calcium**

**Calcium gluconate 10%** (preferred peripheral — less extravasation risk)

- **1–2 amps (10–20 mL) IV over 10–20 min**
- 1 amp = 10 mL = 1 g = **~4.65 mEq elemental Ca** (~90 mg elemental)
- Dilute in NSS or D5W 100 mL
- **Never IV push** — cardiac arrest risk
- Monitor ECG during infusion

**Calcium chloride 10%** (central line only — 3× elemental Ca)

- 1 g (10 mL) = **~13.6 mEq elemental Ca** (~270 mg elemental)
- Use for arrest / severe refractory
- Extravasation = severe tissue necrosis

### After bolus — **Maintenance drip**

- **Ca gluconate 1 mg/kg/hr elemental** (~1 amp in 100 mL over 4–6 hr)
- Or **calcium gluconate drip:** 10 amps in D5W 1 L, run 50 mL/hr
  - = 100 mL/hr delivers ~100 mg elemental/hr
- Recheck ionized Ca q 1–2 hr
- Target ionized Ca &gt; 4.0 mg/dL (or total Ca &gt; 7.5 mg/dL)

### Correct Magnesium (essential — hypoMg refractory hypoCa)

- **Mg level q 6 hr**
- If Mg &lt; 1.8 mg/dL: **Mg sulfate 2 g IV over 30 min**
- Target Mg &gt; 2.0

### Oral maintenance (once stable)

- **Calcium carbonate 1–2 g/day PO** (divided doses with meals)
- **Calcitriol 0.25–0.5 mcg PO BID** (if vitamin D deficient or postparathyroidectomy)
- **Cholecalciferol 50,000 IU weekly × 8** (if 25-OH vitamin D low)

## 📊 การติดตาม (Monitoring)

- Continuous ECG (watch QT, torsades)
- Ionized Ca q 1–2 hr during acute treatment
- Mg, PO₄, K, albumin q 4–6 hr
- Mental status, reflexes, tetany signs
- ABG (alkalosis worsens symptoms)

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Critical Pitfalls)

```
🚩 Correct Mg first (or concurrent)
   - HypoMg → refractory hypoCa
   - Mg 2 g IV over 30 min if &lt; 1.8

🚩 Never IV push calcium
   - Cardiac arrest risk
   - Always diluted + slow (10–20 min)

🚩 Ca chloride vs Ca gluconate
   - Gluconate: peripheral OK; 1 g = 4.65 mEq
   - Chloride: central only; 1 g = 13.6 mEq (3× stronger)
   - Don't confuse in orders

🚩 Check albumin or use ionized
   - Low albumin → falsely low total Ca
   - Correct if albumin &lt; 4.0

🚩 Cardiac arrhythmia + hypoCa
   - QT prolongation → torsades
   - Ca + Mg replacement

🚩 Post-thyroidectomy
   - Common cause; expect PTH drop
   - Prophylactic Ca + calcitriol often needed

🚩 Hyperphosphatemia concurrent
   - Don't over-correct Ca (× PO₄ &gt; 70 = metastatic calcification)
   - Control PO₄ first (binders)

🚩 Alkalosis worsens symptoms
   - ↓ ionized Ca (more protein bound)
   - Correct hyperventilation / alkalosis concurrent
```

## 📝 คำสั่งพร้อมใช้ (Copy-Ready Order)

```
Dx: Symptomatic hypocalcemia [tetany / seizure / arrhythmia / AMS]
Corrected Ca: ___ mg/dL (ionized ___ mmol/L)

1. IV × 1 large-bore; continuous ECG monitor
2. Labs: BMP, Mg, PO₄, albumin, ionized Ca, PTH, 25-OH vit D, lipase
3. ECG: check QT interval (target &lt; 450 ms)
4. CALCIUM GLUCONATE 10%:
   - 1–2 amp (10–20 mL) in NSS 100 mL
   - Infuse IV over 10–20 min
   - Never push; monitor ECG during infusion
5. Maintenance drip:
   - 10 amps (100 mL) in D5W 1 L
   - Start 50–100 mL/hr
   - Target ionized Ca &gt; 4.0 or total Ca &gt; 7.5
   - Recheck q 1–2 hr
6. MAGNESIUM (essential):
   - If Mg &lt; 1.8: Mg sulfate 2 g IV over 30 min
   - Target Mg &gt; 2.0
7. Correct alkalosis if present (↓ ionized Ca in alkalosis)
8. Oral (once stable):
   - Calcium carbonate 1–2 g/day PO divided with meals
   - Calcitriol 0.25–0.5 mcg PO BID if vitamin D deficient
9. If Ca chloride preferred (arrest):
   - 1 g (10 mL) IV push — CENTRAL LINE ONLY
10. Treat underlying cause:
    - PTH replacement (hypopara)
    - Vitamin D replacement
    - Stop offending drug
11. Admit monitored bed (severe); floor for mild after stabilization
```
