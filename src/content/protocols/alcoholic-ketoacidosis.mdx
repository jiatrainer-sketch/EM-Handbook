## 🎯 Clinical Picture

### History (key)
- **Chronic alcohol use** + recent **binge** followed by
- **Abrupt cessation or decreased intake** (usually due to nausea/vomiting)
- Often 1–3 days of not eating after last drink
- Frequent recurrence in known alcoholics

### Symptoms
- **Nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain** (dominant)
- **Dyspnea, Kussmaul respiration**
- **Tachycardia, hypotension, dehydration**
- **Altered mental status** (usually mild, unlike DKA coma)
- Sometimes: chest pain, generalized weakness

### Usually absent
- Severe hyperglycemia (unlike DKA)
- Fruity breath (ketones present but less prominent)

## 🔬 Labs

| Lab | AKA | DKA | HHS |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Glucose** | Normal or LOW | HIGH (&gt; 250) | VERY HIGH (&gt; 600) |
| **Anion gap** | ↑ (HAGMA) | ↑ | Mild or normal |
| **Ketones** | ↑ (β-hydroxybutyrate predominant) | ↑ (acetoacetate + β-hydroxybutyrate) | Minimal |
| **pH** | &lt; 7.35 (variable) | &lt; 7.30 | Normal-mild ↓ |
| **Bicarb** | Low | Low | Normal-mild ↓ |
| **Na** | Variable | Low (pseudohypo) | High |
| **K** | Often low | Elevated (shifts) |
| **Mg, Phos** | Often LOW | Variable | Variable |

### Key distinguishing features
- **BG normal or low** in AKA (not high like DKA)
- **Predominant β-hydroxybutyrate** — urine dipstick ketone may underestimate (dipstick = acetoacetate)
- **Mixed acid-base common** — HAGMA + metabolic alkalosis (vomiting) + respiratory alkalosis

### Calculate
- **Anion gap** = Na – (Cl + HCO₃) — should be &gt; 12 (corrected for albumin)
- **Osmolar gap** — if elevated, consider toxic alcohol → [Toxic alcohol](./toxic-alcohol-ingestion)

### Rule out concurrent problems
- **Pancreatitis** (lipase × 3)
- **GI bleed** (Hct, rectal, NG)
- **Sepsis** (SIRS, lactate, cultures)
- **Liver failure** (LFT, ammonia, coag, albumin)
- **Rhabdomyolysis** (CK, K, UA for blood)
- **Methanol / ethylene glycol** (osmolar gap, EG: Ca oxalate crystals in urine)

## 🚨 Treatment

### Principle
- **AKA is a DEHYDRATION problem, NOT an insulin problem**
- Treat with **dextrose + fluid + thiamine** (reverses metabolic state)
- **INSULIN NOT NEEDED** (unlike DKA)

### 1. Thiamine FIRST (before dextrose)
- **Thiamine 100 mg IV** (or IM) — **BEFORE** any dextrose/carbs
- Prevents **Wernicke's encephalopathy** (triad: AMS, ophthalmoplegia, ataxia)
- Continue 100 mg IV/IM daily × 3 d, then PO

### 2. Fluid resuscitation
- **D5NSS (dextrose 5% in normal saline) 1 L IV bolus**
  - Dextrose → insulin release → stops ketogenesis
  - NSS → volume repletion
- Repeat 1 L bolus PRN
- Maintenance: **D5NSS 150–250 mL/hr**
- LR acceptable if hyponatremia / hyperchloremia

### 3. Electrolyte replacement
- **K**: replace if K &lt; 4 (often needed)
  - 10–20 mEq/hr IV + oral if tolerated
- **Mg**: **MgSO₄ 2 g IV over 15 min** (universally low in alcoholics)
  - Replenishes Mg for proper K absorption
- **Phos**: replace if severely low (&lt; 2) — K-phos 10–15 mmol IV over 6 hr

### 4. Monitor
- **VBG / BMP q 4 hr** until pH &gt; 7.30, anion gap closes
- **Ketones** decrease with treatment
- **Mental status** improves with correction
- Usually resolves within 12–24 hr

---

## 💉 What NOT to Do

### Do not give insulin (unless concurrent DKA)
- AKA responds to dextrose + fluid
- Insulin in AKA with normal/low glucose → hypoglycemia

### Do not give dextrose before thiamine
- **Wernicke's encephalopathy** — irreversible brain damage
- Always thiamine FIRST

### Do not overcorrect alkalosis / bicarb
- AKA self-corrects with treatment
- HCO₃ supplementation rarely needed unless pH &lt; 6.9

## 🧠 Related Alcohol Issues — Look For

### Wernicke-Korsakoff
- **Triad**: confusion + ophthalmoplegia + ataxia (only 10% have all 3)
- Any AMS + alcoholic → presume Wernicke → thiamine 500 mg IV TID × 3 d

### Alcohol withdrawal
- Tremor, anxiety, diaphoresis 6–24 hr after last drink
- Severe: seizures (12–48 hr), delirium tremens (48–96 hr)
- CIWA scoring, benzodiazepine treatment (lorazepam 1–2 mg IV PRN)

### Hypoglycemia
- Alcohol blocks gluconeogenesis + glycogen depleted
- BG check Q hourly, D50W if &lt; 60

### Pancreatitis
- Common in alcoholics
- Lipase > 3× ULN + abd pain
- Supportive + NPO + IVF

### Hepatic encephalopathy
- Jaundice, asterixis, AMS
- Lactulose 30 mL PO/NG q4h (titrate 3–4 BM/day) + rifaximin

### Hepatitis / cirrhosis
- Check LFT, albumin, PT, platelets
- Alcoholic hepatitis (Maddrey DF > 32) may need prednisolone

### Rhabdomyolysis
- CK > 5× ULN
- Aggressive IVF (target UOP 200 mL/hr)

### Trauma (fall, MVA)
- Head CT if trauma + LOC + on alcohol (intoxication masks sx)

### GI bleed
- Varices, Mallory-Weiss, gastritis
- Check Hct, NG if active

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Pitfalls)

<Callout type="danger" title="Alcoholic ketoacidosis (AKA) — Pitfalls">

**Missing AKA as "DKA"**
→ BG normal/low — check ketones in anyone with HAGMA
→ AKA can have BG < 100 — don't dismiss

**Insulin in AKA**
→ Causes hypoglycemia (no hyperglycemia to counter)
→ Only give insulin if concurrent true DKA (rare)

**Dextrose BEFORE thiamine**
→ Precipitates Wernicke — irreversible
→ Thiamine first, then dextrose

**Underdosing thiamine**
→ Wernicke: 500 mg IV TID × 3 d (high-dose)
→ AKA without Wernicke: 100 mg IV daily × 3 d minimum

**Underestimating alkalosis**
→ Vomiting → metabolic alkalosis superimposed
→ Look at pH + anion gap together
→ High AG alone may be "hidden" by alkalosis

**Missing other alcoholic complications**
→ Pancreatitis (lipase), GI bleed (Hct), infection (CXR, UA)
→ Liver failure (LFT, ammonia, INR)

**Missed toxic alcohol**
→ Check osmolar gap (calculate)
→ Methanol → blindness; EG → flank pain, Ca oxalate urine

</Callout>
