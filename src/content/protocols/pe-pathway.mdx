## 🎯 When to suspect

- **Dyspnea** (sudden onset) + **pleuritic chest pain**
- **Tachycardia** (HR &gt; 100) + **tachypnea**
- Unilateral leg swelling (concurrent DVT)
- Hypoxia unexplained by CXR
- Syncope (massive PE)
- Hemoptysis (pulmonary infarction)

### Risk factors

- Immobility &gt; 3 days, recent surgery &lt; 4 weeks
- Active malignancy, pregnancy, OCP / HRT
- Prior DVT/PE, thrombophilia
- Long-haul travel &gt; 6 hr

---

## 🧠 Risk Stratification — Wells Score (PE)

| Criterion | Points |
|---|---|
| Clinical s/s DVT (swelling + tenderness) | 3 |
| PE most likely diagnosis | 3 |
| HR &gt; 100 | 1.5 |
| Immobilization &gt; 3 d or surgery &lt; 4 wk | 1.5 |
| Previous DVT/PE | 1.5 |
| Hemoptysis | 1 |
| Malignancy (active/treated &lt; 6 mo) | 1 |

**Interpretation (3-tier):**
- **&lt; 2 = Low** (1.3%)
- **2–6 = Moderate** (16.2%)
- **&gt; 6 = High** (37.5%)

**Simplified 2-tier:**
- ≤ 4 = PE unlikely → D-dimer
- &gt; 4 = PE likely → CTPA

### PERC rule (use only if Wells low + gestalt &lt; 15%)

All 8 must be NO:
1. Age ≥ 50
2. HR ≥ 100
3. SpO₂ &lt; 95%
4. Unilateral leg swelling
5. Hemoptysis
6. Recent trauma/surgery &lt; 4 wk
7. Prior DVT/PE
8. Exogenous estrogen

All 8 NO + low pretest = **PE ruled out, no D-dimer needed**

---

## 🧪 Workup Algorithm

```
Wells ≤ 4 (unlikely)
  → D-dimer
      ≤ 500 ng/mL (or age × 10 if &gt; 50) → PE ruled out
      &gt; cutoff → CTPA

Wells &gt; 4 (likely)
  → CTPA (skip D-dimer — too many false +)
```

**CTPA contraindications / alternatives:**
- Pregnancy: V/Q scan preferred (less fetal radiation vs chest)
- Renal failure (Cr &gt; 1.5): V/Q scan or bedside US (RV strain)
- Contrast allergy: V/Q scan

### Bedside findings

- **ECG:** sinus tachy (most common), S1Q3T3 (classic but uncommon), new RBBB, T inversion V1-V4
- **Echo:** RV dilation, McConnell sign, TR
- **CXR:** usually normal; Westermark sign, Hampton hump (rare)
- **ABG:** hypoxia + respiratory alkalosis

---

## 🚑 Hemodynamic Classification

### Massive (High-risk)

- **SBP &lt; 90** for &gt; 15 min or on pressor
- **Cardiac arrest** / bradycardia with shock
- Mortality ~ 30–50%
- → **Systemic thrombolysis** unless contraindicated

### Submassive (Intermediate-risk)

- **Normotensive** BUT:
  - RV dysfunction (echo or CTPA) **AND/OR**
  - Positive biomarker (troponin, BNP)
- Mortality ~ 5–15%
- Consider thrombolysis if deteriorating (intermediate-high)

### Low-risk

- Normotensive + no RV dysfunction + biomarker negative
- Mortality &lt; 1%
- Outpatient management possible (if PESI low)

---

## 💊 Anticoagulation — First line

### Hemodynamically stable

**LMWH** (preferred outpatient / stable inpatient):
- **Enoxaparin 1 mg/kg SC q 12 hr** (or 1.5 mg/kg q 24 hr)
- CrCl &lt; 30: reduce to 1 mg/kg q 24 hr

**UFH drip** (preferred if thrombolysis considered, massive PE, severe renal):
- Load 80 U/kg IV bolus, then 18 U/kg/hr drip
- Target aPTT 1.5–2.5× control (or anti-Xa 0.3–0.7)

**DOAC** (outpatient, stable, normal renal):
- **Rivaroxaban** 15 mg PO BID × 21 days, then 20 mg OD
- **Apixaban** 10 mg PO BID × 7 days, then 5 mg BID

### Contraindications to anticoagulation
- Active major bleeding
- Recent (&lt; 2 wk) intracranial hemorrhage or surgery
- Severe thrombocytopenia (&lt; 50,000)
- → IVC filter consideration

---

## ⚡ Massive PE — Systemic Thrombolysis

**Alteplase (tPA) 100 mg IV over 2 hr**

- 10 mg bolus + 90 mg infusion over 2 hr
- Or 0.6 mg/kg (max 50 mg) over 15 min if cardiac arrest
- Stop heparin during tPA, resume after

### Absolute contraindications (thrombolysis)

- Prior intracranial hemorrhage
- Ischemic stroke &lt; 3 mo
- Known intracranial neoplasm / AVM
- Active bleeding
- Suspected aortic dissection
- Recent head/spine surgery or trauma &lt; 2 mo

### Relative contraindications

- SBP &gt; 180 (uncontrolled)
- Recent major surgery &lt; 3 wk
- Pregnancy
- Active peptic ulcer
- Prolonged CPR

### Alternatives if thrombolysis contraindicated

- **Catheter-directed thrombolysis** (lower tPA dose)
- **Surgical embolectomy** (rare, cardiac surgery centers)
- **ECMO** as bridge (very selected)

---

## 📊 การติดตาม (Monitoring)

- Continuous ECG, SpO₂, BP q 15 min × 1 hr after tPA
- Neuro check q 15 min × 2 hr (watch ICH)
- Serial troponin, BNP
- aPTT q 6 hr on UFH (target 1.5–2.5× control)
- CBC, fibrinogen post-tPA
- Daily lower-limb US if DVT confirmed

---

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Critical Pitfalls)

```
🚩 Don't delay anticoagulation while awaiting CTPA
   - If high pretest + stable: start UFH empirically
   - Stop if CTPA negative

🚩 D-dimer has many false positives
   - Elderly, pregnancy, malignancy, sepsis, post-op
   - Use age-adjusted cutoff (age × 10 if &gt; 50 yr)

🚩 Massive PE = thrombolysis ASAP
   - Don't wait for CTPA if arresting — bedside echo
   - RV strain + shock + recent DVT = enough

🚩 Chronic PE ≠ acute PE
   - Pulmonary HT + chronic dyspnea
   - CTPA may show webs, mural thrombus
   - Refer cardiology/pulmonology

🚩 IVC filter only if anticoagulation contraindicated
   - Retrievable preferred
   - Remove when safe to anticoagulate
```

## 📝 คำสั่งพร้อมใช้ (Copy-Ready Order)

```
Dx: Pulmonary embolism — [massive / submassive / low-risk]

1. O₂ to keep SpO₂ ≥ 94%; intubate if severe hypoxia
2. IV × 2 large-bore; NSS cautious (avoid overload in RV strain)
3. Labs: CBC, BMP, coags, troponin, BNP, ABG, D-dimer (if pretest appropriate)
4. ECG + CXR; bedside echo if unstable
5. CTPA (preferred) — or V/Q if contraindicated
6. ANTICOAGULATION (start if high pretest + no contraindication):
   [ ] Enoxaparin 1 mg/kg SC q 12 hr
   [ ] UFH 80 U/kg IV bolus + 18 U/kg/hr drip (if thrombolysis considered)
   [ ] Rivaroxaban 15 mg PO BID × 21 d (outpatient stable)
7. MASSIVE PE → THROMBOLYSIS:
   - Alteplase 100 mg IV over 2 hr (10 mg bolus + 90 mg over 2 hr)
   - Hold heparin during, resume after
   - Monitor for ICH (neuro check q 15 min × 2 hr)
8. Submassive PE — discuss with cardiology / ICU:
   - Catheter-directed thrombolysis if available
   - ICU admit for serial assessment
9. Support:
   - NE 0.05 mcg/kg/min titrate if SBP &lt; 90
   - Avoid aggressive fluids (worsen RV strain)
10. Workup cause: OCP, malignancy, thrombophilia, DVT survey
11. Admit ICU (massive/submassive) or telemetry (stable)
```
