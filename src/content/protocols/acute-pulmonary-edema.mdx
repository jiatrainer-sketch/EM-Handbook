## 🎯 Clinical Picture

- **Severe dyspnea + orthopnea** (can't lie flat)
- **Bilateral crackles** (lower → upper zones)
- **S3 gallop, elevated JVD, peripheral edema**
- **Pink frothy sputum** (advanced)
- **Cold clammy skin, anxious, air hunger**
- **Sitting forward, using accessory muscles**

### Vital signs
- Tachypnea (RR &gt; 30), tachycardia, hypoxia (SpO₂ &lt; 90%)
- BP usually ↑ (SCAPE) or ↓ (cardiogenic shock → [STEMI](./stemi))

---

## 🔬 Diagnostics

### Bedside (first 5 min)
- **Vital signs, SpO₂, rhythm strip**
- **Lung exam** — bilateral crackles
- **Cardiac exam** — S3, JVD, edema
- **ECG STAT** — r/o MI (often precipitating)

### Labs
- **ABG** (hypoxia, hypercapnia if fatigued)
- **BNP / NT-proBNP** (&gt; 400 pg/mL = HF likely; NT-proBNP &gt; 300 pg/mL)
- **Troponin** (ischemia or demand)
- **BUN / Cr / electrolytes** (K for diuretic)
- **Lactate**
- **CBC, LFT**

### Imaging
- **CXR**: bilateral perihilar ("bat wing") infiltrates, Kerley B lines, cardiomegaly, pleural effusions
- **Bedside lung US**: diffuse B-lines (≥ 3/field bilaterally) — fast, specific
- **Echo**: EF, valve (AS/MR/AR), wall motion, RV function

---

## 🚨 LMNOP (classic mnemonic — modernized)

| L | Lasix (furosemide) |
| M | Morphine (controversial, mostly avoided) |
| N | Nitroglycerin |
| O | Oxygen + position (upright) |
| P | Positive pressure (NIV) |

---

## 🚨 Initial Actions (first 15 min)

1. **Sit upright** (legs down), reassure — reduces preload
2. **High-flow O₂** to target SpO₂ ≥ 94%
3. **IV access, continuous monitoring, ECG**
4. **NIV / BiPAP if:**
   - RR &gt; 25, fatigue, SpO₂ &lt; 90% despite O₂
   - Inspiratory pressure (IPAP) 10–15, EPAP 5–10 cmH₂O, FiO₂ titrate
   - Contraindication: hypotension, LOC ↓, vomiting, facial trauma
5. **Furosemide IV** (see below)
6. **Nitroglycerin** if BP allows (see below)
7. **Identify + treat cause** (MI, arrhythmia, valve, fluid overload)

---

## 💧 Diuresis — Furosemide

### Bolus + infusion
- **If diuretic-naive:** 40 mg IV
- **If on chronic PO:** **double home dose IV**
  - e.g., Home 80 mg PO → give 80–160 mg IV
- Repeat in 1–2 hr if inadequate response
- **Maintenance drip:** 5–20 mg/hr if refractory

### Goal
- UOP &gt; 1 mL/kg/hr (or 100 mL/hr target)
- Monitor K, Mg (replace PRN)

### If poor response
- Double dose, add thiazide (metolazone 2.5–10 mg PO)
- Consider dialysis if refractory + renal failure

---

## 🩺 Nitroglycerin (Preload reduction)

### Indications
- SBP &gt; 100–110 mmHg (avoid hypotension)
- Reduces preload + afterload → quick symptom relief
- Gold standard in SCAPE (sympathetic crashing acute pulm edema)

### Dose
- **SL NTG 0.4 mg** q5 min × 3 (bridge to drip)
- **IV NTG infusion** 10–20 mcg/min, **titrate q5 min up to 200–400 mcg/min** in severe
- HIGH dose NTG in SCAPE (400+ mcg/min boluses OK)

### Contraindications
- SBP &lt; 100 mmHg
- PDE-5 inhibitor in 24 hr (sildenafil 24h, tadalafil 48h)
- Severe AS (avoid — ↓ preload)
- RV infarct

---

## 💉 Morphine (Controversial)

### Historical rationale
- Anxiolysis, venodilation, ↓ sympathetic drive

### Modern concerns
- ADHERE registry — **↑ mortality, ↑ intubation**
- Avoid in most acute pulmonary edema
- **Exception**: terminal / palliative symptom control

### If given
- 2–4 mg IV — lowest dose
- Watch respiratory depression

---

## 🎯 Underlying Cause Workup

### Acute / reversible
- **MI** (ECG, trop) → [STEMI](./stemi)
- **Arrhythmia** (afib with RVR — rate control / cardiovert)
- **Hypertensive crisis** (SCAPE — NTG)
- **Valve dysfunction** (acute MR from ruptured chordae/papillary muscle post-MI)
- **Flash pulmonary edema** (renal artery stenosis — ACE-I sensitive)

### Precipitants (dietary, med non-compliance)
- Salt/fluid indiscretion
- Med non-compliance (diuretic stopped)
- NSAID / COXIB
- New β-blocker, verapamil (uptitrate carefully)

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Pitfalls)

```
🚩 SCAPE (Sympathetic Crashing Acute Pulmonary Edema)
   - Severe HTN (SBP > 180) + flash pulmonary edema
   - HIGH-DOSE NTG (400+ mcg/min), NIV, avoid too much diuretic
   - Afterload problem, not volume

🚩 Missing RV infarct as cause of "pulmonary edema"
   - Preload-dependent — AVOID NTG, diuretic
   - Need fluid + inotropic support instead

🚩 Over-diuresis in diastolic HF (HFpEF)
   - Small-volume loss → hypotension
   - Slower, careful diuresis

🚩 Morphine standard use
   - AVOID in most cases — ↑ mortality
   - Exception: terminal dyspnea palliation

🚩 NIV in altered mental status / vomiting
   - Aspiration risk → intubate

🚩 Missing BNP limitations
   - Obese: BNP falsely low
   - Renal failure: BNP falsely high (use NT-proBNP threshold adjustment)

🚩 Cardiogenic shock hiding as "pulmonary edema"
   - Hypotensive + crackles = shock (not SCAPE)
   - Pressor + inotrope + urgent cath, not NTG/lasix
```
