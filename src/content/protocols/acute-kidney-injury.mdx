## 🎯 KDIGO Definition + Stages

### Diagnosis (any 1)
- **↑ Cr ≥ 0.3 mg/dL within 48 hr**
- **↑ Cr ≥ 1.5× baseline within 7 days**
- **Urine output &lt; 0.5 mL/kg/hr × 6 hr**

### Staging
| Stage | Cr | Urine output |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | 1.5–1.9× or ↑ 0.3 mg/dL | &lt; 0.5 mL/kg/hr × 6–12 hr |
| **2** | 2.0–2.9× | &lt; 0.5 mL/kg/hr × ≥ 12 hr |
| **3** | 3.0× OR Cr ≥ 4.0 OR RRT initiated | &lt; 0.3 mL/kg/hr × 24 hr OR anuria × 12 hr |

## 🧠 Etiology — Pre/Intra/Post

### Prerenal (~55%) — **decreased renal perfusion**
- Hypovolemia (GI loss, hemorrhage, burns, diuretic)
- Decreased cardiac output (CHF, cardiogenic shock)
- Systemic vasodilation (sepsis)
- Renovascular (renal artery stenosis + ACE-I)
- Hepatorenal syndrome (cirrhosis)
- Abdominal compartment syndrome

### Intrinsic (~40%)
- **ATN** (acute tubular necrosis — ischemia, sepsis, nephrotoxin — most common)
- **AIN** (acute interstitial nephritis — drugs: PPI, NSAID, antibiotics)
- **Glomerulonephritis** (vasculitis, post-strep, IgA)
- **Vascular** (TTP/HUS, scleroderma, cholesterol emboli)
- Contrast-induced nephropathy (CIN)
- Rhabdomyolysis, hemolysis, tumor lysis, crystal nephropathy

### Postrenal (~5%) — **obstruction**
- Prostate (BPH, cancer)
- Pelvic malignancy
- Kidney stones (bilateral or solitary kidney)
- Retroperitoneal fibrosis
- Neurogenic bladder

## 🧪 การตรวจวินิจฉัย (Workup)

### Essential
- **Full history** (medications, illness, volume status, sepsis, recent contrast)
- **Physical**: volume status, bladder scan, skin (rash, emboli), edema
- **UA + urine microscopy**:
  - Prerenal: normal
  - ATN: **muddy brown granular casts**, renal tubular cells
  - AIN: WBC, WBC casts, eosinophils
  - GN: RBC, RBC casts, dysmorphic RBC, proteinuria
  - Pigment: heme positive, no RBC (rhabdo/hemolysis)
- **Urine Na + FeNa**
- **BMP, Mg, Ca, PO₄, ABG**
- **Urine protein/Cr ratio**
- **Renal US** — R/O obstruction, assess kidney size

### Selected based on clinical
- Serum complement (C3, C4), ANA, ANCA, anti-GBM (GN workup)
- Cryoglobulin, SPEP/UPEP (myeloma)
- CK (rhabdo), LDH + haptoglobin (hemolysis)
- Contrast history → CIN timeline
- Bladder scan (post-void residual)

### FeNa interpretation (if UO &gt; 0)
| FeNa | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| **&lt; 1%** | Prerenal (sodium-avid) |
| **&gt; 2%** | ATN (tubular dysfunction) |
| Note: diuretic use → unreliable; use FeUrea (&lt; 35% = prerenal) |

## 💊 การรักษา (Management)

### General (all AKI)
- **Stop nephrotoxins**: NSAIDs, ACE-I/ARB (hold), aminoglycosides, contrast
- **Adjust renally-cleared drugs** (antibiotics, digoxin, metformin, DOAC, opioids)
- **Monitor strict I/O + daily weight**
- **Electrolytes + acid-base** q 6–12 hr

### Pre-renal (volume depletion)
- **IVF challenge**: 500 mL NSS/LR bolus, reassess
- Continue at maintenance if responds
- Cardiogenic → diuresis (paradoxical: improve CO → ↑ perfusion)
- Sepsis → bundle (see septic-shock)

### Intrinsic
- **ATN**: supportive — remove insult, time; most recover 1–3 wk
- **AIN**: stop offending drug; **prednisolone 1 mg/kg/day** for 2–4 wk (biopsy-proven or strong clinical)
- **GN**: specific immunosuppression per subtype (nephrology consult)
- **Contrast nephropathy**: supportive; usually resolves 48–72 hr

### Post-renal
- **Relieve obstruction**:
  - Foley catheter for bladder outlet
  - Nephrostomy tubes / retrograde stents for upper tract
  - Urology consult

### Hyperkalemia
- See hyperkalemia protocol
- Low threshold for dialysis in AKI

## 🚨 Indications for Emergency Dialysis — **AEIOU**

<Callout type="danger" title="AEIOU = RRT initiation">

- **A**cidosis: severe metabolic pH &lt; 7.15 refractory
- **E**lectrolyte: K &gt; 6.5 refractory, severe hyponatremia/hypernatremia
- **I**ntoxication: dialyzable toxin (lithium, metformin/lactic, salicylate, toxic alcohols)
- **O**verload: pulmonary edema refractory to diuresis
- **U**remia: encephalopathy, pericarditis, BUN &gt; 100 + symptoms

</Callout>

### Modality
- **IHD** (intermittent) — hemodynamically stable, rapid correction
- **CRRT** — unstable, severe fluid overload, cerebral edema (see CRRT protocol)
- **SLED** — sustained low-efficiency; hybrid

## 🔄 Contrast-Induced Nephropathy (CIN) — Prevention

### Risk assessment
- CKD (CrCl &lt; 60) — highest risk
- DM + CKD = very high risk
- Age &gt; 75, HF, volume depletion, NSAIDs, high-volume contrast

### Prevention
- **Minimize contrast volume** (radiologist awareness)
- **IV hydration**: NSS 1 mL/kg/hr × 6–12 hr before + 6–12 hr after
- **Stop NSAIDs, ACE-I, metformin** (hold 48 hr post-contrast)
- Statin (emerging evidence)
- N-acetylcysteine — **evidence weak**; if used, 1,200 mg PO BID × 2 d

### Peri-procedural
- Use iso-osmolar or low-osmolar contrast
- Smallest volume that answers clinical question
- Delay elective contrast if AKI

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Pitfalls)

<Callout type="danger" title="AKI Pitfalls">

**Continue ACE-I/ARB in AKI** — worsens renal hemodynamics  
→ Hold temporarily; restart when Cr stable

**Miss obstruction** — easily treatable cause  
→ US + bladder scan in **any** AKI workup

**Over-resuscitate "for kidneys"** — fluid overload in sepsis/cardiogenic  
→ Assess volume status; dynamic measures (passive leg raise, US IVC)

**Miss drug-induced AIN** — insidious, often weeks after starting drug  
→ Review med list (PPI, NSAID, antibiotic, PPI); eosinophils, rash, fever

**Continue metformin in AKI** — lactic acidosis risk  
→ Hold metformin; resume when Cr normalized

**Delay dialysis in clear indication** — waiting for "maybe improves" → decompensation  
→ AEIOU → dialysis; consult nephrology early

**Contrast in CKD without prevention** — avoidable CIN  
→ IV hydration, minimize volume, nephrotoxins off

</Callout>

```
AKI — KDIGO Stage [1/2/3]

Baseline Cr: __  Current Cr: __ (ratio __)
UOP last 6 hr: __ mL
Etiology suspected: [prerenal / ATN / AIN / GN / obstruction / __]

Workup:
- UA + microscopy
- Urine Na, Cr, urea (for FeNa, FeUrea)
- BMP, Mg, Ca, PO₄, Hb, ABG/VBG
- Urine protein/Cr
- Renal US (if any concern for obstruction)
- Bladder scan (post-void residual)

Management:
1. Strict I/O, daily weight
2. Stop nephrotoxins: NSAIDs, ACE-I/ARB held, hold metformin, hold contrast
3. Adjust renally-cleared drugs
4. [Prerenal] IVF 500 mL NSS/LR bolus → reassess
5. [Post-renal] Foley / urology consult / nephrostomy
6. [AIN] Stop drug, consider prednisolone 1 mg/kg/day
7. Correct hyperkalemia aggressively (see K protocol)
8. Dialysis criteria (AEIOU):
   - Acidosis pH < 7.15 refractory
   - K > 6.5 refractory
   - Uremic symptoms (encephalopathy, pericarditis)
   - Overload not responsive to diuresis
   - Intoxication dialyzable
9. Nephrology consult (Stage 2–3, all dialysis candidates)
10. Admit [ward / ICU if RRT or severe metabolic]
```
