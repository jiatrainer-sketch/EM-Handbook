## 🎯 Criteria / Diagnosis

### Definition

**Convulsive Status Epilepticus (SE):**
- Continuous seizure **≥ 5 min**, **OR**
- **≥ 2 seizures without full recovery** between

**Refractory SE:** SE persists after 2 appropriate drugs (benzodiazepine + 1 second-line)

**Super-refractory SE:** continues &gt; 24 hr despite anesthetics

### Non-convulsive SE (NCSE) — underdiagnosed

- Altered mental status / coma ที่ไม่อธิบายจาก cause อื่น
- Subtle signs: eye deviation, automatisms, facial twitching
- Need **EEG** to diagnose
- Suspect in post-ictal patient ที่ไม่ตื่นใน 10–20 min

### Common causes (find + treat!)

1. **Known epilepsy**: missed meds, subtherapeutic levels
2. **Hypoglycemia** (check BS first!)
3. **Electrolyte**: hypoNa, hypoCa, hypoMg, uremia
4. **Infection**: meningitis, encephalitis, sepsis
5. **Stroke** (ischemic / hemorrhagic)
6. **Head trauma**
7. **Toxic**: alcohol withdrawal, cocaine, INH, TCA
8. **Metabolic**: hepatic encephalopathy, hyponatremia
9. **Anoxic / post-arrest**
10. **Pregnancy**: eclampsia (MgSO₄!)

## ⚡ การรักษาทันที — **Time-based protocol** (Immediate Actions)

### Stage 1: Stabilization (0–5 min)

1. **ABC + position**
   - Left lateral (safety)
   - O₂ via mask
   - Suction secretions
   - Protect from injury (ไม่ต้องยัด mouth guard)

2. **IV access**

3. **Bedside BS** — if &lt; 70 or unknown:
   - **Thiamine 100 mg IV** (if alcoholic/malnourished) — BEFORE glucose
   - **D50 50 mL IV push**

4. **Labs STAT:**
   - BS, electrolyte (Na, Ca, Mg)
   - BUN/Cr, LFT, CBC
   - AED level (if known epilepsy)
   - Toxicology if suspected
   - ABG
   - Pregnancy test (if applicable — think **eclampsia**)

5. **Suspected eclampsia** (pregnant ≥ 20 wk, HTN, proteinuria):
   - **MgSO₄ 4–6 g IV over 15–20 min**, then 1–2 g/hr drip
   - Not standard AED protocol

### Stage 2: Initial therapy — **Benzodiazepine** (5–20 min)

**First-line (in order of preference):**

**A. Lorazepam (IV preferred)**
- **4 mg IV over 2 min** (or 0.1 mg/kg, max single 4 mg)
- May repeat once in 5–10 min if seizure continues
- **Best efficacy + longest duration** among BZD

**B. Diazepam IV** (if no lorazepam)
- **10 mg IV over 2 min** (or 0.15–0.2 mg/kg, max 10 mg)
- May repeat once in 5 min
- Shorter duration — ต้องตาม 2nd line เร็ว

**C. Midazolam IM** (if no IV access)
- **10 mg IM** (or 0.2 mg/kg, max 10 mg)
- Rapid absorption, excellent for prehospital
- Alternatively: **buccal/intranasal 10 mg**

⚠️ **Underdosing is common cause of "refractory" SE** — ใช้ dose เต็ม

⚠️ **Apnea risk** — prepare BVM, have airway kit ready

### Stage 3: Second-line AED (20–40 min)

ถ้า seizure ไม่หยุดหลัง BZD × 2 doses → start **loading + control**

**Choose ONE (no clear winner per ESETT trial):**

**A. Fosphenytoin** (preferred IV)
- **20 mg PE/kg IV** (max rate **150 mg PE/min**)
- @ 60 kg = 1200 mg PE
- Less cardiotoxic than phenytoin
- Can give IM if needed

**B. Phenytoin** (ถ้าไม่มี fosphenytoin)
- **20 mg/kg IV** (max rate **50 mg/min**)
- @ 60 kg = 1200 mg
- ⚠️ Dilute in NSS only (precipitates in D5)
- ⚠️ Cardiac monitor — risk **hypotension, bradycardia, arrhythmia**
- ⚠️ Extravasation → "purple glove" necrosis → **central line preferred**

**C. Levetiracetam (Keppra)**
- **60 mg/kg IV over 10–15 min** (max single 4,500 mg)
- @ 60 kg = 3,600 mg
- **Few drug interactions + hemodynamic stability** — often best practical choice
- Renal dose adjustment needed

**D. Valproate** (avoid in liver dz, pregnancy)
- **40 mg/kg IV over 10 min** (max 3,000 mg)
- @ 60 kg = 2,400 mg
- ⚠️ Contraindicated: pregnancy, liver disease, mitochondrial disease
- Useful if absence / myoclonic SE

**E. Phenobarbital** (alternative, older)
- 15–20 mg/kg IV at 50–100 mg/min
- ⚠️ Heavy sedation + apnea — ต้อง prep intubate

### Stage 4: Refractory SE (40+ min) — **intubate + ICU**

Seizure ยังไม่หยุดหลัง BZD + 2nd line → **anesthetic drip**

**Prep: Intubate + cEEG monitoring**

**A. Midazolam drip**
- Load: 0.2 mg/kg IV
- Drip: **0.05–2 mg/kg/hr** (titrate to seizure suppression / burst-suppression)

**B. Propofol drip**
- Load: 1–2 mg/kg IV
- Drip: **20–200 mcg/kg/min** (= 1.2–12 mg/kg/hr)
- ⚠️ **Propofol infusion syndrome (PRIS)** — prolonged high dose → metabolic acidosis, rhabdo, cardiac failure. Avoid dose &gt; 5 mg/kg/hr &gt; 48 hr

**C. Pentobarbital** (super-refractory)
- Load: 5–15 mg/kg over 30 min
- Drip: 0.5–5 mg/kg/hr
- Risk: hypotension (often need vasopressor), infection, prolonged coma

**Target**: burst-suppression pattern on cEEG × 24–48 hr, then taper

## 💊 การเตรียมยา (Drug Preparation, BW 60 kg)

### Lorazepam (1st line)

```
Dose:     4 mg IV over 2 min (or 0.1 mg/kg, max single 4 mg)
Repeat:   Once in 5–10 min if still seizing
Prep:     Pure IV push (1 amp usually 2 mg/mL)
```

**🫘 Renal:** ไม่ต้องปรับ  
**🧓 Elderly:** ลดเหลือ 2 mg IV, ระวัง respiratory depression

---

### Diazepam (1st line alt)

```
Dose:     10 mg IV over 2 min (or 0.15–0.2 mg/kg, max 10 mg)
Repeat:   Once in 5 min
Prep:     Pure IV push (5 mg/mL)
```

**⚠️ Short duration in CNS (15–20 min)** — must follow with 2nd line AED

**🧓 Elderly:** ลด 5 mg, slow push

---

### Midazolam (1st line if no IV)

```
IM:       10 mg IM (or 0.2 mg/kg, max 10 mg)
Buccal:   10 mg intranasal / buccal (5 mg each nostril)
IV:       0.2 mg/kg load, then drip 0.05–2 mg/kg/hr
```

---

### Fosphenytoin (2nd line preferred)

```
Dose:     20 mg PE/kg IV
          @ 60 kg = 1,200 mg PE
Rate:     Max 150 mg PE/min (≈ 8 min @ 60 kg)
Prep:     Dilute in NSS or D5W (5 mg PE/mL max)
Monitor:  BP, ECG continuous
```

**🫘 Renal (CKD):** ไม่ต้องปรับ dose  
**🧓 Elderly:** ลด rate 25% (≤ 100 mg PE/min)

---

### Phenytoin (2nd line if no fosphenytoin)

```
Dose:     20 mg/kg IV
          @ 60 kg = 1,200 mg
Rate:     Max 50 mg/min (≈ 24 min @ 60 kg)
          Elderly: 25 mg/min
Prep:     Dilute in NSS only (precipitates in D5)
          Filter inline (0.22 micron)
Access:   Central line preferred (extravasation necrosis)
Monitor:  BP, ECG — stop if hypotension or arrhythmia
```

⚠️ **อย่าฉีด IM** (necrosis)  
⚠️ **Dilute in NSS only** (precipitate in D5W)

**🫘 Renal:** ไม่ต้องปรับ  
**🧓 Elderly:** slow rate (25 mg/min), monitor BP

---

### Levetiracetam (2nd line, practical favorite)

```
Dose:     60 mg/kg IV over 10–15 min
          @ 60 kg = 3,600 mg
          Max single dose 4,500 mg
Prep:     Dilute in 100 mL NSS
Maintenance: 1,000–3,000 mg/day divided BID
```

**🫘 Renal (CKD):**
- CrCl 50–80: 2,000 mg/day max
- CrCl 30–50: 1,500 mg/day max
- CrCl &lt; 30: 1,000 mg/day max
- HD: supplement post-HD

**🧓 Elderly:** Start lower maintenance, watch for drowsiness, behavior changes

---

### Valproate

```
Dose:     40 mg/kg IV over 10 min
          @ 60 kg = 2,400 mg (max 3,000 mg)
Prep:     Dilute in NSS or D5W
Maintenance: 1–4 mg/kg/hr drip OR q 6 hr dosing
```

**Contraindications:**
- **Pregnancy** (teratogenic)
- **Liver disease**
- **Mitochondrial disease** (fatal)
- **Urea cycle disorder**

**🫘 Renal:** ไม่ต้องปรับ major  
**🧓 Elderly:** ลด dose, watch LFT / platelet

---

### Midazolam drip (refractory SE)

```
Load:     0.2 mg/kg IV
          @ 60 kg = 12 mg IV
Drip:     0.05–2 mg/kg/hr
          Start 0.1 mg/kg/hr = 6 mg/hr
          Titrate to EEG burst-suppression
Prep:     Midazolam 100 mg + NSS 100 mL = 1 mg/mL
          @ 6 mg/hr = 6 mL/hr
```

---

### Propofol drip (refractory SE)

```
Load:     1–2 mg/kg IV
          @ 60 kg = 60–120 mg IV
Drip:     20–200 mcg/kg/min (1.2–12 mg/kg/hr)
          Start 30 mcg/kg/min = 1.8 mg/kg/hr = 108 mg/hr
Max safe duration high-dose: < 48 hr to avoid PRIS
```

⚠️ **PRIS** (propofol infusion syndrome): metabolic acidosis, rhabdo, cardiac failure  
→ Monitor CK, triglyceride, lactate q 12 hr

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Critical Pitfalls)

<Callout type="danger" title="Status Epilepticus — Pitfalls">

**Underdosing BZD = "refractory"**
→ Lorazepam 4 mg (NOT 1–2 mg)
→ Diazepam 10 mg (NOT 5 mg)
→ Use full dose!

**Check BS + give thiamine first if alcoholic**
→ Hypoglycemia mimics SE
→ Fix BS + thiamine before glucose

**Pregnant seizure = eclampsia**
→ MgSO₄ 4–6 g IV (NOT standard AED)
→ Magnesium toxicity: check reflex, RR, UO

**NCSE missed in "not waking post-ictal"**
→ Altered mental > 10–20 min post-seizure
→ Need EEG
→ Keep treating until EEG confirms no SE

**Phenytoin extravasation → necrosis**
→ Central line preferred
→ NSS only (D5W precipitates)
→ Cardiac monitor during load

**Underdose 2nd line AED**
→ Phenytoin / fosphenytoin = 20 mg/kg (= 1,200 mg @ 60 kg)
→ Levetiracetam = 60 mg/kg (= 3,600 mg)
→ ลด dose น้อย = seizure recur

**Propofol > 5 mg/kg/hr > 48 hr = PRIS**
→ Monitor CK, TG, lactate, pH
→ Switch to midazolam if prolonged

**Don't forget underlying cause**
→ CT brain (trauma, stroke, hemorrhage)
→ LP (meningitis / encephalitis) — after CT
→ Electrolyte, toxicology
→ Treat cause in parallel with seizure control

**Post-SE care**
→ Continue maintenance AED
→ Neurology consult
→ EEG to r/o NCSE
→ Identify + prevent recurrence

</Callout>

## 📝 คำสั่งพร้อมใช้ (Copy-Ready Order)

```
1. ABC — lateral position, O₂ via mask, suction
   (prepare intubation if prolonged or refractory)
2. IV access × 2
3. Continuous ECG, SpO₂, BP
4. Bedside BS STAT
   - If < 70 or unknown:
     Thiamine 100 mg IV (if alcoholic/malnourished)
     D50W 50 mL IV push
5. Labs STAT:
   - CBC, BUN/Cr, electrolyte (Na, Ca, Mg, PO4)
   - LFT, glucose
   - AED levels (if known epilepsy)
   - Toxicology screen
   - ABG / VBG
   - Pregnancy test (if applicable)
   - Blood culture + CSF if febrile
6. FIRST LINE (5–20 min) — choose ONE:
   - Lorazepam 4 mg IV over 2 min (preferred), repeat x1 in 5–10 min
   - Diazepam 10 mg IV over 2 min, repeat x1 in 5 min
   - Midazolam 10 mg IM (if no IV)
7. SECOND LINE (20–40 min) — if still seizing, choose ONE:
   - Fosphenytoin 20 mg PE/kg IV (max 150 PE/min) — 1,200 mg PE @ 60 kg
   - Phenytoin 20 mg/kg IV (max 50 mg/min in NSS) — 1,200 mg @ 60 kg
   - Levetiracetam 60 mg/kg IV over 15 min — 3,600 mg @ 60 kg
   - Valproate 40 mg/kg IV over 10 min (avoid in preg / liver dz)
8. Pregnancy + HTN + proteinuria:
   - MgSO₄ 4–6 g IV over 15 min, then 1–2 g/hr drip
   - Deliver baby (definitive)
9. THIRD LINE (40+ min, refractory):
   - Intubate + ICU transfer
   - Midazolam drip 0.05–2 mg/kg/hr
     OR Propofol drip 20–200 mcg/kg/min
     (monitor for PRIS if > 48 hr)
   - cEEG monitoring
10. Imaging + workup:
    - CT brain non-contrast (trauma, stroke, hemorrhage)
    - LP (after CT ok) if meningitis/encephalitis suspected
    - MRI brain + EEG once stable
11. Treat precipitant (infection, electrolyte, drug withdrawal)
12. Neurology consult
13. Maintenance AED — continue 2nd line drug as daily regimen
14. Admit ICU (refractory) or step-down (controlled)
```
