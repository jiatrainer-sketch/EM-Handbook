## 🎯 Diagnosis

- **Clinical**: cough, fever, sputum, pleuritic pain, dyspnea
- **Exam**: tachypnea, hypoxemia, crackles, bronchial breath sounds, dullness
- **CXR**: new infiltrate (may be delayed 24 hr in dehydrated/neutropenic)
- **± blood/sputum culture, urinary antigens**

---

## 📊 CURB-65 — Severity + Disposition

| Score | Criteria | Point |
|---|---|---|
| **C** | Confusion (new) | 1 |
| **U** | Urea / BUN &gt; 19 mg/dL (&gt; 7 mmol/L) | 1 |
| **R** | Respiratory rate ≥ 30 | 1 |
| **B** | BP: SBP &lt; 90 or DBP ≤ 60 | 1 |
| **65** | Age ≥ 65 | 1 |

### Disposition

| CURB-65 | 30-d Mortality | Disposition |
|---|---|---|
| 0–1 | &lt; 3% | Outpatient |
| 2 | ~9% | Short admit / ward |
| ≥ 3 | 15–40% | Ward / **ICU** (esp. ≥ 4) |

<Callout type="danger" title="ICU if any of the following (IDSA)">

**Major (any 1):**
- Septic shock needing vasopressor
- Respiratory failure needing mechanical ventilation

**Minor (≥ 3):**
- RR ≥ 30, PaO₂/FiO₂ ≤ 250
- Multilobar infiltrate, confusion
- BUN ≥ 20, WBC &lt; 4,000, platelet &lt; 100k
- Hypothermia (T &lt; 36°C), hypotension needing aggressive fluid

</Callout>

## 🧪 การตรวจวินิจฉัย (Workup)

### All inpatients
- CBC with diff, BMP, LFT, glucose, lactate
- CXR (PA + lateral if possible)
- Blood culture × 2 (before abx)
- Sputum G/S + C/S (quality: &gt; 25 PMN + &lt; 10 squamous/LPF)
- Pulse oximetry / ABG if hypoxic
- **Urinary antigens** (pneumococcal, Legionella) in severe CAP

### Consider
- **Influenza/SARS-CoV-2 PCR** during season
- **Procalcitonin** (↓ as guide to stop abx; don't use to withhold in bacterial-suspicion CAP)
- **HIV** (all CAP patients)
- **CT chest** — complicated (abscess, empyema, necrotizing)
- **Thoracentesis** if significant pleural effusion (parapneumonic)

## 🦠 Empirical Antibiotics

### Outpatient

| Pt Group | Regimen |
|---|---|
| Previously healthy, no comorbid, no recent abx | **Amoxicillin 1 g PO TID** × 5–7 d OR **Doxycycline 100 mg PO BID** |
| Comorbid (DM, CKD, COPD, CHF, ↓immune) OR recent abx | **Amox-clav 875/125 BID** + **Azithromycin 500 mg day 1 then 250 mg** OR **Levofloxacin 750 mg QD** |

### Inpatient — non-ICU
- **Ceftriaxone 1–2 g IV daily + Azithromycin 500 mg IV/PO daily** × 5–7 d
- **Alternative**: Levofloxacin 750 mg IV/PO daily
- **Amox-clav 1.2 g IV q 8 hr + Azithromycin** (some Thai centers)

### Inpatient — ICU (severe)
- **Ceftriaxone 2 g IV q24h + Azithromycin 500 mg IV daily** (or Levofloxacin)
- **Pseudomonas risk** (structural lung, recent abx, prolonged steroid): **Pip-tazo 4.5 g IV q 6–8 hr + Azithromycin/Levofloxacin**
- **MRSA risk** (severe, post-influenza, necrotizing): add **Vancomycin 25 mg/kg load** or **Linezolid 600 mg BID**

### Duration
- **Minimum 5 days** + clinically stable (afebrile ≥ 48 hr, stable VS)
- Extend if: complications (empyema, abscess), MRSA, Pseudomonas, Legionella (up to 14 d)

---

## 💊 Adjunct Management

### Oxygen + respiratory support
- Target SpO₂ ≥ 92% (≥ 88% in COPD)
- HFNC, NIV, or intubation as needed

### Fluids
- Treat sepsis: 30 mL/kg crystalloid if septic shock (balanced or NS)
- Avoid over-resuscitation (pulmonary edema worsens oxygenation)

### Corticosteroids — when?
- **Severe CAP + septic shock + CRP &gt; 150**: Hydrocortisone 200 mg/day IV infusion (CAPE COD trial — ↓ mortality)
- Not routine for all CAP
- Routine in ARDS

### Other
- DVT prophylaxis
- Early mobilization
- Nutrition

## 🏥 Special Scenarios

### Aspiration pneumonia
- Risk: altered mental status, stroke, dysphagia, ETOH
- Anaerobes: **Amox-clav** or **Ampicillin-sulbactam** or **Clindamycin**
- Add anaerobe coverage to standard

### Post-influenza pneumonia (Staph aureus)
- **Must cover MRSA** (vancomycin or linezolid)
- Necrotizing, rapid progression

### HAP/VAP (hospital-acquired)
- NOT CAP — different organisms (Pseudomonas, MRSA, resistant GN)
- Empirical: Pip-tazo + Vanco ± Aminoglycoside if severe
- Piperacillin-tazobactam 4.5 g IV q 6 hr OR Cefepime 2 g q 8 hr OR Meropenem

### Thai-specific
- **Melioidosis** (*Burkholderia pseudomallei*) — NE Thailand, diabetics, rice farmers  
  → Ceftazidime 2 g q 6 hr OR Meropenem 1 g q 8 hr for 10–14 d (+ eradication 3 mo)
- **Leptospirosis** pneumonia — exposure to water/rodents
- **Scrub typhus** — Doxycycline; rural exposure + eschar

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Pitfalls)

<Callout type="danger" title="CAP Pitfalls">

**Delay antibiotic** — time-to-antibiotic &gt; 4 hr associated with ↑ mortality  
→ Target 1 hr from triage (especially septic)

**Wrong empirical in healthcare contact** — HAP/VAP need different coverage  
→ Ask about hospitalization &gt; 2 d in past 90 d, dialysis, nursing home

**No atypical coverage** — missing Mycoplasma, Legionella, Chlamydia  
→ Always include macrolide or FQ for inpatient

**Miss parapneumonic effusion / empyema** — persistent fever, loculated effusion  
→ CT + thoracentesis; chest tube if pH &lt; 7.2 or pus

**Missed TB** — upper lobe infiltrate, cavitation, chronic symptoms  
→ AFB + Xpert in any atypical or non-responding pneumonia

**Thai-specific missed melioidosis** — diabetic farmer + severe pneumonia  
→ Blood cx + add ceftazidime coverage empirically

</Callout>

## 📝 คำสั่งพร้อมใช้ (Order Template)

```
CAP — [Date]
CURB-65: __ / Severity: [mild/moderate/severe]

CXR: [lobar/multilobar, cavitation, effusion]
Labs: WBC__ BUN__ Cr__ Lactate__ SpO₂__%
Blood cx × 2: sent
Sputum: sent
UA legionella, pneumococcal: [inpatient severe]

1. O₂ via NC/mask → target SpO₂ ≥ 92%
2. IVF NSS/LR if hypotensive / septic (30 mL/kg if shock)
3. Antibiotic STAT:
   - Ceftriaxone 2 g IV daily
   - Azithromycin 500 mg IV daily
   (Levofloxacin 750 mg if PCN allergy)
   (+ Vancomycin if MRSA risk / post-flu)
   (+ Pip-tazo if Pseudomonas risk)
4. DVT prophylaxis (enoxaparin 40 mg SC daily)
5. Nebulizer PRN (if wheeze)
6. Influenza PCR + SARS-CoV-2 (seasonal)
7. Admit [ward / ICU if ≥ 2 minor or 1 major]
8. Narrow abx in 48–72 hr based on cultures
9. Total course 5–7 d; longer if complications
```
