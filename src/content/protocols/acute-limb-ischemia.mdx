## 🎯 When to suspect — "6 P's"

1. **Pain** (sudden, severe)
2. **Pallor** (then cyanosis/mottling)
3. **Pulseless** (compare to contralateral limb)
4. **Paresthesia** (numbness → progressive)
5. **Paralysis** (motor loss = late, limb-threatening)
6. **Poikilothermia** (cold limb)

### Etiology

- **Embolic** (~ 30%) — sudden, proximal (AFib, LV thrombus, valve, aortic plaque)
- **Thrombotic** (~ 60%) — atherosclerosis background, slower onset
- **Traumatic / iatrogenic** — catheterization, arterial cannulation
- **Dissection extension** (aortic → iliac → femoral)

### Risk factors

- Atrial fibrillation (main embolic source)
- Recent MI (LV thrombus)
- Prior PAD / bypass graft
- Hypercoagulable state
- Arterial catheter

## 🗂 Rutherford Classification

| Class | Description | Sensory | Motor | Doppler arterial | Doppler venous | Viable? |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **I — Viable** | Not threatened | Normal | Normal | Audible | Audible | Yes |
| **IIa — Marginally threatened** | Salvageable with prompt tx | Minimal (toes) | Normal | Inaudible | Audible | Yes |
| **IIb — Immediately threatened** | Salvageable with **immediate** revasc | &gt; toes, rest pain | Mild–moderate | Inaudible | Audible | Yes (urgent) |
| **III — Irreversible** | Limb loss; amputation | Profound, anesthetic | Profound paralysis, rigor | Inaudible | Inaudible | No |

## 💉 Immediate Actions (first 30 min)

1. **O₂ to keep SpO₂ ≥ 94%**, IV × 2
2. **Heparin bolus 80 U/kg IV** (max 5,000 U)
3. **Heparin drip 18 U/kg/hr** — target aPTT 1.5–2.5× control
4. **Pain control:** morphine 2–4 mg IV or fentanyl 25–50 mcg
5. **Keep limb warm** (blanket, not external heat source)
6. **Do NOT elevate** (gravity helps perfusion; do not dangle either)
7. **Mark pulse exam** + Rutherford class
8. **NPO** — anticipate procedure

### Labs

- CBC, BMP, lactate, CPK
- Coags (PT/PTT/INR — baseline for heparin)
- Type and cross 2 units
- ECG (AFib? recent MI?)
- Troponin

### Imaging

- **CTA arterial** (preferred, fast) — identifies level of occlusion + collaterals
- **Duplex US** bedside — screen, Doppler waveforms
- **MRA** if contrast contraindicated

---

## 🏥 Definitive Management

### Rutherford I (Viable)

- Heparin drip
- Elective imaging + revasc planning
- Vascular surgery consult (not emergent)

### Rutherford IIa (Marginally threatened)

- Heparin drip + urgent imaging (&lt; 24 hr)
- Vascular surgery / IR for revascularization
- Options: catheter-directed thrombolysis, surgical thrombectomy

### Rutherford IIb (Immediately threatened) — **STAT**

- Heparin drip
- **Vascular surgery STAT** (revasc within 6 hr)
- Options:
  - **Embolectomy** (Fogarty) — preferred for embolic
  - **Thrombolysis** — catheter-directed if thrombotic
  - **Bypass** — if occlusion long segment
- OR + post-op ICU admit

### Rutherford III (Irreversible)

- **Amputation planning** (primary)
- Supportive: analgesia, fluids, renal protection
- Counsel: limb not salvageable; revasc → rhabdo, hyperK, cardiac arrest (reperfusion syndrome)

---

## ⚠️ Reperfusion Syndrome

After revascularization of IIb/III:

- **Hyperkalemia** → cardiac arrhythmia
- **Acidosis** (lactate release)
- **Myoglobinuria** → AKI
- **Compartment syndrome** → fasciotomy

### Management

- Continuous ECG, K q 1 hr × 6 hr
- IV fluid (NSS) + urine alkalinization if myoglobinuria (pH &gt; 6.5)
- Fasciotomy if compartment pressure &gt; 30 mmHg
- Dialysis stand-by for severe rhabdo/AKI

---

## 📊 การติดตาม (Monitoring)

- Serial pulse exam q 15 min → q 1 hr
- Motor/sensory q 1 hr
- Skin color, temperature
- Urine output (myoglobinuria)
- aPTT q 6 hr on heparin (target 1.5–2.5×)
- Compartment pressure if swelling

---

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Critical Pitfalls)

<Callout type="danger" title="Acute Limb Ischemia — Pitfalls">

**Time is limb — 6 hr golden window**
→ Rutherford IIb → revasc &lt; 6 hr = limb salvage
→ &gt; 12 hr → high amputation rate

**Heparin IMMEDIATELY — don't wait for imaging**
→ Load 80 U/kg + 18 U/kg/hr drip
→ Prevents propagation while evaluating

**Rutherford III = amputation, not revasc**
→ Revasc irreversible limb → reperfusion syndrome
→ Cardiac arrest from hyperK + rhabdo

**Identify source**
→ AFib → anticoagulate long term
→ LV thrombus (echo) → anticoagulate
→ Prior PAD → surveillance, antiplatelet

**Don't warm actively (heating pad)**
→ Can worsen ischemia (↑ O₂ demand)
→ Passive warming only (blanket)

**Bilateral leg ischemia**
→ Think aortic saddle embolus / AAA thrombosis / dissection
→ CTA chest-abdomen-pelvis

**Post-revasc: watch compartment syndrome**
→ Pain on passive stretch, tense compartment
→ Fasciotomy if &gt; 30 mmHg

</Callout>

## 📝 คำสั่งพร้อมใช้ (Copy-Ready Order)

```
Dx: Acute limb ischemia, Rutherford [I/IIa/IIb/III]

1. O₂ to SpO₂ ≥ 94%, IV × 2
2. Pulse exam + Rutherford class documented
3. Labs: CBC, BMP, coags, lactate, CPK, type and cross 2 units
4. ECG (r/o AFib)
5. HEPARIN:
   - Bolus 80 U/kg IV (max 5,000 U)
   - Drip 18 U/kg/hr, target aPTT 1.5–2.5×
6. Pain: morphine 2–4 mg IV or fentanyl 25–50 mcg q 10 min PRN
7. Imaging: CTA arterial (preferred) or Duplex US
8. Keep limb warm (blanket), horizontal, NOT elevated
9. NPO — anticipate procedure
10. CONSULT:
    - Rutherford IIb → vascular surgery STAT (revasc &lt; 6 hr)
    - Rutherford III → vascular surgery for amputation planning
    - Rutherford IIa → urgent (&lt; 24 hr)
11. Post-revasc watch:
    - Reperfusion syndrome (K, acid-base)
    - Compartment syndrome (check q 1 hr)
    - Rhabdo (CPK, urine myoglobin)
12. Source workup: echo, AFib monitor, hypercoag if &lt; 50 yr
13. ICU / monitored bed admit post-revasc
```
