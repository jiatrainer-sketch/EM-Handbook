## 🎯 Pathophysiology

Mass tumor cell destruction → release intracellular contents:
- ↑ K (hyperkalemia)
- ↑ PO₄ (hyperphosphatemia) → ↓ Ca (hypocalcemia)
- ↑ uric acid (urate nephropathy → AKI)
- → **AKI, arrhythmia, seizures, death**

---

## 📊 Cairo-Bishop Criteria

### Laboratory TLS — ≥ 2 of 4 within 3 days before to 7 days after chemo
| Lab | Threshold |
|---|---|
| Uric acid | ≥ 8 mg/dL or ↑ 25% |
| Potassium | ≥ 6.0 mEq/L or ↑ 25% |
| Phosphate | ≥ 4.5 (adult) or ≥ 6.5 (pediatric) or ↑ 25% |
| Calcium | ≤ 7.0 mg/dL or ↓ 25% |

### Clinical TLS — Laboratory TLS + 1 of:
- **Cr &gt; 1.5× ULN** (or AKI)
- **Cardiac arrhythmia / sudden death**
- **Seizure**

---

## ⚠️ Risk Stratification

### High risk (prophylaxis needed)
- **Burkitt lymphoma, ALL** with high WBC
- **Acute leukemias** with high WBC &gt; 100k, high LDH
- Tumor burden (bulky disease, high LDH, organ infiltration)
- Pre-existing CKD or dehydration
- Hyperuricemia at baseline

### Intermediate risk
- Non-bulky lymphoma, CLL
- Indolent lymphoma with high burden

### Low risk
- Most solid tumors (small cell lung can TLS post-chemo)
- Multiple myeloma (rarely)

---

## 💊 Management

### Prevention (HIGH risk — before chemo)

<Callout type="success" title="Prophylaxis for high-risk patients">

1. **Aggressive IV hydration**: NSS 2.5–3 L/m²/day → target UOP ≥ 2 mL/kg/hr
2. **Allopurinol** 300 mg PO daily × 3–7 d (before chemo); or
3. **Rasburicase** 0.2 mg/kg IV × 1–5 doses for very high risk / hyperuricemia
4. **Avoid K + Ca in fluids** initially
5. Monitor labs q 4–6 hr during high-risk period

</Callout>

### Rasburicase vs Allopurinol

| | Allopurinol | Rasburicase |
|---|---|---|
| Mechanism | Xanthine oxidase inhibitor (prevents formation) | Converts uric acid → allantoin (lowers existing) |
| Onset | Slow (2–3 d) | Minutes |
| Uric acid | ↓ gradually | ↓↓ rapidly (to near 0) |
| Cost | Cheap | Expensive |
| Use | Low/intermediate risk; prophylaxis | High risk; established TLS; severe hyperuricemia |

<Callout type="warning" title="Rasburicase contraindications">

- **G6PD deficiency** — hemolysis (screen before)
- **Pregnancy**
- Methemoglobinemia risk
- Past hypersensitivity to rasburicase

</Callout>

### Treat established TLS

**Hyperkalemia:**
- Continuous ECG
- Calcium gluconate (if ECG changes)
- Insulin + glucose, salbutamol, kayexalate
- **Low threshold for dialysis**

**Hyperphosphatemia:**
- Phosphate binders (sevelamer — preferred; calcium-containing if not also hypercalcemic — but risk metastatic calcification if Ca × P &gt; 70)
- Dialysis if severe + symptomatic

**Hypocalcemia:**
- **Do NOT correct** unless symptomatic (tetany, seizure, prolonged QT)
- Calcium + phosphate → metastatic calcification
- IV calcium only for life-threatening

**Hyperuricemia:**
- Rasburicase 0.2 mg/kg IV (give if uric acid &gt; 8 + rising)
- IV hydration + alkalinization (controversial — may worsen Ca phosphate precipitation)

**AKI:**
- Hydration, avoid nephrotoxins
- **Dialysis** if indication (K, volume, uremia, severe acidosis)
- CRRT if unstable or severe — effective at removing K, P, urate

---

## 🏥 Monitoring

- **Labs q 4–6 hr** during high-risk period (24–72 hr post-chemo)
- Continuous ECG
- **ICU** if clinical TLS or high risk
- Strict I/O

---

## ⚠️ Pitfalls

<Callout type="danger" title="TLS Pitfalls">

**Use allopurinol alone in high risk** — slow onset, can't rescue  
→ Rasburicase for high-risk or established TLS

**Correct hypocalcemia routinely** — precipitates Ca-phosphate → metastatic calcification  
→ Only symptomatic (tetany, seizure, QT)

**Alkalinize urine routinely** — precipitates calcium phosphate  
→ Controversial; avoid if significant hyperphosphatemia

**Miss TLS in other tumors** — small cell lung, hepatocellular, aggressive GC  
→ Any high-burden or rapid-turnover cancer can TLS

**Wait for established TLS** — prevention is key  
→ Risk-stratify before chemo; aggressive hydration + urate-lowering

**G6PD + rasburicase** — severe hemolysis  
→ Screen before giving rasburicase (esp. in Asian/African descent)

</Callout>

## 📝 Order template

```
TLS [prophylaxis / laboratory / clinical]

Cancer: [diagnosis, burden]
Risk: [high / intermediate / low]
Pre-chemo labs: UA__ K__ P__ Ca__ Cr__ LDH__

Prevention (high-risk):
1. IVF NSS 2.5–3 L/m²/day (target UOP ≥ 2 mL/kg/hr)
2. Allopurinol 300 mg PO daily × 3–7 d (if intermediate)
   OR
   Rasburicase 0.2 mg/kg IV × 1 dose (if high risk, check G6PD first)
3. Monitor labs q 4–6 hr × 72 hr post-chemo
4. Continuous ECG

Treatment (established TLS):
5. Hyperkalemia: see K protocol; low threshold for dialysis
6. Hyperphosphatemia: sevelamer 800–1600 mg PO TID; dialysis if severe
7. Hypocalcemia: treat only if symptomatic (tetany, seizure, QT)
8. Hyperuricemia: rasburicase 0.2 mg/kg if UA > 8 + AKI
9. Nephrology consult; dialysis (IHD or CRRT) per AEIOU
10. Avoid nephrotoxins; adjust drug dosing
11. Admit ICU for clinical TLS
```
