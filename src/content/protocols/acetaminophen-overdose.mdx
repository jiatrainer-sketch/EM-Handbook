## 🎯 When to suspect

- Ingestion &gt; 7.5 g (adult) or &gt; 150 mg/kg (single dose)
- Chronic supratherapeutic dosing &gt; 4 g/day (esp alcoholic, malnourished, CYP inducers)
- Suicide attempt with unknown pills
- Co-ingestion (often with TCA, benzo, EtOH)
- Late presentation: RUQ pain, jaundice, hepatic failure

### Time course (4 stages)

1. **0–24 hr:** Minimal symptoms (N/V, malaise)
2. **24–72 hr:** RUQ pain, elevated AST/ALT begin
3. **72–96 hr:** Peak hepatotoxicity; coagulopathy, encephalopathy
4. **4 d–2 wk:** Recovery OR fulminant hepatic failure

### High-risk factors (NAC threshold lower)

- Chronic EtOH use
- Malnutrition (↓ glutathione)
- CYP2E1 inducers (isoniazid, rifampin, phenytoin, carbamazepine)
- HIV, chronic hepatitis

## 🧪 การตรวจวินิจฉัย (Workup)

### Key lab: **Acetaminophen level at 4 hr post-ingestion** (or ASAP if &gt; 4 hr)

- Time-zero = ingestion time
- If unknown timing: draw now + consider empirical NAC
- Repeat at 8, 12, 16 hr if initial at borderline

### Other labs

- **LFT** (AST, ALT, bilirubin) — baseline + q 12 hr
- **INR / PT** — q 12 hr (prognostic)
- **BMP** (Cr, BUN, glucose — hypoglycemia late)
- **Lactate, ABG** (prognostic in fulminant)
- **Lipase** (pancreatitis rare)
- Pregnancy test
- **Co-ingestion screen:** salicylate, TCA, EtOH

---

## 📊 Rumack-Matthew Nomogram

### Uses

- **Single acute ingestion** only
- Known time of ingestion
- Level drawn ≥ **4 hr** post-ingestion

### Treatment line

- **Treatment line:** 150 mcg/mL at 4 hr → 4.7 mcg/mL at 24 hr
- Level **above line** → **Treat with NAC**
- Level **below line** → **No NAC needed**

### ⚠️ Nomogram NOT applicable

- Chronic supratherapeutic dosing
- Staggered ingestion (multiple doses over hours)
- Unknown time of ingestion
- Extended-release formulation
- &lt; 4 hr post-ingestion (premature level)
- **In these: treat empirically if any risk of toxicity**

---

## 💊 N-Acetylcysteine (NAC) — mainstay antidote

### When to start

- **As soon as possible** if level above treatment line
- **Empirically** if level pending and &gt; 8 hr since ingestion (don't delay)
- Toxic ingestion (&gt; 150 mg/kg or &gt; 7.5 g) even before level
- Evidence of hepatotoxicity (↑ AST/ALT, INR)

### IV Protocol (21-hour total)

| Phase | Dose | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| Load | **150 mg/kg** in D5W 200 mL | **1 hr** |
| Dose 2 | **50 mg/kg** in D5W 500 mL | **4 hr** |
| Dose 3 | **100 mg/kg** in D5W 1 L | **16 hr** |

**Total: 300 mg/kg over 21 hours**

### Continue NAC beyond 21 hr if:

- LFT still rising
- INR &gt; 2.0 at 21 hr
- APAP level still detectable
- Any sign of hepatic failure

→ Continue dose 3 rate (100 mg/kg / 16 hr) until improvement

### PO Protocol (72-hour total, rare use)

- 140 mg/kg PO load, then 70 mg/kg PO q 4 hr × 17 doses
- Use only if IV not available
- More GI intolerance

### Side effects

- **Anaphylactoid reaction** (common, usually on load)
  - Flushing, urticaria, bronchospasm, hypotension
  - Not true IgE — can continue after pretreatment
  - Stop infusion, antihistamine (diphenhydramine 50 mg IV)
  - Restart at slower rate once resolved
- Nausea, vomiting

---

## 🏥 Disposition + Transfer Criteria

### King's College Criteria — **refer to transplant center**

Meets **any one**:
- Arterial pH &lt; 7.30 after resuscitation
- **All 3 of:**
  - INR &gt; 6.5 (PT &gt; 100 sec)
  - Cr &gt; 3.4 mg/dL
  - Grade III/IV encephalopathy
- **Serum lactate &gt; 3.5** (4 hr) or **&gt; 3.0** (12 hr) after fluid resuscitation

### General criteria

- Admit if any toxicity or NAC indicated
- ICU if encephalopathy, coagulopathy, or fulminant course
- Transplant center referral per King's criteria

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Critical Pitfalls)

<Callout type="danger" title="Acetaminophen (Paracetamol) Overdose — Pitfalls">

**Don't wait for symptoms — treat on level**
→ Early stage asymptomatic
→ By time symptomatic (72 hr) → often irreversible

**Nomogram doesn't work for staggered ingestion**
→ Treat empirically based on total dose
→ LFT + INR as guide

**&gt; 8 hr post-ingestion: start NAC empirically**
→ Don't wait for level — efficacy drops after 8 hr
→ Can stop if level below line

**Check for co-ingestion**
→ TCA, salicylate, EtOH
→ 50% APAP overdoses have co-ingestion

**Elevated INR without bleeding**
→ Don't give FFP routinely — masks prognostication
→ Only if active bleeding or invasive procedure

**Anaphylactoid reaction to NAC**
→ Not true allergy — usually dose-rate related
→ Slow infusion, antihistamine, restart
→ Don't stop antidote entirely

**Pregnancy — treat normally**
→ NAC crosses placenta, protects fetal liver
→ Same dosing

**Chronic EtOH users — lower threshold**
→ Treat at lower levels (Rumack line adjusted)
→ Higher hepatotoxicity risk

</Callout>

## 📝 คำสั่งพร้อมใช้ (Copy-Ready Order)

```
Dx: Acetaminophen overdose [acute / staggered / chronic supratherapeutic]

1. IV × 1; NPO initially; continuous monitor
2. Labs STAT:
   - Acetaminophen level (4 hr post-ingestion minimum)
   - AST, ALT, bilirubin, INR, PT
   - BMP, glucose, lactate, ABG
   - Lipase, CBC
   - Co-ingestion: salicylate, TCA, EtOH
   - βhCG if reproductive-age female
3. ACTIVATED CHARCOAL 1 g/kg PO/NG if &lt; 1 hr + awake + no contraindication
4. NAC (N-acetylcysteine) — start if:
   - Level above Rumack treatment line (4–24 hr), OR
   - &gt; 8 hr since ingestion (empirical), OR
   - Staggered / unknown timing with &gt; 150 mg/kg, OR
   - Any hepatotoxicity (AST &gt; 1000, INR &gt; 1.5)
   
   IV 21-hr protocol:
   [ ] 150 mg/kg in D5W 200 mL over 1 hr (load)
   [ ] 50 mg/kg in D5W 500 mL over 4 hr
   [ ] 100 mg/kg in D5W 1 L over 16 hr
   
5. LFT + INR q 12 hr
6. Extend NAC beyond 21 hr if LFT rising or INR &gt; 2
7. Supportive:
   - Glucose monitoring (hypoglycemia late stage)
   - IV hydration; avoid hepatotoxic meds
   - No acetaminophen, no EtOH
8. Monitor for King's College Criteria — transplant referral if met
9. Psych consult before discharge (suicide)
10. Admit medical ward (mild) or ICU (severe / encephalopathy)
```
