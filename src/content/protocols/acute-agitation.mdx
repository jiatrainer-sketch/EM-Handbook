## 🎯 Initial Approach

<Callout type="danger" title="Safety first — provider + patient + others">

1. **Environment**: clear exits, remove objects, security/staff present
2. **De-escalation first** (verbal, calm tone, offer space/water/choices)
3. **Offer oral medications** before IM
4. **Physical restraints** only if imminent harm + always with chemical restraint + reassess q 15 min
5. **Chemical restraint** when needed — avoid under-dosing
6. **Always rule out medical** cause (not just psych)

</Callout>

---

## 🧠 Differential — Medical vs Psychiatric

### Medical causes of agitation (MUST consider)
- **Hypoxia, hypoglycemia, hypercarbia** (first!)
- **Toxidromes**: sympathomimetic (meth, cocaine), anticholinergic, serotonin syndrome, alcohol/benzo withdrawal
- **CNS**: stroke, head trauma, meningitis, encephalitis, seizure (postictal)
- **Metabolic**: electrolyte (Na, Ca), thyroid storm, hepatic encephalopathy, uremia
- **Infection**: UTI (elderly), pneumonia, sepsis
- **Drug intoxication / withdrawal**

### Psychiatric
- Mania, psychosis (schizophrenia, brief)
- Severe anxiety / panic
- Agitated depression
- Personality disorder
- PTSD

### Excited delirium / hyperactive delirium
- Extreme agitation + hyperthermia + autonomic instability + psychosis
- **High mortality** — medical emergency
- Rx: aggressive cooling, IV sedation (IV benzo + ketamine), monitor K (rhabdo), fluid resuscitation

## 💊 Medications

### First-line: Antipsychotic or Benzodiazepine (or combination)

**Haloperidol**
```
5–10 mg IM (adult) or 5 mg IV slow
Can repeat q 30 min × 2–3 (max 20 mg usually)
Onset: IM 30–60 min; IV 10 min
```
Good for: psychosis, mania
Watch: QT prolongation, dystonia/EPS, NMS

**Lorazepam**
```
2–4 mg IM or IV
Can repeat q 30 min
Max 8–10 mg total
```
Good for: alcohol/benzo withdrawal, stimulant intoxication, unknown
Watch: respiratory depression (esp. with opioid)

**Midazolam**
```
5–10 mg IM (onset 5–15 min, fast!)
2.5–5 mg IV (onset 2–5 min)
Can repeat q 15–30 min
```
Good for: rapid sedation, IM preferred over IV in agitated
Watch: resp depression; short t½ (re-agitation possible)

### Combination (synergistic)
- **"5-2-50"**: Haloperidol 5 mg + Lorazepam 2 mg + Diphenhydramine 50 mg IM  
  (The "B-52"; diphenhydramine for EPS prevention)
- Or **Haloperidol + Midazolam IM** (rapid onset + psychosis control)

### Atypical antipsychotics (alternative)
- **Olanzapine** 5–10 mg IM — less QT/EPS than haloperidol  
  (avoid with IM benzo — respiratory depression)
- **Ziprasidone** 10–20 mg IM
- **Quetiapine** — only oral; sedating

### Ketamine — Severe / excited delirium / refractory

<Callout type="warning" title="Ketamine — potent, monitor airway">

- **4–5 mg/kg IM** (usually 400–500 mg IM adult) — rapid onset
- **1–2 mg/kg IV** slow push
- Use: severely agitated, not responsive to benzo/haloperidol, excited delirium
- Monitor: airway, BP (may go up), emergence reaction
- Consider intubation-ready

</Callout>

## 🧪 Workup (once safe)

### Minimum
- Glucose (fingerstick)
- Vital signs including temperature
- Pulse oximetry
- Brief neuro exam (pupils, focal signs)
- Substance use history + timing

### Laboratory (broader)
- CBC, BMP, LFT, TSH
- ETOH, urine drug screen (cocaine, amphetamines, opioid, benzo, THC, PCP)
- Paracetamol + salicylate (intentional OD)
- Lactate (sepsis, exertion)
- CK (rhabdo from agitation/restraint)
- ECG

### Targeted
- Head CT: trauma, focal signs, new-onset psychosis in elderly
- LP: fever + AMS + signs of meningitis
- Thyroid: if mania / agitation + tachy / weight loss

---

## 🔄 Monitoring After Sedation

- Continuous pulse oximetry
- Capnography if available (resp depression)
- BP, HR q 15 min × 1 hr, then q 30–60 min
- Temperature (esp. if excited delirium, NMS)
- ECG for haloperidol (QT)
- Reassess every 15 min while restrained

---

## 🚨 Extrapyramidal Side Effects / Serotonin Syndrome / NMS

### Acute dystonia (haloperidol, high-potency)
- Oculogyric crisis, torticollis, lip/tongue
- Rx: **Diphenhydramine 50 mg IV/IM** or **Benztropine 1–2 mg IV/IM**

### NMS (neuroleptic malignant syndrome)
- Hyperthermia + rigidity + autonomic instability + ↑ CK + AMS
- Stop antipsychotic; cooling, IVF, benzo, ICU
- Dantrolene, bromocriptine for severe

### Serotonin syndrome
- Hyperthermia + **clonus (lower &gt; upper)** + tremor + hyperreflexia + AMS + autonomic
- Stop serotonergic drugs; benzo; cyproheptadine; cooling

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Pitfalls)

<Callout type="danger" title="Agitation Pitfalls">

**Under-sedate** — repeated small doses delay control + ↑ total dose  
→ Adequate first dose; reassess after onset

**Skip medical workup** — psych label hides hypoglycemia, hypoxia, sepsis  
→ VS + glucose + basic workup always

**Forget about excited delirium** — rapid decompensation + rhabdo/hyperthermia  
→ Aggressive cooling, IV hydration, monitor K, consider ketamine

**IM olanzapine + IV benzo** — respiratory depression, hypotension  
→ Avoid combination

**Ignore QT before haloperidol** — risk of Torsades  
→ ECG prior if possible; Mg IV if prolonged

**Prolonged restraints without reassessment** — rhabdo, hyperthermia, injury  
→ Reassess q 15 min; release as soon as sedated + safe

**Missing polysubstance in withdrawal** — alcohol + benzo + stimulant complex  
→ Thiamine, benzo, supportive; treat the addition

</Callout>

## 📝 คำสั่งพร้อมใช้ (Order Template)

```
Acute Agitation

Possible cause: [psych / substance / medical / unknown]
Vitals: T__ HR__ BP__ SpO₂__ Gluc__

1. Safety: security staff, clear environment, de-escalation attempt
2. Medical workup ASAP:
   - Glucose, ETOH, urine drug screen
   - CBC, BMP, CK, APAP/salicylate, LFT, TSH, lactate, ECG
   - Head CT if trauma / focal / new psychosis elderly
3. Chemical restraint (choose based on cause):
   - Psychosis: Haloperidol 5 mg IM + Lorazepam 2 mg IM (± Benadryl 50 mg)
   - Intoxication / unknown: Midazolam 5 mg IM or Lorazepam 2 mg IM
   - Alcohol/benzo withdrawal: Diazepam 10 mg IV / Lorazepam 2 mg IM
   - Excited delirium / severe refractory: Ketamine 4 mg/kg IM
4. Physical restraints if needed (+ always chem restraint)
5. Continuous SpO₂, reassess q 15 min
6. Cooling if hyperthermia
7. IVF resuscitation
8. Psychiatry consult once stable
9. Watch for EPS, NMS, serotonin syndrome post-Rx
10. Disposition: medical/psych admit vs discharge with follow-up
```
