## 🎯 Epidemiology — **HIGH Thai Relevance**

<Callout type="danger" title="Melioidosis = Thai national disease">

- **NE Thailand endemic** (Khon Kaen ~20 cases/100k/yr); also North, Central
- **Soil + surface water** pathogen (rice paddies, standing water)
- Peak: **rainy season (July–Nov)**
- **High mortality: 40% untreated, 14% even with Rx**
- Often **missed** — mimics TB, pneumonia, sepsis, abscess

</Callout>

### Risk factors (≥ 1 in 80% of cases)
- **Diabetes mellitus** (50–60% of cases — **biggest risk**)
- Chronic kidney disease, thalassemia
- Alcoholism, chronic lung disease
- Immunosuppression (steroid, chemo)
- **Occupational exposure**: rice farmers, construction workers
- Recent skin wound / cuts with soil/water exposure

---

## 🩺 Clinical — Protean Manifestations

### Acute (&lt; 2 months, 85%)
- **Pneumonia** (most common, 50%) — cavitary, looks like TB
- **Bacteremia** (sepsis without focus) — high mortality
- **Skin/soft tissue** — cellulitis, abscess (esp. in children)
- **Septic arthritis / osteomyelitis** (rare but classical)
- **Prostatic / internal abscess** — multi-organ

### Subacute (3 weeks–2 months)
- Fever + weight loss (mimics TB)
- Abscess anywhere (liver, spleen, kidney, parotid — "classic")

### Latent / chronic (&gt; 2 months)
- Reactivation years after exposure possible
- "**Time bomb of Vietnam**" — war veterans; Thailand rare

### Pediatric
- **Parotitis** — suppurative, unique to children in endemic area (consider when child &gt; fever + parotid swelling)

---

## 🧪 การวินิจฉัย (Diagnosis)

### Clinical suspicion
- Thai endemic + diabetic + rice farmer + severe pneumonia/sepsis = **melioidosis until proven otherwise**

### Microbiology
- **Blood culture** (yield 40–60% in septicemic)
- **Sputum, pus, urine culture** (from relevant site)
- **Ashdown's media** (selective for *B. pseudomallei*) — available in Thai labs
- **Latex agglutination** on isolate (confirmation)
- **IHA (Indirect hemagglutination)**: serology — positive in endemic background, not helpful alone

### Imaging
- **CXR/CT chest** — cavitary pneumonia (upper lobe, like TB)
- **US / CT abdomen** — splenic, liver, prostatic abscesses (common internal abscess)
- **MRI** — osteomyelitis, deep abscess

---

## 💊 Treatment — 2 Phases

### Phase 1: Intensive (IV 10–14 days minimum)

| Drug | Dose |
|---|---|
| **Ceftazidime** | 2 g IV q 6 hr |
| **Meropenem** | 1 g IV q 8 hr (preferred if severe/CNS/deep abscess) |
| **Imipenem** | 1 g IV q 6 hr (alternative) |

**Duration intensive phase:**
- **Minimum 10–14 days**
- **14–21 days** for pneumonia, bacteremia
- **4–8 weeks** for deep abscess, osteomyelitis, CNS involvement

### Phase 2: Eradication (PO 12–20 weeks)

<Callout type="warning" title="Eradication phase essential — don't skip!">

- **Prevents relapse** (risk 10–30% without eradication)
- **Do not stop** early even if feeling well

</Callout>

| Regimen | Dose | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| **Trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole** (TMP-SMX) | 8/40 mg/kg BID (max 320/1600 mg BID) | 12–20 weeks |
| Doxycycline (alternative) | 100 mg PO BID | 20 weeks |
| Amox-clav (pregnancy) | 1 g PO TID | 20 weeks (less effective) |

### Adjunctive
- **Abscess drainage** — essential for deep collections
- **Prosthetic material** — usually needs removal (often infected)
- **Diabetes control** — critical for response
- **G-CSF** — no benefit shown in recent trials

---

## 🔄 Follow-up

- Clinical improvement should be seen within 1 week of Rx
- **Fever may persist 7–14 days** despite appropriate Rx — patience!
- Repeat imaging at 2–4 weeks (abscess resolution)
- Serology not useful for monitoring (remains positive)
- Watch for **relapse** up to 1 year

---

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Pitfalls)

<Callout type="danger" title="Melioidosis Pitfalls">

**Miss in diabetic farmer + sepsis** — often treated as pneumonia/UTI, missed  
→ **Add ceftazidime empirical** for severe sepsis in endemic diabetic

**Use ceftriaxone instead of ceftazidime** — **ceftriaxone NOT effective**!  
→ *B. pseudomallei* intrinsically resistant; must use ceftazidime/carbapenem

**Stop abx at 2 weeks** — ↑ relapse  
→ Complete both intensive + eradication phases fully

**Missing internal abscesses** — splenic, prostatic, liver common  
→ CT abd/pelvis + MRI spine in all melioidosis cases

**TMP-SMX dose too low** — often under-dosed in practice  
→ 8/40 mg/kg BID (adult ~320/1600 mg BID); check folate + BUN

**Biosafety** — BSL-3 organism in lab; alert lab early  
→ Notify microbiology of suspicion for safe handling

</Callout>

## 📝 คำสั่งพร้อมใช้ (Order Template)

```
Melioidosis (suspected/confirmed)
Risk: [DM/CKD/thalassemia/alcohol/occupation__]
Exposure: [soil/water/rice farming, __]
Presentation: [pneumonia/sepsis/SSTI/abscess/parotitis]
Site: __

Labs: CBC, BMP, glucose (DM control!), LFT
Cultures: blood × 2, sputum/pus/urine as relevant
Imaging: CXR, US/CT abdomen, MRI if ortho/CNS

1. IV × 2, sepsis bundle if shock
2. **Intensive phase** (IV):
   - Ceftazidime 2 g IV q 6 hr
   - OR Meropenem 1 g IV q 8 hr (severe/deep)
   - Duration: 10-14 d (uncomplicated); longer if deep/CNS/osteo
3. Abscess drainage (IR/surgery)
4. DM optimization — insulin; target BS 140-180
5. **Eradication phase** (oral, start after intensive):
   - TMP-SMX 8/40 mg/kg BID × 12-20 wk
   - Alternative: Doxycycline 100 mg BID × 20 wk
6. Notify lab of suspicion (BSL-3)
7. Report to public health
8. Follow-up 1 year (relapse risk)
```
