## 🎯 Diagnostic Criteria — Tokyo Guidelines 2018 (TG18)

### A. Local inflammation
- Murphy's sign (+)
- RUQ pain / tenderness / mass

### B. Systemic inflammation
- Fever
- ↑ WBC
- ↑ CRP

### C. Imaging findings
- US: GB wall thickening &gt; 4 mm, pericholecystic fluid, sonographic Murphy sign, stones
- CT: GB wall thickening, enhancement, pericholecystic fat stranding

**Suspected**: A + B  
**Definite**: A + B + C

---

## 📊 Severity (TG18)

| Grade | Criteria | Management |
|---|---|---|
| **I — Mild** | No organ dysfunction, mild inflammation | Early laparoscopic cholecystectomy (&lt; 1 week) |
| **II — Moderate** | Any: WBC &gt; 18k, palpable tender mass, &gt; 72 hr, marked local inflammation | Early lap chole OR delay + interval chole; PTC if unfit |
| **III — Severe** | Organ dysfunction (CV, CNS, resp, renal, hepatic, hematologic) | ICU + emergent percutaneous cholecystostomy; delayed chole |

---

## 🧪 Workup

### Labs
- CBC, CRP, ESR
- CMP + LFT (mild ↑ ALT/AST, usually bilirubin &lt; 4; if higher → consider cholangitis/CBD stone)
- Amylase / lipase (rule out pancreatitis)
- Coag
- Blood culture × 2 (if febrile, severe)
- Pregnancy test (women)

### Imaging
- **Abdominal US** — **first-line** (sensitivity 85%, specificity 95%)
  - GB wall &gt; 4 mm, stones, pericholecystic fluid, sonographic Murphy
- **CT abdomen with contrast** — if US equivocal; assess complications (perforation, emphysematous)
- **MRCP** — if suspect concurrent CBD stone
- **HIDA scan** — gold standard but less available; good for acalculous

<Callout type="info" title="ALT/AST/Bili — differentiate cholecystitis vs cholangitis">

- **Cholecystitis**: mild LFT ↑ (ALT/AST 2–3× ULN, bili &lt; 4)
- **Cholangitis**: marked LFT ↑ (ALP, GGT, bili &gt; 4) + fever + RUQ pain (Charcot's)
- **Cholangitis = biliary sepsis** — different urgency (ERCP)

</Callout>

## 💊 Management

### Initial
- NPO, IV fluid, analgesia
- Correct electrolyte, coagulopathy
- Antiemetic (ondansetron)

### Antibiotics (empirical)

| Severity | Regimen | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| **Grade I / II community** | **Ceftriaxone 2 g IV q24h + Metronidazole 500 mg IV q8h** OR **Ampicillin-sulbactam 3 g IV q6h** | Until source control + 4–7 d |
| **Grade III / severe / healthcare** | **Pip-tazo 4.5 g IV q6–8h** OR **Meropenem 1 g IV q8h** | As above |
| **ESBL risk** | Meropenem | As above |
| **PCN allergy** | Ciprofloxacin 400 mg IV q12h + Metronidazole | As above |

**Adjust per culture**; stop after successful cholecystectomy ± 24 hr (uncomplicated Grade I/II)

### Source control

<Callout type="success" title="Early laparoscopic cholecystectomy preferred">

- **Grade I–II**: Laparoscopic cholecystectomy **within 1 week** of onset (best outcomes)
- Delayed chole (6–12 weeks) OK if initial medical management but ↑ conversion rate
- **Grade III** or unfit: **Percutaneous cholecystostomy (PC)** — drain under US/CT → interval chole when stable

</Callout>

### Special scenarios

**Acalculous cholecystitis** (no stones):
- Critically ill, post-operative, burns, prolonged TPN
- Often missed; poor prognosis
- PC first-line; chole only if stable

**Emphysematous cholecystitis** (gas in GB wall):
- DM, elderly, immunocompromised
- **Emergent surgery** (gangrenous GB, perforation risk high)
- Clostridium, E. coli

**Gallstone ileus**:
- Fistula GB → duodenum → stone obstructs ileum
- Rare but classic exam finding: pneumobilia + SBO + ectopic stone

**Mirizzi syndrome**:
- Stone in cystic duct compressing CBD → jaundice
- Usually requires open surgery, skilled surgeon

## ⚠️ Pitfalls

<Callout type="danger" title="Cholecystitis Pitfalls">

**Treat cholecystitis as cholangitis** — very different urgency  
→ Check LFT: marked ↑ bili → consider cholangitis → ERCP

**Delay cholecystectomy** — early (&lt; 1 wk) better than interval  
→ Don't wait for "cooling off" unless unfit

**Miss acalculous cholecystitis in ICU** — no stones, but sick, RUQ  
→ US q 48–72 hr if persistent fever of unknown source in ICU

**Missing emphysematous CC in DM** — rapid deterioration  
→ Look for gas on X-ray/CT; emergent surgery

**Stop abx too early** — longer if complications, adjust per culture  
→ Usually 4–7 d post-source control

**ERCP without indication** — ERCP is for CBD stones, not routine cholecystitis  
→ MRCP first if LFT equivocal

</Callout>

```
Acute Cholecystitis — TG18 Grade [I/II/III]

Pain duration: __ hr
Murphy: [+/−], WBC __, CRP __, LFT: AST__ ALT__ Bili__ ALP__
US: [GB wall __mm, stones+/−, fluid+/−, sonographic Murphy+/−]
CT/MRCP: [if needed]

1. NPO, IV × 2
2. NSS/RL maintenance
3. Analgesia: morphine/fentanyl PRN, ± ketorolac
4. Antiemetic: ondansetron 4 mg IV q 8 hr
5. Antibiotic:
   - Grade I/II: Ceftriaxone 2 g IV daily + Metronidazole 500 mg q 8 hr
   - Grade III / ESBL risk: Pip-tazo 4.5 g IV q 6–8 hr or Meropenem
6. Surgery consult for early lap chole (<1 wk)
   OR
   Interventional radiology for PC (Grade III / unfit)
7. Sepsis management if severe (see septic-shock)
8. Admit [ward / step-down / ICU if Grade III]
```
