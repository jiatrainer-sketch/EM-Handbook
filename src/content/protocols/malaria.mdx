## 🎯 Thai Context

<Callout type="info" title="Malaria in Thailand">

- **Border areas**: Myanmar (Tak, Mae Hong Son), Cambodia (Chanthaburi, Trat), Laos (Northeast)
- **Species**:
  - *P. falciparum* — most lethal; resistance to multiple drugs
  - *P. vivax* — most common in Thailand; relapsing
  - *P. knowlesi* — zoonotic, emerging (forested border)
  - *P. ovale, P. malariae* — rare
- **Peak**: rainy season
- History: forest/border travel, bite by *Anopheles* mosquito

</Callout>

---

## 🩺 Clinical

### Classic triad
- **Fever** (periodic — every 48 hr *P. vivax*, *P. ovale*; 72 hr *P. malariae*; irregular *P. falciparum*)
- **Chills + rigors**
- **Sweats**

### Other features
- Headache, myalgia, fatigue
- N/V, diarrhea
- **Splenomegaly** (chronic)
- Anemia (hemolysis)
- Jaundice (hemolytic + hepatic)

### Severe Malaria — WHO criteria (*P. falciparum* or knowlesi + parasitemia + ≥ 1):

<Callout type="danger" title="Severe malaria = ICU + IV artesunate">

- **Impaired consciousness** (GCS &lt; 11) / Cerebral malaria
- Prostration (unable to sit/stand)
- Multiple convulsions (&gt; 2 in 24 hr)
- Acidosis (pH &lt; 7.25 or HCO₃ &lt; 15)
- Hypoglycemia (&lt; 40 mg/dL)
- Severe anemia (Hb &lt; 5 in children, &lt; 7 in adults)
- Renal impairment (Cr &gt; 3 or oliguria)
- Jaundice (bilirubin &gt; 3)
- Pulmonary edema, ARDS
- Significant bleeding (DIC)
- Shock
- **Parasitemia &gt; 10%** or **schizonts in peripheral blood** (P. falciparum)

</Callout>

---

## 🧪 การวินิจฉัย (Diagnosis)

### Thick + thin blood smears (gold standard)
- **Thick**: screen for parasites (sensitive)
- **Thin**: species identification + **parasitemia %**
- Repeat q 12–24 hr × 3 if initial negative and suspicion high

### Rapid Diagnostic Test (RDT)
- Detects HRP2 (*P. falciparum*) or pLDH (all species)
- Available bedside in Thai hospitals
- Sensitivity ~95% for *P. falciparum*; less for *P. vivax*

### Labs (severity assessment)
- CBC (anemia, thrombocytopenia)
- **Glucose** (hypoglycemia common, esp. with quinine)
- LFT, BUN/Cr
- Lactate, ABG
- Coagulation (DIC possible)
- Hb (hemolysis)

## 💊 การรักษา (Treatment)

### Uncomplicated malaria

| Species | First-line (Thailand) |
|---|---|
| ***P. falciparum*** | **Artemether-lumefantrine** (Coartem) — 4 tabs BID × 3 days (with food) |
| ***P. vivax / ovale*** | **Chloroquine** 10 mg/kg → 10 mg/kg 24 hr → 5 mg/kg 48 hr; **+ Primaquine 0.5 mg/kg × 14 d** (eradicate hypnozoites) |
| ***P. malariae*** | Chloroquine (same as vivax) |
| ***P. knowlesi*** | Artemether-lumefantrine (treat as falciparum) |

<Callout type="warning" title="Primaquine + G6PD">

- Check **G6PD** before primaquine (hemolysis risk)
- Alternative for G6PD deficient: primaquine **0.75 mg/kg weekly × 8 weeks** with close monitoring
- Or: tafenoquine (single dose, but also needs G6PD)
- **Skip primaquine if severe G6PD** — accept higher relapse risk

</Callout>

### Severe Malaria (*P. falciparum* or knowlesi)

**IV Artesunate** — first-line globally (AQUAMAT trial — ↓ mortality vs quinine)

```
Artesunate 2.4 mg/kg IV at 0, 12, 24 hr
Then q 24 hr
Minimum 24 hr IV, continue until PO tolerated + parasitemia < 1%
Then complete with full oral Artemether-lumefantrine course
```

### Alternative (if no artesunate — rare in Thailand):
- **Quinine** 20 mg/kg IV loading over 4 hr, then 10 mg/kg IV q 8 hr (cardiac monitor, hypoglycemia risk)
- + Doxycycline 100 mg BID × 7 d

### Adjunct — severe
- Supportive ICU
- Glucose monitoring (hypoglycemia)
- Transfusion if severe anemia
- Dialysis if AKI
- Mechanical ventilation if ARDS

## 🚨 Specific Complications

### Cerebral malaria
- Seizures → benzodiazepine; avoid phenytoin (no benefit in malaria seizures — different mechanism)
- Low threshold to intubate
- Head CT if focal signs (rule out other causes)

### Blackwater fever (massive intravascular hemolysis)
- Dark urine, severe anemia, AKI
- Often post-quinine treatment
- Stop quinine; supportive; dialysis

### Hypoglycemia
- Common in pregnancy + severe disease
- Check glucose hourly in severe, q 4 hr otherwise
- D10 or D25 infusion if persistent

## 🛡 Prophylaxis (border travelers)

| Drug | Dose | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Atovaquone-proguanil** (Malarone) | 1 tab daily | Start 1–2 d before, continue 7 d after |
| **Mefloquine** | 250 mg weekly | Thai border: high resistance — NOT first-line |
| **Doxycycline** | 100 mg daily | Cheap, covers rickettsia too; photosensitivity |
| **Primaquine** (if G6PD normal) | 30 mg daily | For *P. vivax* prophylaxis (Thai forest) |

**Vector control**: DEET, permethrin-treated clothing, bed nets, avoid dusk/dawn outdoor

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Pitfalls)

<Callout type="danger" title="Malaria Pitfalls">

**Miss travel history** — forest trip, border = think malaria  
→ Blood smear + RDT in ANY febrile returning traveler

**Stop at 1 negative smear** — peripheral levels cycle; repeat q 12 hr × 3  
→ 3 negatives = low probability but monitor clinically

**Treat only with chloroquine** for falciparum — resistance!  
→ ACT (artemisinin combination) for all falciparum

**Miss hypoglycemia in severe** — quinine worsens; pregnancy ↑ risk  
→ Hourly glucose in severe, dextrose infusion early

**Miss co-infection** — malaria + dengue, melioidosis, leptospirosis  
→ Workup broad in endemic overlap

**Forget primaquine** for vivax/ovale — relapse inevitable  
→ 14-day radical cure after G6PD check

**Early discharge post-treatment** — parasitemia may still be active  
→ Re-smear day 7, 14, 28 for clearance confirmation

</Callout>

```

Travel hx: [Thai border __ / forest / international __]

Thick + thin smears: sent (repeat q 12h × 3 if negative + suspicion)
RDT: sent
Parasitemia %: __
Species: [falciparum / vivax / other]

Labs: CBC, glucose, LFT, BUN/Cr, Coag, Hb, lactate, ABG
Urinalysis, G6PD (before primaquine)

Treatment:
1. [Uncomplicated falciparum] Artemether-lumefantrine 4 tab BID × 3 d (with fat)
2. [Vivax/ovale] Chloroquine + Primaquine 0.5 mg/kg × 14 d (after G6PD)
3. [Severe falciparum/knowlesi]:
   - Artesunate 2.4 mg/kg IV at 0, 12, 24 hr, then q 24 h
   - Continue IV × minimum 24 hr, until parasitemia < 1%
   - Then complete with oral Artemether-lumefantrine
4. Supportive:
   - Glucose q 1–4 hr (prevent hypoglycemia)
   - Antipyretic
   - IVF (avoid over-resuscitation — ARDS risk)
   - Transfusion if Hb < 7
   - ICU if any severe criteria
5. Follow-up smears day 3, 7, 14, 28
6. Report to public health
```
