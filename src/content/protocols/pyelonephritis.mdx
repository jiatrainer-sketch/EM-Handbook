## 🎯 Presentation

- **Flank pain / CVA tenderness** (hallmark)
- Fever, chills, rigors
- Dysuria, frequency, urgency (lower tract symptoms, often present)
- N/V (common)
- Elderly: may have **AMS, falls, non-specific decline only**

### vs Cystitis
- Cystitis: dysuria, frequency, suprapubic pain — **no fever, no CVA tenderness, no systemic**
- Pyelonephritis: above + **fever + CVA tenderness + systemic**

---

## 🧪 การตรวจวินิจฉัย (Workup)

### All
- **Urinalysis + urine culture** (before abx)
- CBC, BMP, CRP, lactate (if systemic)
- Blood cultures × 2 (if febrile, admit, or immunocompromised)
- Pregnancy test (women of childbearing age)

### Imaging (not all)
- **US renal** — if obstruction suspected (hydronephrosis, stone), severe sepsis, slow response
- **CT abd/pelvis with contrast** — complicated, abscess, obstructing stone, emphysematous pyelonephritis

<Callout type="warning" title="Imaging indications">

- Not responding to abx within 48–72 hr
- Suspected obstruction (↑ Cr, stone hx)
- Suspected abscess, emphysematous PN (DM)
- Male (R/O prostatitis, structural cause)
- Pregnant with severe symptoms

</Callout>

---

## 💊 การรักษา (Treatment)

### Outpatient (uncomplicated, able to tolerate PO, reliable f/u)

| Option | Dose |
|---|---|
| **Ciprofloxacin** | 500 mg PO BID × 7 d (FQ 1st line if resistance &lt; 10%) |
| **Levofloxacin** | 750 mg PO QD × 5 d |
| **TMP-SMX (Bactrim)** | DS 1 tab PO BID × 14 d (only if sensitive) |
| **Cefpodoxime** | 200 mg PO BID × 10–14 d |

*Initial dose IV* (ceftriaxone 1 g OR gentamicin 5 mg/kg) then PO — helps in areas with high FQ resistance

### Inpatient / Severe

| Scenario | First-line |
|---|---|
| **Standard** | **Ceftriaxone 1–2 g IV daily** × 7–10 d |
| PCN allergy | Ciprofloxacin 400 mg IV q 12 hr |
| Septic shock | **Pip-tazo 4.5 g IV q 6–8 hr** or **Meropenem 1 g q 8 hr** |
| Prior ESBL / recent abx | Meropenem 1 g q 8 hr |
| MRSA risk (uncommon) | Add Vancomycin |

### Duration
- **7 days** (fluoroquinolone)
- **10–14 days** (beta-lactam, TMP-SMX)
- **14 days** male, diabetic, slow response
- **Switch to PO** once afebrile 24–48 hr + clinically improving

### Pregnancy
- Admit all pyelonephritis (even mild)
- **Ceftriaxone 1 g IV daily** × 10–14 d
- Avoid: FQ, tetracycline, TMP-SMX (1st trimester, near term)

---

## 🦠 Thai Local Resistance (important!)

- **E. coli resistance to ciprofloxacin: 30–50%** in Thailand (↑ from 2010)
- Use FQ only if local susceptibility data supports
- Ceftriaxone still effective for most E. coli
- ESBL prevalence rising — consider if recent hospitalization, healthcare exposure

<Callout type="danger" title="ESBL risk factors — use meropenem empirical">

- Prior ESBL infection or colonization
- Recent abx within 90 days (esp. cephalosporin, FQ)
- Hospital or nursing home exposure
- Indwelling urinary catheter
- DM + recurrent UTI
- Travel to high-ESBL area

</Callout>

---

## 🚨 Complications

### Emphysematous pyelonephritis
- Gas in renal parenchyma (CT)
- **DM + severe UTI** — most common
- Mortality up to 50% without intervention
- Rx: abx + **percutaneous drainage** or **nephrectomy**

### Renal / perinephric abscess
- Fever persists despite 72 hr abx
- **CT confirms**
- Rx: abx + drainage (percutaneous or surgical) if &gt; 3–5 cm

### Obstructing stone + infection
- **Urologic emergency** — pus under pressure
- Rx: abx + emergent **stent or percutaneous nephrostomy**

### Sepsis / septic shock
- Standard sepsis management (see septic-shock protocol)

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Pitfalls)

<Callout type="danger" title="Pyelonephritis Pitfalls">

**Use ciprofloxacin empirically** — Thai E. coli resistance 30–50%  
→ Use ceftriaxone unless local susceptibility supports FQ

**Miss obstruction** — Fever + flank pain + hydronephrosis = emergency  
→ US or CT if not improving; urgent stent/PCN

**Discharge pregnant patient** — all pregnant pyelonephritis → admit  
→ Ceftriaxone IV × 10–14 d

**Stop abx early** — uncomplicated 7 d, complicated 10–14 d  
→ Don't shorten based on symptom alone

**Miss prostatitis in male** — dysuria + fever in man  
→ PR exam; different duration (2–4 wk) + FQ preferred (prostate penetration)

**Miss emphysematous pyelonephritis in DM** — gas on X-ray/CT  
→ Urgent imaging + drainage; urology consult

</Callout>
