## 🎯 Timeline

| Time after last drink | Symptoms |
|---|---|
| **6–12 hr** | Anxiety, tremor, tachycardia, HTN, nausea, headache |
| **12–24 hr** | Alcoholic hallucinosis (visual/auditory, intact sensorium) |
| **24–48 hr** | **Withdrawal seizures** (generalized tonic-clonic, can be status) |
| **48–96 hr** | **Delirium Tremens (DT)** — autonomic + confusion + hallucinations |

---

## 📊 CIWA-Ar Score (10 items, 0–67)

Assess q 1–4 hr:
- N/V, tremor, sweats, anxiety, agitation
- Tactile/auditory/visual disturbances
- Headache
- Orientation/clouding of sensorium

| Score | Action |
|---|---|
| **&lt; 10** | Mild — observation, thiamine |
| **10–18** | Moderate — benzodiazepine PRN |
| **&gt; 18** | Severe — benzodiazepine scheduled; admit |

---

## 🚨 Delirium Tremens (DT) — Emergency

<Callout type="danger" title="DT = 5–15% mortality without ICU treatment">

**Diagnostic**: withdrawal + confusion + autonomic instability + hallucinations  
- HR &gt; 120, SBP &gt; 160, T &gt; 38.5°C
- Profuse sweating
- Global confusion, disorientation
- Frightening hallucinations

**Treatment**: ICU, aggressive benzodiazepines, ± dexmedetomidine, phenobarbital

</Callout>

---

## 💊 การรักษา (Treatment)

### First-line: Benzodiazepines (front-loaded, symptom-triggered)

| Drug | Onset | Duration | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Diazepam (Valium)** | Fast (IV 5 min) | Long (active metabolites) | Most common; self-tapers |
| **Lorazepam (Ativan)** | Fast | Intermediate | Hepatic failure (no active metabolites); IM usable |
| **Chlordiazepoxide (Librium)** | Slow | Long | Oral, outpatient |

**Symptom-triggered** (CIWA-guided) preferred over fixed schedule — reduces total dose and length of stay.

### Loading regimen (severe or DT)
```
Diazepam 10–20 mg IV q 10–15 min until CIWA < 8–10
No maximum dose — endpoint is calm but arousable
Can reach 100+ mg in severe DT
```

### Refractory DT (after high-dose benzo)
- **Phenobarbital** — 10–20 mg/kg IV loading over 30 min (barbiturate → GABA)
- **Dexmedetomidine** — 0.2–1.4 mcg/kg/hr (autonomic control; not for spasm/seizure)
- **Propofol** (intubated) — severe refractory DT
- **Ketamine** — emerging evidence for refractory

### Adjunct — Thiamine + glucose + magnesium

<Callout type="warning" title="Thiamine BEFORE glucose!">

- **Thiamine 100 mg IV × 3 days** — prevent Wernicke encephalopathy
- Do not give glucose without thiamine in alcoholic (precipitates Wernicke)
- **Magnesium 2 g IV** if low (common in alcoholics; helps withdrawal)
- **Folate, B12** supplement
- **Check glucose** — hypoglycemia common

</Callout>

### Fluid + electrolytes
- Correct hypovolemia (sweating, vomiting, poor intake)
- Replace K, Mg, PO₄ (often low)
- Watch for refeeding syndrome in severe malnutrition

---

## 🔄 Seizure Management

- First withdrawal seizure: **benzodiazepine** (diazepam 10–20 mg IV)
- Recurrent seizures → phenobarbital
- **Status epilepticus** → follow status-epilepticus protocol
- Phenytoin/levetiracetam generally **not effective** in withdrawal seizures

Once seizure occurs → **high risk of DT** → hospital admission

---

## 🏥 การจัดการผู้ป่วย (Disposition)

### Outpatient candidate
- Mild CIWA (&lt; 10), stable vitals
- Support system, no seizure/DT history
- Can return if worse
- Chlordiazepoxide 25 mg PO QID × 3–4 days (tapering)

### Inpatient
- Moderate-severe CIWA
- Prior DT / seizure
- Unstable comorbidity, pregnancy
- Suicidality

### ICU
- DT (active)
- Seizures, status
- Refractory to initial benzo
- Hemodynamic instability

---

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Pitfalls)

<Callout type="danger" title="Alcohol Withdrawal Pitfalls">

**Give glucose before thiamine** — precipitates Wernicke encephalopathy in alcoholic  
→ Thiamine first (IV), then D5 as needed

**Under-dose benzodiazepine** — leads to DT progression + higher total dose  
→ Aggressive symptom-triggered dosing; no artificial ceiling

**Use phenytoin for withdrawal seizure** — doesn't prevent recurrence  
→ Benzo first-line; phenobarbital for refractory

**Ignore severe hypomagnesemia** — perpetuates seizures + arrhythmia  
→ Mg IV; check K, PO₄ too

**Miss coexisting illness** — pneumonia, pancreatitis, hepatitis, GI bleed, head injury  
→ Full workup; don't attribute all to withdrawal

**Discharge too early** — DT peaks 48–96 hr; seizure can recur  
→ Observe 72 hr minimum; educate family

</Callout>

```
4. Folate 1 mg daily, B12, multivitamin
5. Magnesium 2 g IV if Mg < 2.0
6. Glucose check; D5 if hypoglycemic (AFTER thiamine)

   - Diazepam 10–20 mg IV q 10 min until CIWA < 8
   - Or Lorazepam 2–4 mg IV q 15 min (hepatic failure)
   - NO maximum dose — endpoint is calm arousable
8. [Refractory] Phenobarbital 10 mg/kg IV OR 
   Dexmedetomidine 0.2–1.4 mcg/kg/hr
9. CIWA q 1–4 hr
10. Labs: CBC, BMP, Mg, PO₄, Ca, LFT, lipase, glucose, ETOH
11. CXR, ECG; head CT if trauma / focal
12. Address co-morbidity (pneumonia, pancreatitis, hepatitis, GI bleed)
13. Admit [ward / ICU if DT or refractory]
14. SUD referral, AA, pharmacotherapy (naltrexone, acamprosate, disulfiram)
```
