## 🎯 Diagnosis

### Clinical (Beck's triad) — late, not all present
1. **Hypotension** (usually present)
2. **Muffled heart sounds**
3. **Distended neck veins (JVD)**

### Other key signs
- **Pulsus paradoxus &gt; 10 mmHg** (inspiratory drop in SBP)
- **Tachycardia** (compensatory)
- **Dyspnea, chest pain, anxiety**
- **Kussmaul sign** (JVD ↑ on inspiration) — rare, less sensitive
- **Equalization of diastolic pressures** (on invasive monitoring)

### ECG
- **Low voltage QRS**
- **Electrical alternans** (beat-to-beat QRS amplitude variation) — highly specific
- Non-specific ST-T changes

### CXR
- **Enlarged globular ("water bottle") heart**
- Clear lung fields (vs pulmonary edema)

---

## 🔍 Echo (GOLD STANDARD)

### Findings
- **Pericardial effusion** (size variable — small effusion + tamponade if rapid)
- **RV diastolic collapse** (early diastole) — specific
- **RA collapse** (&gt; 1/3 of cardiac cycle) — sensitive
- **Swinging heart** in large effusion
- **IVC plethora** (&gt; 2.1 cm, no collapse with inspiration)
- **Respiratory variation** in mitral/tricuspid flow (Doppler)

### Bedside POCUS
- Subxiphoid view — best single view
- Can diagnose in seconds in unstable patient

## 🧠 Common Causes

### Acute (rapid accumulation — small volume causes tamponade)
- **Trauma** (cardiac laceration, aortic dissection rupture)
- **Iatrogenic** (CVC, pacemaker wire, PCI, pericardiocentesis complication)
- **Aortic dissection** (type A rupturing into pericardium)
- **Post-cardiac surgery** (pericardial clot)
- **Ventricular rupture post-MI** (day 3–5)

### Subacute / chronic (slow — large volume tolerated)
- **Malignancy** (lung, breast, lymphoma, mesothelioma)
- **Uremia** (CKD stage 5, dialysis)
- **TB pericarditis** (HIV, endemic areas — common in Thailand)
- **Viral / idiopathic pericarditis**
- **Autoimmune** (SLE, RA)
- **Hypothyroidism** (myxedema)
- **Post-MI Dressler syndrome** (weeks later)

## 🚨 Management

### Unstable (peri-arrest)

1. **Call for help, prepare pericardiocentesis kit + US**
2. **IV access × 2, O₂, monitor, pads on**
3. **Gentle IVF bolus 500 mL NS** — preload support (*NOT* aggressive — see pitfall)
4. **Avoid PEEP / PPV if possible** — ↓ preload further
5. **Pericardiocentesis** — do NOT wait for echo/tertiary if crashing
6. **Vasopressor** if needed (norepinephrine) — bridge only

### Stable (with tamponade on echo)

1. **NPO, bed rest, continuous monitor**
2. **Cardiology / thoracic surgery consult**
3. **Echo-guided pericardiocentesis** (preferred over blind subxiphoid)
4. **Surgical pericardial window** if:
   - Recurrent
   - Malignant effusion
   - Purulent / TB
   - Post-op hematoma
5. **Treat underlying cause**

## 💉 Pericardiocentesis (Subxiphoid approach)

### Setup
- Supine, HOB 45°
- US probe (phased array, subxiphoid)
- Long 18G spinal needle (or pericardiocentesis kit)
- Sterile drape, lidocaine, ECG monitor (V1 lead on needle — historical)

### Technique
1. Insertion 1 cm below xiphoid, angle toward left shoulder
2. Advance while aspirating (under US guidance)
3. Fluid return → advance guidewire → dilate → catheter in pigtail
4. Drain slowly (50–100 mL initial → reassess BP) — **avoid rapid drainage**
5. Send fluid for: cell count, culture, gram, cytology, TB PCR, LDH, protein, glucose

### Monitoring post-procedure
- Vitals q 15 min × 2 hr
- ECG, echo post-procedure
- Watch for: re-accumulation, RV puncture, pneumothorax, coronary injury

### Complications
- Cardiac chamber puncture (especially RV)
- Pneumothorax
- Coronary artery laceration (rare)
- Arrhythmia (triggered by needle contact)
- Abdominal viscus injury (subxiphoid)

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Pitfalls)

<Callout type="danger" title="Cardiac tamponade — Pitfalls">

**DO NOT give aggressive IVF if suspecting tamponade**
→ Classic teaching: "gentle fluid" 500 mL bolus, not 2 L
→ Over-filling may worsen RV collapse, ↑ pericardial pressure
→ Fix the underlying problem (drainage)

**DO NOT intubate/ventilate first if avoidable**
→ PPV ↓ preload → cardiac arrest
→ If must intubate: ketamine induction + bag gently + drain fast

**Don't rely on Beck's triad being present**
→ Triad seen in < 30% of acute tamponade
→ Echo is key

**Missing tamponade in type A aortic dissection**
→ Think tamponade if dissection + hypotension
→ Pericardiocentesis may be TEMPORIZING — still need surgery

**Missing TB tamponade (endemic Thailand)**
→ Subacute presentation, HIV+, weight loss
→ Send TB PCR, ADA, culture on fluid
→ Steroid + ATT may prevent constriction

**Traumatic tamponade**
→ OR is the answer — not just pericardiocentesis
→ Temporize while transporting to OR

</Callout>

## 🔄 Constrictive Pericarditis (complication)

- Late sequela (weeks-months) of purulent, TB, post-cardiotomy
- Symptoms: R-sided HF (edema, ascites), Kussmaul sign
- Echo: thickened pericardium, septal bounce
- Definitive Rx: pericardiectomy
