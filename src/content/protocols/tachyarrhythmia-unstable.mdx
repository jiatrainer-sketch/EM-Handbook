## 🎯 Unstable = "SHOCK NOW" criteria

**Tachy + any ONE of:**
- **Hypotension** (SBP &lt; 90 or MAP &lt; 65)
- **Altered mental status** (from hypoperfusion)
- **Ischemic chest pain**
- **Signs of shock** (cold/clammy, lactate ↑)
- **Acute heart failure** (pulmonary edema)

→ **Synchronized cardioversion** (if with pulse)

**Pulseless VT/VF** → see [VF/VT](./vf-vt) (unsynchronized defibrillation)

---

## 🗺 Rhythm Identification (before shock if possible)

### Narrow complex (QRS &lt; 120 ms)

| Rhythm | Features | Shock energy |
|---|---|---|
| **Sinus tachy** | P before every QRS, HR 100–160 | Treat cause, don't shock |
| **SVT (AVNRT/AVRT)** | Regular, no visible P, HR 150–250 | **50–100 J** |
| **Atrial fib (unstable)** | Irregularly irregular | **120–200 J biphasic** |
| **Atrial flutter** | Sawtooth P, regular | **50–100 J** |

### Wide complex (QRS ≥ 120 ms)

| Rhythm | Features | Shock energy |
|---|---|---|
| **VT with pulse (mono)** | Regular wide, may have AV dissociation | **100 J** |
| **VT polymorphic** | Irregular wide, QT normal | **Defibrillation 200 J** (unsync) |
| **Torsades de Pointes** | Irregular wide, long QT | Defib 200 J + Mg 2 g |
| **WPW + AF (pre-excited)** | Irregular wide, delta waves | **200 J** |

### Rule of thumb

- Wide + unstable → treat as VT unless proven otherwise
- Polymorphic / irregular wide → unsynchronized (sync may fail to recognize)

## ⚡ Synchronized Cardioversion — Procedure

### 1. Prepare

- **Explain** to patient (if conscious)
- **IV × 1** large-bore; **continuous ECG + SpO₂ + BP**
- **Defibrillator** at bedside with pads on
- **Airway equipment** + suction
- **Reversal** for sedation available (flumazenil, naloxone)

### 2. Sedate (if conscious)

- **Etomidate 0.1–0.2 mg/kg IV** (preferred — hemodynamic neutral)
- Alternative: **Midazolam 1–2 mg IV** + **fentanyl 25–50 mcg**
- Propofol 0.5–1 mg/kg if stable BP
- Ketamine 1 mg/kg IV if BP borderline

### 3. Set energy + SYNC mode

- **Press SYNC button** (defibrillator highlights QRS)
- Verify synchronization marker on each QRS
- Set energy:
  - **SVT / flutter: 50–100 J biphasic**
  - **AF: 120–200 J biphasic**
  - **VT with pulse: 100 J biphasic**

### 4. Deliver shock

- "Clear" — everyone off bed
- Press and **HOLD** shock button (SYNC mode waits for next R wave)
- Confirm rhythm post-shock
- If unsuccessful: **escalate energy** (next level)
- Re-sync if needed (defibrillator may drop out of sync mode)

### 5. Post-shock

- Assess pulse, BP, rhythm
- 12-lead ECG
- Continuous monitoring
- Antiarrhythmic drip consideration (below)

## 💊 Post-Shock Management

### Recurrent VT / wide complex

**Amiodarone drip:**
- Load: 150 mg IV over 10 min (can repeat × 1)
- Drip: 1 mg/min × 6 hr → 0.5 mg/min × 18 hr

### Atrial fib (post-cardioversion)

- Rate control: **metoprolol 5 mg IV** or **diltiazem 0.25 mg/kg IV**
- Anticoagulate if appropriate (CHA₂DS₂-VASc)

### Torsades de Pointes

- **Mg sulfate 2 g IV over 10 min**
- Correct K (target &gt; 4.0)
- Stop QT-prolonging drugs
- **Overdrive pacing** if recurrent

### Polymorphic VT (normal QT)

- Often ischemic → treat cause (PCI, anti-ischemic)
- Amiodarone 150 mg IV

## ❓ Stable wide-complex — if time permits

(Not the focus of this page — refer to ACLS stable tachy pathway)

- Vagal maneuvers + **adenosine** for suspected SVT with aberrancy
- **Amiodarone 150 mg IV over 10 min** as alternative
- Avoid CCB/β-blocker in WPW + AF (can ↑ conduction via accessory pathway)

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Critical Pitfalls)

```
🚩 Unstable = cardiovert, don't delay
   - Medications slower, less reliable
   - Hypotension + tachy → shock

🚩 Always press SYNC button
   - Unsynchronized on organized rhythm = R-on-T → VF
   - Verify sync marker on QRS

🚩 Irregular polymorphic VT / torsades = defibrillate (not sync)
   - Sync algorithm can fail to detect QRS
   - Treat as VF with Mg

🚩 Sedation for conscious patient
   - Etomidate preferred (hemodynamic neutral)
   - Don't withhold if patient critically unstable

🚩 WPW + AF = do NOT give AV nodal blockers
   - Adenosine, CCB, β-blocker, digoxin = may accelerate accessory pathway
   - Procainamide or cardioversion

🚩 Post-shock bradycardia
   - Common, usually transient
   - Atropine if symptomatic

🚩 Identify + treat cause
   - Electrolyte (K, Mg) correction
   - Ischemia — serial ECG, troponin
   - Digoxin toxicity (think in elderly + AF)
   - Sepsis-driven tachy = treat sepsis, not AF alone

🚩 Sinus tachy is NOT arrhythmia
   - Find cause (sepsis, hypovolemia, PE, anxiety, pain)
   - Don't shock or rate-control blindly
```

## 📝 คำสั่งพร้อมใช้ (Copy-Ready Order)

```
Dx: Unstable tachyarrhythmia — [SVT / AF / VT / polymorphic VT]
Instability: [hypotension / AMS / ischemic pain / HF]

1. ABC, O₂ to SpO₂ ≥ 94%; IV × 2 large-bore
2. Continuous ECG, BP, SpO₂; pads on
3. 12-lead ECG (if time permits; don't delay shock if crashing)
4. Rhythm ID: narrow vs wide; regular vs irregular
5. Pre-procedure labs: BMP (K, Mg), troponin, CBC
6. SEDATION:
   - Etomidate 0.1–0.2 mg/kg IV (preferred)
   - Or midazolam 1–2 mg IV + fentanyl 25–50 mcg
7. SYNCHRONIZED CARDIOVERSION:
   - Press SYNC on defibrillator (verify marker on QRS)
   - SVT / flutter: 50–100 J biphasic
   - AF: 120–200 J biphasic
   - VT with pulse (monomorphic): 100 J biphasic
   - Polymorphic VT / torsades: unsynchronized 200 J + Mg 2 g
8. Post-shock:
   - Assess pulse, rhythm, 12-lead ECG
   - Amiodarone 150 mg IV over 10 min if recurrent
   - Mg 2 g IV if torsades or K repletion
9. Correct electrolytes:
   - K target &gt; 4.0
   - Mg &gt; 2.0
10. Treat cause:
    - Ischemia → PCI pathway
    - Sepsis → fluids + pressor + source
    - Digoxin toxicity → DigiFab
    - WPW + AF → cardiovert; avoid AV nodal blockers
11. ICU admit monitored bed
```
