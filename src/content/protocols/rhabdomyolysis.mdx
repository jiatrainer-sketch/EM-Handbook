## 🎯 Presentation

### Classic triad (only ~50% present)
1. **Muscle pain / weakness**
2. **Dark urine** (tea-colored from myoglobin)
3. **CK &gt; 5× ULN** (often &gt;&gt; 10,000 U/L)

### Causes (5 T's)
- **T**rauma / crush injury (earthquake, compression)
- **T**oxic (cocaine, amphetamine, statins, alcohol)
- **T**emperature (heat stroke, NMS, malignant hyperthermia)
- **T**achymetabolic (seizures, DT, extreme exertion, status asthmaticus)
- **T**otal collapse (immobilization, prolonged down-time)
- Others: infection (Flu, Strep), autoimmune myopathy, genetic

---

## 🧪 Diagnostic

- **Serum CK &gt; 5× ULN** (often 10,000–100,000+)
- **Myoglobin** — serum / urine (cleared fast; not as reliable)
- **UA**: **"blood" positive + no RBC on microscopy** = myoglobinuria  
  (dipstick detects heme in myoglobin + Hb equally)
- **BMP**: AKI (Cr ↑), **HYPERKALEMIA**, hyperphosphatemia, **HYPO**calcemia, ↑ uric acid, metabolic acidosis (AG)
- **LFT**: AST ↑ (muscle origin), LDH ↑
- **Coag**: DIC in severe (platelet ↓, ↑ INR, ↓ fibrinogen)
- **ECG**: hyperkalemia changes

## 💊 การรักษา (Management)

### Phase 1: Aggressive IV fluid (cornerstone!)

<Callout type="success" title="Target: UOP 200–300 mL/hr until CK trending down">

- **NSS or LR 1–2 L bolus**, then 200–500 mL/hr
- **Early + aggressive** = best prevention of AKI
- Continue until CK &lt; 5,000 (or 50% reduction)
- Beware over-resuscitation (pulmonary edema, heart failure)

</Callout>

### Urine alkalinization — controversial
- Consider if severe (CK &gt; 30,000, acidic urine pH &lt; 6.5)
- **Isotonic bicarbonate** (150 mEq in 1 L D5W) at 100 mL/hr
- Target urine pH &gt; 6.5
- Stop if pH &gt; 7.5 (alkalosis) or symptomatic hypocalcemia
- **No strong evidence** vs NSS alone (MR. CALCIUM data mixed)

### Mannitol — not recommended routinely
- No proven benefit; avoid in oliguric AKI

### Hyperkalemia (common complication)
- Treat **aggressively** — see hyperkalemia protocol
- Calcium (membrane stabilization) for ECG changes
- Insulin + glucose, bicarbonate
- Dialysis threshold low (if persistent / refractory)

### Hypocalcemia (common)
- **Do NOT correct unless symptomatic** (seizure, tetany, prolonged QT)
- Calcium will re-deposit in injured muscle + worsen damage
- Once recovery phase → calcium rises (re-released from muscle)

### Dialysis indications
- **AKI** with standard indications (uremia, acidosis, refractory K/volume)
- **Not** effective for removing myoglobin (too big)
- CVVH/CRRT if hemodynamically unstable

## 🚨 Compartment Syndrome

<Callout type="danger" title="Compartment syndrome = surgical emergency">

Signs (6 P's):
- **Pain out of proportion** / pain with passive stretch (earliest)
- Pallor
- Paresthesia
- Paralysis (late)
- Pulselessness (very late)
- Pressure (compartment pressure &gt; 30 mmHg or ΔP &lt; 30)

→ **Emergent fasciotomy** — within 6 hr for salvage

</Callout>

### Risk factors
- Crush injury
- Prolonged immobilization
- Reperfusion after ischemia
- Tight casts/bandages

### Measurement
- Compartment pressure monitor (Stryker device)
- Normal: 0–10 mmHg
- Concerning: &gt; 20
- Surgical: &gt; 30 OR ΔP (DBP − compartment) &lt; 30

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Pitfalls)

<Callout type="danger" title="Rhabdo Pitfalls">

**Under-resuscitate** — AKI prevention = aggressive IVF  
→ Target UOP 200–300 mL/hr; NSS/LR 500 mL/hr initially

**Correct hypocalcemia** — worsens rhabdo damage  
→ Only if symptomatic (tetany, prolonged QT, seizure)

**Bicarbonate universally** — no strong benefit in mild/moderate  
→ Consider only in severe / severe acidic urine; monitor for alkalosis

**Miss compartment syndrome** — pain out of proportion + tense muscle  
→ Measure compartment pressure; surgical consult STAT

**Treat CK alone — ignore cause** — identify + fix (stop drug, cool hyperthermia, etc.)  
→ Treatment of cause is essential

**Restart statin after statin-induced rhabdo** — reassess risk/benefit; often discontinue

</Callout>

```
Rhabdomyolysis

Cause: [trauma/crush/exertion/drug/seizure/immobilization/heat]
Initial CK: __ U/L
UA: [heme positive, no RBC]

Labs: BMP (K__ Ca__ PO₄__ Cr__), LFT, LDH, coag, ABG/VBG
ECG: [hyperK changes?]

1. IV × 2, continuous ECG monitor
2. **NSS or LR 1–2 L bolus, then 200–500 mL/hr**
3. Foley catheter; strict hourly I/O
4. Target UOP 200–300 mL/hr until CK < 5,000 or 50% ↓
5. Treat hyperkalemia per protocol (urgent if ECG changes)
6. Correct hypocalcemia ONLY if symptomatic (tetany, QT)
7. [If severe + acidic urine] Consider bicarb drip (150 mEq + D5W 1L @ 100 mL/hr)
8. Check compartments q 1–4 hr (6 P's); surg consult if concern
9. Remove offending drug / reverse cause
10. CK q 6–12 hr; BMP q 6 hr until stable
11. Dialysis if K / acidosis / uremia / volume refractory
12. Admit [ward / ICU if severe]

Discharge: resolved CK trend, normal K, Cr trending down
```
