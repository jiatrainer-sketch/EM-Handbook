## 🎯 Clinical Red Flags

- **"Worst headache of my life"** / thunderclap (max intensity &lt; 1 min)
- **Sudden onset** severe HA, especially with exertion/valsalva
- **Sentinel bleed** — warning HA 2–8 wk earlier (often missed)
- **+ Neck stiffness, photophobia, vomiting**
- **+ LOC** (transient or ongoing)
- **+ Focal deficits / seizure**
- **CN III palsy** (PCoM aneurysm — dilated pupil, down-and-out eye)

---

## 🧪 Diagnosis

### 1st: **CT head (non-contrast) STAT**
- **93% sensitive &lt; 6 hr** from onset (nearly 100% if within hours)
- Sensitivity drops to ~ 50% by day 5–7
- Look for: blood in basal cisterns, sylvian fissure, interhemispheric

### 2nd: **Lumbar puncture** (if CT negative but high suspicion)
- Timing: wait ≥ 6–12 hr from HA onset (xanthochromia develops)
- Findings suggestive of SAH:
  - **RBC count not clearing** (tubes 1 → 4)
  - **Xanthochromia** (yellow CSF from breakdown) — **most specific**
  - Opening pressure elevated
- Traumatic tap: RBC decreases tube 1 → 4

### 3rd: **CTA** (after diagnosis confirmed)
- Identify aneurysm location + plan for clipping/coiling
- Or DSA (digital subtraction angiography) as gold standard

### Alternative: **MRI FLAIR + GRE**
- Better for subacute SAH (after 24 hr)

## 📊 Severity Scales

### Hunt-Hess
| Grade | Description | Mortality |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Asymptomatic or mild HA | ~ 10% |
| 2 | Mod-severe HA, CN palsy, no focal deficit | ~ 15% |
| 3 | Drowsy / confused, mild focal deficit | ~ 35% |
| 4 | Stupor, moderate-severe hemiparesis | ~ 60% |
| 5 | Coma, decerebrate posturing | ~ 80% |

### WFNS (World Federation Neurosurg)
| Grade | GCS | Motor deficit |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 15 | — |
| 2 | 13–14 | — |
| 3 | 13–14 | + |
| 4 | 7–12 | ± |
| 5 | 3–6 | ± |

### Fisher grade (CT amount of blood) — vasospasm risk predictor
- Higher grade = more vasospasm risk

---

## 🚨 Initial Management (first hour)

1. **ABC, IV × 2, continuous monitor**
2. **HOB 30°, quiet environment, minimize stimulation**
3. **Strict BP control** (see below)
4. **Reverse anticoagulation** if on warfarin/DOAC
5. **Labs:** CBC, coag, T&S, electrolytes (SIADH/CSW), troponin (neurogenic stunned myocardium)
6. **Neurosurgery consult STAT** — transfer to tertiary if no NS available
7. **Prepare for airway** if GCS ≤ 8 or decline

---

## 🩺 BP Control (pre-aneurysm securing)

### Target
- **SBP &lt; 140 mmHg** (some use &lt; 160)
- Avoid hypotension — ↓ cerebral perfusion

### Drugs
- **Nicardipine IV infusion** 5 mg/hr, titrate up to 15 mg/hr
- **Labetalol IV** 10–20 mg bolus → 1–2 mg/min drip (avoid if bradycardia, asthma, CHF)
- Avoid nitroprusside (↑ ICP)
- Avoid sublingual nifedipine (unpredictable)

---

## 💊 Nimodipine (Vasospasm Prevention)

- **Nimodipine 60 mg PO q4h × 21 days** — start within 96 hr
- **Reduces delayed cerebral ischemia + improves outcome**
- If hypotensive: 30 mg q2h instead of 60 mg q4h (don't stop!)
- If cannot take PO: per NG tube (not IV — not approved, hypotension)

---

## ⚡ Complications

### Vasospasm (days 4–14)
- New focal deficit, altered mental status, HA
- **Transcranial Doppler** (TCD) — monitor daily
- **Triple-H therapy (historical)** — Hypertension, Hypervolemia, Hemodilution
  - Modern: Euvolemia + induced hypertension (targeted SBP 180–220 after aneurysm secured)
- **Endovascular**: intraarterial verapamil, balloon angioplasty

### Re-bleeding
- Peak within 24 hr, highest risk first 6 hr
- Aneurysm securing (coiling / clipping) within 24–72 hr — definitive prevention
- **Antifibrinolytics** (tranexamic acid 1 g IV q6h) — bridge to coiling (short course ≤ 72 hr)

### Hydrocephalus
- Acute — obstructive from IVH / blood in aqueduct
- **EVD (external ventricular drain)** — neurosurgery

### Seizures
- Prophylaxis controversial; treat if occurs
- Levetiracetam 500–1000 mg IV BID (shorter duration than older PHY)

### SIADH vs CSW (cerebral salt wasting)
- Hyponatremia common (day 4–10)
- SIADH: euvolemic, high urine Na, restrict fluid
- CSW: hypovolemic, high urine Na, replace NaCl + fluid (opposite!)
- **Do not fluid restrict in SAH** — ↑ vasospasm risk; use NaCl tablets or hypertonic saline if needed

### Neurogenic stunned myocardium
- Troponin ↑, ST changes (not MI)
- Supportive, most recover

### Hyponatremia
- Watch closely (ADH surge + CSW)
- Avoid hypotonic fluids (D5W, ½ NS)

---

## 🎯 Definitive treatment

- **Coiling (endovascular)** — first choice for most aneurysms
- **Clipping (surgical)** — if anatomy unfavorable for coiling or MCA bifurcation
- **Within 72 hr of bleed** for stable pts
- Post-securing: permissive hypertension for vasospasm ppx

## ⚠️ Common Pitfalls

```
🚩 "Migraine" diagnosis in thunderclap HA
   - Don't assume migraine if first-ever thunderclap
   - Always CT + LP if suspicion

🚩 Missing sentinel bleed
   - Prior severe HA 2–8 wk earlier → warning sign
   - Always ask about prior similar HA

🚩 Forgetting LP after negative CT
   - CT sensitivity drops after 6 hr
   - LP (wait ≥ 6–12 hr for xanthochromia)

🚩 Treating SAH like ICH (lowering BP too much)
   - Target SBP < 140 (not < 120)
   - Hypotension → ↓ cerebral perfusion

🚩 Missing nimodipine
   - Reduces vasospasm + mortality
   - Must start < 96 hr, continue 21 d

🚩 Fluid restriction in hyponatremia
   - If CSW (common in SAH), need MORE Na+fluid
   - Check volume status, urine Na
```
