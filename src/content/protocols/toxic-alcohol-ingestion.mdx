## 🎯 Overview

| | Methanol | Ethylene glycol |
|---|---|---|
| **Source** | Wood alcohol, windshield washer, moonshine, adulterated liquor | Antifreeze, brake fluid, coolant |
| **Toxic metabolite** | **Formic acid** (via alcohol DH + aldehyde DH) | **Glycolic → oxalic acid**, Ca oxalate crystals |
| **Target organ** | **Retina, optic nerve** | **Kidneys**, tetany (hypoCa), cardiopulm |
| **Signature finding** | Blindness, "snowfield" vision | Flank pain, calcium oxalate urinary crystals |

---

## 🚩 Clinical Presentation

### Phase 1 (0–12 hr) — "Ethanol-like"
- AMS, ataxia, slurred speech, nausea, vomiting
- May seem just drunk (delays diagnosis)

### Phase 2 (12–24 hr) — Metabolic acidosis
- High anion gap metabolic acidosis develops
- Tachypnea (Kussmaul)
- Abdominal pain, pancreatitis

### Phase 3 (24–72 hr) — Organ-specific

**Methanol**:
- **Visual sx**: blurred, scotomas, "looking through snowfield", **blindness**
- Fundus: disc edema, hyperemia
- Cerebral edema, parkinsonism (late)

**Ethylene glycol**:
- **Flank pain, AKI, oliguria**
- Hypocalcemia (Ca-oxalate precipitation) → tetany, QT prolongation
- Pulmonary edema, CHF

---

## 🔬 Diagnosis

### Key labs
- **ABG / VBG** — high anion gap metabolic acidosis
- **Anion gap** = Na – (Cl + HCO₃) — usually > 20
- **Osmolar gap** = measured osm – calculated osm
  - Calculated osm = 2(Na) + (BUN / 2.8) + (glucose / 18) + (ethanol / 4.6)
  - **Normal &lt; 10, toxic alcohol** usually &gt; 20–50
  - **Note**: osmolar gap ↓ over time (alcohol metabolized to acid)
  - High osmolar gap early → then normalizes as AG widens

### Other labs
- **BMP + lactate** (high AG sources)
- **Serum methanol / ethylene glycol level** — if available (definitive)
- **Ethanol level** (differential + competes with ADH)
- **Ca, Mg** (↓ in EG from Ca-oxalate)
- **Urine** — Wood's lamp fluorescence (antifreeze with fluorescein), **Ca oxalate crystals** (EG)
- **Creatinine** (AKI in EG)
- **CK** (rhabdomyolysis)
- **Fundoscopy** (methanol)

### Differential (high AG + osmolar gap)
- **MUDPILES** — high AG
- **MED CAP LIES** — osmolar gap:
  - **M**ethanol, **E**thylene glycol, **D**iethylene glycol
  - **C**hloroform, **A**cetone, **P**ropylene glycol
  - **L**actated Ringer's (trivial), **I**sopropyl alcohol, **E**thanol, **S**orbitol/mannitol

## 🚨 Initial Management

1. **ABC, decontamination** — activated charcoal NOT effective (small MW)
2. **IV access, monitor, correct hypoglycemia**
3. **Call Poison Center** — Thailand: Ramathibodi 02-201-1083
4. **Begin antidote IMMEDIATELY if suspicion** — don't wait for level
5. **Aggressive IVF** — especially for EG (AKI)
6. **Treat concurrent issues**: hypoglycemia, hypocalcemia (EG), seizures

---

## 💊 Antidote: Fomepizole (4-MP)

### Mechanism
- Competitive inhibitor of **alcohol dehydrogenase (ADH)**
- Blocks conversion of methanol/EG to toxic metabolites
- Allows renal/dialytic elimination

### Dosing
- **Loading: 15 mg/kg IV** over 30 min (diluted in 100 mL NS)
- **Maintenance: 10 mg/kg IV q 12 hr × 4 doses, then 15 mg/kg q 12 hr** (enzyme autoinduction)
- During dialysis: **every 4 hr** (dialyzable)

### Indications
- Documented methanol / EG level &gt; 20 mg/dL
- Strong suspicion + any one of:
  - Metabolic acidosis with AG &gt; 14–16
  - Osmolar gap &gt; 10
  - Urinary oxalate crystals

### Advantages over ethanol
- No CNS depression
- Predictable dosing
- No alcohol monitoring needed
- Safer, easier to use
- BUT: expensive + less available (esp. in Thailand)

---

## 🍷 Alternative: Ethanol (if fomepizole unavailable)

### Mechanism
- Same ADH competition (ethanol has higher affinity)

### Dosing
- **Loading: 600 mg/kg** (mix 10% ethanol in D5W, ~ 7–8 mL/kg)
- **Maintenance: 110 mg/kg/hr** (adjust to level 100–150 mg/dL)
- Goal: blood alcohol level **100–150 mg/dL**
- During dialysis: 2× rate

### Disadvantages
- Causes intoxication, sedation
- Hypoglycemia risk (esp pediatric)
- Needs serial alcohol levels q 1–2 hr
- Avoid if pregnant, liver failure

---

## 🫁 Hemodialysis

### Indications (STRONG)
- **Methanol / EG level &gt; 50 mg/dL**
- **Metabolic acidosis pH &lt; 7.30** despite therapy
- **Visual changes** (methanol)
- **AKI** (EG — rising Cr, oliguria)
- **Hemodynamic instability, end-organ damage**

### HD removes
- Parent compound + toxic metabolites
- Treats concurrent acidosis
- Continue until level &lt; 20 mg/dL + AG closed

### Prepare for
- Transfer to HD-capable center STAT
- Dialysis access (line)
- Adjust fomepizole dosing during/after HD

---

## 💊 Adjuncts

### Sodium bicarbonate
- For severe acidosis pH &lt; 7.10
- 1–2 mEq/kg IV bolus
- Followed by drip; recheck

### Folinic acid (methanol specific)
- **Folic acid 50 mg IV q 4 hr** (enhances formate metabolism to CO₂ + H₂O)
- Folinic acid = leucovorin (preferred if available)

### Thiamine + pyridoxine (EG specific)
- **Thiamine 100 mg IV** (cofactor for glycolate → non-toxic metabolites)
- **Pyridoxine 100 mg IV** (B6, similar pathway)

### Calcium (EG — symptomatic hypoCa)
- Ca gluconate 1 g IV slow (only if symptomatic — may worsen Ca-oxalate precipitation)

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Pitfalls)

```
🚩 Missing diagnosis in "just drunk" patient
   - Check osmolar + anion gap early
   - Methanol/EG may initially look like ethanol intoxication

🚩 Relying on osmolar gap alone
   - Normal OG doesn't rule out — drops as metabolized
   - Use OG + AG trend together

🚩 Waiting for level before treating
   - Empirical treatment if suspicion + OG or AG
   - Lab takes hours (often sent out)

🚩 Inadequate fomepizole loading
   - 15 mg/kg (not 10)
   - Enzyme autoinduction → increase dose after 48 hr

🚩 Giving Ca in asymptomatic EG patient
   - Worsens precipitation of Ca-oxalate
   - Only treat symptomatic hypoCa

🚩 Missing late methanol blindness
   - Visual changes may persist — early optho referral
   - Irreversible if delayed

🚩 Late "concentration" of counterfeit liquor outbreaks
   - Thailand history: adulterated lao khao causing methanol poisoning
   - Cluster of cases → public health notification
```
