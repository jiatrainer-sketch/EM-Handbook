## 🎯 Criteria / Diagnosis

### ECG criteria (2 contiguous leads)

**ST-elevation at J point:**

| Lead | Men &lt; 40 | Men ≥ 40 | Women |
|---|---|---|---|
| V2–V3 | ≥ 2.5 mm | **≥ 2.0 mm** | **≥ 1.5 mm** |
| Other leads | — | **≥ 1.0 mm** | **≥ 1.0 mm** |

**Alternative criteria:**
- **New LBBB** with clinical ischemia (Sgarbossa criteria)
- **Posterior MI**: ST depression V1–V3 + ST elevation V7–V9
- **RV infarct** (inferior MI + V4R elevation)

### Localization + affected artery

| Location | Leads | Artery | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Anterior | V1–V4 | **LAD** | Large territory, high mortality |
| Lateral | I, aVL, V5–V6 | **LCX** or diagonal | |
| Inferior | II, III, aVF | **RCA** (90%) | Check RV (V4R), posterior (V7–V9); bradycardia common |
| Posterior | V7–V9 (ST↑), V1–V3 (ST↓) | **LCX** or RCA | |
| RV | V4R ST elevation | **RCA** proximal | **NO nitrate/morphine** — preload dependent |

### Clinical presentation

- Chest pain / pressure (central, radiate to jaw/arm)
- N/V, diaphoresis, dyspnea
- **Atypical in elderly, diabetic, women**: dyspnea, weakness, epigastric pain
- "Silent MI" in diabetic
- Killip class (prognostic):
  - I: no CHF
  - II: S3 / rales
  - III: pulmonary edema
  - IV: cardiogenic shock (mortality ~50%)

### Labs (supportive, don't delay reperfusion!)

- **Troponin I/T** (hs-cTn): elevated + rising pattern
- CBC, BMP, Mg, PT/aPTT
- Lipid profile, HbA1c (secondary prevention)

## ⚡ Immediate Actions — **Time is myocardium**

### Step 1: Recognition (0–10 min from contact)

```
First medical contact (FMC) → ECG within 10 min
STEMI identified → activate cath lab / decide reperfusion
```

### Step 2: Initial management — **MONA-B** (modified)

**M**orphine:
- 2–4 mg IV prn for refractory pain (after nitrate)
- ⚠️ Avoid if possible (associated with worse outcome — slows antiplatelet absorption)

**O**xygen:
- **Only if SpO₂ &lt; 90%** (routine O₂ not beneficial — may harm)

**N**itroglycerin:
- **SL 0.4 mg q 5 min × 3** or IV drip for persistent pain
- **Contraindications:**
  - **Inferior / RV infarct** (preload-dependent → severe hypotension)
  - SBP &lt; 90
  - PDE5 inhibitor within 24 hr (sildenafil) / 48 hr (tadalafil)

**A**spirin:
- **162–325 mg chewed STAT** (non-enteric-coated)
- Then 81 mg daily lifelong
- ⚠️ True aspirin allergy → clopidogrel alone

**B**eta-blocker:
- **Oral metoprolol 25 mg PO** within 24 hr if stable
- **Hold** if: cardiogenic shock, CHF, heart block, bradycardia, hypotension, severe asthma
- IV only if tachyHTN + stable (rare in community hospital)

### Step 3: Antiplatelet — **DAPT**

**Aspirin 162–325 mg chewed** + **P2Y12 inhibitor loading:**

**Choose ONE P2Y12 based on reperfusion strategy:**

| Drug | Load dose | Maintenance | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Clopidogrel** | 600 mg PO (300 mg if fibrinolytic + age &gt; 75) | 75 mg daily | Universal, cheapest |
| **Ticagrelor** | 180 mg PO | 90 mg BID | Better potency; avoid if severe liver dz, prior ICH |
| **Prasugrel** | 60 mg PO | 10 mg daily | **Avoid if prior stroke/TIA**, age ≥ 75, weight &lt; 60 kg |

⚠️ **Fibrinolytic + age &gt; 75**: clopidogrel 75 mg load (no 300/600), NO prasugrel/ticagrelor

### Step 4: Anticoagulation

**Choose based on reperfusion strategy + availability:**

**Primary PCI:**
- **Unfractionated Heparin (UFH)**: 70–100 U/kg IV bolus (4,200–6,000 U @ 60 kg), adjust to ACT
- Bivalirudin 0.75 mg/kg bolus + 1.75 mg/kg/hr (if available)

**Fibrinolytic:**
- **Enoxaparin** (preferred):
  - Age &lt; 75: 30 mg IV bolus + 1 mg/kg SC q 12 hr (@ 60 kg = 60 mg SC q 12 hr)
  - Age ≥ 75: NO bolus, 0.75 mg/kg SC q 12 hr (@ 60 kg = 45 mg SC q 12 hr)
  - CKD CrCl &lt; 30: 1 mg/kg SC q 24 hr
- **UFH** (alt): 60 U/kg IV bolus (max 4,000 U) + 12 U/kg/hr (max 1,000 U/hr), adjust to aPTT 1.5–2.0×

### Step 5: **Reperfusion decision (most critical!)**

**Goal: Restore flow ASAP**

```
Primary PCI available within 120 min of FMC?
  ├── YES → Primary PCI (preferred)
  │         Door-to-balloon ≤ 90 min
  │
  └── NO  → Fibrinolytic within 30 min
            (door-to-needle ≤ 30 min)
            Then transfer to PCI center (pharmacoinvasive)
```

**Primary PCI preferred if:**
- Available within 120 min
- **Cardiogenic shock** (any time)
- **Fibrinolytic contraindicated**
- Late presentation (3–12 hr)
- Diagnosis uncertain

### Step 6A: Fibrinolytic (Thai community hospital common)

**Indications (ALL must be met):**
- ECG STEMI criteria
- Symptom onset &lt; 12 hr (best &lt; 3 hr)
- No PCI within 120 min
- No contraindications

**Absolute contraindications:**

```
🚫 Any prior ICH
🚫 Ischemic stroke < 3 months
🚫 Known cerebral AVM / aneurysm / neoplasm
🚫 Active internal bleeding (not menses)
🚫 Suspected aortic dissection
🚫 Severe uncontrolled HTN > 180/110 (relative/absolute)
🚫 Significant closed head trauma or face trauma < 3 months
🚫 Intracranial / intraspinal surgery < 3 months
🚫 Known bleeding disorder
```

**Relative:**
- Prior ischemic stroke &gt; 3 months
- Dementia / known intracranial pathology
- CPR &gt; 10 min
- Major surgery &lt; 3 weeks
- Internal bleeding &lt; 2–4 weeks
- Non-compressible vascular puncture
- Pregnancy
- Active PUD
- Anticoagulant use (warfarin, DOAC)

**Fibrinolytic choices:**

**Streptokinase** (Thai common, cheap):
- **1.5 million units IV in 100 mL NSS over 30–60 min**
- ⚠️ Hypotension common (slow infusion if BP drops)
- Antibodies after use — don't repeat within 1 year (use alteplase/TNK next time)
- Fibrin non-specific (more systemic bleeding)

**Tenecteplase (TNK)** — single IV bolus (preferred if available):

| Weight | TNK dose |
|---|---|
| &lt; 60 kg | 30 mg IV bolus |
| **60–69 kg** | **35 mg IV bolus** |
| 70–79 kg | 40 mg IV bolus |
| 80–89 kg | 45 mg IV bolus |
| ≥ 90 kg | 50 mg IV bolus |

⚠️ Age ≥ 75: consider **half-dose TNK** (evidence growing) — discuss risk/benefit

**Alteplase (tPA)** — accelerated regimen:
- 15 mg IV bolus
- 0.75 mg/kg (max 50 mg) IV over 30 min → @ 60 kg = 45 mg
- 0.5 mg/kg (max 35 mg) IV over 60 min → @ 60 kg = 30 mg
- Total @ 60 kg: 90 mg over ~90 min

### Step 6B: After fibrinolytic

**Assess reperfusion at 60–90 min:**
- ECG: ST resolution &gt; 50% in worst lead
- Symptom resolution
- Reperfusion arrhythmia (AIVR)

**Success** → **pharmacoinvasive** approach: transfer to PCI within 3–24 hr  
**Failure (persistent ST, ongoing pain)** → **rescue PCI** STAT

### Step 7: Complications to anticipate

**Arrhythmia:**
- **VF/VT** (any time, especially first 24 hr) → ACLS
- **AIVR** (accelerated idioventricular) = reperfusion marker, don't suppress
- **Heart block** (esp inferior MI) → atropine → pacing → dopamine
- **AF new-onset** → rate / rhythm control

**Mechanical:**
- **Cardiogenic shock** (5–10%, mortality ~50%) → norepi + IABP/Impella + PCI
- **Papillary muscle rupture** (day 2–7) → acute MR → surgery
- **Free wall rupture** → tamponade → surgery
- **VSD** (day 2–5) → surgery

**Other:**
- Pericarditis (early or Dressler late)
- LV thrombus (anterior MI) — anticoagulate

### Aspirin

```
Load:     162–325 mg CHEWED non-enteric
Maint:    81 mg PO daily lifelong
```

### P2Y12 inhibitors (choose ONE)

**Clopidogrel:**
```
Load:     600 mg PO (primary PCI)
          300 mg PO (fibrinolytic, age < 75)
          75 mg PO (fibrinolytic, age ≥ 75 — no load)
Maint:    75 mg daily × ≥ 12 months
```

**Ticagrelor:**
```
Load:     180 mg PO
Maint:    90 mg BID × 12 months
Contra:   Prior ICH, severe liver dz
```

**Prasugrel:**
```
Load:     60 mg PO
Maint:    10 mg daily (5 mg if weight < 60 kg or age ≥ 75)
Contra:   Prior stroke/TIA (any); caution age ≥ 75, wt < 60
```

---

### Anticoagulation

**UFH** (primary PCI):
```
Bolus:    70–100 U/kg IV = 4,200–6,000 U @ 60 kg
Target:   ACT 200–250 sec (with GP IIb/IIIa)
          or 250–300 sec (without)
```

**UFH** (fibrinolytic):
```
Bolus:    60 U/kg (max 4,000 U) = 3,600 U @ 60 kg
Drip:     12 U/kg/hr (max 1,000 U/hr) = 720 U/hr @ 60 kg
Target:   aPTT 1.5–2.0× control (50–70 sec)
Duration: 48 hr or until PCI
```

**Enoxaparin** (fibrinolytic, preferred):
```
Age < 75:
  Bolus:  30 mg IV
  SC:     1 mg/kg q 12 hr = 60 mg SC q 12 hr @ 60 kg
Age ≥ 75:
  No bolus
  SC:     0.75 mg/kg q 12 hr = 45 mg SC q 12 hr @ 60 kg
CrCl < 30: 1 mg/kg q 24 hr
```

**🫘 CKD:** watch bleeding (LMWH accumulates)  
**🧓 Elderly:** no bolus, reduced dose

---

### Streptokinase

```
Dose:     1.5 million units IV
Prep:     Dilute in 100 mL NSS
Rate:     Infuse over 30–60 min
Monitor:  BP q 5 min (hypotension common — slow rate if BP ↓)
Caution:  Allergic reaction (1–5%) — premedicate hydrocortisone
          + chlorpheniramine if prior exposure
One-time: Antibodies persist × 1 year — use TNK/tPA next time
```

---

### Tenecteplase (TNK)

```
Dose (single IV bolus over 5 sec):
  < 60 kg:    30 mg
  60–69 kg:   35 mg    ← @ standard BW 60 kg reference
  70–79 kg:   40 mg
  80–89 kg:   45 mg
  ≥ 90 kg:    50 mg

Age ≥ 75: consider half-dose (emerging evidence)
```

**Must pair with anticoagulant** (enoxaparin or UFH)  
**Must pair with antiplatelet** (aspirin + clopidogrel)

---

### Nitroglycerin

```
SL:       0.4 mg q 5 min × 3 (if SBP > 90, not RV infarct)
IV drip:  Start 5–10 mcg/min, titrate by 5–10 mcg/min q 3–5 min
          Target: pain relief, SBP reduction to ~110
          Max: 200 mcg/min
Prep:     50 mg NTG + D5W 250 mL = 200 mcg/mL
          5 mcg/min = 1.5 mL/hr
Hold if:  SBP < 90, HR < 50, RV infarct, PDE5i use
```

---

### Beta-blocker (oral, if stable)

```
Metoprolol tartrate 25 mg PO BID (start)
Titrate to target HR 55–60
Hold: shock, CHF, HR < 60, block, asthma
```

---

### Morphine (if pain refractory)

```
Dose:     2–4 mg IV, may repeat q 5–15 min
Avoid if possible (slows antiplatelet, worse outcomes)
Never in RV infarct
```

## ⚠️ Critical Pitfalls

```
🚩 Inferior MI → check RV (V4R) + posterior (V7–V9)
   - ~30% inferior MI have RV involvement
   - RV infarct: avoid nitrate, morphine, diuretic
   - Needs FLUID (500 mL bolus) + vasopressor
   - Bradycardia common (atropine / pacing)

🚩 Nitrate + PDE5i = severe hypotension
   - Sildenafil within 24 hr
   - Tadalafil within 48 hr
   - Ask ทุกคน

🚩 Routine O₂ can harm
   - Only if SpO₂ < 90%
   - Hyperoxia → vasoconstriction

🚩 Fibrinolytic contraindications — check all
   - Prior ICH = absolute
   - Recent stroke, trauma, surgery, bleed
   - Severe HTN ต้องคุมก่อน

🚩 Streptokinase one-time only
   - Antibodies × 1 year
   - Next MI: use TNK or tPA

🚩 Prasugrel contraindicated after stroke/TIA
   - Increased ICH risk
   - Use clopidogrel or ticagrelor

🚩 Ticagrelor + strong CYP3A4 inhibitor/inducer
   - Clarithromycin, azoles — avoid
   - Rifampin — avoid (reduces efficacy)

🚩 Morphine = worse outcome
   - Slows oral antiplatelet absorption
   - Use only if severe refractory pain

🚩 Door-to-needle ≤ 30 min (fibrinolytic)
   - Don't wait for labs
   - Don't wait for cardiology call
   - Give ASAP if criteria met + no contraindications

🚩 Door-to-balloon ≤ 90 min (PCI)
   - Activate cath lab from ER
   - Don't wait for troponin

🚩 Post-fibrinolytic monitor
   - Bleeding (GI, GU, puncture, ICH)
   - Reperfusion arrhythmia
   - Re-occlusion (pain recur, ST re-elevate)

🚩 Cardiogenic shock — PCI anytime
   - Fibrinolytic less effective in shock
   - Transfer STAT even if late
   - Norepi + IABP/Impella bridge

🚩 Posterior MI easily missed
   - ST depression V1–V3 ≠ subendocardial NSTEMI
   - Get V7–V9 if any posterior signs
   - Posterior wall = same urgency as STEMI
```

## 📝 Copy-Ready Order

```
STEMI at: _____ (time ECG diagnostic)
Onset of symptoms: _____ (time)
Reperfusion strategy: [  ] PCI   [  ] Fibrinolytic (if no PCI < 120 min)

1. ABC, continuous ECG, SpO₂, BP q 5 min
2. O₂ only if SpO₂ < 90%
3. IV access × 2
4. NPO
5. 12-lead ECG STAT + right-sided (V4R) + posterior (V7–V9)
   if inferior MI
6. Labs STAT:
   - Troponin (q 3–6 hr × 3)
   - CBC, BMP, Mg
   - PT/INR, aPTT, type & screen
   - Lipid, HbA1c
7. CXR
8. ANTIPLATELET STAT:
   - Aspirin 325 mg CHEWED
   - P2Y12: Clopidogrel 600 mg PO (or 300 mg if fibrinolytic + age > 75)
     OR Ticagrelor 180 mg PO (avoid if prior ICH)
9. ANTICOAGULATION:
   - If PCI: UFH 70 U/kg IV (target ACT 200–250)
   - If fibrinolytic, age < 75:
     Enoxaparin 30 mg IV + 60 mg SC q 12 hr (@ BW 60 kg)
   - If fibrinolytic, age ≥ 75:
     Enoxaparin 45 mg SC q 12 hr (no bolus)
10. PAIN CONTROL:
    - NTG 0.4 mg SL q 5 min × 3 (hold if SBP < 90 / inferior MI / PDE5i)
    - Morphine 2–4 mg IV ONLY if pain refractory
11. REPERFUSION:
    [  ] Primary PCI — activate cath lab STAT (door-to-balloon ≤ 90 min)
    [  ] Fibrinolytic (door-to-needle ≤ 30 min):
        - Tenecteplase IV bolus weight-based (35 mg @ 60 kg)
          OR Streptokinase 1.5 MU IV over 30–60 min
        - Transfer to PCI center for pharmacoinvasive (3–24 hr)
12. BETA-BLOCKER: Metoprolol 25 mg PO BID within 24 hr (if stable)
13. STATIN: Atorvastatin 40–80 mg PO STAT + daily
14. ACE-I (within 24 hr if anterior MI, EF < 40%, CHF)
15. Admit CCU / step-down
16. Monitor:
    - ECG continuous (reperfusion arrhythmia, re-occlusion)
    - Pain (0–10 scale)
    - Vitals q 15 min × 2 hr, then per protocol
    - Troponin trend
17. Echocardiogram within 24–48 hr (EF, wall motion, complication)
18. Post-STEMI complications watch:
    - Day 0–1: VF/VT, AIVR, bradyarrhythmia, cardiogenic shock
    - Day 2–5: mechanical (VSD, free wall, papillary rupture)
    - Day 5–10: pericarditis (Dressler)
    - Anterior MI: LV thrombus — consider anticoagulation
19. Secondary prevention + cardiac rehab before D/C
```
