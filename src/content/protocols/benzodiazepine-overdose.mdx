## 🎯 Clinical

- **CNS depression** (sedation → coma)
- Ataxia, dysarthria (early)
- Respiratory depression (usually mild; severe if co-ingestion)
- Normal/stable vital signs usually (unless co-ingestion)
- **Miosis or mydriasis** variable (usually normal pupils)

### Severity
- **Pure benzo OD rarely fatal** in isolation (therapeutic index wide)
- Fatality typically from **co-ingestion** (opioids, alcohol, TCAs)
- Greater risk: elderly, chronic lung disease, ultra-short-acting (midazolam IV)

---

## 🧪 Workup

- **Capillary glucose** (r/o hypoglycemia)
- **VBG/ABG** (r/o respiratory acidosis)
- **ETOH level**
- **Paracetamol + salicylate** level (co-ingestion screen)
- **ECG** (r/o TCA / other cardiotoxic drugs)
- Benzo levels not routinely useful
- CT head if focal neuro deficit or trauma suspected

---

## 💊 Management — **Mostly Supportive**

### Priority: Airway + Breathing
- Position lateral if vomiting
- BVM / intubation if RR depressed, GCS drops, or cannot protect airway
- O₂ via NC/mask to SpO₂ &gt; 94%

### IV fluid + monitoring
- IV access, continuous monitor
- Manage hypothermia if prolonged exposure
- Aspiration precaution

### Decontamination
- **Activated charcoal 1 g/kg** if presentation &lt; 1 hr + airway secured
- Not useful beyond 1 hr for most benzos
- Avoid gastric lavage (risk > benefit)

---

## 💉 Flumazenil — **Use with Caution!**

<Callout type="danger" title="Flumazenil contraindications (most patients)">

**Do NOT use flumazenil if:**
- **Chronic benzo user** (withdrawal seizures!)
- **Co-ingestion with proconvulsant** (TCAs, bupropion, cocaine)
- **Known seizure disorder**
- **Unknown co-ingestion** (most ED cases!)
- **Benzo used for therapeutic reason** (e.g., seizure control)

→ **Risk of precipitating refractory seizures** outweighs benefit in most OD cases

**Cautious selected uses:**
- Iatrogenic oversedation (known single-agent, witnessed procedure)
- Pediatric accidental ingestion (opioid-naive, single agent)

**Dose (if chosen)**: **0.2 mg IV over 30 sec**, repeat q 1 min up to 1 mg total

</Callout>

---

## 🏥 Disposition

### Observation
- **Short-acting** (midazolam, triazolam): 4–6 hr
- **Medium** (alprazolam, lorazepam): 6–12 hr
- **Long-acting** (diazepam, clonazepam, chlordiazepoxide): 12–24 hr

### Discharge if
- Normal mental status
- Normal vital signs
- No co-ingestion concerns
- Psychiatry consult (ingestion → assess suicide risk)
- Safe disposition / follow-up

### Admit if
- Persistent CNS depression
- Respiratory support needed
- Co-ingestion with risk
- Suicide ideation → psych unit
- Elderly, frail, or pulmonary comorbidity

---

## ⚠️ Pitfalls

<Callout type="danger" title="Benzo OD Pitfalls">

**Use flumazenil reflexively** — seizure risk in chronic users or TCA co-ingestion  
→ Only for witnessed iatrogenic oversedation; usually supportive care is adequate

**Miss co-ingestion** — benzo + opioid + alcohol common  
→ Check ETOH, opioid signs, APAP/ASA level

**Under-observe** — long-acting agents sedate for hours  
→ Follow disposition table; observe full duration

**Airway lapse** — mistaking "sleeping it off" for safe  
→ Assess GCS + airway reflexes; intubate if cannot protect

**Miss psychiatric context** — most OD is intentional  
→ Psych consult before discharge; assess/address suicidality

</Callout>

```
Vitals: GCS__ RR__ SpO₂__% HR__ BP__ Temp__

1. Airway assessment; intubate if inadequate
2. O₂ to SpO₂ > 94%
3. IV access, continuous monitor, ECG
4. Labs: glucose, ETOH, APAP, salicylate, VBG, CBC, BMP, CK, tox screen
5. [< 1 hr, airway secure] Activated charcoal 1 g/kg
6. Flumazenil: NOT routine; consider only if isolated iatrogenic OD
7. Observation:
   - Short-acting: 4–6 hr
   - Long-acting: 12–24 hr
8. Psych consult if intentional
9. Admit if persistent CNS depression / co-ingestion risk
```
