## 🎯 Epidemiology (Thailand)

<Callout type="info" title="สถานการณ์ในประเทศไทย">

- พบบ่อยหลังน้ำท่วม, rural areas (NE/North)
- **Rice farmers, livestock workers** — สัมผัสน้ำปนเปื้อนปัสสาวะหนู/โค/สุนัข
- Peak: **rainy season (มิ.ย.–ต.ค.)**
- Incubation: 7–14 วัน (range 2–30)

</Callout>

---

## 🩺 Clinical — 2 Phases (90% mild, 10% severe)

### Phase 1: Septicemic (วันที่ 1–7)
- Sudden fever, chills, severe **myalgia (calves, back — classic)**
- Headache (retro-orbital common)
- **Conjunctival suffusion** (no purulence, non-exudative — **very specific!**)
- N/V, abdominal pain
- ± rash (faint, transient)

### Phase 2: Immune (วันที่ 7–30) — **Weil syndrome if severe**

<Callout type="danger" title="Weil syndrome = severe leptospirosis (5–15% mortality)">

Classic triad:
- **Jaundice** (conjugated hyperbilirubinemia, out of proportion to LFT)
- **AKI** (interstitial nephritis, tubular damage)
- **Hemorrhage** (pulmonary > GI > skin)

Other severe manifestations:
- **Pulmonary hemorrhage syndrome** — hemoptysis, ARDS, ↑ mortality
- **Myocarditis** — arrhythmia, CHF
- **Meningitis** (aseptic, lymphocytic CSF)
- **Rhabdomyolysis** (↑ CK)

</Callout>

---

## 🧪 Diagnosis

### Clinical suspicion (Thai endemic + exposure)
- Fever + myalgia (calves/back) + exposure history + **conjunctival suffusion** = think leptospirosis
- Often misdiagnosed as dengue, malaria, scrub typhus — **co-infection possible**

### Labs
- **CBC**: thrombocytopenia, leukocytosis (left shift)
- **LFT**: ↑ bilirubin (esp. direct, disproportionate to mildly ↑ AST/ALT — classic Weil's)
- **BUN/Cr**: AKI common (check K, acid-base)
- **CK**: ↑ (rhabdomyolysis pattern)
- **UA**: proteinuria, hyaline casts, hematuria
- **CRP, procalcitonin**: ↑
- **Clotting**: ↑ PT/INR in severe, DIC possible

### Specific tests
- **Serology (MAT — microscopic agglutination)**: gold standard, paired sera  
  (IgM ≥ 1:400 single or 4-fold rise)
- **IgM ELISA / rapid test**: positive after day 5–7; **false negative early**
- **PCR** blood/urine: early phase (day 1–7), sensitive
- **Blood culture**: specific media, slow (up to 4 weeks)

### Imaging
- CXR: alveolar hemorrhage, diffuse infiltrates (severe)
- CT chest if pulmonary hemorrhage syndrome

## 💊 Treatment

### Mild disease (outpatient)

| Option | Dose |
|---|---|
| **Doxycycline** | 100 mg PO BID × 7 days |
| **Azithromycin** | 500 mg PO daily × 3–5 days |
| **Amoxicillin** | 500 mg PO TID × 7 days (pregnancy) |

### Moderate / Severe (inpatient)

| Option | Dose |
|---|---|
| **Ceftriaxone** | 1–2 g IV daily × 7 days (1st line inpatient) |
| **Penicillin G** | 1.5 MU IV q 6 hr × 7 days |
| **Doxycycline** | 100 mg IV/PO BID (alternative) |

### Supportive
- **Aggressive IVF** (watch for pulmonary edema — balance carefully)
- **Dialysis** if AKI severe (mortality ↓ significantly with early HD)
- **Mechanical ventilation + lung-protective** for pulmonary hemorrhage
- **Transfusion** (platelet, FFP) for bleeding
- Vasopressor support if septic shock

<Callout type="warning" title="Jarisch-Herxheimer Reaction">

- Within hours of first antibiotic dose in spirochete infection
- Fever ↑, rigors, hypotension, worsening rash
- **Supportive** (IVF, acetaminophen, vasopressor if needed)
- Continue antibiotic — do not stop

</Callout>

---

## 🛡 Prophylaxis

### Post-exposure (high-risk: rescuers, flooded areas)
- **Doxycycline 200 mg PO once weekly** during exposure period

### Pre-exposure (military, prolonged exposure)
- Doxycycline 200 mg PO weekly
- Avoid contaminated water; wear boots; cover wounds

---

## ⚠️ Pitfalls

<Callout type="danger" title="Leptospirosis Pitfalls">

**Miss in dengue-like illness** — Thai endemic, overlapping sx  
→ Ask about exposure (rice paddies, flooding, rodents, livestock); check conjunctival suffusion

**False negative early serology** — IgM positive only after day 5–7  
→ Use PCR in early phase; treat empirically if high suspicion

**Delayed treatment** — mortality rises sharply with delay  
→ Treat empirically within hours of suspicion; don't wait for labs

**Co-infection with dengue/scrub typhus** — esp. in Thai endemic  
→ Workup broad; doxycycline covers lepto + scrub typhus + rickettsia

**Severe disease ≠ need ICU only** — need dialysis + ventilator + specialist  
→ Transfer to tertiary if pulmonary hemorrhage, severe AKI, or MOF

**Bleeding + antiplatelet/AC** — avoid NSAIDs, anticoagulants  
→ Support with platelets + FFP; treat DIC

</Callout>
