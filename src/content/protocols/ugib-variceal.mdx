## 🎯 Recognition

### Presentation

- **Hematemesis** — fresh red blood หรือ "coffee ground"
- **Melena** — tarry black stool
- **Hematochezia** (massive UGIB ~10%)
- **Hypotension**, tachycardia, syncope
- **Anemia** symptoms (fatigue, dyspnea, angina)

### Suspect variceal bleeding

- Known **cirrhosis** / portal hypertension
- **Chronic liver disease** signs: spider, palmar erythema, splenomegaly, ascites, caput medusa, jaundice
- **Alcohol abuse**, chronic **viral hepatitis** (HBV/HCV)
- Previous variceal bleed
- Hemodynamic instability disproportionate to blood seen

### ⚠️ Mortality
- **First bleed:** 15–20%
- **Rebleed within 6 wk:** 30–40% (↓ by secondary prophylaxis)

---

## 🔴 Immediate Actions (first 30 min)

### Step 1 — Resuscitate

1. **2 large-bore IV** (16–18 G) — peripheral OK
2. **Crossmatch 4 units PRBC** STAT
3. **Intubate ก่อน endoscopy** ถ้า:
   - Massive hematemesis
   - Altered mental (grade III–IV HE)
   - Aspiration risk
   - Hemodynamically unstable
4. **Cardiac monitor, continuous BP, SpO₂**
5. **Foley** — urine output monitoring
6. **STAT labs:**
   - CBC (serial Hb)
   - Coag (PT/INR, PTT)
   - BUN/Cr, electrolytes, glucose
   - LFT, albumin, bilirubin
   - Lactate
   - Blood type + crossmatch × 4
   - Ammonia (if HE suspect)
   - Blood culture × 2 (SBP prophylaxis indication)
   - Paracentesis if ascites (SBP)

### Step 2 — Fluid + Blood Transfusion

```
Initial: NSS / LR crystalloid 500-1000 mL bolus
⚠️ ไม่ over-resuscitate (↑ portal pressure → rebleed)

Transfusion target: RESTRICTIVE strategy (better outcomes)
  Target Hb 7-8 g/dL (NOT 10)
  
  Exception — TRANSFUSE MORE if:
  - Active massive bleeding
  - ACS / active cardiac ischemia
  - Elderly with heart disease

FFP if INR > 1.5 + active bleeding (limited benefit in cirrhosis)
Platelets if < 50,000 + active bleeding
Cryoprecipitate if fibrinogen < 150
```

⚠️ **Over-transfusion = WORSE outcomes** ใน variceal (↑ portal pressure)

## 💊 Pharmacologic Treatment — START EARLY

### 1. Vasoactive Agent (reduce portal pressure) — **FIRST-LINE**

```
Option A — Terlipressin (preferred, if available):
  2 mg IV q 4 hr x 24-48 hr
  then 1 mg IV q 4 hr x total 5 days
  
  ⚠️ Monitor for ischemia (MI, limb, bowel)
  ⚠️ Hyponatremia — monitor Na daily

Option B — Octreotide (Somatostatin analog):
  Bolus: 50 mcg IV
  Drip: 50 mcg/hr IV x 3-5 days
  (Prep: 500 mcg + NSS 500 mL = 1 mcg/mL → 50 mL/hr)

Option C — Somatostatin:
  Bolus: 250 mcg IV
  Drip: 250 mcg/hr x 3-5 days
```

**Start ASAP — ก่อน endoscopy** ถ้าสงสัย varices (even before confirmed)

### 2. PPI (for empiric ulcer cover until diagnosis clear)

```
Pantoprazole:
  Bolus: 80 mg IV
  Drip: 8 mg/hr x 72 hr
  (Prep: 80 mg + NSS 100 mL → infuse over 30 min for bolus;
   then 80 mg + NSS 100 mL = 0.8 mg/mL → 10 mL/hr drip)

OR Omeprazole 80 mg IV bolus + 8 mg/hr drip
```

⚠️ ถ้าภายหลัง confirmed variceal only = หยุด PPI drip, switch to daily PO

### 3. Prophylactic Antibiotic (critical — ↓ mortality)

```
Ceftriaxone 1 g IV q 24 hr x 7 days
  (preferred — ↓ SBP + rebleed)

Alternatives:
  - Norfloxacin 400 mg PO q 12 hr (outpatient)
  - Ciprofloxacin 200 mg IV q 12 hr

⚠️ ห้ามลืม — SBP prophylaxis ↓ mortality ~20% ใน cirrhotic bleed
```

### 4. Lactulose (if HE suspect)

```
Lactulose 30-45 mL PO/NG q 6-8 hr
Target: 2-3 soft stools/day
Can repeat per stool frequency
```

### 5. Vitamin K (cirrhotic coagulopathy)

```
Vitamin K 10 mg IV slow (over 10 min) x 3 days
(Mainly for warfarin-reversed or nutritional; 
 limited help in cirrhotic coagulopathy)
```

## 📍 Endoscopy — Within 12 hr (6 hr if unstable)

### Preparation

- Erythromycin 250 mg IV 30–120 min pre-endoscopy (prokinetic — clears blood)
- NG tube ไม่ routine (ไม่ help visualization + ระวัง varices)
- Intubate ถ้า criteria above

### Interventions

- **Endoscopic band ligation (EBL)** — first-line for esophageal varices
- **Sclerotherapy** — alternative if EBL not feasible
- **Cyanoacrylate (histoacryl) glue** — gastric varices
- **Hemoclip** — if ulcer bleeding found (non-variceal)

### Success + follow-up

- ~80–90% initial control with vasoactive + EBL
- **Repeat EBL q 2–4 wk** until obliteration (usually 2–4 sessions)
- Long-term β-blocker prevention

## 🎈 Balloon Tamponade (Temporary Bridge Only)

### When to use
- **Failed** pharmacologic + endoscopic therapy
- **Massive bleeding** preventing endoscopy
- **Transport** to referral center

### Types
- **Sengstaken-Blakemore** (SB tube) — esophageal + gastric balloons
- **Minnesota tube** (esophageal + gastric + aspiration)
- **Linton-Nachlas** (gastric balloon only — for gastric varices)

### ⚠️ Rules

```
- Only AFTER intubation (protect airway)
- Max 24 hr (pressure necrosis, rupture)
- Verify position: gastric balloon inflated + traction + CXR
- Gastric balloon 200-300 mL air first, confirm CXR
- THEN pull back to snug at GE junction
- ONLY inflate esophageal balloon if gastric ไม่พอ 
  (pressure 30-45 mmHg, deflate periodically)
- STOP if chest pain, sudden respiratory distress 
  (esophageal rupture)
```

### After tamponade → TIPS or surgical referral

## 🔄 Refractory Bleeding — Definitive Options

### 1. TIPS (Transjugular Intrahepatic Portosystemic Shunt)
- First rescue for refractory variceal bleed
- ↓ portal pressure quickly
- Performed at referral center
- Risk: HE worsening (30%), shunt stenosis

### 2. Surgical shunt (rare now)
- Distal splenorenal shunt
- Referral only

### 3. Liver transplant (end-stage)
- Definitive treatment for underlying disease

### 4. BRTO (Balloon-occluded Retrograde Transvenous Obliteration)
- For gastric varices with gastrorenal shunt
- Referral center

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Critical Pitfalls)

<Callout type="danger" title="UGIB — Variceal Bleeding — Pitfalls">

**Restrictive transfusion (Hb 7-8)**
→ Villanueva NEJM 2013: lower mortality
→ Over-transfusion → ↑ portal pressure → rebleed + death
→ Exception: active massive bleed or cardiac ischemia

**Prophylactic antibiotic = MUST**
→ Ceftriaxone 1 g IV q 24 hr x 7 days
→ ↓ mortality ~20%
→ ↓ SBP, UTI, rebleed
→ ลืมไม่ได้

**Vasoactive agent ก่อน endoscopy**
→ Start ทันทีที่สงสัย varices
→ ไม่ต้องรอ endoscopy confirm
→ Terlipressin / Octreotide / Somatostatin

**Intubate if high risk aspiration**
→ Massive hematemesis, altered mental, hemodynamic instability
→ Variceal bleed + encephalopathy = ยิ่งต้องรีบ
→ ดีกว่าให้เกิด aspiration แล้วค่อยแก้

**NG tube controversial**
→ ไม่ required — variceal ยิ่งไม่ใส่ (รบกวน varices?)
→ Erythromycin 250 mg IV ดีกว่า ใน clearing

**FFP limited benefit ใน cirrhosis**
→ INR ใน cirrhotic ≠ bleeding risk (rebalanced coag)
→ FFP สำหรับ active bleed + INR > 1.5 เท่านั้น
→ Platelets > 50,000 target ถ้า active bleed

**Balloon tamponade = bridge only**
→ Max 24 hr
→ MUST intubate ก่อน
→ Sengstaken or Minnesota tube
→ Definitive: TIPS / surgery

**Rebleed in 24-48 hr common**
→ ~15-20% rebleed ใน first 5 days
→ Re-endoscopy + TIPS threshold
→ Don't discharge early

**Hepatic encephalopathy**
→ Blood load = ammonia load
→ Lactulose 30-45 mL q 6-8 hr
→ Rifaximin 550 mg PO BID (chronic)
→ Check ammonia if altered (supportive, not diagnostic)

**SBP screening (all cirrhotic UGIB)**
→ Paracentesis ก่อน fluid overload
→ SBP criteria: ascites PMN > 250/mm³
→ Add prophylactic abx anyway

**Portal vein thrombosis**
→ New variceal bleed → US Doppler
→ Anticoagulation controversial (bleeding risk vs. extension)
→ Hematology consult

**MELD score for risk**
→ MELD > 15 = high rebleed + mortality
→ MELD > 20 = consider transplant workup
→ Early TIPS ถ้า MELD 15-20 + Child B/C

**Secondary prophylaxis**
→ Non-selective β-blocker (propranolol, nadolol, carvedilol)
→ Target HR 55-60 หรือ 25% ลดจาก baseline
→ Repeat EBL q 2-4 wk until obliteration
→ ไม่ใช้ carvedilol ถ้า decompensated (hypotension)

**Avoid NSAIDs, nephrotoxins**
→ NSAID worsens renal + GI
→ Aminoglycoside, contrast = AKI risk
→ Careful with BP meds (hypotension)

**Volume status tricky**
→ Cirrhotic: intravascular depleted + third-spaced
→ Use IVC US + lactate + UO + mental
→ Avoid over-resuscitation

</Callout>
