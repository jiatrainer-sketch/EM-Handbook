## 🎯 Epidemiology — Thai Endemic

<Callout type="info" title="Scrub typhus in Thailand">

- **Endemic throughout Thailand**, especially rural / forested areas
- Vector: **chigger mites** (larval Trombiculidae) in grass, leaf litter
- Peak: **rainy season + cool**
- Common in farmers, soldiers, campers, hikers

</Callout>

---

## 🩺 Clinical

### Classic triad (look for all!)
1. **Fever** (sudden, high, 38–40°C)
2. **Eschar** — single **painless** black scab at bite site (60–80% present)
   - **Check hidden areas**: axilla, groin, waistband, under breasts, behind ears, scrotum
3. **Regional lymphadenopathy** (near eschar)

### Other features
- Severe headache (often fronto-retroorbital)
- Myalgia
- Maculopapular rash (5–7 days onset, trunk → extremities)
- Conjunctival injection
- Hepatosplenomegaly
- Cough, dyspnea (interstitial pneumonitis)

### Complications (untreated, 30%+)
- **Acute respiratory distress syndrome (ARDS)**
- **Acute kidney injury**
- **Meningoencephalitis**
- **Myocarditis**
- **Multi-organ failure**
- Mortality untreated: 6–40% (endemic strains vary)

---

## 🧪 Diagnosis

### Clinical + exposure + response to empirical doxycycline
- **Defervescence within 24–48 hr of doxycycline** is strong supportive evidence

### Labs
- **CBC**: thrombocytopenia, lymphocytosis
- **LFT**: mild-moderate transaminitis
- **BMP**: AKI possible, hyponatremia common
- **CRP**: ↑↑
- **CXR**: pulmonary infiltrates (interstitial)

### Specific tests
- **Weil-Felix** (OX-K agglutination) — historical, low sensitivity/specificity
- **IFA IgM/IgG** — gold standard (4-fold rise in paired sera)
- **IgM ELISA / rapid test** — available, sensitivity ~90%
- **PCR** (blood, eschar swab) — early detection, expensive

<Callout type="warning" title="Serology may be negative early">

- IgM positive only after **day 5–7** of illness  
- Early disease → PCR or empirical treatment  
- **In endemic area, treat on clinical suspicion — don't wait for labs**

</Callout>

---

## 💊 Treatment

### First-line (all ages, pregnancy incl. 1st trimester in severe)

| Drug | Dose |
|---|---|
| **Doxycycline** | Adult: 100 mg PO/IV BID × 7 days |
| Pregnancy / children | Doxycycline now safe short-course per WHO; or **Azithromycin 500 mg QD × 3–5 d** |

### Alternatives
- **Azithromycin** 500 mg QD × 3–5 days (pregnancy, children, doxy allergy)
- **Chloramphenicol** 500 mg PO QID × 7 days (historical)
- **Rifampicin** — in some endemic areas with resistance (controversial)

### Severe cases (ARDS, myocarditis, meningoencephalitis)
- **Doxycycline IV 100 mg q 12 hr** × 7–14 days
- Add **azithromycin IV** in severe / resistance suspected
- Supportive: ICU, ventilator, dialysis as needed

### Response expected
- **Afebrile within 24–48 hr** — if not → consider co-infection, wrong diagnosis, resistant strain
- Continue full 7-day course even after fever resolves (relapse prevention)

## 🔬 Differential Diagnosis (Thai Endemic — common overlaps)

| Disease | Clue |
|---|---|
| **Leptospirosis** | Calf myalgia, conjunctival suffusion, jaundice + AKI |
| **Dengue** | Thrombocytopenia, hemoconcentration, tourniquet test + |
| **Malaria** | Fever periodicity, blood smear + |
| **Typhoid** | Rose spots, relative bradycardia, stepwise fever |
| **Meningococcemia** | Petechiae, shock |
| **Viral hepatitis** | Jaundice, elevated LFT |

**Note**: scrub typhus + dengue **co-infection** reported in Thailand — workup both if endemic + features overlap

## ⚠️ Pitfalls

<Callout type="danger" title="Scrub Typhus Pitfalls">

**Miss eschar** — patients don't remember chigger bite; eschar in hidden sites  
→ **Full-body skin exam** in any febrile patient from endemic area

**Test-and-wait** approach — serology negative early = miss diagnosis  
→ Empirical doxycycline if suspicion; response in 24–48 hr = confirms

**Wrong antibiotic** — β-lactams, aminoglycosides, fluoroquinolones **ineffective**  
→ Rickettsial bacteria → need tetracyclines or macrolides

**Doxycycline contraindication fear in pregnancy** — short-course OK per updated WHO  
→ Use azithromycin if unsure; severe disease → doxy justified

**Assume viral due to rash + fever** — Thai endemic → empirical treatment saves lives

**Missing complications** — ARDS, myocarditis, meningoencephalitis  
→ Monitor closely; CXR + ECG + neuro exam in severe

</Callout>
