## 🎯 Clinical Presentation

### Classic triad (ไม่ครบทุกคน — present ~44%)
- **Fever**
- **Neck stiffness** (nuchal rigidity)
- **Altered mental status**

### 95% มีอย่างน้อย 2 จาก 4:
- Fever
- Neck stiffness
- Altered mental
- **Headache**

### Supporting findings
- **Photophobia, phonophobia**
- **Seizure** (30% — esp pneumococcal)
- **Focal neuro deficit** (10–35%)
- **Rash:** petechial/purpuric → meningococcemia (Waterhouse-Friderichsen)
- **Kernig's, Brudzinski's** — low sensitivity (~5%) แต่ specific
- **Jolt accentuation** — pain ↑ เมื่อหมุนหัว horizontal 2–3 times/sec (better sens)

### ⚠️ Elderly / immunocompromised
- Can present atypically: just confusion, falls, ไม่มี fever
- Low threshold for LP

## 🔴 Immediate Actions — **DON'T DELAY ANTIBIOTIC FOR CT/LP**

### Time-critical sequence (first 30 min)

```
1. IV access × 2
2. Blood culture × 2 — DRAW BEFORE antibiotic
3. STEROID + ANTIBIOTIC — TOGETHER, ภายใน 30-60 min
4. CT head (if indicated)
5. LP (if no CT contraindication)
```

### Indications for CT BEFORE LP (per IDSA)

ถ้ามี **any of:**
- Immunocompromised (HIV, immunosuppressive)
- Hx CNS disease (mass, stroke, focal infection)
- **New-onset seizure** (within 1 wk)
- **Papilledema**
- **Altered consciousness (GCS &lt; 15)**
- **Focal neurologic deficit**

**ถ้าไม่มี criteria เหล่านี้ → LP ได้เลย ไม่ต้อง CT**

⚠️ **CT negative ≠ safe for LP** — clinical + CT ร่วม (herniation risk)

### LP contraindications (absolute)
- Herniation risk (mass effect, midline shift)
- Infection ที่ site (skin infection over LP area)
- Platelets &lt; 50,000 (relative)
- Coagulopathy (INR > 1.4, anticoag)
- Unstable cardiopulmonary

## 🧪 CSF Analysis (bacterial vs viral)

| Parameter | Normal | Bacterial | Viral | TB |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Opening pressure** | &lt; 20 cm H₂O | ↑↑ (&gt; 30) | Normal–↑ | ↑↑ |
| **WBC** | &lt; 5 | 100–50,000 | 25–500 | 100–500 |
| **Predominant cell** | Lymph | **PMN** (&gt; 80%) | Lymph | Lymph |
| **Glucose (vs serum)** | &gt; 60% | **&lt; 40%** (low) | Normal | Low |
| **Protein** | &lt; 45 | **&gt; 100** (high) | 50–100 | &gt; 100 |
| **Gram stain** | Negative | Positive 60–90% | Negative | AFB |

### Additional tests
- CSF culture, PCR (meningococcal, pneumococcal, HSV, enterovirus)
- CSF lactate (> 3.5 mmol/L — bacterial)
- Blood glucose concurrent (to calculate CSF/serum ratio)
- Consider: TB PCR/culture, fungal, cryptococcal Ag (HIV)

## 💊 Empiric Antibiotics (by age/host)

### Adult 18–50 yr (community-acquired, immunocompetent)

```
Ceftriaxone 2 g IV q 12 hr
  + 
Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q 8-12 hr
  (target trough 15-20 mcg/mL)
  = 15 mg/kg → 900 mg @ BW 60 kg
  = 20 mg/kg → 1.2 g @ BW 60 kg
```

**Rationale:** Pneumococcus + Meningococcus ± resistant pneumococcus

### Adult > 50 yr OR immunocompromised OR alcoholic

```
Ceftriaxone 2 g IV q 12 hr
  +
Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q 8-12 hr
  +
Ampicillin 2 g IV q 4 hr
  (cover Listeria)
```

### Post-neurosurgery / head trauma / CSF shunt

```
Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q 8-12 hr
  +
Cefepime 2 g IV q 8 hr
  OR Meropenem 2 g IV q 8 hr
  OR Ceftazidime 2 g IV q 8 hr
  (cover Pseudomonas + staph)
```

### Suspected TB meningitis

```
HREZ (Isoniazid 5 mg/kg + Rifampin 10 mg/kg + 
      Pyrazinamide 25 mg/kg + Ethambutol 15 mg/kg)
  + Pyridoxine 50 mg/day
  + Dexamethasone 0.4 mg/kg/day tapered over 8 wk
2-month intensive, then 10+ months continuation (total 12 months)
```

### Suspected HSV encephalitis (temporal lobe, seizure, bizarre behavior)

```
Acyclovir 10 mg/kg IV q 8 hr
= 600 mg q 8 hr @ BW 60 kg
Duration: 14-21 days
⚠️ Adjust in renal impairment
```

### Adjust per culture + sensitivity

- Duration: pneumococcus 10–14 d, meningococcus 7 d, H. influenzae 7 d, Listeria 21 d, GBS/E.coli 21 d

## 💊 Adjunctive Dexamethasone (CRITICAL)

```
Dexamethasone 10 mg IV q 6 hr × 4 days
⚠️ GIVE 10-20 min BEFORE or WITH first antibiotic dose
```

**Rationale:** Proven ↓ mortality + neurologic sequelae ใน **pneumococcal meningitis**

### When to GIVE
- Suspected bacterial meningitis (pending culture)
- Continue ถ้า culture = pneumococcus
- Adults, children > 1 month

### When to STOP
- Culture = non-pneumococcus → stop dexamethasone
- Viral / TB (separate protocol — different duration)
- Septic shock from meningococcal (controversial)

### ⚠️ ถ้าให้ ABX ไปแล้วโดยไม่มี dexamethasone
- Giving later = no proven benefit
- Don't backfill if > 1 hr after first abx dose

## 💊 การเตรียมยา (Drug Preparation, BW 60 kg)

### Ceftriaxone 2 g IV q 12 hr

```
Reconstitute 2 g vial with 10 mL SW → 20 mL NSS/D5W
Infuse over 30 min
No renal adjustment in meningitis dosing
```

### Vancomycin 15–20 mg/kg IV q 8–12 hr

```
Dose: 900 mg - 1,200 mg @ BW 60 kg (use 15-20 mg/kg)
Dilute in NSS 250 mL, infuse over 60-90 min
(Red man syndrome if too fast)

Trough target 15-20 mcg/mL (meningitis)
Check trough before 3rd-4th dose

Renal adjustment:
  CrCl 50-80: q 12 hr
  CrCl 20-50: q 24 hr  
  CrCl < 20: single dose + redose when trough OK
```

### Ampicillin 2 g IV q 4 hr (Listeria cover)

```
Reconstitute with SW → NSS 50 mL
Infuse over 15-30 min
Renal: CrCl 10-50: q 6-8 hr; < 10: q 8-12 hr
```

### Dexamethasone 10 mg IV q 6 hr

```
No dilution needed, IV push over 1-2 min
Duration: 4 days total
```

### Acyclovir 10 mg/kg IV q 8 hr (HSV)

```
Dose: 600 mg q 8 hr @ BW 60 kg
Dilute in NSS 100 mL, infuse over 1 hr
Renal: adjust if CrCl < 50
Hydrate well (prevent crystalluria)
```

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Critical Pitfalls)

```
🚩 ห้าม delay antibiotic for CT/LP
   - Goal: antibiotic ภายใน 60 min (ideally 30 min)
   - Order: IV → Blood cx → Abx + Dex → CT → LP
   - ถ้ายัง LP ไม่ได้ = ไม่ต้องรอ — give empiric

🚩 Dexamethasone ต้องมาก่อน/พร้อม antibiotic
   - ถ้าให้ abx ไปแล้ว > 1 hr = ไม่ได้ประโยชน์
   - Proven benefit ใน pneumococcal meningitis
   - Stop dex ถ้า culture ไม่ใช่ pneumococcus

🚩 LP indications for CT first
   - Altered mental, focal deficit, seizure, papilledema,
     immunocompromised
   - ถ้าไม่มี → LP ได้เลย

🚩 CSF glucose ratio > absolute value
   - เทียบกับ serum glucose (ทำพร้อมกัน)
   - Bacterial: CSF/serum < 40%
   - Viral: ratio > 60% (normal)

🚩 Listeria cover (> 50, immunocompromised, alcoholic)
   - Ceftriaxone ไม่คลุม Listeria
   - ต้องเพิ่ม Ampicillin 2 g IV q 4 hr
   - ถ้า PCN allergy → Bactrim 5 mg/kg (TMP) q 6-8 hr

🚩 Meningococcal disease = droplet isolation + PEP
   - First 24 hr of abx = droplet precautions
   - Close contacts (household, saliva exposure, HCW 
     that intubated) → prophylaxis:
     Rifampin 600 mg PO q 12 hr × 2 days
     OR Ciprofloxacin 500 mg PO × 1
     OR Ceftriaxone 250 mg IM × 1 (pregnancy)

🚩 Repeat LP indications
   - Clinically worsening despite 48 hr abx
   - Resistant organisms
   - Shunt infection

🚩 Cryptococcal meningitis (HIV)
   - Lymphocytic pleocytosis + mild protein
   - Cryptococcal Ag (+), India ink
   - Rx: Liposomal amphoB + flucytosine × 2 wk, 
     then fluconazole

🚩 TB meningitis = subacute
   - Lymphocytic, low glucose, high protein
   - AFB rarely seen on smear
   - Empiric HREZ + steroid ถ้าสงสัย (high mortality)
   - Long course: 12 months total

🚩 Viral encephalitis (HSV)
   - Temporal lobe focal, bizarre behavior, seizure
   - CSF: lymphocytic, RBC may be present, HSV PCR
   - Acyclovir EMPIRIC while awaiting PCR
   - MRI > CT for dx

🚩 Pregnancy dosing
   - Ceftriaxone OK (category B)
   - Vancomycin OK
   - Ampicillin OK
   - Avoid streptomycin, tetracycline
   - Avoid fluoroquinolone (prophylaxis → use ceftriaxone)

🚩 Petechial/purpuric rash = meningococcemia
   - Emergent — can deteriorate in hours
   - Give ceftriaxone IMMEDIATELY (even pre-hospital)
   - Waterhouse-Friderichsen syndrome: adrenal hemorrhage
   - Check adrenal + treat empirically ถ้า shock
```

## 📝 คำสั่งพร้อมใช้ (Copy-Ready Order)

```
SUSPECTED BACTERIAL MENINGITIS

Presentation: [ ] fever [ ] HA [ ] neck stiffness [ ] altered
              [ ] seizure [ ] rash [ ] other: ___
Risk: [ ] immunocompromised [ ] elderly >50 [ ] alcoholic
      [ ] post-neurosurgery [ ] head trauma [ ] HIV
      [ ] pregnant [ ] TB exposure

TIMELINE — abx within 60 min of arrival:

1. IV access x 2 large-bore
2. Blood culture x 2 (DRAW BEFORE antibiotic)
3. Basic labs: CBC, lytes, glucose, BUN/Cr, INR/PTT, 
   lactate, ABG
4. Serum glucose (for CSF ratio)
5. DEXAMETHASONE 10 mg IV — give NOW (10-20 min before abx)
6. ANTIBIOTIC — per risk group below

EMPIRIC REGIMEN (select):

[ ] Adult 18-50 yr, immunocompetent:
    Ceftriaxone 2 g IV q 12 hr
    + Vancomycin 1 g IV q 12 hr (trough 15-20)

[ ] Adult >50 / immunocompromised / alcoholic:
    Ceftriaxone 2 g IV q 12 hr
    + Vancomycin 1 g IV q 12 hr
    + Ampicillin 2 g IV q 4 hr (Listeria)

[ ] Post-neurosurgery / head trauma / shunt:
    Vancomycin 1 g IV q 12 hr
    + Cefepime 2 g IV q 8 hr (or Meropenem 2 g q 8h)

[ ] Suspected HSV encephalitis:
    ADD Acyclovir 600 mg IV q 8 hr (pending HSV PCR)

[ ] Suspected TB meningitis:
    HREZ + pyridoxine + dexamethasone taper 8 wk

[ ] Dexamethasone 10 mg IV q 6 hr x 4 days total
    (continue if pneumococcus, stop otherwise)

THEN (within 60-90 min):
7. CT head if indicated (criteria above)
8. LP if no contraindication
   - Opening pressure
   - Cell count + differential
   - Glucose + protein
   - Gram stain + culture
   - PCR panel
   - Lactate
   - Extra tubes for TB/crypto if indicated

Isolation:
[ ] Droplet precautions x 24 hr (meningococcal cover)

Post-exposure prophylaxis (meningococcal):
[ ] Rifampin 600 mg PO q 12 hr x 2 days — close contacts
[ ] Ciprofloxacin 500 mg PO x 1 — single dose alternative
[ ] Ceftriaxone 250 mg IM x 1 — pregnancy

Monitor:
- Neuro checks q 1 hr x 6 hr, then q 2-4 hr
- Vital signs q 15 min x 2 hr, then q 1 hr
- Seizure precautions
- Check hearing post-discharge (sequela)

Adjust:
- Narrow per culture + sensitivity
- Duration: pneumococcal 10-14 d, mening 7 d, listeria 21 d

Admit ICU / step-down
ID consult
Neurology consult if seizure / focal
```
