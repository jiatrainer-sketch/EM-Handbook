## 🎯 Rabies = 100% Fatal Once Symptomatic

<Callout type="danger" title="PEP = ป้องกันได้ 100% ถ้าให้ถูกต้อง">

Rabies virus มีระยะฟักตัว **2 สัปดาห์ – 1 ปี** — ต้องเริ่ม PEP ภายใน 24 hr หลัง exposure แม้ผู้ป่วยดูปกติ  
→ **ยิ่งเร็วยิ่งดี** — ไม่มี "สาย" (ให้ได้ทุกเวลาถ้ายังไม่มี symptoms)

</Callout>

## 📊 WHO Category & Action (Thailand follows)

### Category I (no risk)
- Touching / feeding animal, licks on intact skin
- **Action**: no PEP (wash + reassure)

### Category II (moderate risk)
- Minor scratches, abrasions without bleeding
- Licks on broken skin
- **Action**:
  - **Wound care** + **vaccine**
  - **No RIG needed** (unless high-risk species, bat)

### Category III (severe risk)
- Single/multiple **transdermal bites/scratches with bleeding**
- Licks on mucous membranes / open wounds
- Contamination of mucous membranes with saliva
- **Contact with bat** (any exposure)
- **Action**:
  - **Wound care** + **RIG** (HRIG) + **vaccine**

---

## 🩹 Wound Care (Essential!)

<Callout type="success" title="Wound cleansing reduces virus by 90%">

1. **Immediate washing with soap + water × 15 min** (viral inactivation)
2. **Povidone-iodine or chlorhexidine** after wash
3. **Do NOT suture** immediately if possible (risk ↑)
4. **Tetanus prophylaxis** (see tetanus PEP protocol)
5. **Antibiotic** if dirty/deep: Amox-clav 875/125 BID × 5–7 d

</Callout>

## 💉 Rabies Vaccines (Thailand)

### Modern cell-culture vaccines (CCV) — **safe, effective**
- PCEC (Purified Chick Embryo Cell vaccine) — Rabipur
- PVRV (Purified Vero-cell Rabies Vaccine) — Verorab
- HDCV (Human Diploid Cell Vaccine) — Imovax
- **0.5 mL per dose**, IM (deltoid; anterolateral thigh in infants — **not gluteal**)

### Regimens

| Regimen | Schedule | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Essen (5-dose)** | Day 0, 3, 7, 14, 28 IM | Standard IM regimen |
| **Zagreb (2-1-1)** | Day 0 (2 doses, left + right deltoid), 7, 21 | IM; fewer visits |
| **Thai Red Cross ID** | Day 0, 3, 7, 28 (2 sites × 0.1 mL intradermal) | Thailand MOPH preferred — **cheaper, equally effective** |

### Previously vaccinated (pre-exposure or complete PEP &lt; 3 years)
- **2 doses only**: Day 0 + Day 3 IM
- **No RIG needed**

## 💉 Rabies Immunoglobulin (RIG) — Category III only

<Callout type="warning" title="RIG ใช้ตอน Category III เท่านั้น + ให้ครั้งเดียว">

**Give with FIRST dose of vaccine** (Day 0); **do not give after Day 7**  
(immune response from vaccine takes over)

</Callout>

| Product | Dose |
|---|---|
| **HRIG (Human RIG)** | **20 IU/kg** — preferred |
| **ERIG (Equine RIG)** | 40 IU/kg — cheaper, available in Thailand; screen for allergy |

### Administration
- **Infiltrate as much as anatomically possible around wound** (key — local neutralization)
- Remaining volume IM **distant from vaccine site**
- **Never mix in same syringe as vaccine**
- Different limb from vaccine if possible

## 🐕 Species-Specific Thai Context

### Dogs, cats
- Most common. Observe for 10 days if safe + owned + healthy.
- If animal becomes sick/dies → continue full PEP
- **Unknown/stray/ferally** → treat as rabid → full PEP

### Bats (ALWAYS Category III)
- Even minor contact = PEP
- Bat bite often undetected

### Monkeys (common in Thailand tourist areas)
- Macaques — PEP per category
- Also risk: herpes B virus (separate consideration)

### Livestock (cow, buffalo, horse, goat)
- Rabies possible; PEP per category + workup source

### Wild animals (jackal, civet, mongoose)
- Category III if exposure with bleeding or saliva exposure

### Rodents, rabbits
- **No PEP needed** (extremely rare)

## 🛡 Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis (PrEP)

### Who?
- Veterinarians, animal handlers
- Travelers to endemic areas (rural, prolonged)
- Lab workers with rabies virus
- Children in endemic areas (consider)

### Schedule
- Day 0, 7, 21 or 28 IM (3 doses)
- OR ID (0.1 mL) Day 0, 7, 21 (2 sites)
- Booster not routinely needed

---

## ⚠️ Pitfalls

<Callout type="danger" title="Rabies PEP Pitfalls">

**Delay PEP** — once symptoms start, fatal  
→ Start ASAP; never too late to begin before symptoms

**Forget RIG in Category III** — vaccine alone takes 7–10 days for immunity  
→ Always infiltrate HRIG/ERIG around wound

**RIG in gluteal** — poor absorption  
→ Infiltrate around wound + IM deltoid/thigh; never gluteal

**Skip wound washing** — reduces viral load significantly  
→ Soap + water × 15 min is cornerstone

**Suture wound** — ↑ rabies risk  
→ Avoid suturing if possible; loose approximation only if cosmetic necessity; RIG infiltrated first

**Test animal brain without full PEP** — don't wait; start PEP, stop if animal negative + healthy 10 days

**Pregnant/immunocompromised** — no contraindication  
→ PEP as usual; vaccine safe in pregnancy

</Callout>

☐ Booster (prior PEP): Day 0 + Day 3 only
