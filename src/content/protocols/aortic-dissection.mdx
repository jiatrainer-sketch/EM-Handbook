## 🎯 When to suspect

- **Sudden, severe, tearing / ripping chest pain** radiating to back
- Pain migration (anterior → back → abdomen)
- **BP / pulse asymmetry** between arms (&gt; 20 mmHg)
- New aortic regurgitation murmur
- Neurologic deficit (stroke-like) + chest pain
- Syncope + chest pain
- Limb ischemia + chest/back pain

### Risk factors

- Uncontrolled hypertension (#1)
- Marfan / Ehlers-Danlos / Loeys-Dietz / bicuspid aortic valve
- Pregnancy (3rd trimester, peripartum)
- Cocaine / amphetamine
- Prior aortic surgery / catheterization
- Age &gt; 60, male

---

## 🗺 Classification — Stanford

| Type | Anatomy | Management |
|---|---|---|
| **A** | **Ascending aorta** involved (± descending) | **Surgery always** |
| **B** | Descending only (distal to left subclavian) | **Medical** (surgery if complicated) |

**"Complicated" Type B = surgery/TEVAR:**
- Rupture / impending rupture
- Malperfusion (limb, visceral, renal, spinal)
- Refractory pain
- Refractory hypertension
- Rapid expansion (&gt; 5 mm / yr or &gt; 10 mm total)

---

## 🧪 Imaging

**First line: CT angiography (CTA) of chest/abdomen/pelvis**
- Fastest, most available
- Shows extent, branch involvement, false lumen

**Alternatives:**
- **TEE** — if hemodynamically unstable (can do at bedside)
- **MRA** — stable patients, follow-up (avoid in acute)

### Labs

- CBC, BMP, lactate
- **Troponin** (MI often concurrent if RCA involved)
- **D-dimer** (sensitivity ~ 97% but non-specific)
- Type and cross 6-10 units
- Coags

### ECG + CXR

- ECG: often normal, may show ischemia (esp inferior if coronary involved)
- CXR: widened mediastinum (&gt; 8 cm), pleural effusion (often left)

---

## 🩸 BP Control — Sequence matters

### Target

- **SBP &lt; 120 mmHg** (lower than usual HT emergency)
- **HR &lt; 60 bpm**
- MAP 60–80

### Step 1 — β-blocker FIRST (always)

**Labetalol** (α + β, good first choice):
- Bolus 10–20 mg IV q 10 min (max 300 mg)
- Drip: 1–2 mg/min (max 10 mg/min)

**Esmolol** (titratable, short half-life):
- Load 500 mcg/kg over 1 min (optional)
- Maintenance 50–300 mcg/kg/min
- Best if β-blocker naïve, comorbid HF

### Step 2 — Vasodilator IF still HT

**Only AFTER β-blockade** (reflex tachy → ↑ shear stress → propagation)

**Nitroprusside:**
- 0.3–2 mcg/kg/min IV
- Watch cyanide toxicity &gt; 24 hr
- Requires arterial line

**Nicardipine:**
- 5–15 mg/hr IV
- Easier titration, less cyanide risk

### ❌ NEVER

- **Vasodilator alone** — reflex tachycardia ↑ shear stress
- Hydralazine (uncontrolled vasodilation)
- Anticoagulation (unless specific indication)
- Fibrinolytics (even if STEMI suspected concurrent)

---

## 🏥 Management by Type

### Type A (ascending) — **Cardiothoracic surgery emergency**

- Consult CT surgery immediately
- Transfer to CT surgery center if not available
- Medical BP control during transport
- Pain control: fentanyl 25–50 mcg IV q 10 min
- Type + cross 6–10 units
- Mortality ~ 1–2% per hour untreated

### Type B uncomplicated — Medical management

- ICU admit
- Target SBP &lt; 120, HR &lt; 60
- IV β-blocker 24–48 hr → PO transition
- Imaging surveillance (CTA q 6 mo initially)

### Type B complicated — TEVAR (endovascular) or open surgery

- Vascular surgery consult
- Same initial medical management

---

## 📊 Monitoring

- **Arterial line** (both arms if asymmetric)
- Continuous ECG, BP
- Urine output q 1 hr (renal malperfusion)
- Neuro check q 15 min (spinal ischemia, stroke)
- Peripheral pulses q 1 hr
- Serial lactate (mesenteric ischemia)

---

## ⚠️ Critical Pitfalls

```
🚩 Tearing chest pain → Aortic dissection until proven otherwise
   - Don't treat as STEMI reflexively
   - Anticoagulation + thrombolysis in dissection = catastrophic

🚩 β-blocker FIRST, then vasodilator
   - Reverse sequence = worse outcomes
   - Reflex tachy = shear stress ↑

🚩 Type A = surgery always, regardless of comorbidity
   - Even elderly, obtain CT surgery opinion
   - Medical alone = ~50% mortality 48 hr

🚩 Pulse asymmetry + chest pain = dissection until imaged
   - Measure both arms
   - Don't dismiss as artifact

🚩 Stroke + chest pain
   - Don't rush to tPA — rule out dissection first
   - tPA in dissection = death

🚩 STEMI in setting of dissection
   - RCA often involved in type A (inferior MI)
   - PCI contraindicated — surgery first
```

## 📝 Copy-Ready Order

```
Dx: Aortic dissection — Stanford [A / B], [uncomplicated / complicated]

1. IV × 2 large-bore, O₂ to SpO₂ ≥ 94%
2. Arterial line (both arms if asymmetric)
3. Labs: CBC, BMP, coags, troponin, D-dimer, lactate
4. Type and cross 6–10 units
5. STAT imaging:
   [ ] CTA chest/abdomen/pelvis (preferred)
   [ ] TEE (if unstable)
6. BP control — β-BLOCKER FIRST:
   - Labetalol 20 mg IV bolus, repeat q 10 min up to 300 mg
   - OR Esmolol 50–300 mcg/kg/min drip
   - Target: SBP &lt; 120, HR &lt; 60
7. Add vasodilator ONLY after adequate β-block:
   - Nicardipine 5–15 mg/hr, OR
   - Nitroprusside 0.3–2 mcg/kg/min
8. Pain control: fentanyl 25–50 mcg IV q 10 min
9. NPO, NG tube if ileus
10. Consult:
    - Type A: CT surgery STAT
    - Type B: Vascular surgery (IR/TEVAR)
11. Do NOT:
    - Give vasodilator alone
    - Anticoagulate
    - tPA for concurrent "stroke/STEMI"
12. ICU admit; transfer if higher level care needed
```
