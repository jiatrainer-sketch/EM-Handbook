## 📊 IDSA / IWGDF Severity Classification

| Grade | Clinical | Systemic | Treatment |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Uninfected** | wound, no sx | No | No abx |
| **Mild** (2) | ≥ 2 local sx (redness, swelling, pain, warmth, discharge) + cellulitis ≤ 2 cm, no deep | No | **Oral abx, outpatient** |
| **Moderate** (3) | deeper than skin (muscle, tendon, bone) OR cellulitis > 2 cm | No | **IV abx, possibly admit** |
| **Severe** (4) | Any local + SIRS (≥ 2 of: T, HR, RR, WBC) | Yes | **IV abx + admit + surgical** |

---

## 🚩 Red Flags

- **Sepsis / hypotension** → [Septic shock](./septic-shock)
- **Rapidly spreading erythema**, crepitus, bullae → **necrotizing fasciitis** — surgical emergency
- **Gas in soft tissue** on X-ray → gas gangrene / nec fasc
- **Deep abscess**, purulent discharge
- **Probe-to-bone positive** → osteomyelitis likely
- **Exposed bone in wound** → osteomyelitis
- **Ischemia** (absent pulse, cyanosis) — wound will not heal without revascularization
- **AKI** (from sepsis or nephrotoxin) — adjust abx

## 🧪 การตรวจเบื้องต้น (Initial Workup)

### Must-do
- **Vital signs** (sepsis screen)
- **Fingerstick glucose** (DKA / hyperglycemia)
- **Wound assessment**:
  - Size (length × width × depth)
  - Depth (probe-to-bone test)
  - Exudate (purulent, serous, malodorous)
  - Surrounding tissue (cellulitis extent, skin changes)
  - Arterial exam (pedal pulses, capillary refill, ABI)
  - Neuropathy check (monofilament 10 g, vibration)

### Labs
- **CBC + differential**
- **ESR / CRP** (osteomyelitis: ESR &gt; 70, CRP persistently ↑)
- **BUN / Cr / electrolytes** (abx dosing)
- **Glucose / HbA1c** (control)
- **Lactate, procalcitonin** if severe
- **Blood culture × 2** if febrile/SIRS
- **Wound culture** (deep tissue sample, not superficial swab)
- **Bone biopsy** (gold standard for osteomyelitis + sensitivity)

### Imaging
- **Plain X-ray** (both feet) — first line
  - Bone erosion, periosteal reaction, sequestrum (lag 2 wk behind clinical)
- **MRI** — gold standard for osteomyelitis, soft tissue abscess
- **CT** (if MRI contraindicated or rapid need)
- **Bone scan / SPECT** (if MRI contraindicated — less specific)
- **Vascular US / ABI** — assess perfusion

## 💊 Empirical Antibiotic Therapy

### Mild (outpatient, no MRSA risk)
- **Dicloxacillin 500 mg PO QID** × 7–14 days
- **Cephalexin 500 mg PO QID** × 7–14 days
- **Clindamycin 300 mg PO QID** × 7–14 days

### Mild + MRSA risk
- **Clindamycin 300 mg PO QID**
- **TMP-SMX DS BID + β-lactam**
- **Doxycycline 100 mg PO BID + β-lactam**

### Moderate (IV, broader coverage)
- **Ampicillin-sulbactam 3 g IV q6h** × 14 days
- **Ceftriaxone 2 g IV + metronidazole 500 mg IV q8h**
- **Clindamycin 600 mg IV q8h + ciprofloxacin 400 mg IV q12h** (if β-lactam allergy)
- **Ertapenem 1 g IV q24h** (simple, no pseudomonas)

### Severe / sepsis
- **Vancomycin 25–30 mg/kg IV load → 15–20 mg/kg q8–12h** (trough 15–20) **+ Piperacillin-tazobactam 4.5 g IV q6–8h**
- **Vancomycin + cefepime + metronidazole** (alternative)
- **Meropenem 1 g IV q8h** (ESBL concern)
- Adjust for renal function
- **Narrow per culture ASAP**

### Osteomyelitis
- Prolonged duration: **4–6 wk IV** (often transition to PO after improvement)
- **6–12 wk total** depending on debridement
- Best chance of cure: surgical debridement + culture-directed abx

## 🏥 การจัดการผู้ป่วย (Disposition)

### Discharge (mild only)
- Close follow-up 2–3 days
- Wound care + offloading
- PCP / surgeon f/u

### Admit
- Moderate or severe
- Failed outpatient Rx
- Sepsis / SIRS
- Deep infection
- Osteomyelitis workup/Rx
- Poor social situation / poor compliance

### ICU
- Sepsis / septic shock
- Necrotizing fasciitis post-op monitoring
- DKA / HHS concomitant

---

## 🔪 Surgical Consultation

### Indications
- Deep abscess (drain)
- Necrotizing fasciitis (emergency debridement)
- Gangrene / non-viable tissue
- Osteomyelitis (debridement/resection)
- Ischemic wound (revascularization — vascular surgery)
- Gas in tissue
- Failed medical Rx

### Amputation considerations
- Unsalvageable limb
- Rapidly progressive + life-threatening
- Chronic non-healing + severe pain + poor function
- Shared decision with patient + multidisciplinary team

---

## 🎯 Adjunct Care

### Glucose control
- Aggressive glycemic control (target 140–180 mg/dL peri-op)
- Often need insulin drip in severe infection + ICU
- Treat DKA / HHS if present

### Offloading
- Non-weight-bearing, total contact cast, crutches, specialized shoe
- Essential for wound healing

### Wound care
- Daily dressing changes
- Remove necrotic tissue (debridement)
- NPWT (wound vac) for complex wounds
- Referral to wound care specialist

### Vascular assessment
- All pts need pulse check ± ABI
- If ischemic (ABI &lt; 0.9) → vascular surgery referral
- Revascularization before major wound healing attempts

### Neuropathy management
- Monofilament test
- Education on foot care (daily inspection, proper shoes)

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Pitfalls)

<Callout type="danger" title="Diabetic foot infection — Pitfalls">

**Missing necrotizing fasciitis**
→ Pain out of proportion, rapid progression, crepitus, bullae
→ Surgery first, don't wait for imaging
→ LRINEC score helps

**"Uncomplicated cellulitis" with osteomyelitis hidden**
→ Always probe-to-bone test
→ X-ray all ulcers > 2 wk duration

**Antibiotic without source control**
→ Drain abscess, debride dead tissue
→ Abx alone won't cure deep infection

**Ignoring ischemia**
→ Wound won't heal despite perfect abx if ABI < 0.5
→ Assess pulses, ABI, consider duplex

**Superficial swab culture**
→ Contaminated with surface flora — misleading
→ Deep tissue sample after debridement

**Stopping abx too early in osteomyelitis**
→ Needs 4–6 wk minimum (longer if inadequate debridement)
→ Follow ESR/CRP trend

**Missing concurrent DKA / HHS**
→ Infection triggers metabolic decompensation
→ Check BG, ketones, ABG, anion gap

</Callout>
