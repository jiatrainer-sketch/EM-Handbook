## 🎯 Presentation

- **Hematemesis** (fresh red or coffee-ground)
- **Melena** (black, tarry, sticky) — UGIB until proven otherwise
- **Hematochezia** — usually LGIB; massive UGIB can present as BRBPR (hemodynamic instability)
- Shock/collapse, syncope, fatigue, anemia
- **Coffee-ground emesis + melena** = UGIB

---

## 🧪 Initial Assessment

### Hemodynamic status FIRST
- **2 large-bore IV (16–18 G)**, O₂, continuous monitoring
- Check: HR, BP, orthostatics, mental status, capillary refill
- **Shock index (HR/SBP) &gt; 1** = significant blood loss

### Labs
- Type & screen / cross-match **2–4 units PRBC**
- CBC, BMP, Coag (PT/INR/aPTT), LFT
- Troponin if risk factors
- Lactate
- ECG

### History clues
- NSAIDs, aspirin, anticoagulation, antiplatelet
- Known ulcer, GERD, H. pylori
- Alcohol (consider variceal)
- Prior bleeding
- Cirrhosis (variceal vs non-variceal)

---

## 📊 Risk Stratification — Glasgow-Blatchford Score (GBS)

| Feature | Points |
|---|---|
| BUN (mg/dL) 18.2–22.4 / 22.4–28 / 28–70 / &gt; 70 | 2 / 3 / 4 / 6 |
| Hb male &lt; 130 / 120 / 100 | 1 / 3 / 6 |
| Hb female &lt; 120 / 100 | 1 / 6 |
| SBP 100–109 / 90–99 / &lt; 90 | 1 / 2 / 3 |
| Pulse ≥ 100 | 1 |
| Melena | 1 |
| Syncope | 2 |
| Hepatic disease | 2 |
| Heart failure | 2 |

**GBS = 0–1**: very low risk — outpatient management possible  
**GBS ≥ 2**: admit, endoscopy within 24 hr

---

## 💊 Initial Management

### Resuscitation

<Callout type="danger" title="Transfusion strategy — restrictive better">

**Target Hb 7–8 g/dL** (TRICC, Villanueva trial — restrictive ↓ mortality)  
Exception: ACS, severe ischemic heart disease → target Hb ≥ 9

- **PRBC**: 1 unit at a time; reassess; don't over-transfuse
- **Platelet**: keep &gt; 50,000 (target &gt; 100k for active bleed with neurosurgery)
- **FFP**: if INR &gt; 2 AND active bleed (reversal of warfarin)
- **Prothrombin complex concentrate (PCC)**: rapid warfarin reversal in severe bleed

</Callout>

### Anticoagulation reversal (if on AC)
| Agent | Reversal |
|---|---|
| Warfarin | Vitamin K 10 mg IV + **4F-PCC** (25–50 U/kg) |
| Dabigatran | **Idarucizumab** 5 g IV (if available); hemodialysis |
| Apixaban/Rivaroxaban | **Andexanet alfa** (if available); PCC 50 U/kg |
| UFH | Protamine 1 mg per 100 U (last 2–3 hr) |
| LMWH | Protamine 1 mg per 1 mg enoxaparin (partial reversal) |
| Antiplatelet | Platelet transfusion (1 apheresis unit) if active bleed |

### PPI (proton pump inhibitor)
- **Pre-endoscopy**: Pantoprazole 80 mg IV bolus → 8 mg/hr infusion (or 40 mg IV q 12 hr high-dose)
- **Post-endoscopy high-risk stigmata** (Forrest Ia/Ib/IIa): PPI infusion × 72 hr → PO BID × 2 weeks → daily
- **Low-risk** stigmata: oral PPI BID

### Prokinetic (before endoscopy)
- **Erythromycin 250 mg IV 30 min pre-EGD** — clears stomach, improves visualization
- Alternative: metoclopramide 10 mg IV

### Octreotide — consider if variceal possible or mixed
- 50 mcg IV bolus → 50 mcg/hr × 3–5 days
- Also in non-variceal if cirrhotic or unclear source

### Empirical antibiotics (if cirrhotic)
- **Ceftriaxone 1 g IV daily × 7 d** — reduces infection + mortality in cirrhotic UGIB

---

## 🔬 Endoscopy

### Timing
- **&lt; 24 hr** for all UGIB
- **&lt; 12 hr** for high-risk (unstable, persistent bleed after resuscitation)
- **&gt; 12 hr delay** if overly delayed may ↑ mortality

### Forrest Classification (bleeding ulcer)
| Class | Finding | Rebleed risk (untreated) |
|---|---|---|
| **Ia** | Active arterial spurting | 90% |
| **Ib** | Active oozing | 10–30% |
| **IIa** | Non-bleeding visible vessel | 50% |
| **IIb** | Adherent clot | 22% |
| **IIc** | Flat pigmented spot | 5–10% |
| **III** | Clean base | &lt; 5% |

### Endoscopic therapy
- **Dual therapy** preferred: injection (epinephrine) + thermal (clips) or mechanical (clips, band)
- For Forrest Ia/Ib/IIa/IIb

---

## 🦠 H. pylori testing + eradication

- **Biopsy** during EGD OR **urea breath test** (after acute)
- Non-responding ulcer / recurrent: eradicate
- Thai first-line: **PAC** (PPI + Amox 1 g + Clarithro 500 mg) BID × 14 d  
  (resistance rising — check local patterns)
- Alternative: **Bismuth quadruple** (PPI + Bismuth + Metro + Tetracycline)

---

## ⚠️ Pitfalls

<Callout type="danger" title="UGIB Pitfalls">

**Over-transfuse** — Hb 10 target → worse outcomes  
→ Target Hb 7–8; 1 unit at a time with reassessment

**Delay endoscopy in unstable patient** — resuscitate first, don't force EGD in shock  
→ Stabilize, then EGD within 12–24 hr

**Miss variceal source in cirrhotic** — presume variceal until proven otherwise  
→ Octreotide + Ceftriaxone for all cirrhotic UGIB

**Forget anticoagulation reversal** — warfarin reversal = PCC, not just vit K  
→ DOAC antidotes if available; PCC as second-best

**Restart anticoagulation wrong** — Most patients should restart within 7 days if indicated  
→ Interruption balance risk (stroke, VTE) vs rebleed

**Underdose PPI** — high-risk stigmata need 72-hr infusion  
→ 80 mg bolus + 8 mg/hr × 72 hr

**Miss Mallory-Weiss / Dieulafoy / malignancy** — consider differential  
→ EGD tells the story

</Callout>

```
UGIB non-variceal — [Date]
Presentation: [hematemesis / melena / hematochezia]
Estimated blood loss: [mL / appearance]
HR__ BP__ SpO₂__% GBS score: __
Meds: [NSAIDs / ASA / AC / antiplatelet]

1. NPO, 2 large IV, O₂ PRN, continuous monitor
2. CBC, BMP, Coag, LFT, T&S, type & cross 2–4 U
3. [Unstable] Activate MTP; PRBC / FFP / platelet
4. Target Hb 7–8 (≥ 9 if ACS)
5. Pantoprazole 80 mg IV bolus → 8 mg/hr infusion
6. Erythromycin 250 mg IV 30 min pre-EGD
7. Anticoagulation reversal (if applicable):
   - Warfarin: Vit K 10 mg IV + 4F-PCC 25 U/kg
   - DOAC: antidote if available
   - Platelet transfusion if antiplatelet + severe bleed
8. Octreotide 50 mcg bolus → 50 mcg/hr (if cirrhosis / unclear)
9. Ceftriaxone 1 g IV daily × 7 d (if cirrhotic)
10. GI consult STAT for EGD < 24 hr (< 12 hr if unstable)
11. Admit [monitored bed / ICU]
12. Post-EGD: PPI 72-hr infusion if Forrest Ia/Ib/IIa/IIb
    Then PO BID × 2 wk → daily; H. pylori testing
```
