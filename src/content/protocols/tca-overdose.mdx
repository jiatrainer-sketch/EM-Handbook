## 🎯 When to suspect

- History of ingestion (amitriptyline, nortriptyline, imipramine, doxepin, clomipramine)
- **Classic triad:** altered mental status + seizure + widened QRS
- Anticholinergic toxidrome (dry/hot/red/blind/mad)
- Hypotension, tachycardia, arrhythmia
- Early: drowsiness, agitation
- Late: coma, seizure, cardiac arrest

### Time course

- Symptoms develop within **1–2 hr**
- Severity peaks **6 hr** post-ingestion
- Death usually within first 24 hr
- Decontamination window: **&lt; 1 hr** (activated charcoal)

---

## 🧠 Mechanism of toxicity

- **Fast Na channel blockade** (cardiac) → **widened QRS, arrhythmia, hypotension**
- **Anticholinergic** (muscarinic block) → dry mouth, mydriasis, urinary retention, delirium, hyperthermia
- **α₁ block** → orthostatic hypotension
- **H₁ block** → sedation
- **GABA antagonism** → **seizure**
- **Biogenic amine reuptake** → early agitation

---

## 📈 ECG = Key diagnostic + prognostic tool

### Cardiac signs of toxicity

| Finding | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **QRS &gt; 100 ms** | Toxicity — start bicarb |
| **QRS &gt; 160 ms** | Ventricular arrhythmia risk |
| **R wave in aVR &gt; 3 mm** | Classic, prognostic |
| **R/S ratio in aVR &gt; 0.7** | Classic, prognostic |
| Right axis shift (terminal 40 ms) | Na-blocking sign |
| Sinus tachycardia | Most common early sign |
| Wide-complex tachycardia | Severe toxicity |

### Other

- Electrolytes: K often low (risk arrhythmia)
- ABG: mixed respiratory + metabolic acidosis

---

## 💊 Immediate Actions

### 1. ABC + monitoring

- Intubate if GCS &lt; 8, airway loss
- Continuous ECG, SpO₂, BP
- Large-bore IV × 2
- Labs: CBC, BMP, coags, lactate, **acetaminophen + salicylate level** (co-ingestion)
- ECG STAT, repeat q 1 hr or if symptoms change

### 2. Sodium bicarbonate (cornerstone)

**Indications:**
- QRS &gt; 100 ms
- Ventricular arrhythmia
- Hypotension refractory to fluid
- Severe acidosis

**Dose:**
- **Bolus: 1–2 mEq/kg IV** (50–100 mL of 8.4% NaHCO₃)
- **Drip: 150 mEq + D5W 1 L at 150–250 mL/hr**
- **Target:** pH **7.45–7.55** (serum pH)
- **Target:** QRS narrowing (&lt; 100 ms) + hemodynamic improvement

**Mechanism:**
- Increases serum Na → overcomes Na channel block
- Alkalosis → ↑ protein binding of TCA (inactive)

**Monitor:**
- ABG q 1 hr (target pH 7.45–7.55; max 7.55)
- Na (watch hypernatremia), K (may drop with alkalosis), glucose
- ECG continuous

### 3. Seizure — benzos FIRST

**Diazepam 5–10 mg IV** OR **Lorazepam 2–4 mg IV** q 5–10 min

**Second line:** Phenobarbital 15–20 mg/kg IV (or propofol)

**❌ AVOID phenytoin**
- Same mechanism (Na channel block) = worsens cardiotoxicity
- Classic pitfall

### 4. Hypotension

- **NSS 1–2 L IV bolus** (first)
- Persistent: **NE** first-line vasopressor (direct α₁)
- Avoid: dopamine (partial agonist, blunted response from α₁ block)

### 5. Decontamination

- **Activated charcoal 1 g/kg PO/NG** if:
  - Within 1 hr of ingestion
  - Awake + airway protected (or intubated)
- NOT helpful &gt; 2 hr or if obtunded without intubation (aspiration)

### 6. Refractory cardiotoxicity

- **Lipid emulsion 20%**: 1.5 mL/kg bolus → 0.25 mL/kg/min × 10 min
- **ECMO** (VA) as bridge (specialized centers)
- **Continue CPR** prolonged if arrest (bicarb-responsive)

---

## 📊 การติดตาม (Monitoring)

- **Continuous ECG** — QRS width q 15 min until improving
- ABG q 1 hr (bicarb titration)
- BMP q 2–4 hr (Na, K, glucose)
- Mental status GCS q 1 hr
- BP q 5–15 min
- **Minimum 6 hr observation** if asymptomatic + normal ECG
- **24 hr ICU** if any toxicity signs

---

## 🚪 Disposition

| Findings at 6 hr | Disposition |
|---|---|
| Normal mental status + normal ECG + asymptomatic | Psych consult, medical clearance |
| Any toxicity (QRS &gt; 100, seizure, hypotension, AMS) | **ICU admit** |
| Severe (intubated, on pressors, refractory) | **ICU + toxicology consult** |

---

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Critical Pitfalls)

<Callout type="danger" title="Tricyclic Antidepressant (TCA) Overdose — Pitfalls">

**QRS &gt; 100 ms = toxic — start bicarb**
→ Don't wait for arrhythmia
→ ECG q 1 hr minimum

**Phenytoin for TCA seizure = worse outcomes**
→ Shared Na-block mechanism
→ Use BENZOS first, phenobarb second

**Don't use Class IA/IC antiarrhythmics**
→ Procainamide, quinidine, flecainide = worsen Na block
→ Amiodarone OK if needed (class III dominant)

**Physostigmine — generally avoid**
→ Old teaching for anticholinergic
→ Risk asystole, seizure in TCA
→ Benzos + supportive = safer

**Co-ingestion check**
→ Acetaminophen level (4-hr post-ingestion)
→ Salicylate level
→ Ethanol

**Activated charcoal only if awake + &lt; 1 hr**
→ Or intubated
→ Aspiration risk in obtunded = death

**Observation 6 hr if asymptomatic + normal ECG**
→ If develops any sign → ICU admission
→ Psych consult before discharge

**Beware "recovery" followed by sudden arrest**
→ Delayed cardiotoxicity possible
→ Keep monitored 24 hr if any signs

</Callout>

## 📝 คำสั่งพร้อมใช้ (Copy-Ready Order)

```
Dx: Tricyclic antidepressant overdose, [mild / moderate / severe]

1. ABC: intubate if GCS &lt; 8
2. IV × 2 large-bore; 2-lead monitor + 12-lead ECG STAT
3. Labs: CBC, BMP, coags, lactate, acetaminophen, salicylate, EtOH
4. ECG: check QRS width, aVR R wave, rhythm
5. ACTIVATED CHARCOAL 1 g/kg PO/NG if &lt; 1 hr + awake or intubated
6. SODIUM BICARBONATE (if QRS &gt; 100, arrhythmia, hypotension, acidosis):
   - Bolus 1–2 mEq/kg IV (50–100 mL 8.4%)
   - Drip 150 mEq + D5W 1 L at 150–250 mL/hr
   - Target pH 7.45–7.55, QRS &lt; 100
7. SEIZURE:
   - Diazepam 10 mg IV q 5 min (or lorazepam 4 mg)
   - Second: phenobarbital 15–20 mg/kg IV
   - DO NOT USE PHENYTOIN
8. HYPOTENSION:
   - NSS 1–2 L IV bolus
   - Norepinephrine 0.05 mcg/kg/min titrate (α₁)
9. REFRACTORY:
   - Lipid emulsion 20% 1.5 mL/kg bolus → 0.25 mL/kg/min × 10 min
   - ECMO if available (VA)
10. Continuous ECG, ABG q 1 hr, BMP q 4 hr
11. ICU admit (any toxicity); 6-hr observation if completely normal
12. Psych consult post medical clearance
13. Poison control / toxicology consult
```
