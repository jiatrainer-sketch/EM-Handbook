## 🎯 Wound Classification

### Clean minor wound
- Small, superficial
- Minimal contamination
- Well-vascularized
- &lt; 6 hr old

### Tetanus-prone wound (any one)
- Contaminated (soil, saliva, feces)
- Puncture wounds (esp. deep, narrow)
- Devitalized tissue, ischemia
- Burns, frostbite, crush injuries
- &gt; 6 hr since injury
- Avulsion, missile injuries
- Signs of sepsis / infection

---

## 💉 Prophylaxis Decision Table

| Vaccination History | **Clean Minor** | **Tetanus-Prone** |
|---|---|---|
| **Unknown or &lt; 3 doses** | **Td/Tdap** | **Td/Tdap + TIG** |
| **≥ 3 doses, last ≥ 10 yr ago** | **Td/Tdap** | **Td/Tdap** |
| **≥ 3 doses, last 5–10 yr ago** | None | **Td/Tdap** |
| **≥ 3 doses, last &lt; 5 yr ago** | None | None |

<Callout type="info" title="Td vs Tdap vs TT">

- **Tdap** — Tetanus, diphtheria, pertussis (preferred for adults in US; now in Thai adult schedule)
- **Td** — Tetanus, diphtheria (adult booster; still common in Thailand)
- **TT** — Tetanus toxoid only (older, only if above unavailable)
- Use **Tdap** at least once in adult life; then Td q 10 years

</Callout>

### TIG (Tetanus Immunoglobulin) — passive immunity
- **250–500 IU IM** (single dose)
- Different site from vaccine
- For tetanus-prone wounds + inadequate vaccination

## 🩹 Wound Care Principles

<Callout type="success" title="Wound cleaning + debridement = primary prevention">

1. **Copious irrigation** with NSS (at least 200 mL)
2. **Debride** devitalized tissue
3. **Remove foreign bodies**
4. **Delayed primary closure** for heavily contaminated
5. **Tetanus prophylaxis** + consider antibiotics (see cellulitis)
6. **Rabies prophylaxis** if animal bite + exposure

</Callout>

### Antibiotic (if indicated)
- **Amox-clav** 875/125 PO BID × 5–7 d (human/animal bite)
- **Cephalexin** + doxycycline (animal bite, PCN allergy)
- Tetanus-prone + antibiotics: per CDC **not routine** — focus on wound care + TIG

## 🦠 Clinical Tetanus (rare with good prophylaxis)

### Presentation
- **Trismus** (lockjaw) — often first sign
- **Risus sardonicus** (facial muscle spasm)
- **Opisthotonos** (arched back)
- **Generalized tonic spasms** provoked by stimuli
- Autonomic dysfunction (tachycardia, HTN, fever, sweating)
- Respiratory failure (diaphragm spasm)

### Treatment (ICU)
1. **Wound debridement** (remove spore source)
2. **Metronidazole** 500 mg IV q 6 hr × 7–10 d (preferred over penicillin)
3. **TIG 500 IU IM** + **3,000–6,000 IU IM infiltrated around wound** (if possible)
4. **Active immunization** (Tdap) — past tetanus doesn't confer immunity
5. **Spasm control**:
   - Benzodiazepines (diazepam, midazolam) — high dose
   - Baclofen intrathecal
   - Magnesium sulfate 40 mg/kg IV loading (helps autonomic + spasm)
6. **Intubation** + paralysis if severe
7. **Autonomic instability**: β-blocker (labetalol), α₂-agonist (dexmedetomidine), magnesium
8. **Stimulus-free quiet environment**

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Pitfalls)

<Callout type="danger" title="Tetanus Pitfalls">

**Forget TIG in unvaccinated + tetanus-prone** — vaccine takes weeks for immunity  
→ TIG + vaccine together (different sites)

**"All wounds need vaccine"** — not always  
→ Use decision table; clean minor with adequate vaccination = no additional dose

**Assume "it's just a cut"** — deep puncture, soil, rust = tetanus-prone  
→ Ask about depth, mechanism, contamination

**Only give TT** — missing opportunity for pertussis booster (adults)  
→ Tdap at least once in adult life

**Clinical tetanus without ICU** — respiratory failure from diaphragm spasm  
→ Early intubation; ICU for autonomic management

**Inadequate wound debridement** — spores persist → continued toxin  
→ Thorough surgical debridement essential

</Callout>
