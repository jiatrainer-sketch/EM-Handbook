## 🎯 Classification

### Non-purulent (Cellulitis / Erysipelas)
- **Cellulitis**: poorly demarcated, warm, erythema, swelling, tender
- **Erysipelas**: sharply demarcated raised border, bright red (superficial dermis/lymphatic)
- Usually **Streptococcus** (group A, B, C, G)

### Purulent (Abscess, Furuncle, Carbuncle)
- Fluctuant mass, ± surrounding cellulitis
- **Staph aureus** (MSSA or MRSA) dominant

### Necrotizing (STAT!)

<Callout type="danger" title="Necrotizing fasciitis = surgical emergency">

**Red flags:**
- Pain **out of proportion** to exam, or **anesthesia** in affected area
- Rapid progression (hours)
- Bullae, skin necrosis, crepitus, foul-smelling exudate
- Systemic toxicity (fever, AMS, hypotension)
- Hx DM, IVDU, immunosuppression, recent surgery

**LRINEC score** (CRP, WBC, Hb, Na, Cr, glucose) — ≥ 6 = consider; ≥ 8 = likely  
→ Imaging (CT for gas) BUT **do not delay surgery for imaging**  
→ **Surgical consult + broad-spectrum abx STAT**

</Callout>

## 🧪 การตรวจวินิจฉัย (Workup)

### Uncomplicated cellulitis
- Often no labs needed (outpatient)
- Mark borders with pen; photograph if possible

### Moderate/severe or complicated
- CBC, BMP, CRP, lactate
- **Blood cultures × 2** if febrile / systemic
- Wound culture if drainage, abscess
- CT/MRI if necrotizing suspected, foreign body, deep abscess
- Pregnancy test if antibiotic choice affects

## 💊 การรักษา (Treatment)

### Non-purulent, mild-moderate (no MRSA risk, outpatient)

| Option | Dose |
|---|---|
| **Cephalexin** | 500 mg PO QID × 7–10 d |
| **Dicloxacillin** | 500 mg PO QID × 7–10 d |
| **Clindamycin** | 300–450 mg PO QID (if PCN allergy) |

### Non-purulent, severe / systemic (inpatient)

| Option | Dose |
|---|---|
| **Cefazolin** | 2 g IV q 8 hr |
| **Ceftriaxone** | 2 g IV q 24 hr |
| **Clindamycin** | 600–900 mg IV q 8 hr (PCN allergy) |

**Convert to PO once improving**; total duration 5–14 d

### Purulent — I&D is primary Rx

- **Small abscess (&lt; 5 cm) + no systemic**: I&D alone, no abx needed
- **Large / systemic / immunocompromised**: I&D + abx

**MRSA coverage** when purulent or risk factors:
| Option | Dose |
|---|---|
| **TMP/SMX (Bactrim)** | DS 1–2 tab PO BID |
| **Doxycycline** | 100 mg PO BID |
| **Clindamycin** | 300–450 mg PO QID (check local sensitivity) |
| **Linezolid** | 600 mg PO/IV BID (severe) |
| **Vancomycin** | 25 mg/kg load → 15–20 mg/kg q 8–12 hr (severe inpatient) |

### Necrotizing fasciitis — empirical
- **Pip-tazo 4.5 g IV q 6 hr + Vancomycin 25–30 mg/kg load + Clindamycin 900 mg IV q 8 hr**
- Clindamycin: **toxin suppression** (group A Strep exotoxin)
- **Immediate surgical debridement** — repeat as needed
- IVIG considered for Strep TSS

### Special scenarios

**Freshwater exposure** (lake, pond):
- *Aeromonas* — Ciprofloxacin or Doxycycline + cephalosporin

**Saltwater / raw seafood**:
- *Vibrio vulnificus* — Doxycycline + Ceftriaxone  
  (cirrhotics esp. vulnerable)

**Human bite**:
- *Eikenella, oral flora* — Amox-clav 875/125 BID × 7–10 d

**Animal bite** (dog/cat):
- *Pasteurella* — Amox-clav; rabies assessment + tetanus; rabies PEP if risk

## 🦠 Thai-Specific Considerations

<Callout type="info" title="Melioidosis in NE Thailand">

- *Burkholderia pseudomallei* — soil/water exposure, esp. **rice farmers, DM**
- Can present as cellulitis, abscess, pneumonia, sepsis
- **Ceftazidime 2 g IV q 6 hr OR Meropenem 1 g IV q 8 hr** for 10–14 d (acute), then eradication 3 mo
- Consider in diabetic farmer with cellulitis not responding to standard abx

</Callout>

## 🏥 การจัดการผู้ป่วย (Disposition)

### Outpatient
- Mild, non-systemic, can tolerate PO
- Reliable follow-up in 24–48 hr
- Mark borders for tracking

### Inpatient
- Systemic symptoms (fever, sepsis)
- Failed outpatient abx
- Facial cellulitis, orbital cellulitis
- Diabetic foot with spread
- Immunocompromised
- Unable to tolerate PO

### ICU
- Septic shock
- Necrotizing fasciitis
- Severe organ dysfunction

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Pitfalls)

<Callout type="danger" title="Cellulitis Pitfalls">

**Miss necrotizing fasciitis** — presentation can be subtle initially  
→ Pain out of proportion, rapid progression, systemic toxicity = surgical consult

**Treat DVT or venous stasis as cellulitis** — bilateral, symmetric, no warmth  
→ Stasis dermatitis is not infection; compression, elevation

**ไม่ I&D abscess** — antibiotics alone don't cure closed abscess  
→ I&D is the primary treatment; abx adjunct only

**Miss MRSA in purulent** — use clindamycin/doxy/Bactrim, not amox-clav  
→ Check local antibiogram for resistance patterns

**Facial cellulitis + unilateral** — R/O orbital cellulitis, cavernous sinus thrombosis, dental abscess  
→ CT orbit, ENT/dental consult

**Diabetic foot cellulitis** — osteomyelitis may underlie  
→ Probe to bone, X-ray, MRI if deep ulcer

</Callout>

```
Cellulitis — [limb/site]
Extent: [marked borders at __]
Systemic: [fever__ HR__ BP__ WBC__]
Purulent: [y/n — I&D if yes]
Risk factors: [DM, immunocompromise, MRSA hx, recent abx, exposure]

Workup:
- CBC, BMP, CRP, lactate
- Blood culture × 2 if febrile
- Wound culture if drainage

Treatment:
1. [If non-purulent, outpatient] Cephalexin 500 mg PO QID × 7–10 d
2. [If inpatient severe] Cefazolin 2 g IV q 8 hr
3. [If MRSA risk] add Vancomycin 25 mg/kg load
4. [If necrotizing] Pip-tazo + Vanco + Clindamycin + STAT surgery
5. Elevation, warm compress, NSAID PRN
6. Mark borders, re-eval 24 hr
7. Tetanus + rabies if indicated
8. Follow-up 48 hr OR return if worsening
```
