## 🎯 Indication / Role

### Use for

- **Symptomatic bradycardia** refractory to atropine
  (bridge while awaiting TCP / transvenous pacing)
- **Cardiogenic shock** (less preferred now — NE + dobutamine preferred)
- **Hypotension** when NE not available (alternative only)

### Less preferred than NE for shock because

- More arrhythmogenic (tachy, PVC, VT)
- Higher mortality in septic shock (SOAP II trial)
- Complex dose-response (3 receptor zones)

Still useful in **bradycardia + hypotension** combo

### Mechanism (dose-dependent)

| Dose (mcg/kg/min) | Predominant effect |
|---|---|
| 1–5 | **DA₁**: renal/mesenteric vasodilation (benefit disputed) |
| 5–10 | **β₁**: inotrope, mild chronotrope — ↑ HR, contractility |
| 10–20 | **α₁**: vasoconstrictor — ↑ BP (+ β₁ still) |
| &gt; 20 | Pure α₁ — NE is better at this point |

---

## 💊 Preparation (standard protocols)

### Standard prep: **200 mg + D5W 250 mL = 800 mcg/mL**

```
Dopamine 200 mg (1 amp, common in Thai hospitals) + D5W 250 mL
= 200,000 mcg / 250 mL
= 800 mcg/mL
```

### Concentrated prep: **400 mg + D5W 250 mL = 1,600 mcg/mL**

Use with fluid restriction or high dose requirement

```
Dopamine 400 mg + D5W 250 mL = 1,600 mcg/mL
```

### Compatibility

- **OK with D5W, NSS, D5 ½ NSS, RL**
- **INCOMPATIBLE with alkaline solutions** (bicarb, aminophylline, ampicillin)
- Use dedicated line or compatible Y-site

## ⚡ Dosing (BW 60 kg)

### Conversion table @ **BW 60 kg** + **prep 800 mcg/mL (standard)**

| Dose (mcg/kg/min) | mcg/min | mL/hr | Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2 | 120 | 9 | DA₁ (renal) |
| **5 (start inotrope)** | **300** | **22** | β₁ inotrope |
| 7.5 | 450 | 34 | β₁ + α₁ transition |
| **10** | **600** | **45** | β₁ + α₁ |
| 15 | 900 | 68 | α₁ vasoconstrictor |
| 20 | 1,200 | 90 | α₁ max (consider switch to NE) |

**Formula:** mL/hr = (dose × weight × 60) / concentration  
- @ 5 × 60 × 60 / 800 = **22.5 mL/hr** ≈ 22

### Starting dose

- **Bradycardia**: start **5 mcg/kg/min** (= 300 mcg/min = 22 mL/hr)
- **Cardiogenic shock**: start **5 mcg/kg/min**, titrate
- **Bridge to pacing**: same start, titrate to HR ≥ 50 + SBP ≥ 90

### Titration

- **Assess q 5–10 min**
- **Increase by 2–5 mcg/kg/min** per adjustment
- Target: HR ≥ 50, SBP ≥ 90, MAP ≥ 65 (shock), mental improvement
- **Maximum typically 20 mcg/kg/min**

### When to switch

**Switch to NE if:**
- Reaching &gt; 10–15 mcg/kg/min without adequate MAP
- Tachy &gt; 120 / new arrhythmia
- Pure vasopressor effect needed

**Add dobutamine if:**
- Low cardiac output + adequate preload
- Cold-wet shock

### Weaning

- Reduce by 2–5 mcg/kg/min q 15–30 min
- Watch for rebound hypo/bradycardia
- Wean faster if definitive treatment (pacing, PCI) achieved

---

## 🔌 Access

### **Central line preferred** for **&gt; 10 mcg/kg/min**

- High-dose vasoconstrictor effect = extravasation risk
- Sustained use (&gt; 6 hr)
- Concentrated prep (400 mg / 250 mL)

### **Peripheral acceptable** for

- Low-dose bridge (&lt; 10 mcg/kg/min)
- Short duration (&lt; 6 hr)
- Standard prep (800 mcg/mL)
- Large proximal vein, 18G
- Watch site q 1 hr

### Extravasation treatment

Same as norepinephrine:
1. Stop infusion, aspirate
2. **Phentolamine 5–10 mg in 10 mL NSS** subcutaneous around site
3. Warm compress, photograph
4. Remove IV after antidote given

---

## 📊 Monitoring

**Continuous:**
- **ECG** (arrhythmia — main concern)
- BP (arterial line preferred &gt; 10 mcg/kg/min)
- SpO₂, RR

**Hourly:**
- HR, rhythm strip
- Urine output (target &gt; 0.5 mL/kg/hr)
- Mental status
- Peripheral perfusion

**Q 2–4 hr:**
- Electrolytes (K, Mg — affect arrhythmia risk)
- Lactate
- Digit perfusion

**Watch for:**
- **Tachyarrhythmia** (#1 dose-limiter)
- **PVC / VT**
- **Ischemia** (MI, stroke) — ↑ myocardial O₂ demand
- **Extravasation**
- **Nausea / vomiting**

---

## ⚠️ Side Effects

**Common (dose-dependent):**
- **Tachycardia** (30–40%)
- **PVC / atrial/ventricular ectopy**
- Nausea, vomiting, headache
- Anxiety

**Serious:**
- **Ventricular tachycardia / fibrillation**
- **Myocardial ischemia / infarction**
- **Peripheral / mesenteric ischemia** (high dose)
- **Digital ischemia / gangrene**
- **Extravasation necrosis**
- **Tissue necrosis at injection site**

---

## 🫘 Renal / 🧓 Elderly / Special populations

**Renal (CKD):**
- ไม่ต้องปรับ dose
- "Renal-dose" dopamine (1–3 mcg/kg/min) **ไม่แนะนำ** — ไม่มี benefit ต่อ kidney (multiple RCTs negative)

**Elderly:**
- **High arrhythmia risk** — start low (3–5 mcg/kg/min)
- Higher MI risk
- Consider NE first if sepsis

**Heart failure:**
- ใช้ได้ถ้า bradycardia + low output
- แต่ afterload + HR ↑ → myocardial O₂ demand ↑
- Consider dobutamine เพิ่ม (inotrope without ↑ afterload)

**Pheochromocytoma:**
- **Contraindicated** (precipitate hypertensive crisis + arrhythmia)

**MAO inhibitor user:**
- ↑ effect 10-fold → start at **1/10 usual dose**
- Risk hypertensive crisis + arrhythmia

**Pregnancy:**
- Category C
- NE usually preferred in pregnant shock

## ⚠️ Critical Pitfalls

```
🚩 Tachyarrhythmia = main dose limiter
   - HR > 120, new PVC/VT → reduce or switch
   - Correct K, Mg first

🚩 "Renal-dose" dopamine = no benefit
   - 1–3 mcg/kg/min for "renal protection" = debunked
   - No effect on dialysis need or mortality
   - Don't use for this indication

🚩 Central line for > 10 mcg/kg/min
   - Lower dose peripheral OK short-term
   - Extravasation → necrosis

🚩 Extravasation = phentolamine
   - Same as NE protocol
   - Phentolamine 5–10 mg subq around site

🚩 Pheochromocytoma contraindicated
   - Hypertensive crisis + arrhythmia
   - Ask hx before giving (rare but catastrophic)

🚩 MAO inhibitor = 10x dose effect
   - Start at 1/10 usual
   - Or avoid, use NE

🚩 Don't mix with bicarbonate
   - Alkaline = degrades dopamine
   - Separate line

🚩 Switch to NE if > 10–15 mcg/kg/min
   - NE = cleaner vasoconstrictor
   - Less arrhythmia
   - Better survival in septic shock

🚩 Bradycardia bridge, not definitive
   - Must arrange TCP / TVP / PCI for inferior MI
   - Dopamine doesn't fix AV block

🚩 Check rhythm ก่อนเริ่ม
   - 12-lead ECG
   - ระบุ type of block (Mobitz II / 3° block = TCP ทันที)
   - Dopamine อาจช่วยเพียง bridge

🚩 β-blocker user = blunted response
   - May need higher dose
   - Or use glucagon (β-blocker OD bradycardia)
```

## 📝 Copy-Ready Order

```
INDICATION (check one):
[ ] Symptomatic bradycardia refractory to atropine, bridge to pacing
[ ] Cardiogenic shock (low output + hypotension)
[ ] Hypotension, NE not available

1. Monitor ECG continuous, BP q 5 min, SpO₂
2. 12-lead ECG — identify rhythm (esp type of block)
3. IV access — central line preferred for dose > 10 mcg/kg/min
4. DOPAMINE DRIP:
   - Prep: Dopamine 200 mg + D5W 250 mL = 800 mcg/mL
   - Start: 5 mcg/kg/min = 300 mcg/min = 22 mL/hr @ BW 60 kg
   - Titrate q 5–10 min by 2–5 mcg/kg/min
   - Target: HR ≥ 50, SBP ≥ 90, MAP ≥ 65
   - Max typically 20 mcg/kg/min
5. Correct electrolytes first (reduce arrhythmia risk):
   - K 4.0–4.5
   - Mg 2.0
6. Labs: BMP, Mg, troponin, lactate, VBG
7. Switch to NOREPINEPHRINE if:
   - > 10–15 mcg/kg/min without adequate MAP
   - Tachy > 120 or new arrhythmia
8. ADD DOBUTAMINE if:
   - Cold/wet shock (low CO + adequate preload)
   - Congestive features
9. Definitive treatment (don't just run dopamine):
   - Bradycardia → TCP → TVP (consult cardiology STAT)
   - Cardiogenic shock → PCI, echo, inotrope optimization
   - Septic shock → source control, NE preferred
10. Wean: reduce 2–5 mcg/kg/min q 15–30 min once stable
11. Watch extravasation — phentolamine bedside
12. Admit ICU / CCU
```
