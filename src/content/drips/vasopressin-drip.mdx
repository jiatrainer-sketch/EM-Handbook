## 🎯 Indication / Role

### Primary use

- **Septic shock adjunct** — add to norepinephrine when NE ≥ 0.25 mcg/kg/min
- **Catecholamine-sparing** — reduces NE requirement, potentially ↓ arrhythmia
- **Refractory vasoplegia** (vasoplegic shock post-bypass, ACE-inhibitor)

### Not first-line

- **Do not use as sole vasopressor**
- **Not for cardiogenic shock** (no inotropy)
- **Not for hypovolemia** (fix volume first)
- Limited evidence in hemorrhagic shock

### Mechanism

- **V1a receptor** (vascular smooth muscle) → vasoconstriction
- **V2 receptor** (renal collecting duct) → water retention
- Works in acidosis (catecholamines fail)
- Synergistic with NE (different receptor)

---

## 💊 การเตรียมยา (Preparation)

### Standard mix

```
Vasopressin 20 U + NSS 100 mL
= 200 milliUnits (mU) / mL
= 0.2 U / mL
```

### Alternative (D5W or NSS, both compatible)

- Via central line preferred (vasoconstrictor)
- Peripheral OK briefly (less vasoactive than NE)

---

## ⚡ Dosing — **FIXED DOSE, do not titrate**

### Standard dose

- **0.03 U/min (= 1.8 U/hr)**
- Equivalent: **9 mL/hr** of standard mix (0.2 U/mL)

### Rarely used higher

- **0.04 U/min** = 12 mL/hr (if MAP still inadequate, though evidence weak)
- Do NOT exceed 0.04 U/min — risk digital/mesenteric ischemia

### When to start

- **NE requirement &gt; 0.25 mcg/kg/min** (Surviving Sepsis)
- Add as second agent (do not replace NE)
- Continue NE; wean NE first when improving

### Weaning

- **Stop last** (after NE is weaned off)
- Some protocols: stop at fixed time, observe for rebound
- No clear evidence-based weaning protocol
- Practical: wean NE to off, then stop vasopressin

---

## 📊 การติดตาม (Monitoring)

- **MAP** — target &gt; 65
- Lactate trend
- Urine output (target &gt; 0.5 mL/kg/hr)
- Digit perfusion (ischemia risk with high dose)
- Mesenteric signs (abdominal pain, lactate)
- **Na (water retention risk)**
- CVP trend

### Expected effect

- NE requirement should decrease ~ 0.05–0.10 mcg/kg/min within 30–60 min
- If no response in 60 min: consider weaning, reassess

---

## 🚨 Contraindications

- **Coronary artery disease** (severe) — vasoconstriction ↑ ischemia
- **Mesenteric ischemia** (active) — worsens
- **Raynaud / digital ischemia** — worsens

### Relative caution

- Heart failure (preload-dependent, vasopressin ↑ afterload)
- Hyponatremia (V2 effect → further water retention)
- Cirrhosis + ascites (similar V2 concern)

---

## ⚠️ ผลข้างเคียง (Side Effects)

- **Digital / mesenteric / coronary ischemia** (dose-dependent)
- Hyponatremia (V2 water retention)
- Skin necrosis if extravasation
- Arrhythmia (less than catecholamines)
- Cardiac arrest (high dose, rare)

---

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Critical Pitfalls)

```
🚩 Fixed dose — do NOT titrate up
   - 0.03 U/min standard
   - &gt; 0.04 U/min = ischemia risk ↑ with no benefit

🚩 Not first-line vasopressor
   - NE first; add vasopressin when NE ≥ 0.25 mcg/kg/min
   - Do not replace NE

🚩 Central line preferred
   - Peripheral OK brief, but risk skin necrosis if extravasation

🚩 Watch digit / mesenteric perfusion
   - Capillary refill + abdomen exam q 1 hr
   - Elevated lactate unexplained = reassess

🚩 Wean NE first, then vasopressin
   - Stopping vasopressin while on high NE can cause rebound hypotension
   - Wean in reverse order of start

🚩 Hyponatremia watch
   - V2 water retention
   - Na q 6 hr if prolonged use
```

## 📝 คำสั่งพร้อมใช้ (Copy-Ready Order)

```
Indication: Septic shock on NE ≥ 0.25 mcg/kg/min

1. VASOPRESSIN DRIP:
   - Prep: Vasopressin 20 U + NSS 100 mL (0.2 U/mL)
   - Dose: 0.03 U/min = 9 mL/hr (FIXED, do not titrate)
   - Central line preferred
2. Continue norepinephrine — vasopressin is adjunct
3. Target MAP ≥ 65 mmHg
4. Monitor:
   - Continuous ECG, BP (arterial line preferred)
   - Urine output q 1 hr
   - Na q 6 hr
   - Digit perfusion + abdomen q 1 hr
   - Lactate q 4 hr
5. Wean:
   - Wean NE first (reduce q 15–30 min)
   - Stop vasopressin AFTER NE is off
6. Switch triggers:
   - Worsening lactate / digital ischemia → reduce to 0.03 or stop
   - No improvement in 60 min → reassess diagnosis, source control
7. ICU admit
```
