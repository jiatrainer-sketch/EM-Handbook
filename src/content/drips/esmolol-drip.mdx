## 🎯 Indication / Role

### Use for

- **Rapid β-blockade needed with titratability**
- **Aortic dissection** — β-block first, especially in β-naïve or HF risk
- **Hypertensive emergency** in setting of tachyarrhythmia
- **Perioperative HT / tachycardia**
- **Thyroid storm** (adjunct β-block)
- **Rate control** in AF with RVR (short trial)
- **Cocaine / stimulant-induced HT** — titratable, can stop quickly if issue

### Mechanism

- **β₁-selective, cardioselective** (relatively; β₂ spared at low dose)
- Ultra-short acting: **half-life ~9 min**
- Metabolized by RBC esterases (not liver/kidney)
- Onset: 1–2 min; offset: 10–20 min after stopping

### Why esmolol vs others

- **Aggressive titration safe** (can stop fast if problem)
- **β-naïve / HF risk patients** — titrate gently
- **Refractory HT** — adjunct before longer-acting

---

## 💊 การเตรียมยา (Preparation)

### Standard mix

```
Esmolol 2,500 mg (2.5 g) + NSS 250 mL
= 10 mg/mL
```

- Available as premixed bags (2.5 g/250 mL)
- Central line preferred for sustained use (ease of higher rates)
- Peripheral OK for short duration

### Alternative concentrations

- 5 g + NSS 500 mL = 10 mg/mL (larger volume)
- 2.5 g + NSS 500 mL = 5 mg/mL (lower concentration, less CHF fluid)

---

## ⚡ Dosing @ BW 60 kg

### Loading (optional, often SKIPPED)

- **500 mcg/kg IV over 1 min** = 30 mg = 3 mL of 10 mg/mL
- Useful for rapid effect; skip if hemodynamically marginal
- Can repeat if needed

### Maintenance — titrate aggressively

| Dose (mcg/kg/min) | mcg/min | mL/hr (10 mg/mL) |
|---|---|---|
| **50 (start)** | 3,000 | **18** |
| 100 | 6,000 | 36 |
| 150 | 9,000 | 54 |
| 200 | 12,000 | 72 |
| 250 | 15,000 | 90 |
| **300 (max)** | 18,000 | 108 |

**Formula:** mL/hr = (dose × BW × 60) / concentration
- @ 50 × 60 × 60 / 10,000 = **18 mL/hr**

### Titration

- Start 50 mcg/kg/min (or load 500 mcg/kg + 50 maintenance)
- Reassess q 5 min
- Increase by 50 mcg/kg/min (often with a mini-load bolus)
- Max 300 mcg/kg/min (higher = risk hypotension, β₂ effects)

### Practical rates (60 kg)

- **Start 18 mL/hr** = 50 mcg/kg/min
- **Target HR 60-80** (aortic dissection: HR &lt; 60)
- **Titrate up quickly** — onset 1-2 min, can back off just as fast

---

## 📊 การติดตาม (Monitoring)

- Continuous ECG, arterial BP
- HR (target range per indication)
- MAP — watch for drop
- Urine output q 1 hr
- No routine liver/renal monitoring needed (esterase metabolism)

### Transition to longer-acting

- Once stable 2–4 hr, transition to:
  - Metoprolol 50 mg PO (then continue PO)
  - Labetalol drip for ongoing IV need
- Taper esmolol over 30 min as oral takes effect

---

## 🚨 Contraindications / Cautions

### Absolute contraindications

- **Cardiogenic shock**
- **Severe bradycardia** (HR &lt; 50)
- **Second or third-degree AV block**
- **Acute decompensated HF**
- **Severe asthma / bronchospasm**

### Relative caution

- Mild COPD (more β₁-selective than labetalol, better tolerated)
- Diabetes (β-block masks hypoglycemia)
- Peripheral vascular disease

---

## ⚠️ ผลข้างเคียง (Side Effects)

- **Hypotension** (dose-dependent, resolves &lt; 20 min after stop)
- Bradycardia
- Nausea, flushing at infusion site
- Bronchospasm (less than non-selective)
- AV block
- Infusion-site phlebitis (with prolonged peripheral use)

---

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Critical Pitfalls)

```
🚩 Short half-life is both feature and bug
   - Can stop quickly if adverse effect (9-min half-life)
   - Need continuous infusion — cannot "bolus and go"

🚩 β₁-selective at low dose, non-selective at high dose
   - &gt; 200 mcg/kg/min: risk bronchospasm
   - Severe asthma: avoid even low dose

🚩 No renal / liver adjustment
   - Esterase metabolism (RBC)
   - Safe in renal / hepatic failure

🚩 Transition to oral when stable
   - Esmolol is bridge, not chronic
   - Plan PO transition within 24 hr

🚩 Aortic dissection — workhorse
   - β-block first, then vasodilator (nicardipine, nitroprusside)
   - Target HR &lt; 60, SBP &lt; 120

🚩 Thyroid storm — β-block adjunct
   - Propranolol traditionally, but esmolol OK
   - Start low in compromised cardiac function

🚩 Rebound after abrupt stop
   - Less pronounced than labetalol (short half-life)
   - But still wean + transition to oral

🚩 Hypotension management
   - Stop/reduce drip — resolves in 10–20 min
   - IV fluid bolus
   - Atropine for brady if persistent
```

## 📝 คำสั่งพร้อมใช้ (Copy-Ready Order)

```
Indication: [aortic dissection / HT emergency / AF-RVR / thyroid storm]

1. Continuous ECG, arterial line BP
2. Labs: BMP, Mg, troponin, TSH (if thyroid storm suspected)
3. ESMOLOL DRIP:
   - Prep: 2.5 g + NSS 250 mL = 10 mg/mL
   - Load (optional): 500 mcg/kg IV over 1 min = 30 mg (3 mL) @ BW 60 kg
   - Maintenance: 50 mcg/kg/min = 18 mL/hr @ BW 60 kg
4. Titrate aggressively:
   - Increase 50 mcg/kg/min q 5 min
   - Max 300 mcg/kg/min (= 108 mL/hr)
   - Optional mini-load before each increase
5. Target:
   - Aortic dissection: HR &lt; 60, SBP &lt; 120
   - HT emergency: MAP reduction ≤ 25% first hour
   - AF-RVR: HR 80–110
6. If over-shoot:
   - Stop drip (resolves in 10–20 min)
   - IV fluid bolus
   - Atropine if brady
7. Add vasodilator if needed (after β-block):
   - Aortic dissection → nicardipine 5–15 mg/hr
   - Or nitroprusside 0.3–2 mcg/kg/min
8. Transition to PO within 24 hr:
   - Metoprolol 50 mg PO (titrate to effect)
   - Overlap 30 min as esmolol weans
9. Monitor:
   - Continuous ECG (HR, AV block)
   - BP q 5 min during titration
   - Bronchospasm signs
10. ICU admit
```
