## 🎯 Mechanism

- **Pure α1 agonist** (no β activity)
- **Vasoconstriction** → ↑ SVR → ↑ BP
- **Reflex bradycardia** (no β1 effect) — useful when tachycardic
- No inotropy; no bronchodilation
- Short half-life (~ 5 min)

---

## 📋 Indications

- **Post-spinal / epidural anesthesia hypotension** (1st choice)
- **Hypotension with tachycardia** (avoid making HR worse)
- **Vasogenic shock bridging** — while preparing norepinephrine
- **Pregnancy hypotension** (spinal C-section) — preferred (less fetal effect than ephedrine in recent evidence)
- **HOCM** (afterload increase without ↑ HR)
- **Septic shock refractory** — add-on with norepi (less common)

---

## ⚠️ Contraindications / Caution

- **Severe bradycardia** (already reflex ↓ HR)
- **Heart block**
- **Severe HTN**
- **MAOI use** (↑ pressor effect)
- **Peripheral ischemia risk** (similar to NE — central line preferred for drip)

---

## 💉 Preparation

### Standard mix
- **10 mg in 250 mL NS** = **40 mcg/mL**
- **50 mg in 500 mL NS** = **100 mcg/mL**
- Stable 24 hr at room temperature

### Concentration choice
- Low concentration (40 mcg/mL) — first-line
- Higher concentration in fluid-restricted pts

---

## 🩺 Dosing

### Bolus (quick push)
- **50–200 mcg IV** (1 mg / 10 mL → give 0.5–2 mL)
- Onset 30 sec, duration 5–10 min
- Repeat q 2–5 min PRN while starting drip

### Infusion
- **Start: 40–60 mcg/min** (~ 0.5–1 mcg/kg/min)
- **Titrate: 40–180 mcg/min** (up to ~ 360 mcg/min in severe)
- Increase by 10–20 mcg/min q 5 min to target MAP

### Target
- **MAP ≥ 65 mmHg** (or per clinical need)
- Check HR, rhythm (watch reflex bradycardia)

### Central vs peripheral
- **Central line preferred** for sustained drip
- Peripheral OK for short course (< 24 hr, large vein, close monitoring for extravasation)
- **Extravasation antidote**: phentolamine 5–10 mg diluted in 10 mL NS, infiltrate locally

---

## ⚠️ ผลข้างเคียง (Side Effects)

- **Reflex bradycardia** (desired if baseline tachy; problematic if bradycardia)
- **Hypertension** (over-correction)
- **Peripheral ischemia** / extravasation necrosis
- **Decreased renal / splanchnic perfusion** (pure vasoconstriction)
- **Headache, restlessness** (high dose)
- **Myocardial ischemia** (demand from ↑ afterload — caution in CAD)

---

## 🎯 Monitoring

- Continuous arterial BP (art line preferred for drip)
- HR, rhythm (bradycardia watch)
- UOP, skin perfusion, lactate
- Check IV site hourly (extravasation)

---

## 🔁 Weaning

- Once stable + source controlled + fluid optimized
- Decrease 10–20 mcg/min q 10–15 min
- Watch for BP drop → pause titration

---

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Pitfalls)

```
🚩 Using in septic shock as first-line
   - Norepinephrine is first-line (has β1 inotropy)
   - Phenylephrine reserved for refractory or HR-limited cases

🚩 Reflex bradycardia in baseline normal HR
   - HR may drop to 40s
   - Atropine 0.5 mg IV if symptomatic

🚩 Peripheral line extravasation
   - Same risk as norepi
   - Phentolamine infiltration for antidote
   - Plan for central line if > 24 hr

🚩 Wrong concentration mixing
   - 10 mg in 250 mL NS vs 250 mL D5W
   - Always label clearly, verify with 2-nurse check

🚩 Masking underlying cause
   - Pressor is symptomatic — identify cause (hypovolemia, cardiac, septic)
   - Don't chase BP with pressor alone
```
