## 🎯 Mechanism

- **Selective α2 agonist** (central, locus coeruleus)
- **Sedation without respiratory depression** — key advantage
- **Anxiolysis + mild analgesia**
- **Sympatholysis** (↓ HR, ↓ BP at higher doses)
- Reduces delirium incidence vs benzodiazepines

---

## 📋 Indications

- **ICU sedation for intubated / non-intubated** — versatile
- **Weaning from mechanical ventilation** (continue sedation through extubation — key advantage)
- **Delirium prevention / treatment** (ICU)
- **Alcohol / benzo withdrawal** adjunct
- **Procedural sedation** (awake fiberoptic intubation, MRI, etc.)
- **Sympathetic storm** (TBI, autonomic dysreflexia)
- **Post-cardiac surgery** sedation

---

## ⚠️ Contraindications / Caution

- **Severe bradycardia / heart block** (can worsen)
- **Sick sinus syndrome**
- **Acute myocardial ischemia** (risk of bradycardia/hypotension)
- **Hypovolemia / shock** (dose-dependent hypotension)

---

## 💉 Preparation

### Standard mix
- **200 mcg in 50 mL NS** = **4 mcg/mL**
- Or ready-to-use premix: **4 mcg/mL**
- Stable 24 hr at room temp

---

## 🩺 Dosing

### Load (optional — often skipped)
- **1 mcg/kg IV over 10 min**
- **Skip or halve load** if:
  - Hemodynamically borderline (common)
  - Elderly (>65 yr)
  - Neuraxial anesthesia / awake
- Loading dose causes transient ↑ BP (direct α2 effect) then ↓ BP (central sympatholysis)

### Maintenance
- **Start: 0.2 mcg/kg/hr**
- **Titrate: 0.2–1.4 mcg/kg/hr** (standard), up to 1.5 mcg/kg/hr in resistant cases
- Titrate q 30 min, watch HR + BP
- **Off-label** extended sedation (up to weeks in some ICUs)

### Example (70 kg)
- 0.5 mcg/kg/hr × 70 = 35 mcg/hr
- With 4 mcg/mL → **8.75 mL/hr**

### Transition from propofol / benzo
- Overlap 30–60 min
- Start dex at 0.4 mcg/kg/hr → titrate
- Stop propofol/benzo once stable

---

## ⚠️ Side Effects

### Common
- **Hypotension** (~ 50%) — dose-dependent
- **Bradycardia** (~ 40%) — sometimes severe
  - HR 40s common, asystole possible at very high doses
  - Atropine / glycopyrrolate if symptomatic + hold infusion
- **Transient hypertension** (loading dose — direct α2 vasoconstriction)

### Less common
- Dry mouth
- Nausea
- Withdrawal on abrupt discontinuation (rebound HTN, tachycardia, agitation) — taper off

### Rare
- Sinus arrest
- Hyperthermia / fever

---

## 🎯 Monitoring

- Continuous HR, BP, SpO₂, rhythm
- RASS target usually **0 to −2** (wake-up arousable, quickly resedate)
- ICU delirium screen (CAM-ICU) daily

---

## 🔁 Weaning

### Wean from drip
- Can wean through extubation (key advantage)
- Taper 0.1–0.2 mcg/kg/hr q 1–2 hr
- Abrupt discontinuation after prolonged use (>24–48 hr) → **withdrawal**: HTN, tachycardia, agitation, anxiety
- Longer taper if use > 7 days

### Post-extubation
- Can continue at low dose for delirium management
- Bridge with clonidine PO (similar mechanism, longer half-life)

---

## 🆚 Dex vs Propofol

| Feature | Dexmedetomidine | Propofol |
|---|---|---|
| **Resp depression** | Minimal | Dose-dependent |
| **Hypotension** | Moderate (bradycardia) | High (vasodilation) |
| **Delirium** | ↓ incidence | Neutral |
| **Extubation** | Can continue through | Must stop before |
| **Maximum dose** | 1.4–1.5 mcg/kg/hr | 67 mcg/kg/min (PRIS risk) |
| **Offset** | 1–2 hr | Few minutes |
| **Cost** | Higher | Lower |
| **TG issue** | None | Risk |

## ⚠️ Pitfalls

```
🚩 Loading dose in unstable pt
   - Causes hypotension + hypertension + bradycardia
   - Skip or halve load in elderly / unstable

🚩 Severe bradycardia
   - HR < 45 common, can reach 30s
   - Hold + atropine 0.5 mg IV if symptomatic
   - Glycopyrrolate alternative (less CNS)

🚩 Missing hypotension trigger
   - Dex has dose-dependent ↓ BP
   - Check volume status, hold IF severe, consider pressor

🚩 Not enough sedation depth
   - Dex is light-to-moderate sedation
   - Deep sedation (RASS -3 to -5): add propofol or benzo

🚩 Abrupt discontinuation after prolonged use
   - Withdrawal: rebound HTN, agitation
   - Taper over 6–12 hr minimum

🚩 Using alone for severe agitation / ARDS with NMB
   - Inadequate depth
   - Deep sedation needs add-on (propofol) + paralysis if NMB

🚩 Cost / shortage
   - Premium price, variable availability
   - Use judiciously; propofol if cost-sensitive + no delirium concern
```
