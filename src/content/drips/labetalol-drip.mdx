## 🎯 Indication / Role

### Use for

- **Hypertensive emergency** — broad use, especially with CAD, aortic dissection, HF
- **Aortic dissection** — β-block FIRST, then vasodilator
- **Pre-eclampsia / eclampsia** — preferred in pregnancy (category C, extensive experience)
- **Post-stroke BP control** — hemorrhagic or ischemic
- **Pheochromocytoma crisis** — after α-block
- **Sympathomimetic overdose** (cocaine, meth) — controversial; balanced α+β avoids unopposed α

### Mechanism

- **α₁ + β (non-selective) blocker** — ratio α:β = 1:7 (IV)
- Reduces SVR (α effect) + HR (β effect)
- Onset: 5–10 min; duration: 3–6 hr
- Less reflex tachycardia than pure vasodilators

### Advantages

- Balanced effect (no reflex tachy)
- Safe in pregnancy (most data)
- Easy to bolus or drip
- No cyanide toxicity concern (vs nitroprusside)

## 💊 การเตรียมยา (Preparation)

### Standard drip mix

```
Labetalol 100 mg + D5W or NSS 100 mL
= 1 mg/mL
```

- Can run peripheral (non-vesicant)
- Compatible: D5W, NSS

### Bolus preparation

- Available as 5 mg/mL ampule (100 mg in 20 mL)
- Bolus directly from vial, no dilution needed

---

## ⚡ Dosing

### Bolus (first-line for acute BP control)

- **Initial: 10–20 mg IV over 2 min**
- Repeat q 10 min, doubling dose (20, 40, 80 mg)
- **Max cumulative: 300 mg**

### Continuous drip

- **Start: 2 mg/min IV** (= 120 mL/hr of 1 mg/mL mix)
- Wait — most references use simpler dosing:
- **Start: 1–2 mg/min** (60–120 mL/hr)
- Titrate by 1 mg/min q 10 min
- **Max: 10 mg/min** (600 mL/hr — rarely needed)

### Target

- **HT emergency:** MAP reduction ≤ 25% in first hour
- **Aortic dissection:** SBP &lt; 120, HR &lt; 60
- **Pre-eclampsia:** SBP &lt; 160, DBP 90–105 (avoid precipitous drop)
- **Post-stroke:** SBP &lt; 185 before tPA; &lt; 220 if no tPA

### Titration tips

- Onset 5–10 min after bolus
- After IV infusion, effect persists 3–6 hr (not short-acting)
- If over-shoot occurs, stop drip; give IV fluid + atropine if brady

---

## 📊 การติดตาม (Monitoring)

- Continuous ECG, BP (arterial line for drip)
- HR — watch bradycardia (&lt; 50)
- Bronchospasm signs (if asthma/COPD)
- Urine output q 1 hr
- Recheck BP q 5 min during titration

---

## 🚨 Contraindications / Cautions

### Absolute contraindications

- **Severe bradycardia** (HR &lt; 50)
- **Second or third-degree AV block**
- **Acute decompensated HF**
- **Severe asthma / bronchospasm** (β blockade)
- **Cardiogenic shock**
- **Severe hypotension**

### Relative caution

- Mild asthma / COPD (start low, monitor)
- Diabetes (may mask hypoglycemia)
- Peripheral vascular disease

---

## ⚠️ ผลข้างเคียง (Side Effects)

- **Bradycardia** (common, dose-related)
- **Hypotension** (rebound possible if rapid correction)
- **Bronchospasm** (β blockade)
- Orthostatic hypotension
- Scalp tingling (benign, classic)
- Heart block
- Rarely: hepatic injury (chronic)

---

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Critical Pitfalls)

<Callout type="danger" title="Labetalol Drip — Pitfalls">

**Avoid in bronchospasm / severe asthma**
→ Non-selective β = may precipitate attack
→ Use nicardipine or clevidipine instead

**Max cumulative 300 mg bolus**
→ Beyond this, switch to drip or alternative

**Pre-eclampsia — preferred (pregnancy)**
→ Safer than nitroprusside (cyanide in fetus)
→ Alternative: hydralazine, nifedipine

**Aortic dissection — bolus + drip**
→ Rapid control SBP &lt; 120, HR &lt; 60
→ Add vasodilator if still HT

**Cocaine / sympathomimetic**
→ Labetalol mixed α+β — theoretically OK
→ Some still recommend avoiding β-only
→ Benzos first-line for these

**HF / cardiogenic shock — avoid**
→ β blockade reduces CO further
→ Use alternative (nicardipine)

**Rebound HT after abrupt discontinuation**
→ Wean over 24–48 hr
→ Transition to oral β-blocker

**Drip effect persists after stopping**
→ Half-life 5–8 hr
→ Don't re-load if BP drops — fluid + atropine

</Callout>

## 📝 คำสั่งพร้อมใช้ (Copy-Ready Order)

```
Indication: [HT emergency / aortic dissection / pre-eclampsia / post-stroke]

1. Continuous ECG, arterial line BP
2. Labs: BMP, CBC, troponin (if chest pain), βhCG
3. LABETALOL BOLUS:
   - 10–20 mg IV over 2 min
   - Repeat q 10 min, doubling (up to 80 mg per bolus)
   - Max cumulative 300 mg
4. If inadequate or continued HT — DRIP:
   - Prep: 100 mg + D5W 100 mL = 1 mg/mL
   - Start 1–2 mg/min (60–120 mL/hr)
   - Titrate by 1 mg/min q 10 min
   - Max 10 mg/min
5. Target:
   - HT emergency: reduce MAP ≤ 25% in 1st hour
   - Aortic dissection: SBP &lt; 120, HR &lt; 60
   - Pre-eclampsia: SBP &lt; 160, DBP 90–105
6. Monitor:
   - BP q 5 min during titration
   - HR (watch &lt; 50)
   - Bronchospasm signs
7. Add vasodilator if needed (after β-block):
   - Aortic dissection: nicardipine 5–15 mg/hr
   - Pre-eclampsia persistent: hydralazine 5–10 mg IV
8. Transition to PO:
   - Labetalol 200–400 mg PO BID-TID
   - Or β-blocker + other agents
9. Avoid stopping abruptly — rebound HT
10. ICU admit (HT emergency) or LR/HDU (pre-eclampsia)
```
