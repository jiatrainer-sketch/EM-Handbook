## 🎯 Mechanism

- **GABAₐ receptor agonist** (short-acting sedative-hypnotic)
- **Rapid onset (30 sec), short duration (5–10 min after stop)**
- Titratable, context-sensitive half-life short
- **Anticonvulsant properties**
- Some antiemetic effect

---

## 📋 Indications

- **ICU sedation for intubated patient** (most common)
- **RSI / induction** (alternative to etomidate)
- **Status epilepticus** (3rd-line, intubated)
- **Procedural sedation** (brief procedures, by trained provider)
- **Burst suppression** (intracranial hypertension, sz refractory)
- **Weaning from long-acting sedatives**

---

## ⚠️ Contraindications / Caution

### Absolute
- **Egg / soy allergy** (vehicle = soybean oil + egg lecithin)
- **Mitochondrial disease** (PRIS risk)

### Relative
- **Hypotension / shock** (dose-dependent ↓ BP)
- **Pediatric prolonged use** (higher PRIS risk)
- **Hypertriglyceridemia** (vehicle is lipid emulsion, 1.1 kcal/mL)
- **Heart failure** (↓ afterload may be helpful; ↓ preload may worsen)

---

## 💉 Preparation

### Standard
- **Supplied as 10 mg/mL (1%) or 20 mg/mL (2%)** — don't dilute
- Use aseptic technique (discard if opened &gt; 12 hr — bacterial growth in lipid emulsion)
- Change tubing + bottle q 12 hr

### Dedicated lumen
- Compatible with many drugs but dedicated lumen preferred
- Incompatible with some electrolytes, ceftriaxone, etc.

---

## 🩺 Dosing

### Induction / RSI
- **1–2 mg/kg IV over 30 sec** (reduce to 0.5–1 mg/kg in elderly / hemodynamically unstable)

### ICU sedation
- **Load: NOT routinely recommended** in unstable pt (severe hypotension)
  - If given: 0.5–1 mg/kg
- **Maintenance: 5–50 mcg/kg/min**
  - Start: **25 mcg/kg/min** (equivalent to ~ 0.3 mg/kg/hr)
  - Titrate: 5–10 mcg/kg/min increments q 5 min
  - Typical range: 25–75 mcg/kg/min
  - Max: **80–100 mcg/kg/min** (avoid &gt; 80 for prolonged — PRIS)

### Example calculation (70 kg)
- 25 mcg/kg/min × 70 kg = 1,750 mcg/min = 1.75 mg/min
- Concentration 10 mg/mL → 0.175 mL/min × 60 = **10.5 mL/hr**

### Status epilepticus (refractory)
- **Load: 1–2 mg/kg IV** → q 3–5 min up to 10 mg/kg
- **Maintenance: 30–200 mcg/kg/min** (higher than sedation)
- EEG target: burst suppression
- Aim duration 24–48 hr, then wean

---

## ⚠️ Side Effects

### Common
- **Hypotension** (vasodilation + ↓ inotropy) — dose-dependent
  - Bridge with norepinephrine/phenylephrine
- **Bradycardia** (rare but possible)
- **Respiratory depression / apnea** (expected in induction)
- **Pain on injection** (peripheral) → use large vein + 1% lidocaine pre-dose

### Drug-specific quirks
- **Green urine** — harmless, phenolic metabolite
- **Pink urine** — uric acid precipitation (hypothermia / dehydration)

### Dangerous
- **PRIS (Propofol Infusion Syndrome)** — see below
- **Hypertriglyceridemia** (lipid overload) — check TG q 48 hr if > 48 hr use
- **Metabolic acidosis** (unexplained — suspect PRIS)

---

## 🔴 Propofol Infusion Syndrome (PRIS)

### Features
- **Metabolic acidosis** (lactic acidosis)
- **Rhabdomyolysis** (high CK, myoglobinuria)
- **Hyperkalemia**
- **Arrhythmias** (bradycardia, asystole, brugada-like pattern)
- **Cardiac failure / cardiomegaly**
- **Hepatomegaly, hypertriglyceridemia**
- **Often fatal** — mortality up to 80% once established

### Risk factors
- **Dose &gt; 4 mg/kg/hr (&gt; 67 mcg/kg/min)**
- **Duration &gt; 48 hr**
- **Pediatric &lt; 16 yr** (avoid in kids)
- **Concurrent vasopressors / steroids / catecholamines**
- **Low carbohydrate intake** (starvation)
- **Inherited mitochondrial disease**

### Monitoring (prevention)
- **Lactate, CK, TG, LFT, K, ABG** q 24 hr if &gt; 48 hr infusion
- **Trend up** in any → **switch to alternative** (dexmedetomidine, midazolam)

### Management if PRIS suspected
- **Stop propofol** immediately
- Switch sedation to dexmedetomidine or midazolam
- Supportive: correct acidosis, K, fluid
- **Hemodialysis / ECMO** in severe (refractory acidosis, shock)

---

## 🎯 Monitoring

### Routine
- Continuous HR, BP, SpO₂, rhythm
- Richmond Agitation-Sedation Scale (RASS) target usually −1 to −2
- Daily sedation interruption (SAT) if stable

### If &gt; 48 hr infusion
- **Lactate, CK, TG (target &lt; 500), K, BMP** q 24 hr
- Watch for PRIS triad (acidosis + rhabdo + K)

### Calorie count
- Propofol 10 mg/mL → **1.1 kcal/mL**
- Example: 70 kg, 30 mcg/kg/min × 24 hr = 302 mL = **332 kcal/day** from sedative
- Adjust nutrition accordingly

---

## 🔁 Weaning

- Daily SAT (spontaneous awakening trial)
- Decrease by 50% q 1–2 hr if tolerated
- Shorter weaning curve due to short half-life
- Transition to PO sedation (quetiapine, other) if longer need

---

## ⚠️ Pitfalls

```
🚩 Rapid IV push causing hypotension
   - Slow push over 30–60 sec
   - Avoid bolus in unstable, give smaller incremental doses

🚩 Using as "set and forget" drip
   - Daily RASS assessment + SAT
   - Over-sedation → ↑ vent days, delirium, ICU length

🚩 PRIS from prolonged high-dose
   - > 48 hr + > 67 mcg/kg/min = danger zone
   - Consider alternative (dex, midaz) if need prolonged deep sedation

🚩 Forgetting TG monitoring
   - Lipid emulsion can cause pancreatitis
   - Check q 48 hr, adjust nutrition if > 500

🚩 Wrong concentration/mixup
   - 1% vs 2% propofol — always verify
   - Default is 10 mg/mL (1%)

🚩 Vehicle allergy
   - Egg + soy — check allergy history
   - Switch to etomidate/midazolam if allergic

🚩 Sedation in shock without pressor support
   - Propofol ↓↓ BP
   - Start pressor first or concurrently
```
