## 🎯 Indication / Role

### First-line vasopressor for

- **Septic shock** (after adequate fluid 30 mL/kg)
- **Cardiogenic shock** (with/without inotrope)
- **Post-arrest** hypotension
- **Distributive shock** ทุกประเภท (neurogenic, anaphylactic refractory)
- **Post-op** hypotension (ถ้ามี arterial tone loss)

### ไม่ใช่ first-line ถ้า

- **Hypovolemic shock** ที่ยังไม่ได้ resuscitate fluid → ให้ fluid ก่อน
- **Obstructive shock** (tamponade, PE, PTX) → แก้สาเหตุก่อน
- **Cardiogenic shock + high SVR** → inotrope (dobutamine) อาจเหมาะกว่า

### Mechanism

- **α₁** (main): vasoconstriction → ↑ SVR → ↑ MAP
- **β₁** (mild): inotropic + mild chronotropic
- **β₂**: negligible (แทบไม่มี peripheral vasodilation)

## 💊 การเตรียมยา (Thai Standard)

### Standard: **"Levo 4:250"** = 4 mg + D5W 250 mL = **16 mcg/mL**

```
Norepinephrine 4 mg (1 amp × 4 mg/4 mL) + D5W 250 mL
= 4,000 mcg / 250 mL = 16 mcg/mL
```

Most community hospital default — ใช้ได้ทั้ง peripheral + central

### Concentrated: **"Levo 4:100"** = 4 mg + D5W 100 mL = **40 mcg/mL**

```
Norepinephrine 4 mg (1 amp) + D5W 100 mL
= 4,000 mcg / 100 mL = 40 mcg/mL
```

Thai ICU standard — ลดปริมาณ fluid ต่อ mL/hr; **central line เท่านั้น**

### Fluid restriction: **8 mg + D5W 250 mL = 32 mcg/mL**

```
Norepinephrine 8 mg (2 amp) + D5W 250 mL = 32 mcg/mL
```

### Severe fluid restriction: **16 mg + D5W 250 mL = 64 mcg/mL**

```
Norepinephrine 16 mg (4 amp) + D5W 250 mL = 64 mcg/mL
```

⚠️ **ใช้ D5W** — NE stable ใน acidic pH; NSS ได้แต่ stability สั้นกว่า  
⚠️ **ห้ามผสม bicarbonate** — alkaline pH ทำลาย NE

## ⚡ Dosing Table (BW 60 kg, Levo 4:100 = 40 mcg/mL)

**Starting dose:** 0.05 mcg/kg/min = 3 mcg/min @ BW 60 kg  
**Target:** MAP ≥ 65 mmHg (หรือ 75–80 ใน HTN/elderly per Surviving Sepsis)

| Dose (mcg/kg/min) | mcg/min | Rate (mL/hr) |
|---|---|---|
| **0.05 (start)** | **3** | **4.5** |
| 0.1 | 6 | 9 |
| 0.2 | 12 | 18 |
| 0.3 | 18 | 27 |
| 0.5 | 30 | 45 |
| 0.75 | 45 | 68 |
| **1.0 (max)** | **60** | **90** |

**Formula:** mL/hr = (dose × BW × 60) / concentration  
- @ 0.05 × 60 × 60 / 40 = **4.5 mL/hr**

⚠️ ถ้าใช้ Levo 4:250 (16 mcg/mL): ×2.5 ทุก rate → 0.05 = 11 mL/hr, 1.0 = 225 mL/hr

### Titration

- **Assess q 5–15 min** ขณะ titrate
- **เพิ่มทีละ 0.02–0.05 mcg/kg/min** ต่อครั้ง
- Target: MAP ≥ 65 ต่อเนื่อง ≥ 15–30 min ก่อนลด rate
- MAP > 90 → ลด rate

---

## 🚨 Max Dose: **1 mcg/kg/min** (= 90 mL/hr @ Levo 4:100, BW 60 kg)

**High-dose warning:** > 0.5 mcg/kg/min = high dose, mortality ↑ — reassess aggressively

### ถ้าถึง max แล้ว MAP ยังไม่ขึ้น:

1. ➕ **Add Vasopressin 0.03 U/min** (fixed dose, ไม่ titrate — catecholamine-sparing)
2. 💊 **Hydrocortisone 200 mg/day IV** (refractory septic shock — ADRENAL study)
3. ➕ **Add Epinephrine drip** ถ้า cardiac output ต่ำ (cold shock)
4. 🔍 **Workup refractory shock:**
   - Volume status (IVC US, echo, passive leg raise)
   - Source control (imaging, drain)
   - Adrenal insufficiency (random cortisol)
   - Missed diagnosis (tamponade, PE, pneumothorax)

### Weaning

- **ลดทีละ 0.02–0.05 mcg/kg/min q 15–30 min** เมื่อ MAP stable
- Monitor MAP หลังลดทุกครั้ง
- Wean ได้เร็วถ้า recovery ชัด

## 🔌 Access

### **Central line preferred** for sustained use

- Internal jugular / subclavian / femoral
- Distal port dedicated to NE

### **Peripheral use — acceptable short-term** (&lt; 6 hr)

- Large proximal vein (antecubital, upper arm)
- Short catheter (18–20G) — ไม่ใช้สาย midline
- Watch site closely q 1 hr
- Low concentration (16 mcg/mL standard)
- Upper extremity preferred over lower
- Transition to central as soon as feasible

⚠️ **Extravasation = skin necrosis** (severe)

### Extravasation treatment

1. **Stop infusion + disconnect syringe, aspirate** residual through cannula
2. **Do NOT remove the IV yet** — use it to administer antidote
3. **Phentolamine 5–10 mg in 10–15 mL NSS**, inject subcutaneously around site (multiple small injections)
4. If no phentolamine: **terbutaline** 1 mg in 10 mL NSS subq, หรือ **nitroglycerin ointment 1 inch** to site
5. **Remove IV after antidote given**
6. Document + photograph
7. Warm compress (avoid cold)

## 📊 การติดตาม (Monitoring)

**Continuous:**
- **Arterial line** (invasive BP) preferred for titration
- If no a-line: NIBP q 5 min while titrating, q 15 min stable
- ECG (arrhythmia)
- SpO₂, RR

**Hourly:**
- Urine output (target &gt; 0.5 mL/kg/hr)
- Mental status
- Lactate clearance (q 2 hr initial)

**Q 2–4 hr:**
- Lactate trend
- Peripheral perfusion (digit color, temp, cap refill)
- Central line site

**Watch for complications:**
- **Peripheral / digital ischemia** (fingers/toes going dark)
- **Mesenteric ischemia** (abdominal pain, distension, rising lactate)
- **Arrhythmia** (tachy > 120, new VT)
- **Acute kidney injury** (renal vasoconstriction — balance with MAP)

---

## ⚠️ ผลข้างเคียง (Side Effects)

**Common:**
- Tachycardia (usually mild)
- Increased afterload → may worsen heart failure
- Headache, anxiety (pt perception)

**Serious:**
- **Digital ischemia** / necrosis (↑ risk in shock, DM, PAD)
- **Mesenteric ischemia**
- **Extravasation necrosis**
- **Tachyarrhythmia** (uncommon unless high dose)
- **Myocardial ischemia** (↑ afterload + ↑ HR)
- **Pulmonary edema** (in CHF)

---

## 🫘 Renal / 🧓 Elderly / Special populations

**Renal (CKD):**
- ไม่ต้องปรับ dose (cleared by MAO/COMT, not kidney)
- แต่ watch: renal vasoconstriction may worsen AKI — balance MAP ≥ 65 vs perfusion

**Elderly:**
- Higher risk digital ischemia, MI, arrhythmia
- Start low (0.03–0.05 mcg/kg/min)
- Titrate slowly
- MAP target may need to be 75–80 (autoregulation shifted)

**Pregnancy:**
- Category C (use if benefit outweighs risk)
- Maternal hypotension in pregnancy usually phenylephrine first-line, but NE OK in septic shock

**Heart failure / low EF:**
- May worsen afterload / CO
- Consider dobutamine or epinephrine เพิ่ม (inotrope)

---

## 🔄 Compatibility

**Compatible in same line:**
- Dobutamine, dopamine, epinephrine, vasopressin, heparin, insulin
- Calcium (central line only)

**INCOMPATIBLE:**
- **Bicarbonate** (precipitate, degradation)
- **Phenytoin** (precipitate)
- **Alkaline drugs**

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Critical Pitfalls)

```
🚩 Fluid first in septic shock
   - 30 mL/kg crystalloid = ~1,800 mL @ 60 kg
   - Don't start NE to "hide" hypovolemia
   - Exception: severe shock, give NE while fluid running

🚩 Central line preferred, peripheral OK < 6 hr
   - Use large proximal vein, 18G
   - Low concentration (16 mcg/mL)
   - Upper > lower extremity
   - Extravasation = necrosis

🚩 Extravasation = phentolamine ASAP
   - 5–10 mg in 10 mL NSS, subcutaneous around site
   - Don't remove IV until antidote given
   - Document + photo

🚩 Don't mix with bicarbonate
   - Alkaline pH degrades NE
   - Use separate line

🚩 MAP 65 ≠ universal target
   - HTN patients may need 75–80
   - Check urine output, mental status, lactate

🚩 Digital ischemia = reduce rate
   - Check fingers/toes q 2 hr
   - Dusky, cold, mottled = warning
   - Add vasopressin, decrease NE

🚩 Reaching max dose?
   - Add vasopressin 0.03 U/min
   - Hydrocortisone 200 mg/day
   - Reassess: volume, source, missed cause
   - Don't just keep pushing NE

🚩 Abrupt discontinuation = rebound hypotension
   - Wean 0.02–0.05 mcg/kg/min q 15–30 min

🚩 Cardiogenic shock + NE alone
   - May worsen output (↑ afterload)
   - Consider dobutamine add-on if low CO

🚩 β-blocker user → less response
   - May need higher dose
   - Consider glucagon, vasopressin add

🚩 Verify prep + label
   - High-alert drug
   - "Norepinephrine 4 mg in D5W 250 mL = 16 mcg/mL"
   - Double-check before change bag
```

## 📝 คำสั่งพร้อมใช้ (Copy-Ready Order)

```
1. NSS / RL fluid resuscitation first (30 mL/kg = ~1,800 mL @ 60 kg)
2. If MAP < 65 after adequate fluid:
   NOREPINEPHRINE DRIP:
   - Prep: Norepinephrine 4 mg + D5W 100 mL = 40 mcg/mL (Levo 4:100)
   - Start: 0.05 mcg/kg/min = 3 mcg/min = 4.5 mL/hr @ BW 60 kg
   - Titrate q 5–15 min by 0.02–0.05 mcg/kg/min
   - Target MAP ≥ 65
   - Access: central line preferred; peripheral OK < 6 hr
     (large proximal vein, 18G, watch extravasation)
3. Monitoring:
   - Arterial line (if available)
   - ECG continuous, SpO₂
   - NIBP q 5 min while titrating
   - UO hourly (target > 0.5 mL/kg/hr)
   - Peripheral perfusion q 2 hr (digits, mottling)
   - Lactate q 2 hr until < 2
   - Mental status q 1 hr
4. Labs: BMP, VBG, lactate, CBC, troponin q 6–12 hr
5. Add second vasopressor if NE > 0.5 mcg/kg/min:
   - Vasopressin 0.03 U/min (fixed)
   - Hydrocortisone 200 mg/day IV (refractory)
   - Consider dobutamine if low CO / mixed
6. Wean: reduce 0.02–0.05 mcg/kg/min q 15–30 min
   once MAP stable + improving
7. Extravasation protocol ready (phentolamine bedside)
8. Admit ICU, reassess source of shock
```
