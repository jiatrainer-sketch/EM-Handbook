## 🎯 Indication / Role

### Use for

- **Low-output heart failure** with preserved SVR (cold/wet, Forrester IV)
- **Cardiogenic shock** as inotrope (alternative to dobutamine)
- **Pulmonary hypertension / RV failure** (pulmonary vasodilation)
- **Post-cardiac surgery** weaning from bypass
- **Bridge to transplant / LVAD**

### Mechanism

- **PDE3 inhibitor** → ↑ intracellular cAMP
- **Inotrope** (independent of β-adrenergic) — works even with β-blockers
- **Systemic + pulmonary vasodilation** (inodilator)
- Longer half-life (~2–3 hr) than dobutamine (~2 min)

### Differences from dobutamine

| Feature | Dobutamine | Milrinone |
|---|---|---|
| Mechanism | β₁ agonist | PDE3 inhibitor |
| β-blocker interaction | Blunted | Unaffected |
| Half-life | 2 min | 2–3 hr |
| Pulmonary vasodilation | Modest | Strong |
| Tachycardia | More | Less |
| Hypotension | Less | More |
| Renal excretion | No | **Yes — dose adjust** |

---

## 💊 Preparation

### Standard mix

```
Milrinone 20 mg + D5W 100 mL
= 200 mcg/mL
```

- Central line preferred (long duration, vasoactive)
- Peripheral OK short-term (non-vesicant)
- Compatible: D5W, NSS

### Alternative

- 40 mg + D5W 200 mL = 200 mcg/mL (larger volume)

---

## ⚡ Dosing @ BW 60 kg

### Loading (optional, often SKIPPED)

- **50 mcg/kg IV over 10 min** = 3 mg (15 mL) over 10 min
- Skip load if hypotensive or marginal BP (causes transient ↓ BP)
- Not recommended in acute decompensated HF ED setting

### Maintenance (continuous drip)

| Dose (mcg/kg/min) | mcg/min | Rate (mL/hr) | Indication |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0.25 | 15 | 4.5 | Very low BP, start cautious |
| **0.375 (start)** | **22.5** | **7** | Standard start |
| 0.5 | 30 | 9 | Titrate up |
| 0.75 | 45 | 13.5 | Max typical |

**Formula:** mL/hr = (dose × BW × 60) / concentration
- @ 0.375 × 60 × 60 / 200 = **6.75 mL/hr** (round to 7)

### Titration

- **Start low** (0.25–0.375) in ED / acute setting
- Titrate by 0.125 mcg/kg/min q 30 min
- Target: improved perfusion (↓ lactate, ↑ UO, better MAP)
- Max **0.75 mcg/kg/min** usually

### Renal dose adjustment

- **CrCl 30–50:** reduce dose 50%
- **CrCl &lt; 30:** reduce dose 75%
- **Dialysis:** 0.2 mcg/kg/min max
- Cumulative effect — monitor closely

---

## 📊 Monitoring

- **Continuous ECG, BP** (arterial line preferred — hypotension risk)
- **Hemodynamic response** within 30 min:
  - ↑ CO / CI
  - ↓ PCWP (if pulmonary cath)
  - ↓ SVR
  - Improved peripheral perfusion
- **Urine output** q 1 hr
- Lactate q 2–4 hr
- ECG for arrhythmia (PVC, VT)
- Electrolytes (K, Mg) — correct to reduce arrhythmia
- **Renal function** daily (dose adjust)

---

## ⚠️ Side Effects

- **Hypotension** (main — treat with IV fluid first, then NE if persistent)
- Ventricular arrhythmia (PVC, VT) — ~10%
- Atrial fib / flutter exacerbation
- Headache
- Thrombocytopenia (rare, reversible)
- Liver enzyme ↑ (rare)

---

## 🚨 Contraindications / Cautions

### Contraindications

- **Severe aortic stenosis** (preload-dependent)
- **Severe hypovolemia** (fluid first)
- **Severe renal failure requiring caution** (half-life prolonged)

### Relative caution

- Recent acute MI (arrhythmia risk)
- Known VT / frequent PVC (pro-arrhythmic)
- Hypotension → may need concurrent vasopressor (NE, vasopressin)
- Atrial fib with rapid response (may accelerate)

---

## ⚠️ Critical Pitfalls

```
🚩 Long half-life (2–3 hr)
   - Cannot quickly reverse hypotension
   - Plan ahead for pressors

🚩 Renal adjustment critical
   - Standard dose in CKD = accumulation → toxicity
   - CrCl 30–50: reduce 50%

🚩 Skip load in ED acute
   - Transient hypotension from bolus
   - Start maintenance directly

🚩 Often need NE concurrent
   - Milrinone drops SVR
   - If MAP drops, add NE 0.05 mcg/kg/min (don't discontinue milrinone)

🚩 Arrhythmia risk
   - Correct K, Mg first
   - Continuous ECG
   - Stop / reduce if sustained VT

🚩 Not first-line for cardiogenic shock
   - If MAP &lt; 65 → NE first, then add inotrope
   - Dobutamine often preferred initially (short half-life, easier titrate)

🚩 Monitor IN-OUT carefully
   - Vasodilation → relative hypovolemia
   - Diuretics cautiously if stable
```

## 📝 Copy-Ready Order

```
Dx: Low-output heart failure / cardiogenic shock / pulmonary HT

1. Central line preferred; continuous ECG + arterial line
2. Labs: BMP (Cr), Mg, troponin, BNP, ABG, lactate
3. Echo: assess LV function, RV function, valvular
4. MILRINONE DRIP:
   - Prep: 20 mg + D5W 100 mL = 200 mcg/mL
   - SKIP loading in ED (hypotension risk)
   - Maintenance: start 0.375 mcg/kg/min = 7 mL/hr @ BW 60 kg
   - Titrate by 0.125 mcg/kg/min q 30 min
   - Max 0.75 mcg/kg/min
5. Renal adjust:
   - CrCl 30–50: reduce 50%
   - CrCl &lt; 30: reduce 75%
   - Dialysis: max 0.2 mcg/kg/min
6. Concurrent vasopressor if MAP &lt; 65:
   - NE 0.05 mcg/kg/min, titrate
   - Do NOT stop milrinone — combine
7. Correct K (&gt; 4.0), Mg (&gt; 2.0) — reduce arrhythmia
8. Monitor:
   - Continuous ECG
   - Arterial BP
   - UO q 1 hr
   - Lactate q 2–4 hr
   - Cr daily (dose adjust)
9. Assess response in 30 min:
   - ↑ UO, ↓ lactate, better perfusion = working
   - No response → reassess diagnosis
10. Wean:
    - Reduce by 0.125 mcg/kg/min q 6–12 hr
    - Cardiology follow-up for chronic HF / transplant
11. ICU / CCU admit
```
