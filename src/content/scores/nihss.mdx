## 🎯 Purpose

ประเมิน **ความรุนแรงของ stroke** — ใช้ตัดสินใจ tPA, thrombectomy, track progress, prognosis

**Range:** 0 (normal) → 42 (severe)

---

## 📊 11 Items (baseline minimum)

### 1a. Level of Consciousness
- 0 — Alert, responsive
- 1 — Drowsy, rouses to minor stim
- 2 — Stuporous, requires repeated/strong stim
- 3 — Coma, reflex only

### 1b. LOC Questions (age, current month)
- 0 — Both correct
- 1 — One correct
- 2 — Neither correct (intubated/aphasic → score 2)

### 1c. LOC Commands (close eyes, grip fist)
- 0 — Obeys both
- 1 — Obeys one
- 2 — Obeys neither

### 2. Best Gaze (horizontal eye movement)
- 0 — Normal
- 1 — Partial palsy
- 2 — Forced deviation / total palsy

### 3. Visual Field
- 0 — No visual loss
- 1 — Partial hemianopia
- 2 — Complete hemianopia
- 3 — Bilateral hemianopia (blind)

### 4. Facial Palsy
- 0 — Normal
- 1 — Minor (asymmetric smile)
- 2 — Partial (total lower face)
- 3 — Complete (both upper + lower)

### 5a & 5b. Motor Arm (each arm, 10 sec)
Extend arm 90° seated / 45° supine, count drift

- 0 — No drift × 10 sec
- 1 — Drifts but doesn't hit bed
- 2 — Some effort against gravity, falls
- 3 — No effort against gravity, drops
- 4 — No movement
- UN — Amputation / joint fusion

### 6a & 6b. Motor Leg (each leg, 5 sec)
Supine, lift leg 30°

- 0 — No drift × 5 sec
- 1 — Drifts but doesn't hit bed
- 2 — Falls to bed within 5 sec
- 3 — No effort against gravity
- 4 — No movement
- UN — Amputation / fusion

### 7. Limb Ataxia
Finger-nose + heel-shin

- 0 — Absent
- 1 — Present in 1 limb
- 2 — Present in 2+ limbs
- UN — Paralyzed / amputated

### 8. Sensory (pin prick face/arm/leg)
- 0 — Normal
- 1 — Mild-moderate loss
- 2 — Severe/total loss (unaware of being touched)

### 9. Best Language
Describe picture, read sentences, name objects

- 0 — Normal
- 1 — Mild aphasia (slow, word-finding)
- 2 — Severe aphasia (fragmented expression)
- 3 — Mute / global aphasia / coma

### 10. Dysarthria
Read list of words

- 0 — Normal
- 1 — Mild-moderate slurring
- 2 — Severe / unintelligible
- UN — Intubated / physical barrier

### 11. Extinction / Inattention (neglect)
- 0 — No abnormality
- 1 — Visual / tactile / auditory inattention to 1 side
- 2 — Severe hemi-inattention across &gt; 1 modality

## 🚦 Severity Categories

| Total | Category | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | No stroke symptoms | |
| 1–4 | **Minor** | Consider tPA case-by-case (disabling deficit?) |
| 5–15 | **Moderate** | Typical tPA candidate if within window |
| 16–20 | **Moderate–severe** | Consider thrombectomy (if LVO) |
| 21–42 | **Severe** | High tPA bleed risk (&gt; 25 relative contra); thrombectomy candidate |

---

## ⚡ Clinical use

### Guides treatment:

**tPA (Alteplase):**
- NIHSS ≥ 4 typically (but any disabling deficit)
- NIHSS &gt; 25: relative contraindication (bleed risk)

**Mechanical thrombectomy:**
- NIHSS ≥ 6 with LVO
- Window: 0–6 hr standard, 0–24 hr with perfusion imaging (DAWN/DEFUSE-3)

### Trend monitoring:
- Document q 1 hr × 6 hr post-tPA
- Then q 2–4 hr × 24 hr
- **Increase ≥ 4 points** = worsening → CT brain (bleed? re-occlusion?)
- **Decrease ≥ 4 points** = improving

### Prognosis:
- NIHSS &lt; 10: 60–70% independent at 1 year
- NIHSS &gt; 20: 4–16% independent at 1 year

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Pitfalls)

<Callout type="danger" title="NIH Stroke Scale (NIHSS) — Pitfalls">

**Untrained rater = unreliable**
→ Certification recommended (AHA training)
→ Different raters can differ by 2+ points

**Posterior circulation strokes**
→ NIHSS underestimates
→ Cerebellar / brainstem signs not well captured
→ Consider treatment despite low score

**Right hemispheric strokes**
→ Often lower NIHSS (language preserved)
→ Neglect scored lower weight
→ May still have large territory

**NIHSS 0 ≠ no stroke**
→ Small deep infarcts, posterior circulation
→ Clinical judgment + imaging

**Intubated / aphasic**
→ LOC questions = 2 automatically
→ Language = assess what you can
→ Document limitations

**Preexisting deficit**
→ Score current assessment
→ Document old deficit separately
→ Use "pre-stroke baseline NIHSS" for comparison

**Don't delay tPA for NIHSS**
→ NIHSS is guidance not absolute
→ Window + clinical decision primary

</Callout>
