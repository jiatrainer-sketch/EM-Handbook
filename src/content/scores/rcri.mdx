## 🎯 Purpose

**30-day risk of major cardiac event** (cardiac death, MI, cardiac arrest) for patients undergoing **non-cardiac surgery**.

Use to stratify pre-op cardiac workup + optimize before elective surgery.

## 📋 Criteria (6 — each 1 point)

| Criterion | Definition |
|---|---|
| 1. **High-risk surgery** | Intra-peritoneal, intra-thoracic, or suprainguinal vascular |
| 2. **Ischemic heart disease** | Prior MI, abnormal stress test, current chest pain from ischemia, nitrate use, or pathologic Q waves |
| 3. **Heart failure** | History of HF, pulmonary edema, or dyspnea of cardiac origin |
| 4. **Cerebrovascular disease** | Prior stroke or TIA |
| 5. **Insulin-dependent diabetes** | Requires insulin (regardless of type) |
| 6. **Serum creatinine &gt; 2.0 mg/dL** | Renal insufficiency |

**Total: 0–6 points**

---

## 📊 Risk Interpretation

| Score | Risk Category | 30-day MACE |
|---|---|---|
| **0** | Very Low | 0.4% |
| **1** | Low | 0.9% |
| **2** | Intermediate | 6.6% |
| **≥ 3** | High | &gt; 11% |

---

## 🏥 Management by Risk

### Low risk (0–1)

- **Proceed with surgery** (if otherwise optimized)
- **Routine labs** per usual pre-op
- **No additional cardiac testing** needed (yield very low)

### Intermediate risk (2)

- **Functional capacity assessment** (METs)
- If poor functional capacity (METs &lt; 4) and high-risk surgery: consider **stress test**
- **Optimize comorbidities** (HT, DM, HF)
- **Baseline ECG**
- Usually proceed with surgery + optimized medical management

### High risk (≥ 3)

- **Cardiology consultation**
- **Echocardiogram** (baseline LV function, valvular disease)
- Consider **stress testing / CAG** based on findings
- **Optimize** (β-blocker, statin, ACE-I, volume status) before elective surgery
- **Continue chronic cardiac meds** perioperatively (β-blocker, statin)
- **ICU postop** if also high-risk surgery
- For urgent/emergent surgery: proceed with enhanced monitoring

---

## 🏃 Functional Capacity (METs)

Often used alongside RCRI to decide if non-invasive testing needed.

| METs | Activity |
|---|---|
| **&lt; 4** | Light housework, eat/dress/use toilet, slow walking |
| **4–10** | Climb stairs 1 flight, heavy housework, golf, tennis (doubles) |
| **&gt; 10** | Strenuous sports, swimming, tennis singles |

**&gt; 4 METs** without symptoms = "good" functional capacity → proceed

**&lt; 4 METs** or cannot assess → consider further evaluation (esp with RCRI ≥ 2)

---

## 💊 Perioperative Medication

### Continue perioperatively

- **Statins** (continue chronic; start if high-risk + not on)
- **β-blockers** (continue; **do NOT initiate day of surgery** — POISE trial harm)
- **Aspirin** (continue if indicated for CAD; risk/benefit if new)
- **ACE-I / ARB** — often hold morning of surgery to avoid perioperative hypotension

### Hold

- **DOACs / warfarin** (per surgical bleed risk)
- **SGLT2 inhibitors** (2–3 days pre-op; euglycemic DKA risk)
- **Metformin** (hold day of surgery)

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Critical Pitfalls)

<Callout type="danger" title="RCRI — Revised Cardiac Risk Index — Pitfalls">

**RCRI doesn't capture aortic stenosis or active unstable angina**
→ Check separately — these = red flags regardless of score
→ Severe AS requires cardiology consult

**Do NOT initiate β-blocker day of surgery**
→ POISE trial: harm (stroke, death)
→ Must be started &gt; 1 week before with titration

**Functional capacity matters**
→ Low RCRI + poor METs = may still need evaluation
→ High RCRI + good METs = less worry

**Emergent surgery — don't delay for RCRI**
→ Document, proceed, enhance monitoring postop
→ Cardiac eval after surgery

**Active ACS = absolute contraindication to elective surgery**
→ Not captured in RCRI as "active"
→ Must ask about unstable symptoms

**Recent MI &lt; 60 days = postpone elective**
→ Even if otherwise low RCRI
→ Wait ideally 60 days post-MI

**Decompensated HF = optimize first**
→ Pulmonary edema, active volume overload
→ Postpone elective

**Uncontrolled HT &gt; 180/110 = defer**
→ Optimize meds, then proceed
→ Unless emergent surgery

</Callout>

## 📝 Clinical Use — Example

**Patient:** 72F, planned laparoscopic cholecystectomy (intermediate-risk surgery)
- Prior MI 3 years ago: ✓ (1 pt)
- DM on insulin: ✓ (1 pt)
- Cr 2.5: ✓ (1 pt)
- No HF, CVD, and surgery is intermediate not high: 0 pts

**Total: 3 points → High risk (&gt; 11% 30-day MACE)**

**Plan:**
- Cardiology consultation
- Echo (baseline LV function)
- Continue ASA, statin, β-blocker perioperatively
- Hold ACE-I morning of surgery
- Postop: step-down unit monitoring
