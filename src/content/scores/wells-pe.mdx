## 🎯 Purpose

**Pre-test probability** of pulmonary embolism → decides whether to pursue D-dimer vs CTPA

---

## 📋 Criteria

| Criterion | Points |
|---|---|
| Clinical s/s of DVT (leg swelling + pain on palpation) | **3.0** |
| **PE most likely diagnosis** (no alternative equally likely) | **3.0** |
| Heart rate &gt; 100 bpm | **1.5** |
| Immobilization (&gt; 3 days) or surgery within 4 weeks | **1.5** |
| Previous DVT or PE | **1.5** |
| Hemoptysis | **1.0** |
| Active malignancy (treatment ongoing, stopped within 6 mo, palliative) | **1.0** |

**Total: 0–12.5 points**

---

## 📊 Interpretation — Two schemes

### 3-tier (traditional)

| Score | Risk | PE prevalence |
|---|---|---|
| **&lt; 2** | Low | ~1.3% |
| **2–6** | Moderate | ~16.2% |
| **&gt; 6** | High | ~37.5% |

### 2-tier (simplified, preferred in many protocols)

| Score | Category | Next step |
|---|---|---|
| **≤ 4** | **PE unlikely** | D-dimer |
| **&gt; 4** | **PE likely** | CTPA directly |

---

## 🗺 Algorithm

```
Wells ≤ 4 (unlikely)
  ↓
D-dimer
  ├── Negative (&lt; 500 ng/mL, or age × 10 if &gt; 50) → PE ruled out
  └── Positive → CTPA

Wells > 4 (likely)
  ↓
CTPA directly (skip D-dimer — too many false +)
```

### Age-adjusted D-dimer

- Age &gt; 50: cutoff = **age × 10 ng/mL** (instead of 500)
- Improves specificity in elderly
- Only use if Wells unlikely

---

## 📍 PERC Rule (adjunct, for very low pretest)

**Use only if:** Wells **low** AND clinician gestalt probability &lt; 15%

All 8 must be **NO** for PE ruled out without D-dimer:

1. Age ≥ 50
2. HR ≥ 100
3. SpO₂ &lt; 95%
4. Unilateral leg swelling
5. Hemoptysis
6. Recent trauma or surgery &lt; 4 weeks
7. Prior DVT / PE
8. Exogenous estrogen (OCP, HRT)

All 8 NO → **PE ruled out, no D-dimer needed** (&lt; 1% miss rate)

---

## ⚠️ When Wells is less reliable

- **Pregnancy** — consider alternative (YEARS criteria preferred)
- **Young age &lt; 30** — low base rate, PERC may be sufficient
- **Cancer with recent hospitalization** — often high baseline
- **ICU / post-op** — tachycardia non-specific

### Alternative: Revised Geneva Score

- Similar purpose, more objective (no subjective "PE most likely")
- Consider if provider bias concern

---

## 📝 Clinical Use — Example

**Patient:** 65M with COPD, sudden dyspnea, HR 110, unilateral calf swelling, no prior DVT

**Wells:**
- Clinical s/s DVT: 3
- PE most likely: 3 (in context)
- HR &gt; 100: 1.5
- **Total: 7.5 → High (&gt; 6)**

**Action:** Straight to CTPA; consider empiric anticoagulation while awaiting imaging

---

## ⚠️ Critical Pitfalls

```
🚩 "PE most likely dx" is subjective
   - Requires judgment about alternative diagnoses
   - Don't use rigidly in unclear cases

🚩 Don't use D-dimer in high pretest
   - False-positive rate too high to rule in
   - Go directly to CTPA

🚩 Don't skip CTPA just because Wells is low
   - If D-dimer positive, still need CTPA
   - Clinical gestalt matters (some high-risk patients score low)

🚩 Age-adjusted D-dimer not validated in high pretest
   - Use only with Wells ≤ 4

🚩 PERC is complement, not standalone
   - Only applicable in low pretest + clinician gestalt &lt; 15%
   - Fails in pregnancy, cancer, ICU

🚩 Massive PE can present atypically
   - Syncope alone may be only sign
   - Have low threshold for imaging in cardiovascular collapse

🚩 Document Wells in chart
   - Medicolegal benefit
   - Helps team members understand reasoning
```
