## 🚩 Red Flags (shock-the-team signs)

- **SpO₂ &lt; 90%** on room air
- **RR &gt; 30** or **&lt; 8** per min
- **Cyanosis** (central)
- **Accessory muscle use** / nasal flaring / tripoding
- **Altered mental status** / somnolent ("CO₂ narcosis")
- **Unable to speak in full sentences**
- Paradoxical abdominal breathing (diaphragm fatigue)
- Hemodynamic instability

→ **Prepare for airway management** (NIV trial or intubation)

---

## 🧠 Common Causes (differential by system)

### Pulmonary

- **Asthma / COPD exacerbation** (wheezing, prior history)
- **Pneumonia** (fever, productive cough, crackles)
- **Pulmonary embolism** (sudden, pleuritic, risk factors) → [PE pathway](./pe-pathway)
- **Pneumothorax** (sudden, unilateral decreased BS)
- **Pleural effusion** (decreased BS, dullness)
- **ARDS** (bilateral infiltrates, severe hypoxia)
- **Upper airway obstruction** (stridor) → [Anaphylaxis](./anaphylaxis)

### Cardiac

- **Acute CHF / pulmonary edema** (crackles, JVD, S3, orthopnea)
- **Acute MI** (may present as SOB esp elderly/diabetic) → [STEMI](./stemi)
- **Arrhythmia** (palpitations, syncope)
- **Pericardial tamponade** (JVD, muffled heart sounds, pulsus paradoxus)

### Metabolic / other

- **DKA** (Kussmaul breathing, fruity breath) → [DKA](./dka)
- **Sepsis** (fever, lactate) → [Septic shock](./septic-shock)
- **Anemia** (pallor, exertional onset)
- **Anaphylaxis** (urticaria, angioedema, stridor) → [Anaphylaxis](./anaphylaxis)
- **Anxiety / panic** (diagnosis of exclusion in ED)

## 🧪 การตรวจเบื้องต้น (Initial Workup)

### Bedside (first 5 min)

- **Vital signs + SpO₂** continuous
- **Rapid assessment:** speaking ability, accessory muscle use, mental status
- **Lung exam:** wheezing, crackles, decreased BS, stridor
- **Cardiac exam:** JVD, S3, murmur, edema
- **Focused history:** onset, exertion, fever, chest pain, prior episodes

### Immediate labs / imaging

- **ABG** (severity of hypoxia + CO₂ retention + acid-base)
- **CBC** (anemia, infection)
- **Troponin** (if cardiac concern)
- **BNP / NT-proBNP** (CHF vs non-cardiac cause)
- **D-dimer** (if PE in differential; low-probability only)
- **Lactate**
- **CXR** (consolidation, effusion, pneumothorax, cardiomegaly)
- **ECG** (ischemia, arrhythmia, right heart strain)

### Advanced (based on suspicion)

- **Bedside lung US** (B-lines = CHF, pleural slide = PTX, consolidation)
- **Echo** (LV function, RV strain, pericardial effusion)
- **CTPA** (if PE likely per Wells)
- **CT chest** (if mass, unclear infiltrate)

---

## 💨 Initial Stabilization

### Oxygen

- **Target SpO₂ 92–96%** (88–92% if COPD retainer)
- Nasal cannula → simple mask → NRB → NIV → intubation

### NIV indications

- COPD exacerbation with respiratory acidosis (pH 7.25–7.35)
- Cardiogenic pulmonary edema
- Hypoxic resp failure without contraindication
- Post-extubation

### Intubate if

- Apnea / near-apnea
- GCS &lt; 8
- Severe fatigue despite NIV
- Hemodynamic instability
- Inability to protect airway

---

## 🎯 Cause-Specific Actions

| Cause | First-line action |
|---|---|
| Asthma / COPD | Nebulized β-agonist + anticholinergic + steroid |
| CHF | IV furosemide, NTG if BP stable, NIV |
| Pneumonia | Empirical antibiotic within 1 hr (Sepsis bundle) |
| PE | Anticoagulation (heparin) + CTPA |
| PTX | Needle decompression (tension) → chest tube |
| Anaphylaxis | IM epinephrine 0.3 mg, IV access, steroid, antihistamine |

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Common Pitfalls)

<Callout type="danger" title="Dyspnea — Pitfalls">

**"Anxiety" as primary diagnosis in ED**
→ Diagnosis of exclusion — rule out organic first
→ PE often misdiagnosed as anxiety/panic

**COPD retainer hypoxia target**
→ Target SpO₂ 88–92% (not 100%)
→ High FiO₂ can worsen CO₂ retention in chronic CO₂ retainers
→ Don't under-oxygenate if genuinely hypoxic (life > worry)

**"Cardiac vs pulmonary" in elderly**
→ Often mixed picture
→ BNP, echo, lung US all help

**Silent MI in elderly / diabetic**
→ Dyspnea may be anginal equivalent
→ Get ECG + troponin reflexively

**PE presents atypically**
→ Syncope alone, unexplained tachycardia
→ Pleuritic pain may be absent
→ Have low threshold for D-dimer / CTPA

**NIV in vomiting / decreased LOC**
→ Aspiration risk
→ Intubate instead

</Callout>
