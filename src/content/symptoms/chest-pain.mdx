## 🚩 Red Flags — "killer 6" chest pain

Rule out these 6 life-threats first:

| Cause | Hallmark |
|---|---|
| **ACS / STEMI** | Pressure/squeeze, radiates to jaw/arm, SOB, diaphoresis, risk factors |
| **Aortic dissection** | Sudden **tearing / ripping**, radiates to back, BP/pulse asymmetry |
| **Pulmonary embolism** | Sudden, pleuritic, SOB, tachycardia, risk factors (DVT, immobility) |
| **Tension pneumothorax** | Sudden, unilateral decreased BS, tracheal shift, hemodynamic collapse |
| **Cardiac tamponade** | JVD, hypotension, muffled heart sounds (Beck's triad), pulsus paradoxus |
| **Esophageal rupture** | Severe retrosternal + vomiting (Boerhaave), subcutaneous emphysema |

### Lower-acuity but still ruling out

- Pericarditis (sharp, positional, friction rub)
- Myocarditis (viral prodrome, troponin, reduced LV)
- Peptic ulcer / GERD (burning, relieved by antacid)
- Musculoskeletal (reproducible by palpation — costochondritis)
- Herpes zoster (pain precedes rash, dermatomal)

## 🗣 OLDCARTS Assessment

- **O**nset (sudden vs gradual)
- **L**ocation (substernal / left / right / radiation)
- **D**uration (seconds vs minutes vs hours)
- **C**haracter (pressure / sharp / tearing / burning)
- **A**ggravating (exertion, deep breath, position, food)
- **R**elieving (rest, NTG, antacid)
- **T**iming (episodic vs constant)
- **S**everity (NRS 0–10)

### High-risk features

- Radiation to jaw / left arm (sensitivity moderate)
- Diaphoresis (esp with nausea)
- Exertional onset
- Prior CAD / MI / PCI / CABG
- Age &gt; 45 M / 55 F
- Smoking, DM, HT, hyperlipidemia, family hx

### Low-risk features

- Reproducible by palpation (reduces ACS likelihood but NOT zero)
- Sharp, pleuritic (less typical for ACS)
- Lasting seconds (atypical)
- Improved by NSAID (pericarditis, MSK)

## 🧪 Immediate Workup

### Within 10 minutes

- **12-lead ECG** — STAT, within 10 min of arrival
- **IV access** + CBC, BMP, **troponin** (baseline + 3 hr)
- **CXR** (r/o PTX, widened mediastinum, pneumonia)
- **SpO₂ + vitals**, cardiac monitoring
- **Aspirin 162–325 mg chew** if no contraindication (pending ACS workup)

### Based on differential

- **Tearing pain or pulse asymmetry** → CTA aorta STAT → [Aortic dissection](./aortic-dissection)
- **Pleuritic + risk factors** → Wells PE score → D-dimer or CTPA → [PE pathway](./pe-pathway)
- **Pericarditis suspected** → echo (effusion), ESR, CRP, viral studies
- **GI suspected** → lipase, PPI trial

### ECG — actionable findings

| Finding | Action |
|---|---|
| **ST elevation** (contiguous leads) | **STEMI pathway** → PCI / fibrinolytic |
| ST depression / T inversion | NSTEMI/UA workup (serial troponin) |
| New LBBB with hx concerning | Treat as STEMI equivalent |
| Deep S Q3 T3, right heart strain | Consider PE |
| Electrical alternans | Pericardial effusion / tamponade |
| Diffuse ST elevation + PR depression | Pericarditis |

## 🎯 Risk Stratification

### HEART score (low-risk chest pain)

See [HEART score](./heart-score) content page.

### TIMI risk score

- Age ≥ 65, ≥ 3 CAD risk factors, known CAD, ASA use, severe angina, ST deviation, elevated biomarker
- Each = 1 pt; ≥ 3 = high risk

### Disposition

- **Low risk + normal ECG + normal troponin × 2** — consider outpatient follow-up (or obs unit)
- **Any ischemic ECG or positive troponin** — admit, cath lab consult
- **High-risk story with negative initial workup** — obs unit + serial labs

## 💊 Initial Management (ACS-focused)

### MONA (modernized)

- **M** — Morphine 2–4 mg IV PRN (only if pain refractory; **may ↑ mortality if given early**)
- **O** — Oxygen (only if SpO₂ &lt; 90%)
- **N** — Nitroglycerin 0.4 mg SL q 5 min × 3; IV if refractory and BP stable
- **A** — Aspirin 162–325 mg chew

### Antiplatelet (after ECG/dx clear)

- **Clopidogrel 300–600 mg load** (or **Ticagrelor 180 mg load**)
- Hold until PCI vs conservative decided

### Anticoagulation

- Heparin 60 U/kg IV bolus + 12 U/kg/hr (max bolus 4000 U, max drip 1000 U/hr)
- Or Enoxaparin 1 mg/kg SC BID

### If STEMI

- **Door-to-balloon &lt; 90 min** (transfer if needed)
- Fibrinolytic (Streptokinase, Alteplase) if PCI not available within 120 min

## ⚠️ Common Pitfalls

```
🚩 Don't anchor on "MSK" or "anxiety"
   - 1–2% of discharged chest pain = missed MI
   - Document why it's not ACS (+ negative troponin × 2)

🚩 NTG test isn't diagnostic
   - Relieves esophageal spasm too
   - Relief doesn't rule in ischemia

🚩 Don't give NTG if:
   - BP &lt; 90 (may precipitate shock)
   - Recent PDE5i (sildenafil 24 hr, tadalafil 48 hr) — severe hypotension
   - RV infarct suspected (inferior STEMI + preload-dependent)

🚩 Tearing + pulse asymmetry = CTA, not ECG alone
   - Aortic dissection can cause STEMI + stroke simultaneously
   - tPA in dissection = death

🚩 Don't delay ECG
   - Goal: within 10 min of arrival
   - Recheck if pain changes / returns

🚩 Elderly and diabetic patients often atypical
   - May present as SOB, nausea, fatigue only
   - Low threshold for ECG + troponin

🚩 Troponin can be negative in first 2 hours
   - Repeat at 3 hr (high-sens) or 6 hr (conventional)
   - Single negative doesn't rule out

🚩 Cocaine chest pain
   - Benzos first; avoid β-blockers (unopposed α)
   - Still get ECG + troponin — cocaine-induced MI is real
```
