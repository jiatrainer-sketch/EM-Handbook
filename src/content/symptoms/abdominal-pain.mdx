## 🚩 Red Flags — "can't miss" abdominal pain

| Finding | Suggests |
|---|---|
| **Rigid abdomen + rebound + guarding** | Peritonitis (perforation, ischemia) |
| **Hypotension + abdominal distension** | Ruptured AAA, massive hemoperitoneum |
| **Pulsatile abdominal mass** | AAA (don't compress — may rupture) |
| **Ripping back pain + hypotension** | Ruptured AAA / aortic dissection extension |
| **Hematemesis / melena** | UGIB — [UGIB](./ugib-variceal) |
| **Absent bowel sounds + pain out of proportion** | Mesenteric ischemia |
| **Fever + jaundice + RUQ pain** | Ascending cholangitis (Charcot triad) + hypotension + AMS (Reynolds pentad) |
| **Pregnancy + pain + shock** | Ruptured ectopic |

→ **Two large-bore IVs, blood on hold, surgical consult, imaging STAT**

## 🗺 Location Guide

### Right Upper Quadrant (RUQ)

- **Cholecystitis / biliary colic** (fatty meal, Murphy sign, WBC ↑)
- **Cholangitis** (Charcot: fever + jaundice + RUQ)
- **Hepatitis** (viral, drug, alcoholic)
- **Perforated duodenal ulcer** (free air on CXR)
- **Pneumonia (RLL)** — can refer to RUQ

### Right Lower Quadrant (RLQ)

- **Appendicitis** (migratory periumbilical → RLQ, McBurney point)
- **Ovarian torsion / ruptured ovarian cyst** (sudden, unilateral, F)
- **Ectopic pregnancy** (F of reproductive age + βhCG +)
- **Meckel diverticulitis**, **cecal volvulus** (less common)

### Left Upper Quadrant (LUQ)

- **Gastritis / peptic ulcer**
- **Splenic infarct / rupture** (trauma or infection history)
- **Pancreatitis** (epigastric → LUQ, radiates to back)
- **Pneumonia (LLL)**

### Left Lower Quadrant (LLQ)

- **Diverticulitis** (elderly, LLQ pain + fever + tenderness)
- **Ovarian torsion** (F)
- **Ectopic pregnancy** (F)
- **Sigmoid volvulus**

### Epigastric / Periumbilical

- **Pancreatitis** (radiates to back, ↑ lipase)
- **Peptic ulcer / gastritis**
- **Abdominal aortic aneurysm (AAA)** — pulsatile mass
- **Mesenteric ischemia** (pain out of proportion, lactate ↑)
- **Early appendicitis** (before migration)
- **MI (inferior)** — can present as epigastric pain

### Diffuse

- **Peritonitis** (any cause of perforation)
- **Bowel obstruction** (N/V, distension, absent BM + flatus)
- **Ischemic colitis**
- **Gastroenteritis** (usually benign, self-limited)
- **DKA** (mimicker; check glucose, ketones) → [DKA](./dka)

## 🧪 การตรวจเบื้องต้น (Initial Workup)

### Bedside

- **Full vitals** + orthostatic if stable
- **Abdominal exam:** inspection, auscultation, palpation (gentle), percussion
  - Look for: distension, scars, visible pulsation, rigidity
  - Murphy sign, McBurney point, Rovsing, psoas, obturator
  - DRE if bleeding / obstruction suspected
- **Pelvic exam** (F) + βhCG
- **Rectal exam** (bleeding, mass)

### Initial labs

- **CBC** (WBC ↑ in infection, Hb for bleeding)
- **BMP** (AKI in hypovolemia, glucose)
- **Lactate** (ischemia — bowel, AAA)
- **LFT** (biliary, hepatic)
- **Lipase** (pancreatitis)
- **Coags** (if bleeding or surgery)
- **βhCG** (reproductive-age F)
- **Type and screen** if bleeding or surgical
- **Urinalysis** (UTI, ketones, hematuria — stone)
- **ABG** (severe sepsis, ischemia)
- **Troponin** (if epigastric + risk → r/o MI)

### Imaging — choose based on differential

| Suspected | First imaging |
|---|---|
| Appendicitis | CT abd/pelvis with contrast; US in kids/pregnant |
| Cholecystitis | US RUQ |
| Obstruction | Upright CXR + abd (free air, air-fluid levels) → CT |
| AAA | CT angiography |
| Mesenteric ischemia | CT angiography |
| Ectopic | Transvaginal US + βhCG |
| Pancreatitis | Clinical + lipase; CT if unclear or complicated |

## 💊 Initial Stabilization

- **IV × 2 large-bore** if unstable or surgical suspicion
- **NSS 500 mL–1 L bolus** if hypovolemic
- **NPO** (anticipate surgery)
- **Analgesia:**
  - Low-risk: PO NSAID or acetaminophen
  - Moderate: IV acetaminophen, tramadol
  - Severe: morphine 2–4 mg IV or fentanyl 25–50 mcg (pain relief does NOT mask diagnosis — old myth)
- **Antiemetic** (ondansetron 4–8 mg IV)
- **Antibiotic early** if:
  - Sepsis signs
  - Peritonitis
  - Cholangitis, pyelonephritis
  - e.g., **ceftriaxone 2 g IV + metronidazole 500 mg IV** (intra-abdominal empirical)

## ⚠️ Special populations

### Elderly

- Atypical presentations (no fever despite sepsis)
- Lower threshold for imaging
- Mesenteric ischemia, diverticulitis, strangulated hernia more common

### Pregnant

- Normal physiology shifts appendix position (appendicitis may be atypical)
- Avoid ionizing radiation if possible (US first, MRI second)
- βhCG urgently — ectopic, HELLP

### Immunosuppressed

- Attenuated signs (no fever, minimal tenderness despite perforation)
- Low threshold for imaging + early empiric antibiotic
- Neutropenic enterocolitis (typhlitis)

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Common Pitfalls)

```
🚩 "Soft belly" in peritonitis
   - Elderly / steroid users may not develop classic rigidity
   - CT when clinical suspicion despite benign exam

🚩 Pain relief doesn't mask diagnosis
   - Old teaching debunked — adequate analgesia is humane
   - Surgeon can re-examine with analgesia

🚩 Don't forget βhCG
   - Any reproductive-age female with abdominal pain
   - Ectopic is missed ~5% of time

🚩 Pulsatile mass + hypotension = AAA
   - Do NOT palpate deeply (may rupture)
   - CTA immediately; vascular surgery STAT

🚩 Pain out of proportion to exam
   - Mesenteric ischemia classic
   - Check lactate + CTA
   - High mortality if delayed

🚩 Epigastric pain in elderly
   - Rule out MI (ECG + troponin)
   - Especially if risk factors

🚩 DKA mimics acute abdomen
   - Check glucose + ketones + anion gap
   - Kussmaul respirations, fruity breath

🚩 Plain XR has low yield
   - Useful only for free air / obstruction
   - Don't delay CT if suspicion high

🚩 Negative CT doesn't rule out early appendicitis
   - Repeat if persistent symptoms
   - Serial exam + labs
```
