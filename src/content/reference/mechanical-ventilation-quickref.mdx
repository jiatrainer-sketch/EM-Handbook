## 🎯 Initial Settings by Indication

### Default "safe" initial settings
- **Mode**: Volume Control (AC/VC) or PRVC
- **Vt**: 6–8 mL/kg **IBW** (not actual weight)
- **RR**: 14–18
- **PEEP**: 5 cmH₂O
- **FiO₂**: 100% initially → titrate down to maintain SpO₂ 92–96%
- **I:E**: 1:2

### IBW formulas
- **Male IBW** = 50 + 2.3 × (height inches − 60)
- **Female IBW** = 45.5 + 2.3 × (height inches − 60)
- Or shortcut: **height(cm) − 105** (rough estimate)

## 📋 Scenario-Based Settings

### ARDS (P/F ≤ 300)

<Callout type="danger" title="ARDSNet Lung-Protective (LPV)">

- **Vt 4–6 mL/kg IBW**
- **Plateau pressure ≤ 30 cmH₂O**
- **PEEP**: high PEEP strategy (ARDSNet table or ≥ 10)
- **FiO₂ + PEEP** per ARDSNet table:
  - Mild P/F 200–300: FiO₂ 0.5, PEEP 8
  - Moderate P/F 100–200: FiO₂ 0.7, PEEP 12
  - Severe P/F &lt; 100: FiO₂ 1.0, PEEP 18–24 + prone
- **Prone positioning** for severe (&lt; 150): 16 hr/day
- Permissive hypercapnia (pH &gt; 7.20 OK)
- Deep sedation ± paralysis first 48 hr if severe

</Callout>

### Obstructive (COPD / Asthma)
- **Low RR** 10–12 (long expiratory time to avoid breath-stacking)
- **I:E 1:3 to 1:5**
- **PEEP 5** (low; some argue matching intrinsic PEEP)
- **FiO₂** as needed
- **Vt** 8 mL/kg IBW
- Permissive hypercapnia (pH 7.20–7.25 OK)
- Monitor **auto-PEEP**: expiratory hold; disconnect briefly if severe hemodynamic compromise

### Neurologic (TBI, stroke, status)
- **Target PaCO₂ 35–40** (normocapnia)
- Avoid hypoxia + hypercapnia (↑ ICP)
- Low Vt 6–8 mL/kg IBW
- PEEP 5 (higher risks ICP → usually fine up to 10)
- Head of bed 30°

### Cardiogenic pulmonary edema
- NIV (CPAP 10) first-line if tolerated
- If intubated: Vt 6–8 mL/kg IBW, PEEP 8–10 (reduces preload + afterload)
- Avoid over-resuscitation

### Sepsis / shock + no lung injury
- Vt 6–8 mL/kg IBW, PEEP 5
- Titrate FiO₂ to SpO₂ 92–96%
- Lung-protective even in non-ARDS

## 📊 ABG Interpretation Basics

### pH
- **&lt; 7.35**: acidemia
- **&gt; 7.45**: alkalemia

### Primary disorder (compare pH + PaCO₂)
- ↓ pH + ↑ PaCO₂ = Respiratory acidosis
- ↑ pH + ↓ PaCO₂ = Respiratory alkalosis
- ↓ pH + ↓ HCO₃ = Metabolic acidosis
- ↑ pH + ↑ HCO₃ = Metabolic alkalosis

### Compensation formulas
- **Metabolic acidosis**: expected PaCO₂ = 1.5 × HCO₃ + 8 ± 2 (Winter's)
- **Metabolic alkalosis**: PaCO₂ ↑ 0.7 per 1 HCO₃ ↑
- **Respiratory acidosis acute**: HCO₃ ↑ 1 per 10 PaCO₂ ↑
- **Respiratory acidosis chronic**: HCO₃ ↑ 3–5 per 10 PaCO₂ ↑
- **Respiratory alkalosis acute**: HCO₃ ↓ 2 per 10 PaCO₂ ↓
- **Respiratory alkalosis chronic**: HCO₃ ↓ 4–5 per 10 PaCO₂ ↓

## 🔧 Vent Adjustments

### To lower PaCO₂
- ↑ **RR** (first — less barotrauma)
- ↑ **Vt** (only if plateau pressure allows)
- Increase minute ventilation = RR × Vt

### To raise PaO₂
- ↑ **FiO₂** (first; titrate to minimum needed)
- ↑ **PEEP** (if FiO₂ &gt; 60% or refractory)
- Increase mean airway pressure (prolonged I-time, APRV)
- Consider recruitment maneuver (moderate evidence)

### To manage high plateau pressure
- ↓ **Vt** (keep plateau ≤ 30)
- Watch for: auto-PEEP, reduced compliance (ARDS, fluid overload), stiff chest wall
- Check endotracheal tube patency, kinking

### Auto-PEEP (dynamic hyperinflation)
- Common in COPD/asthma
- Measure: expiratory hold
- Fixes: ↓ RR, ↑ expiratory time (longer I:E), check ETT, ensure adequate sedation/paralysis
- Emergency: disconnect briefly to let exhale

## 🔄 Weaning / Extubation

### SBT (spontaneous breathing trial) — daily in stable patients
- Pressure support 5–8 + PEEP 5 × 30–120 min
- OR T-piece

### Ready for extubation if
- SpO₂ &gt; 92% on FiO₂ ≤ 40% + PEEP ≤ 5
- RR &lt; 35, stable hemodynamics
- Awake, follows commands, cough + gag present
- RSBI (RR/Vt_L) &lt; 105 during SBT
- No high airway pressure
- Cuff leak present (if high-risk for stridor)

### Post-extubation
- HFNC often reduces re-intubation
- Stridor: nebulized racemic epinephrine + steroid
- Observe 24 hr for failure

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Pitfalls)

<Callout type="danger" title="Vent Pitfalls">

**Use actual weight for Vt** — over-ventilate obese patients → VILI  
→ Always IBW

**Ignore plateau pressure** — Pplat &gt; 30 → barotrauma  
→ ↓ Vt; if still high, reconsider ARDS management

**High PEEP always better** — no, in hypovolemic → low CO  
→ Titrate individually; ensure fluid status

**Miss auto-PEEP** — worsening hemodynamics on vent  
→ Expiratory hold; disconnect briefly + lower RR

**Rapid weaning without SBT** — failure, re-intubation  
→ Formal SBT + checklist before extubation

**Forget sedation vacation** — accumulate → prolonged vent  
→ Daily awakening trial if not contraindicated

</Callout>

## 📝 Quick-ref card

```
Initial vent (general):
  Mode: VC (or PRVC)
  Vt: 8 mL/kg IBW (6 mL/kg in ARDS)
  RR: 14–18 (10–12 in COPD/asthma)
  PEEP: 5 (ARDS: high; obstructive: low)
  FiO₂: 100% → titrate ↓
  I:E: 1:2 (1:3–1:5 in obstructive)

ARDSNet escalation:
  Low P/F (<150): Vt 4 mL/kg + PEEP 12–18 + PRONE

Targets:
  Pplat ≤ 30 cmH₂O
  pH ≥ 7.20
  SpO₂ 92–96%

Adjust PaCO₂: change RR first
Adjust PaO₂: change FiO₂ (then PEEP if > 60%)
```
