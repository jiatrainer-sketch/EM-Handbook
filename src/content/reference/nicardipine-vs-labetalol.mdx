## 🎯 Quick Choice

| Scenario | 1st choice | Alternative |
|---|---|---|
| **Hypertensive emergency (general)** | Nicardipine | Labetalol |
| **Ischemic stroke** | Nicardipine | Labetalol |
| **Hemorrhagic stroke (ICH/SAH)** | Nicardipine | Labetalol |
| **Aortic dissection** | **Labetalol** (β-blocker first!) | Esmolol; then add nicardipine after β |
| **Pregnancy (pre-eclampsia/eclampsia)** | **Labetalol** | Hydralazine |
| **Asthma / COPD exacerbation** | **Nicardipine** (avoid β) | Hydralazine |
| **Acute CHF / pulmonary edema** | **Nicardipine** + NTG | (avoid β during decompensation) |

| **Hypertensive encephalopathy** | Nicardipine | Labetalol |
| **Bradycardia** | Nicardipine | Hydralazine |
| **Tachycardia (want HR control)** | Labetalol / esmolol | |

## 💊 Nicardipine

### Mechanism
- **Calcium channel blocker** (dihydropyridine)
- Vasodilator — peripheral + cerebral + coronary
- **Minimal ↓ HR, minimal ↓ contractility** (unlike verapamil/diltiazem)
- No reflex tachycardia at therapeutic doses

### Pharm
- Onset: 5–15 min
- Peak: 30–60 min
- Half-life: 2–4 hr
- Metabolism: hepatic

### Dose
- **Start: 5 mg/hr IV infusion**
- **Titrate: ↑ 2.5 mg/hr q 5–15 min** (can be faster for urgent: q 5 min)
- **Max: 15 mg/hr**
- Once stable → maintain or transition to PO (amlodipine)

### Preparation
- **25 mg in 250 mL NS or D5W** = 0.1 mg/mL (100 mcg/mL)
- **Large vein** preferred (phlebitis with peripheral > 12 hr) → move to central if possible

### Advantages
- Predictable dose-response
- Maintains cerebral autoregulation
- Safe in asthma, CHF (no negative inotropy)
- Titratable drip, short half-life

### Side effects
- Reflex tachycardia (mild at therapeutic)
- Headache, flushing
- Hypotension (titrate carefully)
- Phlebitis with peripheral IV > 12 hr

## 💊 Labetalol

### Mechanism
- **Mixed α1 + β1 + β2 blocker** (ratio β:α ~ 7:1 IV)
- ↓ HR, ↓ contractility, ↓ SVR
- Minimal reflex tachycardia
- No change in CO in most cases

### Pharm
- Onset: 2–5 min
- Peak: 5–15 min
- Half-life: 5–8 hr (LONG for IV — consideration)
- Metabolism: hepatic

### Dose
- **Bolus: 10–20 mg IV over 2 min**
  - Repeat **40 mg → 80 mg q 10 min** PRN
  - **Max cumulative: 300 mg**
- **Infusion: 1–2 mg/min IV** (titrate up to 8 mg/min)
  - Or 0.5–2 mg/kg/hr
- Prep: 200 mg in 200 mL D5W = 1 mg/mL

### Advantages
- α + β combined — good for aortic dissection (lowers dP/dt)
- Long history, widely available, cheap
- Safe in pregnancy
- Maintains cerebral perfusion

### Contraindications
- **Asthma / COPD** (β2 blockade)
- **Bradycardia (< 55 bpm)** or heart block
- **Acute CHF / decompensated**
- **Severe peripheral vascular disease**
- **Cocaine / MA intoxication** (unopposed α → may worsen HTN)

### Side effects
- Bradycardia
- Bronchospasm
- Scalp tingling (common, benign)
- Orthostatic hypotension
- Hepatotoxicity (rare)

## 🧭 Clinical Decision Guide

### "My patient is stroking out"
- **Ischemic stroke**: don't lower BP aggressively unless >220/120 (or 185/110 if giving tPA)
  - Use nicardipine (smooth titration)
- **Hemorrhagic stroke (ICH)**: target SBP 140–160 (some institutions 180)
  - Nicardipine first; labetalol OK

### "My patient has chest pain radiating to back"
- **Aortic dissection** — target SBP 100–120, HR 60
- **β-blocker FIRST** (labetalol or esmolol) to reduce dP/dt
- **Then add vasodilator** (nicardipine) if still HTN
- Never give vasodilator alone → reflex tachycardia → worsens shear force

### "My patient has severe HTN + dyspnea + crackles"
- **Acute pulmonary edema + HTN** (SCAPE)
- NTG + diuretic primary
- Nicardipine if afterload reduction needed
- Avoid labetalol (β-blocker in decompensated CHF)

### "My patient is pregnant"
- **Labetalol 1st** (safe in pregnancy, extensive data)
- Hydralazine 2nd line
- Avoid nitroprusside (cyanide toxicity to fetus)
- Avoid ACE-I / ARB

### "My patient used meth / cocaine"
- Phentolamine (α-blocker)
- Nicardipine
- Benzo (lorazepam 2–4 mg IV)
- **AVOID pure β-blocker** → unopposed α → worse

### "My patient is bradycardic"
- Nicardipine (no negative chronotropy)
- Hydralazine
- AVOID labetalol

### "My patient is asthmatic"
- Nicardipine (no β-blockade)
- AVOID labetalol / esmolol

## 📝 Drip setup cheat sheet

### Nicardipine
```
Mix: 25 mg in 250 mL NS = 100 mcg/mL
Start: 5 mg/hr = 50 mL/hr
Titrate: + 2.5 mg/hr (= 25 mL/hr) q 5 min
Max: 15 mg/hr (150 mL/hr)
Monitor: BP q 5 min during titration, A-line ideal
Line: large vein or central (> 12 hr)
```

### Labetalol
```
Bolus: 10–20 mg IV over 2 min, repeat q 10 min PRN
Cumulative max: 300 mg
Drip: 200 mg in 200 mL D5W = 1 mg/mL
Start: 1 mg/min (60 mL/hr)
Titrate: up to 8 mg/min
Monitor: BP, HR, resp (bronchospasm)
```

## ⚠️ Pitfalls

```
🚩 Labetalol in asthma / COPD exacerbation
   - β2 block → bronchospasm
   - Use nicardipine

🚩 Nicardipine alone in aortic dissection
   - Reflex tachycardia → ↑ dP/dt → worse shear
   - Always β-block FIRST, then add vasodilator

🚩 Aggressive BP lowering in ischemic stroke
   - Maintain perfusion — don't drop BP unless > 220/120
   - Or 185/110 if tPA candidate

🚩 Pure β-blocker in cocaine / meth overdose
   - Unopposed α → coronary vasospasm + HTN
   - Use phentolamine, nicardipine, benzo

🚩 Peripheral nicardipine > 12 hr
   - Phlebitis — move to central or rotate site

🚩 Abrupt stop of labetalol in chronic use
   - Rebound HTN/tachycardia
   - Taper if on chronic therapy
```
