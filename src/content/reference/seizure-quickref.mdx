## 🎯 ILAE 2017 Classification

### Focal (partial) — one hemisphere onset
- **Focal aware**: conscious, motor/sensory/autonomic/psychic
- **Focal impaired awareness**: confused/unresponsive during
- **Focal to bilateral tonic-clonic**: spreads to become generalized

### Generalized — bilateral from onset
- **Tonic-clonic** (classic "grand mal")
- **Absence** (staring, brief, common in kids)
- **Myoclonic** (sudden jerks)
- **Atonic** (drop attacks)
- **Tonic** (stiffening only)
- **Clonic** (jerking only)

### Unknown onset

## 🧠 Common Seizure Mimics

| Condition | Clues |
|---|---|
| **Syncope** | Pre-syncope, pale, warm, brief, quick recovery |
| **Psychogenic non-epileptic seizures (PNES)** | Prolonged, pelvic thrusting, eyes closed, side-to-side head, post-event distress, no postictal confusion |
| **Convulsive syncope** | Brief myoclonus at end of syncope; no true seizure |
| **Migraine with aura** | Headache, visual scintillations, photophobia |
| **TIA** | Focal negative symptoms, vascular territory |
| **Movement disorders** | Repetitive, stereotyped, no AMS |
| **Cataplexy** (narcolepsy) | Triggered by emotion, brief loss of tone, no LOC |

## 🔍 First-time Seizure Workup

### History
- Aura / prodrome?
- Duration, type of movements
- Tongue bite (lateral = seizure; tip = non-seizure)
- Urinary incontinence
- Postictal period, confusion, Todd's paralysis
- Triggers: sleep deprivation, alcohol, drugs, fever, flashing lights
- Past seizures, family history

### Physical
- Neuro exam (focal deficit → structural lesion)
- Skin (neurocutaneous syndromes — NF, tuberous sclerosis)
- Trauma from seizure

### Essential labs (all)
- Glucose (r/o hypoglycemia)
- BMP (Na, Ca, Mg)
- CBC
- Pregnancy test (women)
- Urine tox screen
- ECG (r/o syncope from arrhythmia)

### Imaging (first seizure → CT in ED; MRI outpatient)
- **Head CT in ED if**: focal deficit, AMS, trauma, fever, cancer, HIV, bleeding dx, anticoagulated, age > 40
- **MRI outpatient** for all first unprovoked seizures (structural)

### EEG
- **Urgent EEG** if prolonged AMS (r/o non-convulsive status)
- **Outpatient EEG** for new seizure workup

## 💊 When to Start AED After First Seizure?

### Not automatically — consider if:
- **Structural lesion** (tumor, stroke, AVM) — ↑ recurrence risk
- **Abnormal EEG** (epileptiform)
- **Nocturnal seizure** (higher recurrence)
- **Status epilepticus** as first presentation
- **Family history**, prior brain injury

### Often no AED if:
- First unprovoked, normal workup
- Normal EEG, no structural lesion
- Provoked (alcohol withdrawal, drug, hypoglycemia, metabolic) — treat cause

### AED choice (outpatient, discussion)

| Seizure type | First-line |
|---|---|
| **Focal (incl. 2° gen)** | Lamotrigine, Levetiracetam, Oxcarbazepine |
| **Generalized tonic-clonic** | Levetiracetam, Valproate (avoid in pregnancy), Lamotrigine |
| **Absence** | Ethosuximide (first-line pediatric) or Valproate |
| **Myoclonic / JME** | Valproate, Levetiracetam |
| **Status (acute)** | Lorazepam → fosphenytoin/levetiracetam → see status protocol |

---

## 🤰 Pregnancy + AED

- **Valproate contraindicated** (teratogenic: neural tube, ↓ IQ)
- **Preferred**: levetiracetam, lamotrigine (consider levels q trimester)
- **Folate 4 mg daily** (all women of childbearing age on AED)
- Vitamin K 10 mg daily in last month (if on enzyme inducer)

## 🛑 When to worry (red flags)

<Callout type="danger" title="Seizure red flags — urgent workup">

- **Status epilepticus** (&gt; 5 min continuous or recurrent without recovery)
- **New focal deficit** post-seizure (Todd's or structural)
- **Fever + seizure** + AMS (meningitis, encephalitis)
- **Pregnancy** (eclampsia until proven otherwise — give magnesium)
- **Head trauma** preceding
- **Non-convulsive status** — prolonged AMS without overt seizure activity

</Callout>

---

## 🎯 Eclampsia = Different Management

Seizure + pregnancy + HTN + proteinuria or after-delivery:
- **Magnesium sulfate** 4–6 g IV over 15–20 min → 1–2 g/hr
- Control BP (SBP &lt; 160)
- Delivery definitive
- Benzodiazepine if refractory to Mg

## 📝 ER workflow (new seizure)

```
New seizure — ER workflow

Active seizure?  →  see status-epilepticus protocol
Post-ictal?

History:
  □ Witness description
  □ Aura, duration, type
  □ Tongue bite, incontinence
  □ Postictal confusion
  □ Medications, drugs, alcohol
  □ Fever, infection
  □ Head trauma
  □ Prior seizures, family hx

Exam:
  □ Vitals including glucose
  □ Full neuro
  □ Skin (neurocutaneous)
  □ Trauma secondary

Labs:
  □ Glucose stat
  □ BMP, Ca, Mg
  □ CBC, UA
  □ Pregnancy
  □ Urine tox
  □ ECG
  □ (AED level if on drug)

Imaging:
  □ Head CT in ED (per criteria)
  □ MRI outpatient

Consult neurology:
  □ New-onset unprovoked seizure (most cases)
  □ Status
  □ Focal deficit

AED:
  □ Start if: structural, status, EEG+, high recurrence risk
  □ Hold if: provoked, normal workup

Discharge:
  □ Driving restrictions (check local laws)
  □ Return precautions
  □ Follow-up 1–2 wk neuro
  □ Medic alert
```
