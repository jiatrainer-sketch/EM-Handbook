## 🚩 SNOOP10 — Red Flags in Headache

<Callout type="danger" title="Red flags = workup for secondary cause">

- **S** — **S**ystemic symptoms (fever, weight loss, cancer, HIV)
- **S** — **S**econdary risk factors (cancer, HIV, trauma, anticoag)
- **N** — **N**eurologic signs (focal deficit, AMS, CN palsy, papilledema)
- **O** — **O**nset sudden ("**thunderclap**" — peak in &lt; 1 min)
- **O** — **O**lder age (&gt; 50 years new-onset)
- **P** — **P**attern change (different from prior headaches)
- **P** — **P**apilledema
- **P** — **P**osture-related (worse lying down → IIH; worse upright → low CSF)
- **P** — **P**ainkiller-related (medication overuse)
- **P** — **P**ulsatile tinnitus + new
- **P** — **P**recipitated by Valsalva / exertion / sex
- **P** — **P**ostpartum (CVT, PRES, eclampsia)
- **P** — **P**regnancy (eclampsia)
- **P** — **P**athology of the eye (acute glaucoma — vision loss + pain)
- **P** — **P**ain + immunosuppression (CNS infection, lymphoma)

</Callout>

## 🧪 Essential Workup by Red Flag

### Thunderclap headache (onset &lt; 1 min)
- **Subarachnoid hemorrhage** first — until proven otherwise
- **Non-contrast CT head** immediately (sensitivity ~100% if &lt; 6 hr)
- **LP** if CT negative + presented &gt; 6 hr after onset (xanthochromia)
- Differential: RCVS, CVT, pituitary apoplexy, carotid dissection, third ventricle colloid cyst

### Focal neuro deficit
- **MRI + MRA/CTA** (or CT if urgent)
- Stroke protocol if acute — see stroke-pathway
- Mass lesion workup

### Fever + headache + AMS
- **LP** (after CT if any concern for ↑ ICP or focal deficit)
- Empirical antibiotics + acyclovir for meningitis/encephalitis

### New headache + age &gt; 50
- **Giant cell arteritis** (temporal arteritis) screen:
  - ESR, CRP
  - Temporal artery biopsy
  - **Start prednisolone 1 mg/kg/day immediately** if suspicion high (before biopsy — don't delay!)
  - Risk: vision loss if untreated (ischemic optic neuropathy)

### Postpartum headache
- Cerebral venous thrombosis (CVT) — MRV
- Posterior reversible encephalopathy syndrome (PRES) — MRI
- Eclampsia (late presentation up to 4 wk)
- Post-dural puncture (if epidural)

### Pulsatile tinnitus + new headache
- **Cerebral venous thrombosis** — MRV
- Idiopathic intracranial hypertension — LP + ophthalmology
- Vascular lesion (AVM, dural AVF)

### Morning headache + posture worse
- **IIH** (papilledema, obese young women) — LP + MRV
- Chronic migraine
- Sinus pathology

### Worse with Valsalva / exertion
- Chiari malformation
- Colloid cyst of third ventricle
- RCVS

### Immunocompromised (HIV, transplant, chemo)
- CNS lymphoma, toxoplasmosis, cryptococcus
- MRI + LP (after CT)

## 💊 Primary Headache — after red flags excluded

### Migraine — acute
- **Triptans** (if CV safe): sumatriptan 6 mg SC / 50–100 mg PO
- **NSAIDs** (ibuprofen 600–800 mg, naproxen 500 mg)
- **Metoclopramide 10 mg IV + diphenhydramine 25 mg IV** (avoids dystonic reaction)
- **Prochlorperazine 10 mg IV**
- **Dexamethasone 10 mg IV** — prevent recurrence
- IV fluids for dehydration
- Ketorolac 30 mg IV
- Avoid opioids (≥ ED morbidity; rebound headache)

### Cluster headache
- **100% O₂ 12–15 L/min via non-rebreather × 15 min** (lifesaving)
- **Sumatriptan 6 mg SC** (fast onset)
- Prednisolone bridge + verapamil long-term prevention

### Tension-type
- Acetaminophen, NSAIDs
- Stress management, relaxation

### Medication overuse
- Detox (remove offending drug); bridge with naproxen 500 mg BID × weeks
- Start prophylaxis

## 🚨 Catastrophic Don't-Miss Dx

### Subarachnoid hemorrhage (SAH)
- Thunderclap; worst headache of life
- Rx: neuro/surgery; nimodipine; BP control

### Meningitis
- Fever, neck stiffness, photophobia, AMS, rash
- LP; abx + acyclovir before imaging if no red flag for ↑ ICP

### Carotid / vertebral dissection
- Neck pain + Horner's; hemispheric / posterior symptoms
- CTA or MRA neck; antiplatelet vs AC

### Giant cell arteritis
- &gt; 50, temporal tenderness, jaw claudication, vision change
- ESR, CRP, biopsy; steroid immediately

### Cerebral venous thrombosis
- Subacute headache; papilledema; focal deficit; seizure
- MRV; anticoagulate

### Pituitary apoplexy
- Sudden, severe + vision loss + CN palsy (esp. 3rd)
- MRI pituitary; steroid (adrenal insufficiency); neurosurgery

### Acute angle closure glaucoma
- Severe eye pain, red eye, mid-dilated pupil, decreased vision
- Ophthalmology STAT; IOP reduction

### Intracranial mass with herniation
- Progressive headache, AMS, focal deficit, Cushing reflex
- CT/MRI; neurosurgery

## 📝 ED workup card

```
Headache ED approach

History:
  □ Onset (sudden? < 1 min = thunderclap)
  □ Quality
  □ Pattern (new? different?)
  □ Associated: neuro, fever, vision, neck
  □ SNOOP10 screen

Exam:
  □ Full neuro incl. CN
  □ Fundoscopy (papilledema)
  □ Temporal artery (> 50)
  □ Meningeal signs
  □ Visual acuity, pupils

Red flag present → imaging:
  □ Thunderclap → CT head + LP if neg
  □ Focal deficit → MRI + MRA/CTA
  □ Fever + neck → LP (after CT if risk)
  □ > 50 new → ESR, CRP, consider steroid

Primary headache (no red flags):
  □ Migraine: metoclopramide + diphenhydramine + dexa + IVF
  □ Cluster: 100% O₂ + sumatriptan SC
  □ TTH: APAP/NSAID

Disposition:
  □ Admit if ↑ ICP, hemorrhage, meningitis, intractable
  □ Discharge with neuro follow-up + return precautions
```
