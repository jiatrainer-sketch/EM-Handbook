## 🎯 RSI 7 P's

1. **Preparation**: SOAP ME (Suction, O₂, Airway, Pharm, Monitor, ETCO₂)
2. **Preoxygenation**: 3–5 min or 8 vital capacity breaths; NC + NRB + BiPAP as needed
3. **Pre-treatment** (rare): lidocaine for ↑ ICP (controversial)
4. **Paralysis + Induction**: simultaneous
5. **Positioning + Protection**: sniffing, cricoid (avoid routine now)
6. **Placement + Proof**: laryngoscopy, tube, ETCO₂ + CXR
7. **Post-intubation care**: sedation, analgesia, vent, sedation assessment

## 💉 Induction Agents

| Drug | Dose | Onset | Duration | Hemodynamic | Use |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Ketamine** | **1–2 mg/kg IV** | 30 sec | 10–15 min | ↑ BP/HR (preserves) | **Hemodynamically unstable, asthma, shock, sepsis** |
| **Etomidate** | **0.3 mg/kg IV** | 30–60 sec | 5–15 min | Neutral | Stable patients; avoid sepsis (adrenal suppression) |
| **Propofol** | **1.5–2.5 mg/kg IV** | 30 sec | 5–10 min | ↓ BP | Stable, status epilepticus |
| **Midazolam** | 0.1–0.3 mg/kg IV | 2–3 min | 30–60 min | ↓ BP | Rarely first-line for RSI (slow onset) |

<Callout type="success" title="Ketamine = most versatile">

- Safe in shock (↑ BP), asthma (bronchodilator), TBI (not contraindicated now)
- Dissociative — maintains protective reflexes
- Avoid in: severe HTN (&gt; 180/110), acute MI, psychosis (relative)

</Callout>

## 💪 Paralytic Agents

| Drug | Dose | Onset | Duration | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Rocuronium** | **1.2 mg/kg IV** | 45–60 sec | 30–60 min | **No contraindications** (except recent sugammadex); preferred |
| **Succinylcholine** | 1.5 mg/kg IV | 30–45 sec | 5–10 min | Fast onset; short duration; **multiple CI!** |
| **Vecuronium** | 0.1 mg/kg IV | 2–3 min | 30–40 min | Slower onset; not ideal for RSI |

### Succinylcholine — CONTRAINDICATIONS

<Callout type="danger" title="Succinylcholine contraindications = use rocuronium">

- **Hyperkalemia** (or at risk: crush, burns &gt; 48 hr, denervation, rhabdo)
- **Malignant hyperthermia** history / family
- **Muscular dystrophy** (hyperK, rhabdo)
- **Myopathy**
- **Organophosphate poisoning** (AChE inhibited, prolonged paralysis)
- **Major burns / denervation &gt; 48 hr** (upregulated receptors → massive K release)
- **Severe sepsis** (up-regulated receptors in prolonged immobilization)

</Callout>

### Dose adjustments
- Obesity: **rocuronium use TBW** (total body weight) up to a limit
- Shock / low perfusion: full or slightly ↑ dose (slower delivery to site)
- Elderly: standard dose, anticipate longer effect

## 🧪 Scenario-Based Drug Selection

### Septic shock
- **Ketamine 1.5 mg/kg IV + Rocuronium 1.2 mg/kg IV**
- Avoid etomidate (adrenal suppression — controversial but prudent)

### Status asthmaticus
- **Ketamine 1–2 mg/kg IV + Rocuronium**
- Ketamine is bronchodilator
- Succinylcholine OK unless hyperK

### Head injury / ↑ ICP
- **Etomidate or Ketamine + Rocuronium**
- Ketamine no longer contraindicated (↑ CPP beneficial)
- Fentanyl 1–3 mcg/kg slowly to blunt sympathetic response

### Cardiogenic shock / HF
- **Ketamine 0.5–1 mg/kg IV** (reduced dose) + **Rocuronium**
- Avoid propofol (↓ BP)

### Organophosphate
- **Ketamine + Rocuronium** (NEVER succinylcholine — prolonged paralysis)

### Pregnancy
- **Ketamine or Etomidate + Rocuronium** (both safe)
- Sodium citrate antacid pre-op; LUD position; rapid induction

### Awake / cooperative
- **Ketamine 0.1–0.3 mg/kg IV** + topical anesthesia + mild sedation = "delayed sequence intubation"

## 🔁 Post-Intubation Sedation + Analgesia

**Analgesia-first approach (A1F)** — treat pain before/during sedation:
- **Fentanyl 1–3 mcg/kg** IV bolus + 25–100 mcg/hr infusion
- OR **Morphine** 2–10 mg q 2–4 hr

**Sedation:**
- **Propofol 5–50 mcg/kg/min** (quick titration, wakeup; hypotension)
- **Dexmedetomidine 0.2–1.4 mcg/kg/hr** (no respiratory depression)
- **Midazolam 0.05–0.1 mg/kg/hr** (long-acting; avoid prolonged)
- **Ketamine 1–3 mg/kg/hr** (asthma, bronchospasm, hemodynamic)

**Target**: RASS 0 to −2; reassess q 4 hr with sedation break if no CI

## ⚠️ Pitfalls

<Callout type="danger" title="RSI Pitfalls">

**Forget post-intubation sedation** — paralyzed but awake!  
→ Immediate propofol/fentanyl/midazolam right after tube placement

**Succinylcholine in at-risk patient** — fatal hyperK  
→ Use rocuronium if any doubt (crush, burn, denervation, rhabdo, CKD)

**Under-dose paralytic** — failed first-pass  
→ Rocuronium full 1.2 mg/kg; succinylcholine 1.5 mg/kg; no economy here

**Use etomidate in septic shock** — adrenal suppression  
→ Ketamine or reduced-dose propofol

**Ignore preoxygenation** — desaturation during intubation  
→ 3–5 min NRB + NC 15L; BiPAP if refractory; apneic oxygenation with NC

**BP crash post-propofol** — ↓ preload + ↓ afterload + ↓ inotropy  
→ Fluid pre-load + push-dose pressor ready (phenylephrine 100 mcg bolus)

</Callout>
