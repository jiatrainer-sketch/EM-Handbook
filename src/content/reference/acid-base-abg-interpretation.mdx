## 📋 Systematic Approach (5 steps)

### Step 1: Acidemia vs Alkalemia
Look at **pH**
- **pH < 7.35** → acidemia
- **pH > 7.45** → alkalemia
- pH 7.35–7.45 → may be compensated / mixed (don't stop here!)

### Step 2: Primary Disorder
Look at **PaCO₂** and **HCO₃⁻**

| pH | PaCO₂ | HCO₃ | Primary disorder |
|---|---|---|---|
| ↓ | ↑ | ↑ | Respiratory acidosis |
| ↓ | ↓ | ↓ | Metabolic acidosis |
| ↑ | ↓ | ↓ | Respiratory alkalosis |
| ↑ | ↑ | ↑ | Metabolic alkalosis |

### Step 3: Check Compensation (is it appropriate?)

**Appropriate compensation:**

| Disorder | Expected compensation |
|---|---|
| **Met acidosis** | **PaCO₂ = 1.5(HCO₃) + 8 ± 2** (Winter's) |
| **Met alkalosis** | PaCO₂ ↑ 0.7 mmHg per ↑1 HCO₃ (or PaCO₂ ≈ HCO₃ + 15) |
| **Acute resp acidosis** | HCO₃ ↑ 1 per ↑10 PaCO₂ |
| **Chronic resp acidosis** | HCO₃ ↑ 3.5 per ↑10 PaCO₂ |
| **Acute resp alkalosis** | HCO₃ ↓ 2 per ↓10 PaCO₂ |
| **Chronic resp alkalosis** | HCO₃ ↓ 4 per ↓10 PaCO₂ |

**If compensation is outside expected range → MIXED DISORDER**

### Step 4: Calculate Anion Gap (if metabolic acidosis)

**AG = Na – (Cl + HCO₃)**
- Normal **8–12 mEq/L** (varies by lab)
- **Albumin correction**: AG + 2.5 × (4 – albumin)
  - Low albumin masks AG elevation

### Step 5: In HAGMA → check Delta Ratio (Δ AG / Δ HCO₃)

**Δ AG / Δ HCO₃** = (AG − 12) / (24 − HCO₃)
- **≈ 1** → pure HAGMA
- **&lt; 1** → HAGMA + NAGMA (concurrent)
- **&gt; 2** → HAGMA + metabolic alkalosis (concurrent)

## 🧠 High Anion Gap Metabolic Acidosis (HAGMA)

### MUDPILES mnemonic

| | |
|---|---|
| **M** | Methanol → [Toxic alcohol](./toxic-alcohol-ingestion) |
| **U** | Uremia |
| **D** | DKA / AKA / starvation ketosis → [DKA](./dka) / [AKA](./alcoholic-ketoacidosis) |
| **P** | Paraldehyde / propylene glycol / paracetamol (acetaminophen) |
| **I** | Isoniazid, iron, ibuprofen |
| **L** | Lactic acidosis (sepsis, shock, metformin, ischemia) |
| **E** | Ethylene glycol → [Toxic alcohol](./toxic-alcohol-ingestion) |
| **S** | Salicylates |

### GOLD MARK (newer mnemonic)
- **G**lycols (ethylene, propylene)
- **O**xoproline (5-oxoproline, chronic acetaminophen)
- **L**-lactate
- **D**-lactate (short bowel)
- **M**ethanol
- **A**spirin
- **R**enal (uremia)
- **K**etoacidosis

## 🔬 NAGMA (Non-Anion Gap Metabolic Acidosis)

**HARD-UP** mnemonic:
- **H**yperalimentation
- **A**cetazolamide
- **R**TA (renal tubular acidosis types 1, 2, 4)
- **D**iarrhea (GI HCO₃ loss) — most common
- **U**reteroenteric fistula
- **P**ancreatic fistula

### Urine Anion Gap
**UAG = Urine Na + Urine K – Urine Cl**
- Normal or positive → renal cause (RTA)
- Negative → GI / extrarenal (diarrhea, losses)

## 💨 Respiratory Disorders

### Respiratory Acidosis
- ↓ ventilation → CO₂ accumulation
- **Acute**: COPD, asthma, opioid, CNS depression, fatigue
- **Chronic**: COPD (baseline), OSA, kyphoscoliosis

### Respiratory Alkalosis
- ↑ ventilation → CO₂ loss
- Anxiety/pain, pregnancy, high altitude, salicylates (early), PE, sepsis (early)

---

## ⚗️ Metabolic Alkalosis

### Chloride-responsive (urine Cl &lt; 20)
- Vomiting / NG suction
- Diuretics (acute)
- Post-hypercapnia

### Chloride-resistant (urine Cl &gt; 40)
- Hyperaldosteronism (primary)
- Cushing's
- Severe hypokalemia
- Exogenous alkali

---

## 🔄 Common Mixed Disorders

1. **DKA + vomiting**: HAGMA + metabolic alkalosis
2. **Salicylates**: HAGMA + respiratory alkalosis (early) / + respiratory acidosis (late, severe)
3. **Septic shock**: lactic acidosis + respiratory alkalosis
4. **Chronic COPD + diuretics**: respiratory acidosis + metabolic alkalosis
5. **Cardiac arrest**: mixed acidosis (lactic + resp) + post-arrest respiratory alkalosis (overventilation)
6. **Cirrhosis**: respiratory alkalosis + dilutional NAGMA + metabolic alkalosis (diuretic)

## 📝 Worked Example

```
Pt: DKA + vomiting

ABG: pH 7.25, PaCO₂ 22, PaO₂ 100, HCO₃ 10
Electrolytes: Na 140, K 5, Cl 95, glucose 500, albumin 4

Step 1: pH 7.25 → acidemia
Step 2: HCO₃ ↓ + PaCO₂ ↓ → metabolic acidosis, with respiratory compensation
Step 3: Winter's: expected PaCO₂ = 1.5(10) + 8 = 23 ± 2 → actual 22 → appropriate
Step 4: AG = 140 – (95 + 10) = 35 → high AG (normal ~ 12)
         Albumin normal, no correction needed
Step 5: Δ AG / Δ HCO₃ = (35 – 12) / (24 – 10) = 23/14 = 1.6 → HAGMA + metabolic alkalosis

Interpretation: HAGMA (DKA) + superimposed metabolic alkalosis (vomiting)
Clinical: treat DKA, expect HCO₃ to normalize but watch alkalosis 2/2 vomiting
```

## 🎯 Common Clinical Scenarios

### Sepsis
- Initial: respiratory alkalosis (tachypnea)
- Progression: + lactic acidosis (HAGMA)
- Late shock: mixed severe acidosis

### Cardiac Arrest
- During: severe mixed acidosis (lactate + retained CO₂)
- Post-ROSC: initial respiratory alkalosis (over-ventilation) — avoid hyperventilation
- Target PaCO₂ 35–45 post-arrest, avoid hypocapnia (↓ cerebral perfusion)

### DKA
- HAGMA from ketones
- Appropriate Winter's compensation expected
- Mixed with alkalosis if vomiting

### COPD Exacerbation
- Chronic baseline: compensated respiratory acidosis (high HCO₃)
- Acute on chronic: further CO₂ retention, pH drop
- Watch for acute decompensation: pH &lt; 7.25 → NIV / intubation

### Salicylate Overdose
- Early: respiratory alkalosis (direct stimulation)
- Late: HAGMA (interfering with oxidative phosphorylation)
- Mixed disorder = hallmark

## 📊 Quick Reference

### Expected compensation formulas
```
Metabolic acidosis:
  Expected PaCO₂ = 1.5(HCO₃) + 8 ± 2   (Winter's)

Metabolic alkalosis:
  Expected PaCO₂ = 0.7 × HCO₃ + 20 ± 5

Acute respiratory acidosis:
  Expected HCO₃ = 24 + (PaCO₂ − 40) / 10

Chronic respiratory acidosis:
  Expected HCO₃ = 24 + 3.5 × (PaCO₂ − 40) / 10

Acute respiratory alkalosis:
  Expected HCO₃ = 24 − 2 × (40 − PaCO₂) / 10

Chronic respiratory alkalosis:
  Expected HCO₃ = 24 − 4 × (40 − PaCO₂) / 10
```

### Anion gap
```
AG = Na − (Cl + HCO₃)          Normal 8–12
Corrected AG = AG + 2.5 × (4 − albumin_measured)
Δ/Δ = (AG − 12) / (24 − HCO₃)
    ≈ 1 → pure HAGMA
    < 1 → HAGMA + NAGMA
    > 2 → HAGMA + metabolic alkalosis
```

### Osmolar gap
```
Calculated osm = 2 × Na + glucose/18 + BUN/2.8 + ethanol/4.6
Osm gap = measured osm − calculated osm
Normal < 10; > 20 suggests toxic alcohol
```

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Pitfalls)

```
🚩 Forgetting albumin correction
   - Low albumin masks AG elevation
   - AG_corrected = AG + 2.5 × (4 − albumin)

🚩 Not checking Δ/Δ ratio
   - HAGMA alone vs + NAGMA vs + alkalosis
   - Changes management

🚩 Confusing acute vs chronic respiratory acidosis
   - Chronic (COPD): HCO₃ much higher for same PaCO₂
   - Acute: 1 per 10 vs chronic 3.5 per 10

🚩 Missing mixed disorders
   - Compensation outside formula = mixed
   - Always run all 5 steps

🚩 Over-correcting pH with bicarb
   - Rarely indicated (pH > 7.10 usually OK)
   - Wait for primary treatment to work
   - Risks: CSF acidosis, overshoot alkalosis, volume overload
```
