## 🩸 Packed Red Blood Cells (PRBC)

### Indications (Restrictive strategy preferred)

| Scenario | Transfusion threshold |
|---|---|
| **Hemodynamically stable, general** | Hb **&lt; 7 g/dL** |
| **Cardiac disease (ACS, CAD)** | Hb **&lt; 8 g/dL** (some target 9–10 in acute MI) |
| **Active GI bleed** | Hb &lt; 7 (restrictive — improved outcome vs liberal) |
| **Acute trauma / hemorrhagic shock** | Transfuse per clinical, not Hb (Hb lag) |
| **Chronic anemia (CKD, oncology)** | Case-by-case — focus on symptoms + hemodynamics |
| **Sickle cell crisis** | Per Hb + symptoms (often 8–9 target) |

### 1 unit PRBC
- Volume: ~ 300 mL
- **Expected ↑ Hb: 1 g/dL** per unit (adult)
- Hct ↑ 3%
- Duration of transfusion: 2–4 hr (max 4 hr for infection risk)

### Massive transfusion (trauma)
- **1:1:1 ratio PRBC : FFP : Platelets** (or 2:1:1)
- Activate MTP if anticipated > 10 units PRBC / 24 hr
- **Early TXA**: 1 g IV load within 3 hr + 1 g over 8 hr (CRASH-2)

### Cross-matching
- **T&S** (type and screen): ABO, Rh, antibody screen — valid 3 days (72 hr)
- **T&C** (type and cross-match): full compatibility test with specific units
- Emergency: O-negative (universal donor) — limited supply

## 🟣 Platelets

### Indications

| Scenario | Platelet threshold |
|---|---|
| **Prophylactic (no bleeding)** | &lt; 10,000 |
| **Febrile / minor bleeding** | &lt; 20,000 |
| **Major surgery / procedure** | &lt; 50,000 |
| **Active bleeding** | &lt; 50,000 (procedure) or &lt; 100,000 (CNS/ocular) |
| **Neurosurgery / spinal / eye** | &lt; 100,000 |
| **Cardiac bypass + bleeding** | Consider transfusion if platelet dysfunction even if count &gt; 50k |

### AVOID transfusion in:
- **ITP** (unless life-threatening bleed — destroys rapidly)
- **TTP** (can worsen thrombosis — plasma exchange instead)
- **HIT** (heparin-induced thrombocytopenia — worsens thrombotic state)
- HUS, DIC without bleeding

### Product
- **Single donor apheresis unit** = 6 pooled random-donor (200–300 mL, usually 1 "adult dose")
- Expected ↑ platelet: **~ 20,000–50,000** per apheresis unit (adult)
- Split into 2 units for smaller or pediatric patients

### Short shelf life (5–7 days) + stored at room temp → bacterial contamination risk

## 💧 Fresh Frozen Plasma (FFP)

### Indications

- **Active bleeding + INR &gt; 1.5** or PTT &gt; 1.5× normal
- **Planned procedure + INR &gt; 1.8** (or &gt; 1.5 for high-risk procedure)
- **Warfarin reversal** (if PCC unavailable) — see below
- **Massive transfusion** (1:1:1 ratio)
- **DIC with bleeding**
- **TTP** — **plasma exchange** (TPE) — FFP can be replacement fluid
- **Multiple coagulation factor deficiencies** (liver failure, DIC)
- **Undefined coagulopathy in sepsis with bleeding**

### NOT indicated for
- Volume expansion (use crystalloid)
- Nutritional supplementation
- INR correction without bleeding (unless high-risk procedure)
- Reversal of warfarin (if PCC available — faster, less volume)

### Dose
- **10–15 mL/kg** (adult: ~ 4 units = 800–1000 mL)
- Compatible ABO (RH not required)
- Raises coag factors **~ 20%**
- ~ 1 unit = 200–250 mL

### Warfarin reversal preferred
- **PCC (4-factor) 25–50 U/kg IV + Vitamin K 10 mg IV**
- Faster, less volume, less TRALI risk than FFP

## 🧊 Cryoprecipitate

### Contains
- Fibrinogen (~ 250 mg/unit)
- Factor VIII, XIII
- vWF, fibronectin

### Indications

- **Fibrinogen &lt; 100 mg/dL + bleeding** (or planned procedure)
- **Uremic bleeding** (+ DDAVP)
- **Massive transfusion** protocol (replaces fibrinogen)
- **DIC with bleeding + low fibrinogen**
- **Hypofibrinogenemia** (inherited / acquired)
- **vWD or Hemophilia A** (if specific factor unavailable)

### Dose
- **1 unit per 10 kg** body weight (adult: 10 units)
- Expected ↑ fibrinogen: 50–100 mg/dL per 10 units
- Volume: ~ 20 mL/unit (concentrated)

## 🧬 Other Blood Products

### Prothrombin Complex Concentrate (PCC)
- **4-factor PCC** (II, VII, IX, X): warfarin reversal, DOAC reversal (limited data)
- Dose: 25–50 U/kg based on INR
- Faster + less volume than FFP

### Cryo-poor plasma
- TTP (plasma exchange)

### Granulocyte transfusion
- Rare, severe neutropenia with uncontrolled infection (hospital-specific)

### IVIG
- ITP, Guillain-Barré, CIDP, autoimmune diseases

### Albumin
- Severe burn (after resuscitation phase)
- Cirrhosis with SBP, LVP > 5 L
- Not for routine volume expansion

## ⚠️ Transfusion Reactions

### Acute (during / within 24 hr)

| Reaction | Timing | Mechanism | Features | Mortality |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Acute hemolytic** | Minutes | ABO mismatch | Fever, chills, back pain, hypotension, dark urine, DIC | High |
| **Febrile non-hemolytic** | 1–6 hr | Cytokines from stored units | Fever + chills (no hemodynamic change) | Low |
| **Allergic (mild)** | Minutes–hours | Plasma proteins | Urticaria, itch (no systemic) | Very low |
| **Anaphylaxis** | Minutes | IgA deficient pt + anti-IgA | Shock, bronchospasm, angioedema | Moderate |
| **TRALI** | &lt; 6 hr | Anti-HLA/HNA Ab (donor) | ARDS, bilateral infiltrates, fever, hypotension | 5–10% |
| **TACO** | &lt; 6 hr | Volume overload | CHF, pulm edema, HTN (opposite of TRALI) | Low |
| **Bacterial contamination** | Minutes | Bacteria in unit (plt > PRBC) | Sepsis, fever, shock | High |

### Delayed

- **Delayed hemolytic** (3–10 days): anamnestic response, fall in Hb, +DAT
- **GVHD (Graft-vs-host)**: immunocompromised + non-irradiated blood — fatal
- **Iron overload**: chronic transfusion (thalassemia, SCD)
- **Post-transfusion purpura**: thrombocytopenia 5–10 d later
- **Infection transmission** (HBV, HCV, HIV, CMV, parasitic — rare with modern screening)

### Management of Acute Reaction

1. **STOP transfusion immediately**, **DON'T remove IV line** (save line for Rx)
2. **Saline bolus** (maintain perfusion)
3. **Check vital signs, assess severity**
4. **Report to blood bank**, send bag back + **patient sample** for investigation
5. **Specific treatment**:
   - Hemolytic: fluids, lasix, alkalinize urine, transfusion medicine
   - Allergic: antihistamine; if anaphylaxis → epinephrine IM
   - TRALI: supportive ARDS care (PPV, PEEP)
   - TACO: diuretic, nitroglycerin, NIV, sit upright

### TRALI vs TACO

| Feature | TRALI | TACO |
|---|---|---|
| Cause | Immune (donor Ab) | Volume |
| Timing | &lt; 6 hr | &lt; 6 hr |
| BP | ↓ or normal | ↑ |
| CVP / JVP | Normal | ↑ |
| BNP | Normal / mildly ↑ | ↑↑ |
| Response to diuretic | Minimal | Improves |
| Management | ARDS care, supportive | Diuretic, NIV |

## 🎯 Pre-Transfusion Checklist

1. **Indication** documented (threshold met)
2. **Consent** obtained
3. **ID check** (2-nurse, patient ID + blood label)
4. **ABO/Rh compatibility** verified
5. **Baseline vitals** before start
6. **Start slowly (first 15 min)** — watch for acute reaction
7. **Vitals q 15 min × 1 hr, then q 1 hr**
8. **Complete within 4 hr** (bacterial risk)
9. **Post-transfusion Hb** at least 1 hr after end

## 📊 Quick Reference

### Single unit effect (adult)
| Product | Volume | Expected ↑ |
|---|---|---|
| 1 PRBC | 300 mL | Hb + 1 g/dL |
| 1 apheresis platelet | 300 mL | Plt + 20–50k |
| 1 FFP | 250 mL | Coag factors ~ 5% |
| 10 cryo units | 200 mL | Fibrinogen + 50–100 mg/dL |

### Thresholds cheat sheet
```
Hb transfuse:
  < 7  — stable anemia
  < 8  — cardiac disease, hospitalized
  Goal Hb 7–9 restrictive (not 10)

Platelet transfuse:
  < 10  — ppx (no bleeding)
  < 20  — febrile / minor bleed
  < 50  — major sx / procedure
  < 100 — CNS / ocular / cardiac surgery

INR + bleeding:
  > 1.5 + bleeding / procedure → FFP or PCC
  Warfarin reversal + severe: PCC > FFP

Fibrinogen:
  < 100 + bleeding → cryoprecipitate
```

### AVOID transfusion
```
ITP (unless life-threatening)
TTP (plasma exchange instead)
HIT (non-heparin anticoagulation)
Asymptomatic stable anemia
```
