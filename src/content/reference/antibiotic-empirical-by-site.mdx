## ⚠️ **Always adjust to local antibiogram & culture**

Empiric choices below are starting points — **confirm with hospital/local susceptibility data and narrow once culture returns**.

---

## 🫁 Pneumonia

### Community-Acquired Pneumonia (CAP) — outpatient, mild

- **Amoxicillin 1 g PO TID** × 5–7 d (1st line, young healthy)
- **Doxycycline 100 mg PO BID** (β-lactam allergy, atypical coverage)
- **Azithromycin 500 mg PO day 1, 250 mg × 4 d** (atypicals — watch macrolide resistance rise)

### CAP — inpatient (non-ICU)

- **Ceftriaxone 1–2 g IV daily + Azithromycin 500 mg IV/PO daily** × 5–7 d
- **Levofloxacin 750 mg IV/PO daily** (monotherapy alternative)
- Duration: 5–7 d uncomplicated

### CAP — severe / ICU

- **Ceftriaxone 2 g IV + Azithromycin 500 mg IV**
- **If MRSA risk** (prior MRSA, HD pt, cavitary lesion, post-influenza): **+ Vancomycin** or Linezolid
- **If Pseudomonas risk** (structural lung dz, chronic steroids, GNR in prior cx): replace CTX with **Piperacillin-tazobactam** or **Cefepime** + continue azithromycin

### HAP / VAP (Hospital or ventilator)

Risk for MDR: hospitalization &gt; 5 d, IV abx prior 90 d, septic shock, ARDS, pre-hospitalization &gt; 5 d, dialysis

- **Pip-tazo 4.5 g IV q6h + Vancomycin** (MSSA/MRSA coverage)
- Or **Cefepime + Vancomycin**
- If high MDR risk: **Meropenem + Vancomycin ± Aminoglycoside**
- Consider anti-MRSA + anti-pseudomonal if concerns
- Duration: 7 d (IDSA 2016), longer if non-glucose-fermenting GNR

### Aspiration pneumonia
- Community-acquired: **Amp-sulbactam 3 g IV q6h** or Ceftriaxone + metronidazole (if anaerobic concern — usually covered by amp-sulb)
- Hospital-acquired: add anti-pseudomonal

## 💧 Urinary Tract Infection

### Uncomplicated cystitis (non-pregnant ♀)

- **Nitrofurantoin 100 mg PO BID × 5 d** (1st line; avoid CrCl &lt; 30)
- **Fosfomycin 3 g PO × 1** (single dose, easy compliance)
- **TMP-SMX DS BID × 3 d** (if local resistance &lt; 20%)
- Avoid: fluoroquinolones for simple cystitis (overuse, resistance, ADRs)

### Pyelonephritis — outpatient

- **Ciprofloxacin 500 mg PO BID × 7 d** (if local Ecoli resistance &lt; 10%)
- **Levofloxacin 750 mg PO × 5 d**
- TMP-SMX (if susceptible, only if proven)

### Pyelonephritis — inpatient / severe

- **Ceftriaxone 1–2 g IV daily** (1st line, widely effective)
- **Piperacillin-tazobactam** if nosocomial / recent abx
- **Meropenem or Ertapenem** if ESBL prevalence high
- Transition to PO once afebrile + improving (total 7–14 d)

### Urosepsis

- **Pip-tazo + vanco** (empirical)
- **Meropenem + vanco** if high MDR/ESBL
- Narrow per culture within 48–72 hr

### Pregnancy UTI

- **Cephalexin 500 mg PO QID × 7 d**
- **Nitrofurantoin** (avoid 3rd trimester — neonatal hemolysis)
- **Amoxicillin-clavulanate**
- **AVOID**: fluoroquinolones (cartilage), TMP-SMX (1st trimester)

## 🧠 Meningitis

### Bacterial meningitis — community-acquired adult

- **Ceftriaxone 2 g IV q12h + Vancomycin 15–20 mg/kg IV q8–12h** (empirical)
- **+ Ampicillin 2 g IV q4h** if age > 50 or immunocompromised (Listeria coverage)
- **+ Dexamethasone 10 mg IV q6h × 4 d** — give **before/with 1st abx dose** (Strep pneumo outcome)

### Post-neurosurgery / shunt

- **Vancomycin + Cefepime**
- Or **Vancomycin + Meropenem**

### HSV encephalitis suspected

- **+ Acyclovir 10 mg/kg IV q8h** (don't delay for PCR)

---

## 🩸 Sepsis — Unknown Source

**Broad-spectrum within 1 hr** of recognition. Then narrow.

- **Piperacillin-tazobactam 4.5 g IV q6h**
- **+ Vancomycin** if MRSA risk or hemodynamic instability
- **+ Anti-fungal** (echinocandin or fluconazole) if high suspicion (chronic immunosuppression, total parenteral nutrition, lines, candidemia risk)

### If meropenem level of concern
- **Meropenem 1 g IV q8h** (ESBL / severe shock)

### Always address
- Source control (drain abscess, remove line, etc.)
- Cultures BEFORE antibiotics (don't delay abx for cx)

---

## 👕 Skin / Soft Tissue Infection

### Uncomplicated cellulitis (non-purulent, likely Strep)

- **Cephalexin 500 mg PO QID × 5–10 d**
- **Dicloxacillin 500 mg PO QID**
- Clindamycin if β-lactam allergy

### Purulent SSTI (abscess / furuncle — MRSA likely)

- **Incise and drain** (most important)
- **TMP-SMX DS BID × 7 d** (MRSA CA)
- **Doxycycline 100 mg PO BID** (MRSA, kids &gt;8 yr)
- **Clindamycin** (covers MRSA if sensitive; D-test if ERY-R CLI-S)

### Severe / hospitalized

- **Vancomycin IV** (MRSA coverage)
- **+ Pip-tazo** or **Cefepime** for broad GNR coverage if systemic sx

### Necrotizing fasciitis

- **SURGERY FIRST** (debridement — diagnostic + therapeutic)
- **Vancomycin + Pip-tazo + Clindamycin** (triple coverage)
- Clindamycin specifically for **toxin suppression** (Group A Strep, Clostridium)

### Diabetic foot
- See [Diabetic foot infection](./diabetic-foot-infection)

## 🩹 Intra-Abdominal Infection

### Community-acquired (mild–moderate, e.g., uncomplicated appendicitis, diverticulitis)

- **Ceftriaxone 2 g IV daily + Metronidazole 500 mg IV q8h**
- **Ertapenem 1 g IV daily** (once-daily, good enterococci coverage)
- **Amp-sulbactam 3 g IV q6h**

### Severe / healthcare-associated

- **Piperacillin-tazobactam 4.5 g IV q6h**
- **Meropenem** if high ESBL / severe
- **+ Vancomycin** if post-op, recent hospital, enterococcus concern (esp VRE → linezolid)
- **Antifungal**: echinocandin if recurrent, severe, immunocompromised

### Cholangitis
- See [Acute cholangitis](./acute-cholangitis)
- Pip-tazo or CTX + metronidazole

### Spontaneous bacterial peritonitis (SBP — in cirrhosis)

- **Ceftriaxone 2 g IV daily × 5 d**
- Albumin 1.5 g/kg day 1, 1 g/kg day 3 (renal protection)
- Secondary ppx: norfloxacin 400 mg PO daily

---

## 🦴 Bone & Joint Infection

### Osteomyelitis — empirical (pending culture)

- **Vancomycin + Ceftriaxone** (adult)
- **Vancomycin + Cefepime** (hospital-acquired, pseudomonas risk)
- Duration: 4–6 wk (longer if inadequate debridement)

### Septic arthritis

- **Joint aspiration** (diagnostic + therapeutic)
- **Vancomycin + Ceftriaxone** (adult)
- Duration: 2–4 wk (joint-dependent)

---

## 🫀 Endocarditis

Empiric only — tailor to culture.

### Native valve — subacute (most cases)

- **Ampicillin + Gentamicin** (endocarditis dosing: gent 1 mg/kg q8h)
- Or **Amp-sulbactam**
- Step down per culture: β-lactam anti-staph vs streptococcal vs enterococci

### Native valve — acute (severe, rapidly progressive, likely S. aureus)

- **Vancomycin + Ceftriaxone** (cover MRSA + HACEK)

### Prosthetic valve

- **Vancomycin + Gentamicin + Rifampin** (MRSA + coagulase-negative Staph coverage)

### Duration
- Native: 4–6 wk (adjust per organism)
- Prosthetic: 6 wk minimum
- Consult ID + cardiac surgery

---

## 👶 Neonatal / Pediatric (quick pointers)

### Neonatal sepsis (&lt; 28 d)
- **Ampicillin + Gentamicin** (+ Acyclovir if HSV risk)
- Late-onset (&gt; 7 d): add Vancomycin

### Pediatric meningitis (&gt; 3 mo)
- **Ceftriaxone + Vancomycin + Dexamethasone**

### Pediatric pneumonia
- Amoxicillin PO (uncomplicated)
- Ceftriaxone IV (hospitalized)

## 💉 Renal Dose Adjustments (Common Drugs)

| Drug | CrCl adjustment |
|---|---|
| Ceftriaxone | None up to CrCl 10, avoid < 10 (rare) |
| Cefepime | 2 g q12h (50–60), q24h (30–50), q24h half dose (11–29) |
| Pip-tazo | 3.375 g q6h or 4.5 g q8h if CrCl &lt; 40 |
| Meropenem | q12h (26–50), q24h (10–25) |
| Vancomycin | Trough-guided (15–20 for severe infection) |
| Ciprofloxacin | q24h if CrCl &lt; 30 |
| Levofloxacin | q48h if CrCl &lt; 30, reduce dose if &lt; 20 |
| Aminoglycosides | Extended interval, trough-guided |
| TMP-SMX | Half dose if CrCl &lt; 30 |
| Ertapenem | Half dose if CrCl &lt; 30 |
| Fluconazole | Half dose if CrCl &lt; 50 |

### Dialysis
- Vanco: post-HD dose (trough-guided)
- CTX: 1–2 g q24h (both HD + CVVHDF)
- Meropenem: q12h with HD (once daily OK)
- Consult pharmacist for precise dosing

## ⚠️ ข้อควรระวัง (Pitfalls)

<Callout type="danger" title="Empirical antibiotic by infection site — Pitfalls">

**Delaying abx for blood cx**
→ Blood cx × 2 + start abx within 1 hr of sepsis recognition

**Vanco for cellulitis alone**
→ Non-purulent = mostly Strep (cephalexin fine)
→ Purulent = MRSA possible (TMP-SMX or IV vanco if severe)

**Ciprofloxacin for cystitis**
→ Overuse, resistance rising, tendinopathy, C. diff
→ Nitrofurantoin or fosfomycin better

**Missing Listeria in elderly / immunocompromised meningitis**
→ Add ampicillin 2 g IV q4h

**Dexamethasone AFTER abx in meningitis**
→ Must be with/before 1st abx dose (pneumococcal outcome)

**Anti-pseudomonal + anti-MRSA for everything**
→ Narrow once culture known
→ Each additional abx = ADR risk, C. diff, resistance

**Forgetting renal dose adjustment**
→ Especially vanco, pip-tazo, aminoglycosides
→ Check CrCl (adjusted BW in obese)

**Ignoring source control**
→ Abx fail without drainage of abscess, source removal (line, stone)

**Using fluconazole for candidemia**
→ Echinocandin (micafungin, caspofungin) if unstable or prior azole
→ Fluconazole OK only if proven susceptible + stable

</Callout>
