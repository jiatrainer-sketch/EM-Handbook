## Na⁺ (Sodium) — 135–145 mEq/L

### Hyponatremia (&lt; 135)

**Rate of correction is the key — NOT absolute value**

| Scenario | Target correction |
|---|---|
| **Severe sx** (seizure, coma) — any Na | **3% NaCl 100 mL IV over 10 min**, can repeat ×3 |
| **Mod sx** (confusion, vomiting) | 3% NaCl at slower rate |
| **Chronic, asymptomatic** | Slow correction |

### Correction rate limits
- **Max 8 mEq/L in 24 hr** (safer 6)
- **Max 18 mEq/L in 48 hr**
- Exceeding → **Osmotic Demyelination Syndrome (ODS)** — central pontine myelinolysis
- High risk for ODS: chronic, alcoholism, malnutrition, hypokalemia, liver disease

### Symptomatic hyponatremia emergency dose
- **3% NaCl 100 mL IV over 10 min** — can give ×3 (check Na after each)
- Target: raise Na by 4–6 mEq/L → stops herniation risk
- Once Na > 130 or symptoms resolved → slower correction

### By volume status

**Hypovolemic** (urine Na &lt; 20 if non-renal loss, > 20 if renal):
- GI loss, renal loss (diuretics), third-spacing
- **Treat**: NS infusion (slow), treat underlying cause

**Euvolemic** (SIADH most common — urine osm > 100, urine Na > 40):
- SIADH (CNS, pulmonary, drug), hypothyroidism, adrenal insufficiency, psychogenic polydipsia
- **Treat SIADH**: fluid restrict 800–1000 mL/day; tolvaptan if refractory; NaCl tab if GI-tolerable
- Tolvaptan: not pregnant, not cirrhosis (hepatotoxic)

**Hypervolemic** (CHF, cirrhosis, CKD):
- Dilutional from RAAS activation
- **Treat**: fluid restrict, diuretic, treat underlying (CHF meds, etc.)

### Hypernatremia (&gt; 145)

- Cause: water deficit (access impaired or loss > intake)
- Usually elderly + sepsis, dementia, restrained
- **Correction: max 10 mEq/L in 24 hr** (cerebral edema risk)

**Water deficit** = TBW × (Na/140 − 1)
- TBW = 0.6 × weight (M), 0.5 × weight (F, elderly)

**Correction formula:**
- Change in Na per L IVF = (infusate Na − serum Na) / (TBW + 1)
- Free water needed slowly
- D5W (free water) + PO water — start slow

**Example**: 70 kg M, Na 160, target 150 in 24 hr
- TBW = 42 L
- Water deficit = 42 × (160/140 − 1) = 6 L
- Replace 6 L over 48 hr = 125 mL/hr D5W

## K⁺ (Potassium) — 3.5–5.0 mEq/L

### Hypokalemia (&lt; 3.5)

**1 mEq/L drop ≈ total body deficit of 200–400 mEq**

| K level | Replacement |
|---|---|
| **3.0–3.5** (mild) | KCl 40–60 mEq PO divided (20 mEq TID) |
| **2.5–3.0** (mod) | KCl 40 mEq PO + 10–20 mEq/hr IV |
| **&lt; 2.5 or symptomatic** | KCl 10–20 mEq/hr IV (peripheral max 10; central max 20) + PO |

### IV replacement rules
- **Peripheral: max 10 mEq/hr, max 40 mEq/L concentration** (phlebitis, pain)
- **Central: max 20 mEq/hr, higher concentration OK**
- **ECG monitor if &gt; 10 mEq/hr** (U waves, flattened T)
- Always replace **Mg simultaneously** (Mg deficit → renal K wasting)

### Causes
- GI (vomiting, diarrhea)
- Renal (diuretics, hyperaldosteronism, DKA, RTA)
- Cellular shift (insulin, β-agonist, alkalosis)

### Hyperkalemia (&gt; 5.0)

**EMERGENCY if:**
- K &gt; 6.5, or K &gt; 5.5 with ECG changes (peaked T, QRS wide, sine wave)

### Treatment ladder (sequentially, not all at once)

**1. Stabilize myocardium (immediate)**
- **Calcium gluconate 1–2 g IV over 10 min** (or CaCl 0.5–1 g central only)
- Onset 1–3 min, duration 30–60 min
- Repeat if persistent ECG changes

**2. Shift K intracellularly**
- **Insulin 10 U IV + D50W 25 g IV** (+ dextrose drip if BG < 250)
- **Albuterol neb 10–20 mg** (higher than asthma dose)
- **NaHCO₃ 50 mEq IV** (only if acidotic — controversial alone)
- Onset 15–30 min, duration 2–6 hr

**3. Remove K from body**
- **Furosemide 40–80 mg IV** (if renal function adequate)
- **Patiromer or sodium zirconium** (newer K binders)
- **Kayexalate (SPS) — avoid if possible**, GI necrosis risk
- **Hemodialysis** — definitive for CKD / refractory

### ECG changes (progressive)
1. Peaked T waves (K 5.5–6.5)
2. Flattened P, prolonged PR (K 6.5–7.5)
3. Widened QRS (K 7.5–8)
4. Sine wave, VT, VF arrest (K > 8)

## Ca²⁺ (Calcium) — 8.5–10.5 mg/dL (total)

### Correction for albumin
**Corrected Ca = measured Ca + 0.8 × (4 − albumin)**

### Or use **ionized Ca** (best): normal 4.5–5.3 mg/dL (1.1–1.3 mmol/L)

### Hypocalcemia

**Symptoms**: paresthesia (perioral, fingers), cramps, tetany, Chvostek, Trousseau, QT prolongation, seizure

### Treatment

**Severe (symptomatic, Ca &lt; 7 or iCa &lt; 0.9)**:
- **Ca gluconate 1–2 g IV over 10–20 min** (1 g = 93 mg elemental Ca)
- Or CaCl 0.5–1 g IV (3× more elemental — central line only, irritant)
- Followed by **drip: 1 g Ca gluconate/hr** if recurrent

**Mild–moderate** (asymptomatic, Ca 7–8):
- Oral calcium (carbonate or citrate) 1–2 g/day elemental
- Vitamin D supplement
- Check albumin first!
- Correct Mg (Mg deficit → PTH resistance)

### Hypercalcemia (&gt; 10.5, especially &gt; 12)

**Symptoms**: "Stones, bones, groans, moans, psychiatric overtones"

### Treatment

1. **IV fluid** — NS 200–300 mL/hr (goal UOP 100–150 mL/hr)
2. **Furosemide** 40 mg IV after volume-repleted (NOT first)
3. **Bisphosphonate**: zoledronic acid 4 mg IV (peak 24–72 hr)
4. **Calcitonin** 4 U/kg SC/IM q12h — fast onset, tachyphylaxis
5. **Glucocorticoids** if granulomatous / myeloma
6. **Hemodialysis** for severe / refractory / renal failure
7. **Treat underlying**: parathyroidectomy, chemo, denosumab

## Mg²⁺ (Magnesium) — 1.8–2.4 mg/dL

### Hypomagnesemia (&lt; 1.8)

**Very common, often overlooked**. Causes K wasting, torsades.

### Treatment

**Severe (sx: tetany, torsades, seizure)**:
- **MgSO₄ 2 g IV over 15 min** → repeat × 2 q 15 min PRN
- Or drip 1–2 g/hr × 6–12 hr (refractory or to replenish stores)

**Moderate (Mg 1.0–1.8, asymptomatic)**:
- MgSO₄ 1–2 g IV over 1–2 hr × 1–2 doses
- PO Mg oxide 400 mg TID (loose stools — dose-limiting)

**Mild (Mg 1.5–1.8)**:
- PO Mg oxide 400 mg BID

### Causes
- Diuretics, PPI (chronic), diarrhea, malnutrition, alcoholism, pancreatitis, aminoglycoside

### Torsades de pointes
- **MgSO₄ 2 g IV bolus over 1 min** (even if Mg normal)
- Works via membrane stabilization
- Can repeat PRN

## PO₄³⁻ (Phosphate) — 2.5–4.5 mg/dL

### Hypophosphatemia (&lt; 2.5)

**Severe: &lt; 1 (significant sx)**

### Symptoms
- Muscle weakness (diaphragm → resp failure!), rhabdomyolysis
- Hemolysis, ↓ immune function
- Cardiac dysfunction, CNS sx
- **Refeeding syndrome** — classic trigger

### Treatment

**Severe (&lt; 1.0 or symptomatic)**:
- **K-phos** (or Na-phos if hyperkalemic) **15–30 mmol IV over 4–6 hr**
- 1 mmol = 31 mg elemental phosphorus
- Slow infusion — risk of hypocalcemia, metastatic calcification

**Moderate (1.0–2.5)**:
- 0.3 mmol/kg IV over 4–6 hr
- PO K-phos or Na-phos 250 mg QID

**Mild (&gt; 2.0)**:
- PO phos 250–500 mg BID–QID
- Dietary (dairy, meat)

### Dose-danger
- Too rapid IV: HYPOCALCEMIA (Ca-phos precipitation) + phlebitis
- Max rate: 7.5 mmol/hr peripheral, up to 15 mmol/hr central with Ca monitoring

## 🚩 Quick Red Flag Summary

```
🚩 Hyponatremia with seizure → 3% NaCl 100 mL IV × 10 min
🚩 Hyperkalemia + ECG changes → Ca gluconate FIRST
🚩 Torsades → MgSO4 2 g IV (any Mg level)
🚩 Hypocalcemia + stridor → Ca gluconate STAT
🚩 Hypercalcemia + AKI → IVF, bisphosphonate, dialysis
🚩 Refeeding risk → check phos/K/Mg before starting nutrition
🚩 Alcoholic / starved → thiamine BEFORE dextrose
🚩 MgSO4 toxicity → Ca gluconate IV
🚩 Overcorrection of hyponatremia → risk ODS
🚩 Low albumin → correct Ca before interpreting
```

## 📝 Quick reference tables

### IV limits
| Drug | Peripheral max | Central max |
|---|---|---|
| KCl | 10 mEq/hr | 20 mEq/hr |
| K-phos | 7.5 mmol/hr | 15 mmol/hr |
| MgSO₄ | 1 g/hr (sustain) | 2 g/hr |
| CaCl | Avoid peripheral | 1 g/hr max |
| Ca gluconate | 2 g/hr | 2 g/hr |
| NaHCO₃ | 1–2 mEq/kg bolus OK | — |

### Oral doses (elemental)
- K: KCl 8 mEq capsule, up to 40–60 mEq/day divided
- Ca carbonate: 500–1500 mg TID with food
- Mg oxide: 400 mg BID–TID
- Phos: K-phos or Na-phos 250 mg TID–QID
